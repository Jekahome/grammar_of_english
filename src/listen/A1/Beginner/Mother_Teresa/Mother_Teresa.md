<summary>Chapter One</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter One</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Agnes</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">There are a lot of good people in the world, but not many of them are world famous. Mother Teresa was a small, quiet nun. She died in 1997 in India, but people in many countries remember her. They talk about her and they love her. Why? Read her story. The answer is here.</p>

<p>For her first eighteen years, her name was not Teresa. It was Agnes. Her family name was Bojaxhiu. There were two children in the family before her, a boy and a girl. Then little Agnes Gonxha came into the world on the 26th of August 1910. Her mother and father were from Albania, but they lived in the town of Skopje. At that time Skopje was in Serbia. They had a good home because her father did well in his work.</p>

<p>Then her father suddenly died. Agnes was only eight years old. Her sister was fourteen and her brother was eleven. Things were difficult for Agnes's mother, but she was strong for her children. She worked and did well. She loved the children and they had a happy home. There were some poor people near their home. These people did not have much money for food, and Agnes's mother was good to them too.</p>

<p>She was a Christian, a Catholic, and God was very important to her. She prayed to God every evening with her children, and they all went to church every week. At the church there were stories about Christian missionaries in India and poor African countries. These people lived for God. They went to India and Africa because they wanted to bring God's love to poor people there. Some of the missionaries in India were men from Skopje.</p>

<p>Agnes loved hearing about the work of these missionaries. She started to think, "What am I going to do for God?" She wanted to live for God, but how? Did she want to be a nun? Did she want to live only for God? She prayed for answers. After five years the answer came to her. She wanted to live only for him. She wanted to be a nun in India.</p>

<p>Her mother was very unhappy about this. She loved her daughter and did not want to say goodbye to her. She went to her bedroom and stayed there all day. But after a day and a night she came out and said to Agnes, "Put your hand in his hand and walk with him."</p>

<p>On the 25th of September 1928, Agnes said goodbye to her friends and her home in Skopje. At the station she said goodbye to her family. She was eighteen years old and she did not see her mother or her sister again.</p>

<summary>Chapter Two</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Two</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">To India</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">She went by train and boat to Ireland. There she went to the home of the Loreto nuns. She wanted to be a Loreto nun because many of them worked in India. Agnes stayed with the nuns at Loreto House in Dublin for six weeks. Then she was on a boat again to India. She was now Sister Teresa.</p>

<p>These days you can get from Ireland to India very quickly, but in those days people were on the boat for seven weeks. It went from Ireland to Spain, then across the Mediterranean Sea to Suez in Egypt, down the Red Sea, across the Arabian Sea to Sri Lanka, and then up to Calcutta in India. From Calcutta she went by train to Darjeeling. Calcutta was a big place with a lot of noise, but Darjeeling was quiet and beautiful.</p>

<p>Young Sister Teresa did not know much English, but she was a good student. She was friendly too, and she often smiled. She was very happy there. After two years her English was good and she started to teach children in the nuns' school. Then she moved to one of the Loreto nuns' schools in Calcutta. Its name was Loreto Entally, a big school for girls. Many of the girls were from poor families.</p>

<p>The school day started early in the morning, but the nuns' day started before that. Every morning the nuns prayed for a long time. Then they worked in the school in the morning and the afternoon. They worked in the evening too, because many of the girls lived at the school. After a long day they prayed again and then went to bed. The nuns did not have holidays. They did not listen to music or go shopping. They did not see their families. But they were happy because they lived for God. They were happy and strong all day because they remembered his love every morning.</p>

<p>Sister Teresa liked teaching and she was good at it. She was a teacher at Loreto Entally for twelve years, and then from 1944 she was the head teacher for four years.</p>

<summary>Chapter Three</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Three</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">The Streets of Calcutta</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Kolkata, or Calcutta, today has fifteen million people. In the 1940s it was a very big place too, and many new people arrived every year. The people were Hindus, Muslims, Christians, Sikhs and Buddhists. Calcutta had beautiful buildings, big hotels, offices, banks and houses. Many writers and famous people lived there. But Calcutta had some big problems.</p>

<p>A lot of people were very poor. They did not have any money and they lived in slums — bad places with bad houses. Many people did not have a home. They lived and died in the streets. The years 1943 to 1946 were very bad years for Calcutta. There was not much food and it was very expensive. Poor families did not have money for this expensive food, and in those three years about three million people died. Every day there were dead people in the streets.</p>

<p>Mother Teresa wanted to help the poor people in the slums, but this was difficult for her. She was a nun, and nuns did not often go out into the streets. Many nuns helped poor people in schools, big food kitchens and hospitals, but nuns did not usually go out to poor people in slums. She prayed to God for an answer to her problem.</p>

<p>On the 10th of September 1946, she was on a train from Calcutta to Darjeeling, and the answer came to her. The answer was: go and work in the slums with the poor people. This was new work for a nun, and the head of the Catholic Church in Calcutta said, "Wait." He wanted to think about it first. Mother Teresa wanted to start quickly, but she waited two years. Then, in August 1948, a letter came from him. He was happy about her new work.</p>

<p>Loreto Entally was her home and the nuns were her only friends, but she said goodbye and moved into a small room near a slum.</p>

<summary>Chapter Four</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Four</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">The Blue and White Sari</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Indian women usually dress in saris. There are beautiful, expensive saris and there are poor saris for poor women. Mother Teresa dressed in a poor blue and white sari because she wanted to work with very poor people. Today there are many nuns in these blue and white saris, but on her first day in the slum Mother Teresa was alone. She was only one small woman with no helpers and no money.</p>

<p>How did she start her new work? First she prayed to God, and then she walked into the slum. There she talked to poor children. Many of them lived in the street. They had no home, no family and no school. She started to teach them in the street. The children liked her, and the day after that a lot of children came to her new school. Then they came every day.</p>

<p>One day there was a poor old woman in the street. She did not have a home and she was very ill. The doctors in the hospital did not want her because she was poor and very old. She was a dying woman. She did not have a family. She was alone. People in the street did not want to go near her, but Mother Teresa went to her and talked to her. She washed the woman and stayed with her. The woman died, but she did not die alone. She died with a friend, with her hand in Mother Teresa's hand.</p>

<p>There were often dying people in those streets. Mother Teresa stayed with them, and they did not die alone. Many poor street people did not have any food. Mother Teresa asked people for food for them. Some people helped her. Some people were angry and did not help. For two months Mother Teresa worked alone. It was a difficult time for her. Then two of her Indian students from Loreto Entally came to her and worked with her.</p>

<p>After two years there were twelve nuns with her. They all had blue and white saris and they had a name — the Missionaries of Charity. They worked in street schools and open street clinics. These clinics were not hospitals, but they were a big help for people in the slums.</p>

<p>People in Calcutta started to talk about Mother Teresa and her Missionaries of Charity. Many people wanted to help her and to give money to her. In 1952 the government of Calcutta gave her a big building, and she started a home for dying people there. After this the nuns started a big home for children. Many poor children in Calcutta did not have a home or a mother or father. At the nuns' new children's home there was food and a bed for them, and love too.</p>

<p>For ten years Mother Teresa and her Missionaries of Charity worked only in Calcutta. Every year there were new nuns. After ten years they started to work away from Calcutta too. They opened new homes for dying people, homes for children, schools, clinics and food kitchens in twenty-three places in India.</p>

<summary>Chapter Five</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Five</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">A Love Letter to the World</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">There are poor people in every country. In 1965 the Missionaries of Charity started working in Venezuela, South America. After that they opened a home in Rome. Then they went to Tanzania and many countries in Africa, Europe and Asia.</p>

<p>In 1969 there was a television programme about Mother Teresa and her work. It was a BBC programme and it was on British TV first. After the programme, many letters arrived for her. A lot of people wanted to work with her or give money. Then people in Europe, the US and Asia watched the programme. People in many countries wanted to help her. Suddenly Mother Teresa was world famous.</p>

<p>She did not like being famous. "God's work is not about me," she often said. And she is sending a love letter to the world. People often asked her about her work. One question was, "You are with ill people and dying people every day. Is it difficult? How do you do it?"</p>

<p>Her answer was, "I see God in every person." For Mother Teresa, love of God was the important thing. She wanted to give her love to God, but we cannot see God. We can only see God in people. She loved poor people and dying people because she loved God in them. For her, this was beautiful, not difficult.</p>

<p>She was on television again in 1979 because the Nobel Peace Prize went to her. This famous prize was $150,000 in that year — a lot of money then. Every year the prize goes to an important worker for world peace. Many important people go to Oslo from many countries for the prize day. On that day in 1979, Mother Teresa talked to them about love and peace. They asked her, "How can we get world peace?" She answered, "Go home and love your family."</p>

<p>In the 1980s there were Missionaries of Charity in a hundred countries. They were not only in poor countries. There was a home for dying people in New York too. Mother Teresa often talked to heads of government in these countries, but she always dressed in her poor woman's sari.</p>

<p>In 1990, Mother Teresa was eighty years old, but she did not stop working. Her day started at four o'clock in the morning. She worked all day and into the night. She had little sleep.</p>

<summary>Chapter Six</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Six</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">A Light in a Dark Time</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Mother Teresa died in September 1997 at the home of the Missionaries of Charity in Calcutta. At that time there were 4,000 Missionaries of Charity in 123 countries, and 3 million people worked with them. People wanted to see her and say goodbye to her. Poor people from Calcutta and people from the government of India came. Important people arrived from many countries.</p>

<p>One person said about her, "She was a light in a dark time."</p>

<p>Mother Teresa was a light for the poor people of Calcutta, but she is a light for all of us too, in every country of the world.</p>

* Какое имя было у Матери Терезы при рождении и где она родилась?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Её звали Агнес Гонкса Бояджиу (Agnes Gonxha Bojaxhiu). Она родилась 26 августа 1910 года в Скопье (Skopje) (тогда Сербия, сейчас Северная Македония).
  </details>

* Как умер отец Агнес и сколько ей было лет?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Он умер внезапно (suddenly died). Агнес было всего восемь лет (only eight years old).
  </details>

* Куда Агнес поехала сначала, чтобы стать монахиней, и как её стали звать?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Она поехала в Ирландию (Ireland) в дом монахинь Лорето (Loreto nuns). Её стали называть сестра Тереза (Sister Teresa).
  </details>

* В каком городе Индии она начала работать учительницей и сколько лет там проработала?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Она работала в Калькутте (Calcutta) в школе Лорето Энталли (Loreto Entally). Она была учительницей 12 лет, а затем директором 4 года.
  </details>

* Когда и где Мать Тереза получила ответ от Бога, что должна работать в трущобах?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  10 сентября 1946 года в поезде из Калькутты в Дарджилинг (train from Calcutta to Darjeeling).
  </details>

* Какого цвета сари носила Мать Тереза и почему?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Она носила синее и белое сари (blue and white sari), потому что хотела работать с очень бедными людьми и быть похожей на них.
  </details>

* Как называлась организация, которую основала Мать Тереза?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  «Миссионеры милосердия» (The Missionaries of Charity).
  </details>

* Что правительство Калькутты дало Матери Терезе в 1952 году?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Большое здание (a big building), где она открыла дом для умирающих (home for dying people).
  </details>

* Какую телевизионную программу о Матери Терезе показал BBC в 1969 году?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Программа называлась «Любовное письмо миру» (A love letter to the world) (по словам самой Матери Терезы).
  </details>

* Какую премию Мать Тереза получила в 1979 году и сколько денег она получила?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Она получила Нобелевскую премию мира (Nobel Peace Prize). Денежная часть составила 150 000 фунтов.
  </details>

* Что Мать Тереза ответила на вопрос «Как достичь мира во всём мире?»
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Она ответила: «Идите домой и любите свою семью» (Go home and love your family).
  </details>

* Сколько миссионеров милосердия было в мире на момент смерти Матери Терезы в 1997 году?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Было 4000 миссионеров милосердия (4,000 Missionaries of Charity) в 123 странах.
