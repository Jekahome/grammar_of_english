  <div class="chapter-title">A Story in Rhyme</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
  <p class="first-para"> This is Homer, a shepherd boy.</br>
    He loves to play and laugh with joy.</br>
    He takes the sheep to eat all day,<br>
    up in the hills, as his parents say.</p>
    <p> But as he goes to work one day,<br>
    he feels so bored and wants to play.<br>
    "What can I do? Nobody's here.<br>
    How can I play? No one is near."</p>
    <p>He looks around and tries to see<br>
    the other shepherds. Where can they be?<br>
    "I know what I can do," he says.<br>
    "I'll play a game and scare them too."</p>
    <p>So he stands up and starts to cry<br>
    to all the farmers who are nearby.<br>
    "Friends, help me. Please, come here.<br>
    I'm all alone and a wolf is near."</p>
    <p>Down in the fields, the farmers stop<br>
    and run to help on the hilltop.<br>
    But when they go up on the hill,<br>
    no wolf is there and all is still.</p>
    <p>"My son, what's wrong? What happened here?<br>
    The sheep are fine. No wolf is near."<br>
    "A wolf? There's no wolf near.<br>
    It was a joke to bring you here."</p>
    <p>"Come on, let's go. Our job is done.<br>
    Sorry, my friends, about our son."<br>
    So off they go, down the path,<br>
    and Homer starts to laugh and laugh.</p>
    <p>Sometimes you're happy, sometimes you're sad.<br>
    But if you're bored, do not be bad.<br>
    It's not very nice to have your fun<br>
    when it makes things bad for everyone.</p>
    <p>When other people worry about you,<br>
    do not play games that are not true.<br>
    It's not very nice to have your fun<br>
    when it makes things bad for everyone.</p>
    <p>Now Homer is bored. He wants to play<br>
    and not to watch the sheep all day.<br>
    "Help me, help me! A wolf is there.<br>
    He's got big teeth. He's got grey hair."</p>
    <p>The farmers stop and run up the hill,<br>
    but Homer laughs. He can't keep still.<br>
    "There is no wolf and I scared you.<br>
    It's funny, but it's not true."</p>
    <p>"Homer, my boy, you are so bad.<br>
    Father and I feel very mad."<br>
    "Homer, that's right. Why do you lie?<br>
    We won't come back if we hear you cry."</p>
    <p>People hear you scream and cry.<br>
    They run to help, so do not lie.<br>
    Be very careful what you say.<br>
    You may need help another day.</p>
    <p>Homer feels sad. He doesn't like sheep.<br>
    He lies on the ground and goes to sleep.<br>
    Now here's a wolf. He's big. He's strong.<br>
    He likes to eat sheep. His teeth are long.</p>
    <p>Homer wakes up. What does he see?<br>
    His sheep aren't there. Where can they be?<br>
    "Help me, help me! The sheep are gone!<br>
    There's a big wolf here. He's bad and strong."</p>
    <p>Down in the fields, the farmers hear.<br>
    "Help me, help me! A wolf is near."<br>
    "It's Homer again. Don't listen or run.<br>
    He's such a bad boy. He's just having fun."</p>
    <p>"A wolf! A wolf! The sheep are gone!<br>
    His hair is grey. His teeth are long.<br>
    A wolf! A wolf! It's not a lie.<br>
    It's real this time. Is that why you cry?"</p>
    <p>"We're very angry with you and your game.<br>
    Lying is bad. It's not a game."<br>
    "I'm sorry, Mum. I'm sorry, Dad.<br>
    Lies aren't funny. They're very bad."</p>
    <p>Sometimes you don't want to go to school.<br>
    You say you're ill when it isn't true.<br>
    Your mother may just feel very sad<br>
    if you're not ill but say you feel bad.</p>
    <p>It isn't fun to play a game<br>
    with people who don't feel the same.<br>
    She'll be angry and not trust you the same<br>
    because you lied. Trust is not a game.</p>
    <p>Then one day, if you are really sick,<br>
    who'll believe you? They'll think it's a trick.<br>
    It isn't fun to play a game<br>
    with people who don't feel the same.</p>

<summary>Проверить понимание текста</summary>

* Как зовут мальчика-пастуха и чем он обычно занимается?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Его зовут Гомер (Homer). Он пасёт овец на холмах, как велят ему родители.
  </details>

* Что Гомер сделал, когда ему стало скучно?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Он решил разыграть фермеров и закричал: «Помогите! Волк!» (Help me! A wolf is near!).
  </details>

* Как отреагировали фермеры на первый крик Гомера?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Они остановили свою работу и побежали на холм, чтобы помочь.
  </details>

* Что сказал отец Гомеру, когда они с фермерами не нашли волка?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Он сказал: «Простите, друзья, за нашего сына». И они ушли.
  </details>

* Что случилось, когда Гомер закричал о волке во второй раз?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Фермеры снова прибежали, но волка опять не было. Гомер снова смеялся над ними.
  </details>

* Что сказали родители Гомеру после второй шутки?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Они сказали, что не вернутся, если снова услышат его крик («We won't come back if we hear you cry»).
  </details>

* Что случилось, когда волк пришёл на самом деле?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Гомер закричал о помощи, но фермеры подумали, что он снова шутит, и не пришли. Волк съел овец.
  </details>

* Какая мораль у этой истории?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Не лги и не играй в игры с людьми, которые тебе верят. Когда тебе действительно понадобится помощь, тебе могут не поверить. (Lying is bad. Trust is not a game.)
  </details>
