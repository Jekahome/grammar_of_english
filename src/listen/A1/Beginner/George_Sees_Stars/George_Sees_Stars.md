<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Two</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">The Telephone Call</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">In the Happy Holiday Hotel, it is three o'clock. George is talking to Mary. The telephone rings.</p>

<p>"Hello. Happy Holiday Hotel. George Harris here. Yes, I am the manager. Tony who? Oh, Tony Maddock, the TV star. You're Tony Maddock's secretary. I see. Yes, that's fine. You want a large room for Tony and he wants to see the sea. Right? Yes, yes, I know. Tony is very important. A TV star. Yes, fish, you say? Yes, that's easy. Right then. Today? Tony's coming today. Fine. Goodbye and thank you."</p>

<p>George puts down the telephone.</p>

<p>"Today? Tony's coming today. Mary, listen, I've got good news."</p>

<p>"Don't shout, George. What is it?"</p>

<p>"Tony Maddock. You know, the television star, is coming to our hotel this afternoon."</p>

<p>"Oh, Tony Maddock. He's famous."</p>

<p>"He's coming here at five o'clock today."</p>

<p>"He's a TV star, you say? Is he in Neighbours?"</p>

<p>"I don't watch TV very much."</p>

<p>"Neighbours? Probably."</p>

<p>"He's going to have our best room and our best food. I'm going to look at the room now."</p>

<p>"Fine, George, but I'm busy. I'm going to the supermarket."</p>

<p>"Don't be silly, Mary. You can't go to the shops now. He is a famous man. Do you understand? An important person. Tony Maddock."</p>

<p>"I don't know him. Is he very rich?"</p>

<p>"I don't know, Mary, but he's very important. I know that. Yes."</p>

<p>"Well, I'm going to the shops now. See you later, George."</p>

<p>"But, Mary, stop!"</p>

<summary>Chapter Six</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Six</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">An Old Woman Arrives</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">It is five o'clock. A woman is getting out of the taxi.</p>

<p>"Good afternoon. Are you the manager?"</p>

<p>"No. Yes. Yes, I am."</p>

<p>"Have you got a room here for me?"</p>

<p>"A room? Oh, no. I'm afraid we're full. We are very busy this year."</p>

<p>"What? No rooms? Busy?"</p>

<p>"But I think my secretary... That's right. No rooms. We've got important people in the hotel today."</p>

<p>"But what's your name?"</p>

<p>"Margaret Adams. And I'm tired, too."</p>

<p>"Oh, all right. Come in."</p>

<p>In the hotel again.</p>

<p>"Yes, you can have a room for one or two nights. But it's small and you can't see the sea."</p>

<p>"But I want to see the sea. Have you got any other rooms?"</p>

<p>"I don't know. No. There isn't another room. We're very busy, you see."</p>

<p>"And I'm very tired. The small room's okay. Why are you busy?"</p>

<p>"A famous TV star is coming."</p>

<p>"Oh, very interesting. Do you like TV?"</p>

<p>"No. Yes. Very much."</p>

<p>"Yes, I do, too. I like films best." She laughs.</p>

<p>"Here's your key. Your room is number five."</p>

<p>"Thank you. I'm going up to my room now. Please bring my bag. It's outside in the street."</p>

<p>The woman goes out.</p>

<p>"A bag? I haven't got time. I can't take her bag to her room."</p>

<p>George carries the woman's bag into the hotel and quickly puts it in a cupboard. Mary comes in. She is drinking coffee.</p>

<p>"Who's that woman, George?"</p>

<p>"That's Miss Adams. She's in room five."</p>

<p>"Oh, that's a horrible room. It's very small. And you can't see the sea."</p>

<p>"That's right. But it is quiet. It's fine for her. She's only going to stay for one or two nights. Do you like the new light in the big room? It's nice, I think."</p>

<p>"Very nice."</p>

<p>"It's horrible, George. Red and yellow flowers. And it's very big. Is it safe?"</p>

<p>"Safe? Yes. Oh, yes, it's safe."</p>

<summary>Chapter Seven</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Seven</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">The TV Star</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">It is half past five. A young man is coming into the hotel carrying a small bag. He is wearing jeans.</p>

<p>"Oh, hello, hello. Hello. Welcome. Hello. Thank you. Thank you. Thank you." He takes the man's hand.</p>

<p>"That's okay."</p>

<p>"Why? You've got the big room. And there's a new light in there for you too. A beautiful new light. Very nice."</p>

<p>"Thank you very much. But I don't understand."</p>

<p>"Well, you're famous. You're a TV star."</p>

<p>"Me? Famous? A TV star?" He laughs.</p>

<p>"And here's the key to your room. I can take your bag."</p>

<p>"Oh, thank you. But it's not heavy. I can carry it. And I'm not famous."</p>

<p>"Yes, you are."</p>

<p>"But I'm not."</p>

<p>"Ah, I see. You're not famous. You don't want any TV cameras here. Yes, I understand."</p>

<p>"You understand?"</p>

<p>"Yes. You want a quiet holiday. You're not famous. All right. I understand."</p>

<p>"Good. Can I see the sea from my room?"</p>

<p>"Oh, yes. It's one of the rooms in the front of the hotel. It's very nice."</p>

<p>"Is it expensive? I haven't got a lot of money, you know."</p>

<p>"That's okay. I like you. I don't need the money. For you, the room is free."</p>

<p>"I don't understand."</p>

<p>"Mary. Mary. Come here. Come and meet Mr. Maddock."</p>

<p>Mary comes in. She's holding a cup of coffee.</p>

<p>"What do you want now?"</p>

<p>"Mary. Meet Mr. Maddock. This is my wife, Mary."</p>

<p>George takes Mary's arm. Her coffee goes over the young man's jeans.</p>

<p>"Oh, I'm very sorry. Your jeans. I'm sorry, Mr..."</p>

<p>"Mr. Brown. Paul Brown."</p>

<p>"I'm sorry, Mr. Maddock. Please excuse my wife. We can clean your jeans for you here. I'm very sorry."</p>

<p>"Oh, it's all right. It isn't important. I can wash my jeans."</p>

<p>"I'm sorry, Mr. Brown."</p>

<p>"Don't be silly, Mary. His name is Mr. Maddock."</p>

<p>"Er, no. My name is Paul Brown."</p>

<p>"Yes, Mary. In this hotel, his name is Paul Brown. But on TV, his name is Tony Maddock. You understand? He wants a quiet holiday." He smiles at the young man.</p>

<p>"What?"</p>

<p>"Mary, please ask Peter to take Mr. Brown's bag to his room quickly now. Thank you."</p>

<p>"Can I get another cup of coffee?"</p>

<p>"No, Mary, you can't. The bag, Mary."</p>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Eight</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">A Quiet Holiday</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">"Do you like flowers, Mr. Brown? You've got some nice flowers in your room. Yeah, from our garden, you know."</p>

<p>"Oh, no. I'm sorry. I don't like flowers very much."</p>

<p>"Fine, fine. I don't like flowers either. Mary, put the flowers in the kitchen, please."</p>

<p>"But Peter's going to be very angry, George."</p>

<p>"In the kitchen, Mary."</p>

<p>Mary and the young man go out. The woman comes in.</p>

<p>"Excuse me, but where's my bag?"</p>

<p>"What? Oh, your bag. It's here."</p>

<p>"I need it in my room."</p>

<p>"Yes, yes. I'm sorry, Mrs... But not now. I am going to take it to your room, but I'm very busy now. We have a very important person in the hotel."</p>

<p>"But my glasses are in the bag. I can't learn my new play without my glasses."</p>

<p>"Sorry, Mrs... Not now."</p>

<p>The young man comes in again.</p>

<p>"Mr. Brown, there you are. Is the room okay, Mr. Brown?"</p>

<p>"Yes, it's fine. Thank you."</p>

<p>"Good, good. Would you like anything? A drink? Some champagne? A television?"</p>

<p>"No, nothing, thank you."</p>

<p>"Please have a drink. Mary, bring a bottle of champagne."</p>

<p>Mary comes in.</p>

<p>"Champagne, George?"</p>

<p>"Yes, bring a bottle of champagne and ask Peter to put the television in Mr. Brown's room."</p>

<p>"But I don't like television and I don't drink champagne. Thank you very much."</p>

<p>"He doesn't want them, George. I'm going out for a walk near the sea now. What time is dinner?"</p>

<p>"Dinner's at half past seven, Mr. Brown. Mary, go to the kitchen and talk to Judy about dinner, please."</p>

<p>"Don't shout, George. I'm going."</p>

<p>Mary goes to the kitchen. The young man goes out. George goes back to reception.</p>

<p>"My bag, please, Mr. Harris."</p>

<p>"Ah, yes. It's here in the cupboard. You can take it to your room now."</p>

<p>"But I can't carry the bag. It's very heavy and I'm 74 years old."</p>

<p>"Oh, all right. Give it to me."</p>

<p>He goes to the cupboard and takes out her bag. They go out.</p>

<summary>Chapter Nine</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Nine</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">I Don't Like Champagne</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">In the hotel's restaurant, it is after eight o'clock. George is looking at a picture on the wall. The old woman is sitting at a table.</p>

<p>"Mr. Harris."</p>

<p>"Yes?"</p>

<p>"I'd like my dinner now, please. It's ten minutes past eight now, you know."</p>

<p>"Oh, I'm sorry. We're very busy today. What would you like?"</p>

<p>"I'd like some fish."</p>

<p>"I'm sorry, Mrs... We haven't got any fish. No, there isn't any fish."</p>

<p>"Oh, well. What about a pizza? There's pizza here on the menu."</p>

<p>"Sorry. There's no pizza today."</p>

<p>"No pizza? Well, what have you got?"</p>

<p>"Chicken. We've got chicken."</p>

<p>"All right. I'd like some chicken, please."</p>

<p>George is not listening. The young man is coming into the restaurant. George goes to Mr. Brown's table.</p>

<p>"Hello, Mr. Brown. Here's your table."</p>

<p>Mr. Brown sits down.</p>

<p>"Can I help you? What can I get for you?"</p>

<p>"I'd like some chicken, please."</p>

<p>"All right, Mrs... All right. Don't shout."</p>

<p>"I'm sorry, Mr... I'd like a glass of champagne, Mr. Brown. Would you like some champagne now? It's free."</p>

<p>"No, thanks. A glass of water, please."</p>

<p>"Please have some champagne."</p>

<p>"No, no. I don't like champagne. I'd like a glass of water, please."</p>

<p>George goes to the woman's table. He is angry.</p>

<p>"Mrs..."</p>

<p>"My name is Adams. Miss Adams."</p>

<p>"Miss Adams. We have a famous TV star here. And we're very busy. I can't do everything. Do you understand that?"</p>

<p>"Oh, yes. I understand."</p>

<p>"I am a TV star."</p>

<p>"Who? What?"</p>

<p>"You're a TV star."</p>

<p>"That's right. My name is Tony Maddock."</p>

<p>"But Tony Maddock is a man."</p>

<p>"Oh, no. Tony is a woman's name, too. T-O-N-I. My real name is Margaret Adams. But on TV, my name is Tony Maddock."</p>

<p>"What? Oh, no."</p>

<p>"Yes, Mr. Harris. And now I'm going to leave this hotel, the Happy Holiday Hotel. I don't know about happy, but I know it isn't good. And I'm not happy here. I've got a small room. I can't see the sea. And, Mr. Harris, you're a very silly man."</p>

<p>Tony Maddock goes out of the restaurant. George looks at the young man sadly.</p>

<p>"And who are you?"</p>

<p>"I'm Paul Brown."</p>

<p>"You're not the TV star."</p>

<p>"No, Mr. Harris. I'm a student."</p>

<p>"A student?"</p>

<p>He runs to the door.</p>

<p>"Miss Maddock, Miss Maddock, please don't leave. Have some champagne, please. It's free."</p>

<summary>Chapter Ten</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Ten</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">The Falling Lamp</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">In the corridor, outside the big room, the woman is talking to Mary. Mary has got the woman's bag.</p>

<p>"I'm very sorry. George is the only one. He's silly sometimes. We can move you to the big room. And the young man can have the other room. Please don't leave now."</p>

<p>In the big room, Mary and Tony Maddock are looking out of the window. George runs into the room.</p>

<p>"Miss Maddock, here you are. Please stay. You can have the room for nothing. Free." He takes her arm.</p>

<p>"No, I'm sorry. Goodbye, Mr. Harris."</p>

<p>She is standing under the new lamp. Suddenly the lamp begins to fall.</p>

<p>"George, stop. Listen. Oh! Dad!"</p>

<p>George pushes the woman. She falls over. George falls on top of her. The lamp falls on George's head. He lies there and doesn't move.</p>

<p>"Miss Maddock, are you all right?"</p>

<p>"I'm fine." She gets up. "But how is Mr. Harris?"</p>

<p>"George? George. Speak to me, George."</p>

<p>"Oh, Mr. Harris. Are you all right?"</p>

<p>They look at George. He doesn't speak or move.</p>

<p>"That horrible light isn't safe. Oh, George, can you hear me? Please say something, George."</p>

<p>"Now I understand. That lamp nearly... and Mr. Harris... Oh, what a nice man. Because of Mr. Harris, I'm safe. Thank you, Mr. Harris. Thank you, George."</p>

<p>"Champagne? What a nice man."</p>

<p>"Thank you, Mrs. Harris."</p>

<p>"Don't thank me. Thank silly George."</p>

<p>"Would you like a glass of champagne now, Miss Maddock?"</p>

<p>They go out. George is lying on the floor. He slowly opens one eye.</p>

<p>"He's very... No, she's very, very famous. She's a star."</p>

  <details>

  Пожилая женщина (an old woman) по имени Маргарет Адамс (Margaret Adams).
