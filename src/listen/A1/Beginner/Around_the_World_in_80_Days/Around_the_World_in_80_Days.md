  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">It is London in 1872. And this is Mr Phileas Fogg's house. Mr Phileas Fogg is an English gentleman. He lives in London. He hasn't got a wife or children. But he has got a French servant, Passepartout.</p>

<p>Fogg goes to his club every day. He eats at his club at 12 o'clock. In the afternoon he reads there. In the evening he eats there again. Then he talks with his friends. And at midnight he goes home. Passepartout is happy. He wants a quiet job.</p>

<p>But one evening Fogg sees something interesting in The Times. "Gentleman thief takes 5,000 pounds from the Bank of England."</p>

<p>At the club Fogg's friends talk. "That gentleman thief can't go far," says Ralph.</p>

<p>"With trains and ships, today's detectives move fast," says Flanagan.</p>

<p>"But thieves can move fast too, Flanagan says."</p>

<p>"Today you can go round the world in 80 days," says Fogg.</p>

<p>"Do it and I pay you 20,000 pounds," says Stuart.</p>

<p>"I'm leaving tonight," answers Fogg.</p>

<p>"Perhaps I can't do it, Stuart. But then I pay you 20,000 pounds."</p>

<p>The train for France leaves before 9 in the evening. Fogg must be back in London by the 21st of December. Fogg gives 20,000 pounds in a bag to his servant. "We're leaving for France," he says. Passepartout isn't very happy.</p>

<p>They go to France. Then they take a train to Italy and a ship to Port Said in Egypt. When their ship arrives in Suez, an English detective, Mr Fix, is waiting. He's looking for the gentleman thief. Fogg visits the passport office. Mr Fix sees him there.</p>

<p>"It's the gentleman thief," he thinks. "I must tell London."</p>

<summary>Chapter 2</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter 2</div>
  <div class="chapter-title"></div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Fix looks at Passepartout. "Where are you going?" he asks.</p>

<p>"Around the world," says Passepartout. "But right now I need some new shirts."</p>

<p>Fix takes him to a shop and leaves him there. Then he sends a telegram to London. "The gentleman thief is going to Bombay. Stop. Fix."</p>

<p>Later he gets on the ship for Bombay with Fogg and Passepartout.</p>

<p>It is the 20th of October. They all arrive in Bombay, two days early. At 4.30 in the afternoon, Fogg and Passepartout leave the ship. They want to get a train across India to Calcutta. It leaves at 8 o'clock. First Fogg goes to the passport office. Then he eats at the station.</p>

<p>Passepartout visits the temple at Malibar Hill. But he doesn't take off his shoes. The priests get angry. They take his shoes and Passepartout runs away. Fogg and Passepartout get on the train. But Fix stays in Bombay. He is waiting for a telegram from London.</p>

<p>After three days, the train stops. The railway from Colby to Allahabad isn't ready. Some men are building the railway. But there are 80 kilometres without a railway between Colby and Allahabad. In Colby, Passepartout gets some new shoes. He also finds an elephant to take them to Allahabad.</p>

<p>They stop near a temple in the village of Pilaji. The old Indian prince is dead. His young wife, the princess, must die with him in a big fire. "We must help that woman," says Fogg.</p>

<p>The priests start the fire. Through the smoke, Fogg sees something beautiful. The prince stands up and takes the princess out of the fire. The prince and the princess come nearer. Fogg sees that the man is not the prince. It is Passepartout in the prince's clothes.</p>

<p>"Quickly, let's go," says Passepartout to Fogg quietly.</p>

<p>Suddenly, the priests see the prince's body on the fire. They see that the man in the prince's clothes is not the prince and they become angry. Fogg, Passepartout and the princess leave very quickly on the elephant.</p>

<summary>Chapter 3</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter 3</div>
  <div class="chapter-title"></div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">At last, they arrive in Allahabad. Fogg buys some European clothes for the princess. "What a beautiful woman," he thinks.</p>

<p>On the train going to Calcutta, she speaks for the first time. "My name's Mrs Aouda. Thank you for helping me. But I don't know what to do now. I can never go home."</p>

<p>"We can take you to Hong Kong," Fogg says.</p>

<p>"Thank you," says Mrs Aouda. "I have a cousin there."</p>

<p>At Calcutta station, a policeman stops them. He takes them to see a judge. Mrs Aouda goes too. Fix is in Calcutta. Detectives must move fast to find gentleman thieves. He's waiting in court. But Fogg and his friends don't see him.</p>

<p>"Why are we here?"</p>

<p>"For breaking the rules in a temple," says the judge.</p>

<p>"In Pilaji?" asks Fogg.</p>

<p>"No, in Bombay," says the judge. And he puts Passepartout's shoes on the table. "You must go to prison for this," says the judge.</p>

<p>But Fogg gives £2,000 to the court, and they're free again. They get onto the ship for Hong Kong. Fix follows them. But Passepartout sees him. "Perhaps he's a detective," he thinks. "Perhaps he's working for Mr Stuart from the gentleman's club in London."</p>

<p>In Hong Kong, Mrs Aouda learns that her cousin now lives in Holland. "Come with us to Europe," says Fogg. Passepartout wants to buy three tickets on the Carnatic. It leaves for Yokohama in Japan the next morning. But at the ticket office, he learns that it's leaving early that evening.</p>

<p>Passepartout meets Fix near the ticket office. "Come for a drink," says Fix. Fix tells Passepartout, "I think your Mr Fogg is a gentleman thief, and I want to arrest him." Passepartout doesn't want to help Fix, but the detective buys drink after drink. Passepartout goes to sleep on the table. Now he can't tell Fogg about the Carnatic. Fix thinks, "It leaves tonight, but without Fogg."</p>

<summary>Chapter 4</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter 4</div>
  <div class="chapter-title"></div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Later, Passepartout wakes up. He goes onto the Carnatic, and the ship leaves. But Fogg and Mrs Aouda aren't with him.</p>

<p>Next morning, Fogg and Mrs Aouda go to get on the Carnatic, but it isn't there. Fix arrives. "The Carnatic's on its way to Japan," he says. "And the next ship to Yokohama leaves next week."</p>

<p>"Where's Passepartout?" asks Mrs Aouda.</p>

<p>"I don't know," says Fogg, "but we must go on without him. We must find another ship."</p>

<p>Fogg finds a small ship, the Tankadere, to go to Shanghai. There, they can get a big ship to Yokohama.</p>

<p>"Can I go with you?" asks Fix.</p>

<p>"Of course," Fogg answers.</p>

<p>The Tankadere travels through storms. And at last, arrives near Shanghai. Suddenly, they see a big ship in front of them. It's on its way to Yokohama. "Signal to that ship," says Fogg.</p>

<p>Poor Passepartout arrives in Yokohama, but he has no money. He leaves the Carnatic and finds work in Mr Batulkar's Circus. "I can go to America with the circus," he thinks.</p>

<p>Fogg, Mrs Aouda and Fix get on the big ship. And a week later, they arrive in Japan. The Carnatic is in Yokohama. Fogg and Mrs Aouda go onto the Carnatic. They find Passepartout's name in the book of passengers' names, but they can't find Passepartout.</p>

<p>Their ship leaves for San Francisco in a few hours. Before they leave, Fogg goes to the circus. Passepartout sees him there. "Mr Fogg!" he says happily. Fogg wants to take Passepartout with him. But first, he must pay Mr Batulkar.</p>

<p>At six o'clock in the evening, Fogg, Passepartout and Mrs Aouda go onto the American ship. Fix follows them.</p>

<summary>Chapter 5</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter 5</div>
  <div class="chapter-title"></div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">"I'm sorry about Hong Kong," says Passepartout. "I don't usually drink much, but..."</p>

<p>Later, Passepartout sees Fix on the ship. "My master isn't a gentleman thief," he says.</p>

<p>"Listen," says Fix. "I now have papers from London to arrest Mr Fogg. But I can't stop him now. It must be in England. When we get there..."</p>

<p>They arrive in San Francisco in the morning. The New York train leaves that evening. Fogg and Mrs Aouda meet Fix in the street. "I am travelling to Europe now. Can we travel together?" he asks.</p>

<p>"Of course," answers Fogg.</p>

<p>They walk into a meeting. "Cameron for Judge!" shout the people on the left. "Mandy boy for Judge!" shout the people on the right. Then they start fighting. One man wants to fight Fogg, but Fix stands in front of him and stops him. Passepartout finds it all very strange. "So, Mr Fix is helping us now," he thinks.</p>

<p>Fogg, Mrs Aouda, Passepartout and Fix get on the train that evening. They cross one old bridge very fast and it falls into the river behind them.</p>

<p>Then, some Sioux Indians arrive. They kill some passengers. Passepartout fights them. But they take him and ride away with him.</p>

<p>Fogg, Mrs Aouda and Fix get off the train. Fogg goes to look for Passepartout. He finds him and brings him back. But they must wait until evening, until the next train to New York.</p>

<p>"How can we travel fast over the snow?" thinks Fix.</p>

<p>"On a sledge with sails," says Fogg.</p>

<p>Soon, they arrive in Omaha. There, they take a train to New York. They arrive in New York 45 minutes late. Their ship, the China, is on its way to Liverpool.</p>

<summary>Chapter 6</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter 6</div>
  <div class="chapter-title"></div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">The next morning, Fogg, Mrs Aouda, Passepartout and Fix leave on a small ship, the Henrietta, going to France. Later, Fogg gives the sailors money and they agree to go to Liverpool, not to France. The sailors lock the captain in his room.</p>

<p>The Henrietta is in the middle of the Atlantic and there's no coal. "Burn all the wood on the ship," says Fogg. The ship arrives in Queenstown, in the south of Ireland, and there's no more wood on it.</p>

<p>"Let's get off here," says Fogg to Mrs Aouda, Passepartout and Fix. Fix doesn't arrest Fogg in Queenstown. He's waiting for them to arrive in England. They get on a train and travel to Dublin. There, they get a fast ship to Liverpool.</p>

<p>In Liverpool, Fix arrests Fogg at last and takes him to prison. In prison, Fogg looks at his diary. At two o'clock he looks at his watch. "There's time to go to London on a fast train and to be at my club by 8.45 this evening," he thinks.</p>

<p>At half past three, Fix arrives with Mrs Aouda and Passepartout. "I'm sorry, Mr Fogg," says Fix. "The gentleman thief, James Strand, is in prison. You're free to go." Angrily, Fogg punches Fix in the face.</p>

<p>Then Fogg, Mrs Aouda and Passepartout run to Liverpool station. They get a fast train to London. The train arrives at ten minutes to nine. Fogg is five minutes late for his meeting with Stuart.</p>

<p>Fogg doesn't want to get to his club late, so he goes home with Mrs Aouda and Passepartout. Around the world in eighty days and five minutes, he's the loser. He has only twenty thousand pounds in the bank and he must give it all to Stuart.</p>

<p>"I'm sorry for bringing you here to a poor man's home," he says to Mrs Aouda the next morning.</p>

<p>"Will you marry me?" asks Mrs Aouda.</p>

<p>"Yes," says Fogg.</p>

<p>That afternoon, Fogg tells Passepartout, "I want to marry Mrs Aouda tomorrow, the twenty-third of December. Can you speak to the people at Marylebone Church about it?"</p>

<p>Soon, Passepartout returns. "Today's the twenty-first of December. When you travel east around the world, you get an extra day. You aren't late for your meeting at the club after all, but you must run."</p>

<p>At a quarter to nine, Fogg enters his club. "I win," he says to Stuart, "where's my twenty thousand pounds?"</p>

<p>Next Monday, Fogg, the world traveller, marries Mrs Aouda. He's a very happy man.</p>

* Где и когда начинается действие книги?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Действие начинается в Лондоне в 1872 году.
  </details>

* Кто такой Филеас Фогг (Phileas Fogg) и как он обычно проводит свой день?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Филеас Фогг — английский джентльмен (English gentleman). Он каждый день ходит в свой клуб (club), там обедает, читает, ужинает, разговаривает с друзьями и возвращается домой в полночь.
  </details>

* Какое пари заключает Фогг со своими друзьями в клубе?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Он заключает пари, что сможет объехать вокруг света за 80 дней (go round the world in 80 days). Ставка — 20 000 фунтов.
  </details>

* Кто такой детектив Фикс (Fix) и за кем он следит?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Фикс — английский детектив (English detective). Он следит за Фоггом, потому что считает его джентльменом-вором (gentleman thief), который украл 5000 фунтов из Английского банка.
  </details>

* Где Фогг, Паспарту (Passepartout) и миссис Ауда (Mrs. Aouda) встречаются впервые?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Они встречаются в Индии, в деревне Пеладжи (Pelaji), где Фогг и Паспарту спасают миссис Ауду от сожжения на погребальном костре вместе с умершим принцем.
  </details>

* За что Паспарту и Фогга судят в Калькутте (Calcutta)?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Паспарту осуждён за то, что не снял обувь в храме на Малабарском холме (temple at Malibar Hill) в Бомбее.
  </details>

* Что происходит с Паспарту в Гонконге (Hong Kong) и почему он не успевает предупредить Фогга об отплытии «Карнатика» (Carnatic)?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Фикс поит его алкоголем (drink after drink), и Паспарту засыпает прямо на столе.
  </details>

* Кто такие сиу (Sioux Indians) и что они делают с Паспарту?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Сиу — индейское племя. Они нападают на поезд, убивают пассажиров и увозят Паспарту с собой.
  </details>

* Почему Фогг проигрывает пари, когда возвращается в Лондон?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Он думает, что опоздал на 5 минут (five minutes late). На самом деле, путешествуя на восток, он выигрывает один дополнительный день (extra day), и Паспарту объясняет ему это.
  </details>

* На ком женится Фогг в конце книги?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Он женится на миссис Ауде (Mrs. Aouda).
  </details>
