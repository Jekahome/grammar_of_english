    <p class="first-para">On the border of England and Scotland. The year is 1605.</p>

<p>"Halt! Who goes there?"</p>

<p>"Let us in. We have a prisoner."</p>

<p>"Who is this man?"</p>

<p>"Robert Mackenzie. He's a traitor. He has to be hanged."</p>

<p>"Place him in the dungeon. Take him to the dungeon."</p>

<p>"Get up, Mackenzie."</p>

<p>"Take your hands off me, you English pig!"</p>

<p>"Why you..."</p>

<p>"Put your sword away. He will be hanged as every traitor should be, and then his body will be thrown from the castle walls. Take him to the dungeon. Put him in there."</p>

<p>The following night, the night before Robert Mackenzie was due to be hanged, a hay wagon arrived at the gates of the castle.</p>

<p>"Halt! Who goes there?"</p>

<p>"Gabriel West. What is your business here?"</p>

<p>"You know me. I bring hay for your horses."</p>

<p>"Wait. Let me look."</p>

<p>"Can I go in now?"</p>

<p>"You may pass."</p>

<p>"Thank you. Get up! Whoa!"</p>

<p>"Yes, stupid Mack. Stand still."</p>

<p>"Wait a minute. Who's that?"</p>

<p>"Don't say a word."</p>

<p>"Who are you? And how did you get into my wagon?"</p>

<p>"Don't ask questions."</p>

<p>"I will call for help."</p>

<p>"If you do anything, I will kill you."</p>

<p>"No, stop. What do you want from me?"</p>

<p>"Tell me where the dungeons are."</p>

<p>"You don't know where the dungeons are?"</p>

<p>"Can you feel the blade of my knife on your throat?"</p>

<p>"Alright."</p>

<p>"I will kill you if you don't do as I say."</p>

<p>"Stop. Where are the dungeons?"</p>

<p>"On the other side of this courtyard."</p>

<p>"Right. Now listen to me. Unload your hay and wait here. I will be back in five minutes. I intend to leave the castle in your wagon."</p>

<p>"Why should I wait for you?"</p>

<p>"Because if you don't, I will kill you."</p>

<p>"And what is to stop me leaving the castle without you before you come back?"</p>

<p>"I have friends outside the castle waiting for this wagon. If I am not on it, they will kill you."</p>

<p>"I will do as you ask. But why do you want to go to the dungeons?"</p>

<p>"My husband is there."</p>

<p>"Your husband? The traitor Robert Mackenzie is your husband."</p>

<p>"Don't dare use the word traitor in front of me again."</p>

<p>"Don't tell me again. Where are the dungeons?"</p>

<p>"Across the courtyard and then down some steps."</p>

<p>"Thank you."</p>

<p>"But you'll never get there."</p>

<p>"Why not?"</p>

<p>"There is a guard at the top of the steps and another at the bottom."</p>

<p>"There will not be a problem. Just make sure you are ready to leave when I return. And if you breathe a word to these guards, you will die an ugly death when you leave this castle."</p>

<p>"I understand."</p>

<p>"I see."</p>

<p>"You know, people in the village think..."</p>

<p>"I know. I know they think that the farmhouse is haunted."</p>

<p>"Yes."</p>

<p>"Well, I'm not sure. For a start, it isn't the original farmhouse. The original farmhouse burnt down. This one is only two hundred years old."</p>

<p>"I know, but it's built on exactly the same spot."</p>

<p>"Really?"</p>

<p>"Really. And take my word for it, Walter. People in Eddington believe that the farmhouse and the castle are haunted. So many strange things have happened there."</p>

<p>"I know. I spent months researching that book. I know all about the horses galloping across the hills near the castle late at night."</p>

<p>"Well, a lot of people have heard them."</p>

<p>"Have you?"</p>

<p>"Well, no."</p>

<p>"You see? You actually ask anyone if they've seen these horses and they say, no, but my uncle McTavish has. So, I asked if I could meet these uncles and grandfathers and of course they were all dead. Well..."</p>

<p>"Anyway, I don't care. It's my house and I'm going to live there."</p>

<p>"You're going to live at McPherson's farmhouse?"</p>

<p>"Yes."</p>

<p>"What? At the weekends?"</p>

<p>"No. All the time."</p>

<p>"Goodness! Well, you'll feel safe there."</p>

<p>"Safe? Jean, I've lived in London for the last twenty years. In my street, every day I see tramps, drug dealers, hooligans, football fans. I've had enough."</p>

<p>"You've had enough of London?"</p>

<p>"Absolutely. It's dirty and expensive. And I'm a writer, not a banker, so I can't afford to live there any more."</p>

<p>"I see."</p>

<p>"And I'm too old for it. Everyone seems to be under thirty."</p>

<p>"How old are you?"</p>

<p>"Thirty-one."</p>

<p>"Goodness. I'm thirty-one."</p>

<p>"I was joking, Jean. I'm a little bit older than thirty-one. Anyway, I decided it was time for a change."</p>

<p>"But... you want to live in McPherson's farmhouse? No one has lived there for ages. I mean apart from..."</p>

<p>"Apart from Alistair, I know. But I am going to live there. In fact, I moved all my books and a lot of my furniture up there last month. And a nice lady from the village has cleaned the place up for me. She's done a wonderful job."</p>

<p>"I'm sure she has. Eddington people are very hardworking."</p>

<p>"Tell me something, Walter."</p>

<p>"What?"</p>

<p>"Have you ever spent the night at the farmhouse?"</p>

<p>"Not yet, no. Tonight will be the first night."</p>
