<summary>Chapter One</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter One</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Tom and His Friends</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">"Tom! Tom! Where are you?"</p>

<p>No answer.</p>

<p>"Where is that boy? When I find him, I'm going to..." And Aunt Polly looked under the bed. Then she opened the door and looked out into the garden. "Tom!"</p>

<p>She heard something behind her. A small boy ran past, but Aunt Polly put out her hand and stopped him.</p>

<p>"Ah, there you are. And what's that in your pocket?"</p>

<p>"Nothing, Aunt Polly."</p>

<p>"Nothing. It's an apple. I can see it. Now listen, Tom. Those apples are not for you. And I..."</p>

<p>"Oh, Aunt Polly! Quick! Look behind you!"</p>

<p>So Aunt Polly looked. And Tom was out of the house in a second. She laughed quietly. "Oh, I never learn. I love that Tom. My dead sister's child. But he isn't an easy boy for an old lady. Well, it's Saturday tomorrow and there's no school. But it isn't going to be a holiday for Tom. Oh, no. He's going to work tomorrow."</p>

<p>Saturday was a beautiful day. It was summer and the sun was hot. And there were flowers in all the gardens. It was a day for everybody to be happy. Tom came out of his house with a brush and a big pot of white paint in his hand. He looked at the fence. It was three metres high and thirty metres long. He put his brush in the paint and painted some of the fence. He did it again. Then he stopped and looked at the fence. Put down his brush and sat down. There were hours of work in front of him. And he was the unhappiest boy in the village.</p>

<p>After ten minutes, Tom had an idea. A wonderful idea. He took up the brush again and began work. He saw his friend, Joe Harper, in the street. But he didn't look at him. Joe had an apple in his hand. He came up to Tom and looked at the fence.</p>

<p>"I'm sorry, Tom."</p>

<p>Tom said nothing. The paintbrush moved up and down.</p>

<p>"Working for your aunt," said Joe. "I am going down to the river. I am sorry, you can't come with me."</p>

<p>Tom put down his brush. "You call this work?" he said.</p>

<p>"Painting a fence," said Joe. "Of course it's work."</p>

<p>"Perhaps it is. And perhaps it isn't. But I like it," said Tom. "I can go to the river any day. I can't paint a fence very often."</p>

<p>Joe watched Tom for about five minutes. Tom painted very slowly and carefully. He often stopped, moved back from the fence, and looked at his work with a smile. Joe began to get very interested and said, "Tom, can I paint a little?"</p>

<p>Tom thought for a second. "I am sorry, Joe. You see, my aunt wants me to do it because I am good at painting. My brother Sid wanted to paint too, but she said no."</p>

<p>"Oh, please, Tom. Just a little. I am good at painting too. Hey, do you want some of my apple?"</p>

<p>"No, Joe. I can't."</p>

<p>"Okay, you can have all my apple."</p>

<p>Tom gave Joe the brush. He did not smile, but for the first time that day, he was a very happy boy. He sat down and ate Joe's apple.</p>

<p>More friends came to laugh at Tom, but soon they all wanted to paint too. By the afternoon, Tom had three balls, an old knife, a cat with one eye, an old blue bottle, and a lot of other exciting things. He was the richest boy in St Petersburg. And the fence, all thirty metres of it, was a beautiful white. He went back to the house. "Aunt Polly, can I go and play now?"</p>

<p>Aunt Polly came out of the house to look. When she saw the beautiful white fence, she was very pleased. She took Tom into the house and gave him an apple. "Well, you can go and play, but don't come home late!" Tom quickly took a second apple and ran off.</p>

<p>On Monday morning, Tom didn't want to go to school, but Aunt Polly got him out of bed, and then out of the house. In the street near the school, he met his friend, Huckleberry Finn. Huck had no mother, and his father drank whisky all the time. So Huck lived in the streets. He didn't go to school. He was always dirty, and he never had a new shirt. But he was happy. The mothers of St Petersburg didn't like Huck, but Tom and his friends did.</p>

<p>"Hello, Huck!" said Tom. "What have you got there?"</p>

<p>"A dead cat."</p>

<p>"What are you gonna do with it?" asked Tom.</p>

<p>"I'm gonna take it to the graveyard tonight," Huck said. "At midnight, a dead cat can call ghosts out of their graves."</p>

<p>"I never heard that!" said Tom. "Is it true?"</p>

<p>"Well, I don't know," said Huck. "Old Mrs Hopkins told me. Come with me and see. Or are you afraid of ghosts?"</p>

<p>"Of course not!" said Tom. "Come and meet me at my window at eleven o'clock."</p>

<p>After this, Tom was late for school. And the teacher looked at him angrily. "Tom Sawyer, why are you late again?" he said. Tom began to speak. And then stopped. There was a new girl in the school room. A beautiful girl with blue eyes and long yellow hair. Tom looked and looked. Oh, how beautiful she was. And in two seconds, Tom was in love. He must sit next to her. But how?</p>

<p>In the girl's half of the room, there was only one empty chair. And it was next to the new girl. Tom thought quickly. And then looked at the teacher. "I stopped to talk with Huckleberry Finn," he said. The teacher was very, very angry. Boys were often late for school. That was bad. But talking with Huckleberry Finn was worse. Much worse. The teacher took his stick. And two minutes later, Tom's trousers were very hot. And the teacher's arm was very tired. "Now, Tom Sawyer, you go and sit with the girls."</p>

<p>Some of the children laughed. Tom walked to the chair next to the new girl. Sat down and opened his book. The other children began to work again. After ten minutes, the girl looked up. There was an apple on the table in front of her. She put it back on Tom's half of the table. A minute later, the apple was in front of her again. Now, it stayed.</p>

<p>Next, Tom drew a picture of a house and put it in front of her.</p>

<p>"That's nice," the girl said. "Now draw a man."</p>

<p>Tom drew a man next to the house. The man was taller than the house. And he had very big hands and very long legs. But the girl liked him.</p>

<p>"Can you draw me now?" she asked.</p>

<p>Tom drew a girl next to the man.</p>

<p>"You draw beautifully. I can't draw pictures."</p>

<p>"I can teach you," said Tom, after school.</p>

<p>"Oh, please."</p>

<p>"What's your name?" Tom asked.</p>

<p>"Becky. Becky Thatcher."</p>

<p>Just then, Tom felt a hand on his head. It was the teacher. He took Tom by the ear and moved him back to his chair. In the boy's half of the room.</p>

<summary>Chapter Two</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Two</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">In the Graveyard</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">That night, Tom went to bed at half past nine. He waited for Huck to come. And at eleven o'clock, he came. He climbed quietly out of the bedroom window. And then he and Huck walked out of the village with the dead cat.</p>

<p>The graveyard was on a hill, about a mile from St Petersburg. When the boys got there, they put the dead cat on a grave and sat down behind some trees. They watched and waited. It was very dark and very quiet.</p>

<p>"Do you see that new grave there?" whispered Huck. "That's Hoss Williams's grave. He died last week."</p>

<p>"Perhaps he can hear us," Tom whispered back. "Do you think he can, Huck?"</p>

<p>"I don't know. But I..."</p>

<p>"Oh, Tom. What is it?"</p>

<p>"Shh!" whispered Tom. "I can see something. Look!" Huck moved near to Tom.</p>

<p>"Ghosts!" he said. "Three of them. They're coming here, Tom. Oh, let's go home."</p>

<p>"They can't see us," Tom whispered. "Not here."</p>

<p>"Ghosts can see through trees," said Huck unhappily. "They can see through everything."</p>

<p>The ghosts moved quietly through the graveyard and came near to the trees. Huck and Tom watched. Very afraid. Then, after a minute, Huck said, "Tom, they're not ghosts. That's Muff Potter."</p>

<p>"So it is. And that's Injun Joe. And the other man is Dr Robinson."</p>

<p>"What are they doing here?"</p>

<p>"They're grave robbers, Tom. They're going to rob a grave. My father told me about it. The doctor wants a dead body, you see. He cuts it up because he wants to learn about..."</p>

<p>"Shh!" said Tom. "They're getting near."</p>

<p>The three men stopped at Hoss Williams's grave. And Injun Joe and Muff Potter began to dig. Ten minutes later, the grave was open.</p>

<p>"Now, Doc," said Muff Potter. "You want us to take the body to your house? That's five dollars more."</p>

<p>"No," said the doctor. "I gave you the money this morning. I'm not giving you any more."</p>

<p>"Now you listen to me, Doc," said Injun Joe. "I want that money. Do you remember a day five years ago? I came to your house and asked for something to eat. And you gave me nothing. Nothing. So give me that money." He took the doctor's arm. But suddenly, the doctor hit him. And Injun Joe fell to the ground.</p>

<p>"Don't hit my friend!" cried Muff Potter. He jumped on the doctor. And the two men began to fight. It all happened very quickly. And the two boys watched with open mouths. Injun Joe got up. He had Muff Potter's knife in his hand now. And he moved behind the doctor. Then the doctor hit Muff Potter on the head. Muff fell to the ground. And at the same moment, the knife in Injun Joe's hand went into the doctor's back. The doctor fell to the ground on top of Muff Potter. And he did not move again.</p>

<p>The two boys could watch no more. Very quietly, they moved away from the trees. And then ran out of the graveyard. And back to the village.</p>

<p>Injun Joe stood by Hoss Williams's grave. And looked down at the two men. Then he put the knife into Muff Potter's hand and sat down. Three, four, five minutes went by. Potter moved a little and opened his eyes.</p>

<p>"What? What happened, Joe?" he asked.</p>

<p>"This is bad, Muff," said Joe. "Why did you kill him?"</p>

<p>Muff looked at the doctor's dead body. Then at the knife in his hand. "Me? Did I kill him?" His face went white. And the knife fell from his hand. "It's the whisky, Joe. I never fight with knives. I never fight with knives, usually. Oh, why did I drink all that whisky tonight? I don't remember anything."</p>

<p>"It's okay, Muff," said Joe. "I'm not going to tell anyone. You get away quickly. Go on. Go now."</p>

<p>Muff Potter got up and ran away. Joe watched him for a minute. Then he carefully put the knife next to the doctor's body. Then he, too, left the graveyard.</p>

<p>The next day, the sheriff's men found the doctor's body in the graveyard. And Muff Potter's knife. That night, Muff came to the graveyard to get his knife. But the sheriff's men were there. And they took Muff to St Petersburg's little jail. And there Muff sat for four weeks and waited for his trial.</p>

<p>Tom and Huck could not forget that night in the graveyard. They were very unhappy and very afraid.</p>

<p>"What are we going to do?" said Tom. "Muff Potter didn't kill the doctor. Injun Joe did. We saw him."</p>

<p>"I know," Huck said. "But what can we do? We can't tell anyone. I'm afraid of Injun Joe. He's dangerous. And he's a killer. Do you want a knife in your back?"</p>

<p>"Yes. I'm afraid of him, too," Tom said. He thought for a minute. "I'm sorry for Muff Potter. But you're right, Huck. We can't tell anyone about Injun Joe."</p>

<summary>Chapter Three</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Three</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">On Jackson's Island</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">The summer holidays came. And there was no school. Tom didn't want to think about Muff Potter and Injun Joe. But it wasn't easy. At night, when he was in bed, he saw Injun Joe's face in the dark. And he couldn't sleep. But he couldn't talk to anyone about it.</p>

<p>One hot summer's day, he and Joe Harper were down by the Mississippi River. They sat and watched the boats, and fished and talked.</p>

<p>"Let's get away from here," said Tom, suddenly. "Let's go and do something exciting somewhere."</p>

<p>"Okay," said Joe. "But what? And where?"</p>

<p>"I know," Tom said. "Let's run away. Let's go and live on Jackson's Island. We can sleep out under the trees."</p>

<p>Jackson's Island was in the Mississippi. Three miles south of St Petersburg. Nobody lived there.</p>

<p>"Let's ask Huck Finn too," said Tom. "But don't tell your mother or father or anyone. Go home and get some things to eat. And meet me here at midnight."</p>

<p>So that night, three boys in a small boat went down the river to Jackson's Island. They had some bread and some meat. And Huck had his pipe too. When they got there, they carried everything on to the island and made a fire under a big old tree. Then they cooked some of the meat over the fire. And oh, that meat was good. The best dinner in the world. Soon, they stopped talking. Their eyes closed. And they slept.</p>

<p>The next morning, Tom woke up with the sun on his head and a smile on his face. Then Huck and Joe woke up. And the three boys ran down to the river to swim. After that, they fished. And soon they had about six big fish for their breakfast. They cooked the fish on their fire and ate them all. They were very hungry.</p>

<p>"That," said Joe happily, "was a wonderful breakfast."</p>

<p>After breakfast, they walked through the island. Swam some more, talked, fished and swam again. They came back to their fire in the afternoon. Suddenly, Tom looked up and said, "Listen, can you hear boats?"</p>

<p>They listened and then ran across the island. To look down the river. There were twenty or more boats on the water. Every boat in St Petersburg was out.</p>

<p>"What are they doing?" asked Joe.</p>

<p>"They're looking for a dead body, I think," said Huck. "They did that last summer. When Bill Turner fell in the river and drowned."</p>

<p>"Who's dead, do you think?" asked Joe.</p>

<p>The boys watched the boats. Suddenly, Tom cried. "I know who's dead. It's us. They're looking for us."</p>

<p>This was wonderful. Tom looked at his friends. "We're famous," he said. "Everybody in St Petersburg is talking about us. And they all feel sorry for us."</p>

<p>Night came. And the boys went to sleep. But Tom did not sleep. And when morning came, he wasn't there.</p>

<p>"Huck. Where's Tom?" cried Joe.</p>

<p>"I don't know," Huck began. "But, look. Look. There he is. He's swimming across to the island now. Hey, Tom!"</p>

<p>At breakfast, Tom told his story. "I went home last night," he said. "And listened at the window. Joe, your mother was there too. And she and Aunt Polly cried and cried. I heard some very interesting things. On Sunday, there's going to be a big funeral at the church. For us. And listen, I've got a wonderful idea."</p>

<p>Huck and Joe listened and laughed. And yes, it was a wonderful idea.</p>

<p>That night, the boys cooked some more fish. And after dinner, Huck got out his pipe and began to smoke.</p>

<p>"Can we smoke too?" asked Tom. "I want to learn."</p>

<p>So Huck made pipes for Tom and Joe. And the three boys sat and smoked.</p>

<p>"Hey, I like smoking," said Tom. "It's easy."</p>

<p>"It's nothing," said Joe. "I'm going to smoke every day."</p>

<p>But after ten minutes, Tom and Joe got quieter and quieter. And their faces went an interesting colour.</p>

<p>"I'm going for a little walk now," said Tom. He stood up carefully and walked away into the trees.</p>

<p>"Me too," said Joe. Quickly. The two boys came back an hour later. But they didn't smoke their pipes again.</p>

<p>On Sunday morning, there were no happy faces in St Petersburg. Aunt Polly and Joe's mother and father were in the church and all the boys' friends. The minister said some very nice things about the three boys. And the boys' families cried and cried. Everybody cried. And little Becky Thatcher did not stop crying for one second.</p>

<p>There was a small noise at the back of the church. But at first, nobody heard it. Then the minister looked up and suddenly stopped speaking. Everybody turned to look. Their mouths opened and stayed open. And into the church came the three dead boys. Tom first, Joe next, and then Huck. For a second, nobody moved or spoke. And then the noise began. Aunt Polly and Joe's mother ran to the boys and took them in their arms. Aunt Polly cried and laughed and cried again.</p>

<p>"Oh, Tom!" she said. "You're a bad boy, but I love you."</p>

<p>Suddenly the minister called out. "All happy days. Sing, good people of St Petersburg. Sing and be happy." And everybody sang and smiled and laughed for a long time. It was St Petersburg's happiest funeral.</p>

<p>The weeks went by and the judge came to St Petersburg. On the day before Muff Potter's trial, Huck and Tom met in the street near Tom's house. Huck was unhappy.</p>

<p>"Tom, you didn't tell anyone about, you know..."</p>

<p>"No, I didn't. But Huck, what about Muff? People are saying he's the killer. And he's going to die."</p>

<p>"But we can't tell anyone about Injun Joe," said Huck. "I don't want to die, too. Do you?"</p>

<p>"No." Tom didn't want to die. But he couldn't forget Muff Potter's face in the jail. Old, tired and unhappy. And Injun Joe was a free man. It wasn't right.</p>

<p>That night, Tom came home late and very excited. He could not sleep for two or three hours. The next morning, all the village was at Muff Potter's trial. Injun Joe was there, too. Muff waited. A tired old man with a dirty face. The judge began the trial. Questions, questions, questions. Answers, answers, answers. And the answers were all bad for Muff Potter.</p>

<p>"Yes, I found the knife in the graveyard next to Dr Robinson's body."</p>

<p>"Yes, that's Muff Potter's knife. He always carries it."</p>

<p>"Yes, I saw Muff Potter in the village that afternoon. He had the knife with him then."</p>

<p>Muff Potter began to look more and more unhappy. Then the judge said, "Call Thomas Sawyer."</p>

<p>St Petersburg sat up. What did young Tom Sawyer know? Everybody looked at him and waited.</p>

<p>"Thomas Sawyer, where were you on the 17th of June at the hour of midnight?"</p>

<p>"In the graveyard."</p>

<p>"Why?"</p>

<p>"I went there to see ghosts with a dead cat."</p>

<p>St Petersburg laughed and the judge looked angry. "And where were you in the graveyard, Thomas?"</p>

<p>"Behind the trees near Hoss Williams's grave."</p>

<p>Injun Joe's face suddenly went white.</p>

<p>"Now, my boy," said the judge, "tell us your story."</p>

<p>And so Tom told his story. And St Petersburg sat and listened to him with open mouths. "And then Muff Potter fell. And Injun Joe jumped with the knife and... crash!"</p>

<p>Injun Joe jumped through the window and was out and away in a second.</p>

<p>St Petersburg loved Tom for a week. But Tom was not happy. Injun Joe was not in jail. And he was a dangerous man. Tom slept badly for weeks. The slow summer days went by. Injun Joe did not come back to St Petersburg. And Tom began to forget.</p>

<summary>Chapter Four</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Four</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Treasure</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">There is a time in every boy's life when he wants to go and dig for treasure. And that time came for Tom. So, one hot summer's day, he went to find Huck. Huck liked the idea of treasure.</p>

<p>"Where are we going to dig?" he asked.</p>

<p>"An old dead tree is best."</p>

<p>"Who puts the treasure under old trees?"</p>

<p>"Robbers!" said Tom. "And then they go away. Or they forget to come back for the treasure."</p>

<p>"There's a dead tree on Cardiff Hill," said Huck. "Let's go there. I've got an old pick and shovel."</p>

<p>It was three miles to the old tree. And the boys arrived tired and hot. They dug for an hour or two. But they didn't find any treasure. Then they stopped. And Tom looked down at an old house at the foot of the hill.</p>

<p>"Hey, look!" he said. "Nobody lives in that old house. Let's go there. Old houses are always good for treasure."</p>

<p>"Good for ghosts, too," said Huck.</p>

<p>They took the pick and shovel with them. Went down the hill and into the old house. They looked in all the rooms downstairs. And then went upstairs. But there was no treasure and no ghosts. Then they heard a noise.</p>

<p>"What's that?" said Tom suddenly.</p>

<p>"Ghosts?" whispered Huck.</p>

<p>There were holes in the floor. And through them, the boys could see into the rooms downstairs.</p>

<p>"No," Tom whispered. "It's two men. One is the old Spaniard. He came to live in the village last week. I don't know the other man. Shh. Let's listen to them."</p>

<p>The two men sat down on the floor. The Spaniard had a green hat and long white hair. The other man was small and dark. He took out a bag and began to open it.</p>

<p>"It's hot in here," the Spaniard said. He took off his green hat. And then he took off his long white hair.</p>

<p>"Tom!" Huck whispered upstairs. "That's Injun Joe."</p>

<p>"We took $650 when we robbed that house," said the second man. He took some money out of the bag. "We can take $50 with us now. What are we going to do with the $600?"</p>

<p>"Leave it here," said Injun Joe. "We can come back and get it next week. Here. Give me the bag." He walked across the room to the fireplace. Moved two big stones from the floor. And began to dig with his knife.</p>

<p>Upstairs, the two boys watched excitedly. Treasure. Six hundred dollars of wonderful treasure. Injun Joe stopped digging.</p>

<p>"Hello. What's this?" he said. "There's something here. It's an old box." The two men got the box out. And opened it.</p>

<p>"It's money," said Injun Joe's friend.</p>

<p>Injun Joe put his hand into the box. "There are thousands of dollars here," he said. And the two men looked at the money with happy smiles. "But who...?" began Injun Joe's friend.</p>

<p>"Don't ask," Injun Joe said. "It's our money now."</p>

<p>"We can't take it with us today," said his friend. "What can we do with it?"</p>

<p>"Put it back under the floor?"</p>

<p>"Yes," said Injun Joe.</p>

<p>Happy faces upstairs. "No..." Very unhappy faces upstairs.</p>

<p>"Let's put it under the cross. Nobody goes there. We can take it there tonight."</p>

<p>When night came, the two men carried all the money away. The boys did not go after them, because they were afraid of Injun Joe. But they wanted very much to find that cross. For a week, the two boys thought and thought about the treasure. It was under the cross. But where was the cross? In St Petersburg, the boys watched the Spaniard carefully. But they didn't see a cross. And they didn't find the treasure.</p>

<summary>Chapter Five</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Five</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">In the Cave</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">The next Saturday was Becky Thatcher's birthday. And all Becky's friends were very excited. "It's going to be a wonderful day," Becky told Tom. "We're going to have a picnic by the river. And after that, we can visit McDougal's cave."</p>

<p>So in the morning, a big boat took Becky, Tom, and all their friends down the river. There were some older children on the boat, too. But all the mothers and fathers stayed at home. Picnics are better without them. And it was a very happy, noisy picnic.</p>

<p>After the picnic, the children took out their candles and ran up the hill to the cave. The mouth of the cave was dark. And some of the children were afraid at first. But caves are exciting. So in the end, everybody went in.</p>

<p>McDougal's cave was very, very big. With hundreds of tunnels and rooms. The tunnels went up, down, and into the hill for miles. You could walk for days in McDougal's cave. Nobody knew all the cave. But many people knew the tunnels near the door. You could play all day in these tunnels. Tom, of course, knew them well.</p>

<p>For hours, the children walked and ran through the cave, up and down the tunnels, in and out of the rooms.</p>

<p>In the evening, they came out and walked down the hill to the boat, tired, but happy. When the boat arrived back in St Petersburg, it was dark. Huck Finn saw the boat, but he did not know about the picnic. He did not go to birthday picnics, of course, because the mothers of St Petersburg did not like him. But tonight, Huck was only interested in treasure, Injun Joe's treasure.</p>

<p>Injun Joe was in an old building by the river. And Huck waited in the street near the building. Perhaps, he thought, Injun Joe's cross isn't there. And the box of money. I must wait and watch. I can tell Tom about it tomorrow.</p>

<p>But Injun Joe didn't come out. At midnight, it began to rain, and Huck waited all night in the cold street. In the morning, he could not move, and he could not speak. He felt cold, then hot, then cold. Then hot again. Mrs Douglas, a woman from the church, found him in the street. She took him to her home and put him to bed. And there he stayed for two weeks. He was very ill. And so, he did not hear about Tom and Becky.</p>

<p>But on Sunday morning, all St Petersburg knew about Tom and Becky. Because they were not on the boat when it came back to the village. Where were they? Were they lost in the cave? And were they alive? Or dead?</p>

<p>At first, Tom and Becky played with their friends in the cave. Then Tom wanted to go down a new tunnel, and Becky went with him. They walked and talked, and went into a second tunnel, then a third. Sometimes Tom put a mark with candle smoke on the tunnel wall. He wanted to find the mouth of the cave again. Then they came out of the tunnels into a big room. There were hundreds of bats in this room, and the candles woke them up. Tom took Becky's hand, and they ran into the nearest tunnel with the bats behind them. But one bat hit Becky's candle, and it went out. The children ran and ran through the tunnels, and at last, they got away from the bats. They stopped and sat down. Suddenly, it was very, very quiet.</p>

<p>"Where are we now, Tom?" Becky whispered, afraid.</p>

<p>"I don't know," said Tom. "I think it's time to go back, but we can't go through that big room because of the bats. Let's go down this tunnel."</p>

<p>They went down one tunnel, then a second, a third, a fourth. Then they wanted to find the big room with the bats again. But they couldn't. Becky began to cry. "Tom, we can't get out! We're lost, Tom! We're lost!"</p>

<p>They walked and walked. When they were tired, they sat down. Then they got up and walked again. Time went by. Was it day or night? They didn't know. Then Tom wanted to find water. They had nothing to eat, and they must have something to drink. They found a very small river, and sat down next to it. Becky said, "Tom, we must stay here, near this river. This is our last candle," and he did not finish. But Becky understood.</p>

<p>"Tom?"</p>

<p>"Yes, Becky."</p>

<p>"Are they going to come and look for us?"</p>

<p>"Of course! When the boat gets to St Petersburg..."</p>

<p>"But how can they find us in these hundreds of tunnels? Oh, Tom! Tom! We're going to die in here!" Becky began to cry again. Then the candle went out, and the two children were in the dark. They sat for hours and hours. They slept a little, then woke up, then slept again. Was it Sunday now or Monday?</p>

<p>Suddenly Tom sat up. "Listen!" Somebody's calling! The two children listened. They heard it again, a little nearer. They called back. Then they began to walk down the tunnel in the dark, with their hands on the wall. They stopped and listened again. But now they couldn't hear anything. Slowly, they went back to their river. They slept again and woke up very, very hungry. Perhaps it's Tuesday now, Tom thought. What can I do? I must do something. Then he had an idea.</p>

<p>"Becky, listen. I've got a long string in my pocket. I can go down some of the small tunnels and get back to you with the string. You wait here."</p>

<p>Slowly and carefully, Tom went down the first tunnel on his hands and knees. Then the tunnel wall on his right finished, and there was nothing. Tom put out his hand to feel the floor. And just then, away to his right, he saw a hand. A hand with a candle. At once, Tom called out. "Help!" he cried. The hand moved, and Tom saw an arm and a face. It was Injun Joe. Tom was very afraid, but Injun Joe was afraid too. And he quickly ran away down the tunnel.</p>

<p>Tom went back to Becky, but he did not tell her about Injun Joe. Tom waited for an hour. Then went into a different tunnel with his string. Then a third tunnel.</p>

<p>It was Tuesday evening, and St Petersburg waited. Many of the villagers were in the cave, and they looked for the children day and night. But they heard nothing, saw nothing, and found nothing. Then, late that evening, there was a sudden noise in the streets. People began to run to the Thatchers' house. "They're here! Becky and Tom are here!"</p>

<p>Most of the village came to listen to Tom's story. "It was in the sixth tunnel," he told them. "I went to the end of my string, and suddenly, I could see daylight. There was a little hole in the cave wall. I put my head out, and there was the river, right under my nose. I went back and got Becky, and we climbed out through the hole. Then we stopped a boat on the river. We were five miles from the mouth of the cave."</p>

<p>Tom was very tired after his three days in the cave, and he went to bed and stayed there for two days. He heard about Huck, and went to see him on Sunday, and then every day. But Mrs Douglas was always in the room. "You can just say hello," she told Tom. "And then you must go. Huck is very ill, and he needs to sleep." So Tom could not talk about anything exciting, and he could not tell Huck about Injun Joe.</p>

<p>One day, about two weeks after the picnic, Tom was in Becky's house, and her father came in.</p>

<p>"Well, Tom," Mr Thatcher said, "would you like to go back to the cave again, one day?"</p>

<p>"I'm not afraid of that cave," said Tom. Mr Thatcher laughed. "There are a lot of people like you, Tom. But nobody's going into that cave again. There are big doors across the cave mouth now, and nobody can open them."</p>

<p>Tom's face went white. "But Mr Thatcher, Injun Joe's in that cave."</p>

<p>An hour later, fifty men were at the cave, and they opened the doors. Injun Joe was on the ground, dead, his face to the door, and his knife in his hand.</p>

<summary>Chapter Six</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Six</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Under the Cross</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">The day after Injun Joe's funeral, Huck was out of bed. He and Tom walked slowly out of the village. They had a good long talk, and Huck heard all about the picnic, the cave, and Injun Joe.</p>

<p>"We're never going to find the money now," said Huck.</p>

<p>"Huck," said Tom. "The money isn't in St Petersburg. It's in the cave. I know it is."</p>

<p>"Why was Injun Joe in the cave?"</p>

<p>"Because he took the box of money there, right?" Huck looked excited. "Say that again, Tom."</p>

<p>"The money's in the cave. And we can get to it easily. Let's go there now. I've got some candles and a long string. We can take a boat and put it back later."</p>

<p>Twenty minutes later, the boys were in a boat on the Mississippi. They went eight miles down the river, and then Tom stopped by some small trees. "Here we are!" he said. Tom's hole was just behind the trees. Tom took a candle and climbed in. Huck climbed in after him.</p>

<p>"Injun Joe never found this hole," said Tom. "Or he did find it and couldn't get through it. It's very small."</p>

<p>The boys went carefully through the tunnels with their string. Then Tom stopped. "I saw Injun Joe about here," he said. "And look, Huck! There's the cross!"</p>

<p>There was a big smoke cross on the tunnel wall. The boys looked up and down the tunnel. But there was no box of money.</p>

<p>"Injun Joe said, under the cross," said Tom. "Perhaps it's under the ground. Look, we can move these stones."</p>

<p>The boys took their knives and began to dig by the tunnel wall. Very soon, they found a second, smaller tunnel under the wall. They climbed down into it and came into a small room. There was a bed, two whisky bottles, some old shoes, and the box of money.</p>

<p>When somebody finds treasure, everybody hears about it very quickly. The two boys carried the box through the village. And when they got to Aunt Polly's house, half the village was with them. Everybody went into the house.</p>

<p>"Oh, Tom! Tom!" cried Aunt Polly. "What is it now? And what have you got there?"</p>

<p>Tom put the box on the table and opened it. There were $12,000 in that box. And suddenly, Tom Sawyer and Huckleberry Finn were the richest people in St Petersburg.</p>
