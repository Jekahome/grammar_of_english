  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter One</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">The Boy with No Shoes</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">His family name is Edson Arantes do Nascimento, but people call him Pelé. He is the number one player in the story of soccer. In 1363 games, he scored 1264 goals. For Brazil, he won the World Cup three times. He visited 88 countries, and his picture was in every newspaper.</p>

<p>But Pelé is not only a famous soccer player. He knows Spanish, Italian, English, and French. He writes music, and he works for the Brazilian government.</p>

<p>Today, Pelé is famous. In 1950, he was a boy of ten with no shoes. This is his story.</p>

<p>Edson Arantes do Nascimento came into the world on the night of October 23, 1940. His family called him Dico. They lived in a small house in Três Corações, Brazil.</p>

<p>"Dico has soccer player's legs," his father said.</p>

<p>"He's going to be a doctor, not a soccer player," Dico's mother Celeste said. Celeste was angry.</p>

<p>Dondinho, Dico's father, was a soccer player, but he didn't make much money. Dondinho smiled. There were problems in the family about soccer, but there was a lot of love in the house too.</p>

<p>In 1944, the manager of Bauru Atlético, a big soccer club, talked to Dondinho. "We want a good soccer player," the man said. "It's not much money, but there's work in the town too. Do you want the job?"</p>

<p>Dondinho said yes, and the family moved to Bauru. The manager was wrong about one thing. There was no work for Dondinho in the town. He only played soccer, and the family had the same money problems. But Dico was happy in Bauru with his new friends.</p>

<p>They played their first game of soccer in Rua Rubens Arruda, a street near Dico's house. Their football was an old newspaper. Little Dico played a lot of soccer. Celeste wasn't happy about this.</p>

<p>"There's no money in the game," she said. Dico smiled. His mother was right. He loved soccer, but he didn't want to be a soccer player. He wanted to work at the airport. He often went there and looked at the airplanes.</p>

<p>But Dico's first job wasn't in a soccer stadium or an airport. It was at Bauru Station in 1948. He cleaned shoes. In that year, things were difficult for the family. But then, Dondinho started work in a doctor's office. He cleaned the building and answered the telephone.</p>

<p>In the afternoons, Dico often visited his father. "In ten years, you're going to be a young man," Dondinho said one day. "What do you want to do?"</p>

<p>"I want to work with airplanes," Dico said.</p>

<p>"Okay, but school is important for that," said his father.</p>

<p>Later that year, Dico started school. He was a good student. He only stayed in school because he wanted a job at the airport. But one day, an airplane came down near the school. Dico and his friends went to the doctor's office and looked in one of the windows. The man from the airplane was dead.</p>

<p>Dico never talked about a job with airplanes again.</p>

<summary>Chapter Two</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Two</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">A New Name</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">The important thing for Dico now was soccer. Evenings and weekends, he played with his friends. In the afternoons, he was with Dondinho. Dondinho was his soccer teacher and Dico was a very good student. After two years, Dico was the number one young player in Bauru. His friends started to call him Pelé.</p>

<p>"Where does the name come from?" people often ask. Pelé doesn't know.</p>

<p>In 1951, Pelé started a club, September 7, with his friends. People called them the team with no shoes. They wanted to play in Bauru Atlético's championship for young teams. But the shoes were a problem.</p>

<p>One day, a father of one of the players came with shoes for the team. "Today, I'm giving you a new name. Ameriquinho," he said. "Now they can't call you the team with no shoes."</p>

<p>Americano played very well and won the championship. After the final, Dondinho looked at his son and smiled. "You played a beautiful game, Dico," he said.</p>

<p>Bauru Atlético liked Pelé's beautiful game too. They wanted him for the club's new team of young players, Baquinho. The team manager was Waldemar de Brito, a famous man in Brazilian soccer. Pelé was very happy. Waldemar was a good manager and a good teacher.</p>

<p>In 1953, Baquinho won the Bauru Young Team Championship. After this, Waldemar moved away. Pelé was very sorry.</p>

<p>One year later, Pelé finished school and started to work in a shoe factory. At weekends, he played for Baquinho, but he didn't like the new manager. 1954 was a bad year.</p>

<p>In the winter of the new year, Waldemar came to Pelé's house. He wanted to talk to the family.</p>

<p>"Your son is fifteen now," he said. "He's a very good soccer player. He can try for a job with Santos."</p>

<p>Dondinho and Pelé were happy. Santos was a very famous club in Brazil. Celeste didn't like it, but she said yes.</p>

<p>Some days later, Dondinho and Pelé went to Santos on the train.</p>

<p>"The Santos players aren't going to like me," Pelé said.</p>

<p>But there was no problem. Pelé played well, and the club was happy with its new player. Pelé was happy too. After some weeks, he had a good friend on the team. His name was Zito.</p>

<p>Pelé wasn't in the first team, but one day, a first team player, Vasconcelos, had an injury. "Let's put young Pelé in the first team," the manager said.</p>

<p>Pelé played well, and stayed in the first team. He scored his first goal for them on September 7th, 1956. After that, he started to score a lot of goals. The team were very happy.</p>

<p>In June 1957, they played in the Maracanã Championship. Pelé scored eight goals. Suddenly, he was famous.</p>

<p>"Thank you, Waldemar," said Pelé.</p>

<summary>Chapter Three</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Three</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">The World Cup Games</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">"Pelé, Pelé, Pelé!" called the Brazilian soccer fans. The managers of the Brazilian team listened. Late in 1957, the names of the new Brazilian World Cup team were on the radio. Castilho, Gilmar, Mazola, Pelé.</p>

<p>"Can that be right?" Pelé asked. "They want me?" He was only seventeen.</p>

<p>The 1958 World Cup Games were in Sweden. Pelé played very well. Brazil won their first games with no problems. Then they beat Russia to nothing. But it was a difficult game.</p>

<p>Wales were a strong team too, but Pelé scored a goal. And Brazil won one to nothing. The game with France wasn't difficult. Brazil won five to two. And Pelé scored three goals.</p>

<p>Now they were in the final with Sweden. Brazil didn't start well, and Sweden scored first. But then, Brazil scored five goals. Two of those goals were Pelé's and Brazil won five to two. Pelé scored six goals in his first World Cup. Brazil were the champions.</p>

<p>Now Pelé wasn't only famous in Brazil.</p>

<p>Pelé didn't play in the later games of the 1962 World Cup in Chile because he had an injury. He watched his team, and they beat Czechoslovakia 3-1 in the final. Brazil were the champions again. And Pelé was very happy for the team.</p>

<p>The 1966 World Cup was in England. Pelé doesn't like remembering this championship. Usually, Brazil went to the games with 22 players. In England, they had 43. This was bad for the team because there were new players in every game. In an early game, Portugal beat them 3-1. The day after that game, Brazil went home.</p>

<p>In 1970, the World Cup championship was in Mexico. Pelé was only twenty-nine, but he was the old man of the Brazilian team. They beat Czechoslovakia 4-1. Then they played England, the World Champions. It was a very interesting game, and Brazil won 1-0.</p>

<p>After this, they beat Romania 3-2, and Pelé scored two goals. Then they played Peru in the Jalisco Stadium. And they won 4-2. Now, there was only one game with Uruguay before the final. It was difficult, but Brazil won 3-1. The team went to Mexico City and waited for the final with Italy.</p>

<p>The 1970 World Cup final started in the Aztec Stadium on the afternoon of June 21, 1970. Pelé quickly scored a very good goal. There was a goal from Italy, but then Brazil scored three times. They won 4-1.</p>

<p>After the game, Pelé said goodbye to World Cup soccer. People were very sorry.</p>

<p>"How many players in the world can say I won three World Cups?" they asked.</p>

<p>There was only one answer. There was only one man. His name was Pelé.</p>

<summary>Chapter Four</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Four</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">A Difficult Girl</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">It was a Saturday night in 1958. Pelé and Zito were in Santos again, after the World Cup.</p>

<p>"There's a girls' ball game in the stadium," Zito said. "Let's go and watch."</p>

<p>There was a young white girl at the ball game. She looked at Pelé many times, and he looked at her.</p>

<p>"You're the famous Pelé, and you play for Santos," she said after the game.</p>

<p>"That's right," Pelé said. "Are you from the town?"</p>

<p>"Yes," the girl said. "But I don't like soccer." Then she walked away.</p>

<p>"Who was that?" Zito asked.</p>

<p>Pelé smiled. "A difficult girl," he said.</p>

<p>Pelé liked the difficult girl, but he didn't know her name. He talked to her friends.</p>

<p>"Her name's Rosemeri Cholbi," they said. "She works in a music store."</p>

<p>That same week, Pelé went to the store. Rosemeri was there. They talked for some time, then Pelé asked, "Can you come out with me sometimes?"</p>

<p>"I'm sorry," Rosemeri said. "I'm only fourteen. I don't go out with boys. But come to my house one afternoon."</p>

<p>Pelé was a visitor at Rosemeri's house for seven years. Then, in February 1965, they married. The boy with no shoes and the difficult girl were very happy.</p>

<summary>Chapter Five</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Five</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Pelé's Years with Santos</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Pelé played for Santos from August 1956 to October 1974. In those years, they were the number one club in Brazil and the world. They won the Brazilian championship in 1961, 1962, 1963, 1964, and 1965. They won the South American club championship in 1962, 1963, and 1968. And they won the world club championship in the same years.</p>

<p>Pelé and the Santos team visited many countries and played a lot of games. Sometimes this was difficult for Pelé. He didn't like staying in hotels all the time. He wanted to be home in Brazil with his family.</p>

<p>Pelé's final game for Santos was on October 2nd, 1974. The game was with Ponte Preta. After a short time, Pelé stopped playing. He had the ball in his hands and looked at the fans in the stadium.</p>

<p>"Thank you," he said, and he walked away from the game.</p>

<p>"We love you!" called the fans.</p>

<p>After Santos, Pelé went to New York for a job with an American team, New York Cosmos. The job was for $4,700,000. Many people in Brazil were unhappy about this.</p>

<p>"Pelé is only going for the money," they said, but they were wrong. Pelé wanted to make soccer famous in the US. He did this, and his new team were very happy.</p>

<p>Pelé stayed with New York Cosmos for three years. His final game was on October 1st, 1977. It was with his old club, Santos. Pelé played for his new team and then for Santos. He scored a good goal and said goodbye to soccer.</p>
