  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">There lived six little children: Clever, Fat, Noisy, Naughty, Slow, and Funny. They were all eight years old.</p>

<p>Clever was a nice little girl. She was short and thin. She had long dark hair and big, brown eyes. She liked reading, and she was very clever.</p>

<p>Fat was a nice little boy. He was short and fat. He had short fair hair and grey eyes. He liked eating very much.</p>

<p>Noisy was a nice little girl. She was tall and slim. She had long fair hair and big, blue eyes. She liked talking, and she was very noisy.</p>

<p>Naughty was a nice little boy. He was tall and thin. He had short curly brown hair and big, blue eyes. He liked riding a bike, and he was very naughty.</p>

<p>Slow was a nice little girl. She was short and plump. She had long curly dark hair and brown eyes. She liked wearing beautiful clothes, but she was very slow.</p>

<p>Funny was a nice little boy. He was tall and thin. He had short fair hair and dark eyes. He liked walking, and he was very funny.</p>

<p>They were in the same form, and they were friends.</p>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Two</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Birthday Present</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">It was Sunday, and it was Fat's birthday. In the morning, his mum and dad came into his room.</p>

<p>"Wake up, dear. Happy birthday to you," they said. "Here is your present."</p>

<p>Fat opened his eyes and saw a bike. It was very beautiful. It was red and blue, and had very big wheels.</p>

<p>"Oh, thank you very much," cried Fat. "Can I go to the yard and cycle?"</p>

<p>"Yes, you can," his parents said. "But come home at noon. Your friends are coming at three o'clock. You must tidy your room, lay the table, and cook pizza."</p>

<p>"Okay, mummy," said Fat. "Don't worry, I know I must do it."</p>

<p>Fat's mum looked at him and said, "Oh dear, look out of the window. It's sunny and warm. Take off your trousers, jumper, and trainers. Put on some shorts, a T-shirt, and sandals."</p>

<p>Fat took off the warm clothes, and put on his yellow shorts, a red T-shirt, and green sandals.</p>

<p>"Oh, I look cute," he said, when he looked in the mirror, and he went downstairs with his bike.</p>

<summary>Chapter Three</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Three</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">In the Yard</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">In the yard, he saw his friends, Funny and Naughty. They were on the playground.</p>

<p>"Hi," cried Fat.</p>

<p>"Oh, Fat, we're glad to see you. Happy birthday."</p>

<p>"Why do you look like traffic lights?" laughed Naughty.</p>

<p>"I don't. I look cute," said Fat.</p>

<p>"What have you got?" asked Naughty.</p>

<p>"I've got a bike. It's my birthday present."</p>

<p>"Oh, great. Can I ride it?" asked Naughty.</p>

<p>"Oh, Naughty... But I want to ride it myself," said Fat.</p>

<p>"Okay," said Naughty. So Fat climbed upon his bike, and started cycling. There was a small gate in the fence of the yard. Fat wanted to ride through the gate. But the gate was too small for him. So his bike went through, and Fat got stuck in the gate.</p>

<p>"Help me! Help me!" cried Fat. "Catch my bike!"</p>

<p>Funny and Naughty ran to catch the bike. It wasn't easy, but at last they did it. They climbed upon the bike, and came back to Fat.</p>

<p>"Oh, now help me, please. I can't hang here like this. I must be at home at noon," cried Fat.</p>

<p>Funny and Naughty ran up to him. "Oh, you look so funny," laughed Funny.</p>

<p>"Don't laugh. Please get me down," cried Fat.</p>

<p>Funny and Naughty first pulled him, then pushed him, but nothing helped.</p>

<p>"Run to my mum," cried Fat. So Funny and Naughty ran to Fat's mum very fast. They told her all the story. She took a bottle of olive oil and ran to her poor son.</p>

<p>When she came up to him, she opened the bottle and poured the oil down on Fat.</p>

<p>"What are you doing?" shouted Fat.</p>

<p>"I'm helping you, dear," answered his mum.</p>

<p>"Oh, I'm falling," cried Fat and fell down on the grass.</p>

<div class="book-page">

  <div class="chapter-title">Fat's Birthday Party</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">So Fat went home with his mum and his bike. Mum put him into the bathtub and washed him with a lot of soap. When he was clean, it was half past two.</p>

<p>"Oh, dear," cried Fat's mum. "Your friends are coming soon and we are not ready."</p>

<p>So Fat went to tidy his room and his mum went to cook pizza and lay the table.</p>

<p>At ten past three, two of Fat's friends came. They were Funny and Naughty.</p>

<p>"Happy birthday to you," said Naughty and gave Fat a big bottle of olive oil. The boys laughed, but Fat was angry. He wanted to cry, but his friend said, "Don't be angry. It's a joke. Best wishes on your ninth birthday." And they gave Fat a big box. When Fat opened it, he saw a CD, a book about animals and a big jar of jam.</p>

<p>"Thank you, friends," said Fat and smiled. They sat down to watch a cartoon. Soon came Noisy and Clever. They gave Fat a big cake, an apple pie and a box of chocolates. Fat was happy.</p>

<p>It was half past four, but Slow wasn't there.</p>

<p>"Where is Slow?" asked Fat.</p>

<p>"We don't know," answered the children. "Let's phone her." And they phoned Slow. But she wasn't at home.</p>

<p>So the children sat down at the table. They ate and drank, talked and watched films and cartoons. Then they danced and sang and Clever played the piano. After that, they listened to new CDs. It was a very merry birthday party.</p>

<summary>Chapter Six</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Six</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Good Friend</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">It was Monday morning. Fat was in bed. His mum came to wake him up. Fat could hear her, but he couldn't get up. He had a terrible stomachache.</p>

<p>"Oh no! It is your birthday party. You ate too many sweets yesterday," cried his mother. She phoned Fat's teacher.</p>

<p>"Mrs Smart, my son is ill. He can't go to school. He has got a terrible stomachache."</p>

<p>"I see," said Mrs Smart. "I hope he feels better soon. I think it's his birthday party."</p>

<p>So Fat didn't go to school. He stayed in bed. In the afternoon, his friend Naughty visited him.</p>

<p>"How are you, Fat?" asked Naughty. "You look awful."</p>

<p>"I'm not well. My stomach hurts," said Fat.</p>

<p>"I see," said Naughty. "So you mustn't eat your jam, honey, and sweets?"</p>

<p>"No, I mustn't," said Fat. "And it's awful. I like sweets, cakes, pies, and chocolate so much."</p>

<p>"I can help you," said Naughty. "I can eat them up."</p>

<p>Fat started, but stopped. "Okay. Eat them up. I am too fat. And I have got a stomachache."</p>

<p>So Naughty sat down at the table and started eating. Fat closed his eyes. He didn't want to see that. First, Naughty ate all the cakes and slices of the pie. Then all the sweets and chocolate. Fat opened his eyes.</p>

<p>"Thank you," he said. "You are a very good friend. I hope your stomach is stronger than mine."</p>

<p>On Tuesday, Fat went to school. But Naughty wasn't there. After school, Fat visited his friend. Naughty was in bed. He had a terrible toothache.</p>

<p>"Oh, really?" asked Noisy. "Then I must learn French or Spanish. I hope our butterflies can't understand these languages."</p>

<summary>Chapter Eight</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Eight</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Dogs and Cats</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Fat had a cat. His name was Fluffy. Fat loved him very much. He went with Fluffy to the park every day. They usually walked in the park in the afternoon.</p>

<p>It was Thursday. It was time to go to the park. But Fat didn't go.</p>

<p>"Fat? It's time to go for a walk with Fluffy," his mother said.</p>

<p>"I can't, Mum. I'm still doing my homework. I've got a lot of homework today," said Fat.</p>

<p>"Okay, go later," said his mum.</p>

<p>It was seven o'clock in the evening when Fat's homework was ready. He took Fluffy in his arms and off they went. They came to the park and sat down on the grass. Suddenly, Fat saw Clever. She was with her dog, Dolly.</p>

<p>"Hey, Clever," shouted Fat. "Come here. Let's play together."</p>

<p>Now Clever saw Fat. But at the same time, Clever's dog saw Fat's cat. Dolly was very angry. She didn't like cats. In fact, she hated cats. So she barked and ran to Fluffy. Fluffy wasn't silly. In fact, he was a very clever cat. He ran fast to the nearest tree and climbed it up. Now Fluffy sat in the tree and Dolly jumped and barked under the tree. Clever and Fat were under the tree too. They didn't know what to do.</p>

<p>"Take away your dog," cried Fat.</p>

<p>"I can't," cried Clever. "She's stronger than me. Do it yourself, please. Or climb up the tree and take your cat down."</p>

<p>"I can't," said Fat. "Your dog is very angry and she is barking. She can bite me. I'm afraid."</p>

<p>"Get another cat," said Clever.</p>

<p>"Okay," said Funny and ran away. Soon, he came back. He had a cat in his arms.</p>

<p>"Show the cat to Dolly," commanded Clever, "and put it on the grass next to her." Funny showed the cat to Dolly and put it on the grass. Dolly looked at the cat for a moment and ran after it. And Clever ran after Dolly. But the cat wasn't sillier than Fluffy. It ran up to a tree and climbed it up very fast.</p>

<p>"Thank you, Funny," said Fat. Now he wasn't afraid. He climbed up the tree and took Fluffy down. But poor Clever was under another tree now.</p>

<p>"I can run to your parents," said Funny.</p>

<p>"But they're at work now," said Clever.</p>

<p>"I know what to do," said Funny. "Clever. Your house is near the park on the left. I can catch another cat, show it to Dolly, and put it on the grass on the left of this tree. I can do it again and again, and we can get to your house soon."</p>

<p>"That's a great idea," smiled Fat. "I'm sorry, friends, but I must go." And he went away, very fast.</p>

<p>"Oh, mummy, you're back. Come here," shouted Clever. Clever's mother ran out and took Dolly away. Clever told her all the story.</p>

<p>"Poor children," laughed Clever's mother. "Now let's have tea with sweets and forget cats."</p>

<p>"I like PE," answered Funny. "Do you?"</p>

<p>"Yes, I like PE too. But my favourite subject is Art. I like drawing and painting very much. And you, Fat and Clever?"</p>

<p>"I like Science because I like animals," said Fat.</p>

<p>"I don't like History. It's boring," said Noisy.</p>

<p>"How do you know? You never listen to the teacher. You always talk," said Slow.</p>

<p>"Yes, I always do when I sit with Mary. But at History lessons, I sit with Quiet. And she is very boring," said Noisy.</p>

<p>After Science, the children had Art. The teacher of Art, Mr Pencil, was a very kind and funny man. He asked the children to sit down and said, "I'm glad to see you again. Today, we are going to draw or paint animals. I hope you all have got a favourite animal. Maybe it's your pet or a farm animal. Maybe you saw it at the zoo. Now start."</p>

<p>All the children started drawing or painting. Mr Pencil walked around the classroom and looked at the children's pictures.</p>

<p>"What are you drawing, Slow?" he asked when he came up to Slow.</p>

<p>"I'm drawing a panda. I saw it at the zoo," said Slow.</p>

<p>"And what are you drawing, Funny?" asked Mr Pencil.</p>

<p>"I'm drawing a goat. I saw it in the country," said Funny.</p>

<p>Clever painted her dog, Dolly. Noisy drew the python from the zoo. Fat drew his cat, Fluffy. Then Mr Pencil came up to Naughty. He saw a picture of a man.</p>

<p>"Why are you painting a man? I asked you to paint your favourite animal," said Mr Pencil angrily.</p>

<p>"Yes, I know," answered Naughty. "But our Science teacher said at the lesson that people are animals too. And you are my favourite teacher. I'm painting you, Mr Pencil," smiled Naughty.</p>
