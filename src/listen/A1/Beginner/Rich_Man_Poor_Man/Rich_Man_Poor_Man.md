<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter One</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">A Letter for Adam</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">One day a postman came to my village. The postman brought me a letter from my son, Sol.</p>

<p>"Is your name Adam?" the postman asked.</p>

<p>"Yes," I said.</p>

<p>"I've got a letter for you." The postman read the envelope. "Adam of the village of Minta."</p>

<p>"A letter for me? Who is it from?" I asked.</p>

<p>The postman looked at the envelope again. "From Sol," he said. He gave me the letter and walked away.</p>

<p>"Martha, Martha!" I called to my wife. "Come here. We have a letter from our son Sol."</p>

<p>Martha came out and looked at the letter. I was excited, but she was also worried. "A letter from Sol," she said. "Is he alive and well? I'm going to find the schoolteacher. He can read the letter."</p>

<p>There was no school fifty years ago, so I cannot read or write. I live in a small village. The only work is farming. My only son Sol left the village two years ago, and my three daughters are married. Sol is making a lot of money in a foreign country.</p>

<p>Martha and the schoolteacher came back. A lot of other people came. Everyone wanted to hear my letter. The schoolteacher opened the envelope and read the letter.</p>

<p>"20 Taylor Street, London E-19, England. 16th of March.</p>

<p>"Dear Father, I am living in London. I have a job in a factory. The work is very hard. I often work at night, but the pay is good. I am well, and I live with people from my country. I am sending you one hundred pounds in this letter. This is for you and my mother. Love to you and Mother, Sol."</p>

<p>"One hundred pounds!" I said to the schoolteacher. "You're wrong. It's a mistake."</p>

<p>"No," the schoolteacher said, "I'm not wrong. It's not a mistake. Here is the money." And he gave me a piece of paper.</p>

<p>"What is this?" I asked.</p>

<p>"A money order," the schoolteacher said. "Go to Darpur. Take this money order to the post office in Darpur. The money order is worth one hundred pounds. The post office official will give you the money."</p>

<p>"One hundred pounds," I said again.</p>

<p>Everyone laughed and said, "Adam, you are a rich man. You can buy many things for your farm and for your house."</p>

<p>"And I can buy some good food and drink in Darpur. I am going to give a party for you all," I told my friends.</p>

<p>Martha said, "Sol is a good son."</p>

<p>That evening, the village people talked about the money order and my money. Martha and I also talked about the money. We needed many things for the farm.</p>

<summary>Chapter Two</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Two</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Adam Goes to Darpur</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">The next morning, I got up very early. It was dark and everyone was asleep, but I was going to Darpur. I washed and dressed carefully. I put on my best clothes and I carried my best stick. I put the money order carefully in my pocket and I said goodbye to Martha.</p>

<p>I walked ten miles to the main road. I sat down at the main road and ate my breakfast. I waited for the bus. I waited for two hours. Then the bus came and I got on.</p>

<p>It is a long way to Darpur. The bus takes three hours. I arrived in Darpur and walked to the post office immediately.</p>

<p>I do not often go to Darpur. I only know the market and one shop. This is the shop of Rikh. I buy things for my farm from Rikh.</p>

<p>There were a lot of people in the post office. I asked about money orders. A man showed me the queue. There was a long line of people and I waited at the back. Finally it was my turn. I was at the front of the queue. But the official did not serve me.</p>

<p>"Excuse me," I said, "it's my turn. I'm next."</p>

<p>"You are next, old man. I'm very busy. Look at my papers. Look at all these people. I am very busy and you must wait."</p>

<p>So I waited. Finally, the official looked at me. "What do you want?" he asked.</p>

<p>I gave him my money order. "This is my money order for one hundred pounds," I replied.</p>

<p>The official held out his hand. "Identity card," he said.</p>

<p>"Excuse me, I don't understand," I replied.</p>

<p>"Your identity card," the official said again. "Give me your identity card."</p>

<p>"What is an identity card?" I asked.</p>

<p>"I can't give you any money for this money order. First, I must see your identity card. Your identity card gives your name and your address. Your identity card describes you. There is a photograph of you in your identity card. I don't know you. Who are you?"</p>

<p>The official was a little angry, but I was also angry. "Who am I?" I said. "Everyone knows me. I am Adam of the village of Minta. I haven't got an identity card and I don't need an identity card."</p>

<p>"Old man, I'm very busy and you're very stupid," the official said. "Who are you? Where is Minta?"</p>

<p>"Give me my money. Give me my one hundred pounds," I said.</p>

<p>The official looked angry and said, "Show me your identity card. I don't know you." The official gave back my money order and he turned away.</p>

<p>"Where can I buy an identity card?" I asked the official. He did not speak to me. He did not answer.</p>

<p>"Go to the Ministry of the Interior," a man said. He was standing in the queue and he told me the way.</p>

<summary>Chapter Three</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Three</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">An Identity Card</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">I walked to the Ministry of the Interior. I waited in another queue. I spoke to another official. I asked for an identity card.</p>

<p>"Fill in this application form," the official said, "and bring the form and three photographs of yourself and two pounds. Come back tomorrow."</p>

<p>"Tomorrow!" I said. "Can I have an identity card today, please? I live in Minta. I live five hours' journey from Darpur. I'm an old man."</p>

<p>"Yes, come back tomorrow." And the official turned away.</p>

<p>I walked away from the Ministry of the Interior. I walked to the market. At the market, I looked at everyone. I was looking for a man from my village, but I could not find a villager. So then I walked to the shop of Rikh. I spoke to Rikh.</p>

<p>"I want an identity card," I told him, "but I need three photographs of myself."</p>

<p>"I see. You need some photographs." And he showed me the way to a photographer.</p>

<p>I found the house. The photographer was asleep, but he came to me quickly. The man's clothes were dirty, and he looked tired.</p>

<p>"I need an identity card," I said. "I want three photographs of myself."</p>

<p>"Yes, you want three photographs of yourself," the photographer replied, "and I take very good photographs. Come and see my camera."</p>

<p>We walked into his room. In the middle of the room was a large camera. "This is the best camera in Darpur. This camera is very, very good," the photographer said proudly.</p>

<p>"I've never seen a camera," I said. "I don't know about cameras. Hurry up and take a photograph of me."</p>

<p>"Please do not hurry me, old man," the photographer said. "I am an artist." And he gave me a mirror and a comb.</p>

<p>"I don't want a mirror. I don't want a comb. Please take my photograph. I'm going to Minta this afternoon, and I'm in a hurry," I said.</p>

<p>"Yes," he said. "But first, the price. This is the best camera in Darpur, and I'm the best photographer. Three photographs will cost you two pounds fifty."</p>

<p>"Two pounds fifty!" I laughed.</p>

<p>"Two pounds fifty. And pay me now, please," the photographer answered.</p>

<p>I did not know the price of photographs. What could I do? Then the photographer said, "You're an old man. For you, the price is two pounds." So I gave him the money, and he took the photographs.</p>

<p>"Come back tomorrow morning," he said.</p>

<p>"I want my three photographs now, immediately," I said.</p>

<p>"Don't be stupid," the photographer said. "Photographs take twenty-four hours. Come back tomorrow."</p>

<p>What could I do? So I said, "Yes, tomorrow morning."</p>

<p>"Good," said the photographer. "Now go. I have a lot of work. I'm very busy."</p>

<p>I went back to the bus station. I sat on the bus for three hours. I walked ten miles back to my village. It was night time, and I was very tired. Martha and my friends were waiting for me.</p>

<p>"Where is the money?" Martha asked.</p>

<p>"I have no money. I cannot change the money order. First I must have an identity card." And I told Martha everything. "Tomorrow I'm going to Darpur again," I said. Then I did not talk again. I was very tired, and it was late at night. I lay on my bed, and I slept.</p>

<summary>Chapter Four</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Four</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">No Photographs</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">I woke up late the next morning. The sun was already high. I did not walk to the main road and catch a bus. All the buses go to Darpur early in the morning. So I stayed at home on Wednesday. I was still very tired. I rested and talked to the villagers about the money order. I told them about the identity card and the photographer.</p>

<p>The schoolteacher said, "Yes, the official is correct. In a post office you always show your identity card." The schoolteacher filled in my application form for an identity card.</p>

<p>Application Form for Identity Card</p>
<p>Name: Adam Jami</p>
<p>Village: Minta</p>
<p>Age: 72 years</p>
<p>Place of birth: Village of Minta</p>
<p>Occupation: Farmer</p>
<p>Colour of eyes: Brown</p>

<p>On Thursday I travelled to Darpur again. I walked to the main road and I caught a bus. In Darpur I walked to the house of the photographer. I knocked on the door of the house. No one came to the door. I knocked again loudly with my stick. A woman opened the door.</p>

<p>"Who are you? What do you want?" she asked.</p>

<p>"Can I have my photographs, please?" I said.</p>

<p>"Your photographs? I have no photographs," the woman replied.</p>

<p>"I came here on Tuesday. Where is the photographer?" I asked.</p>

<p>"He's out. He's not here." And the woman closed the door. I shouted at her. "I'm waiting here for him."</p>

<p>After a long time the photographer came back. He looked tired and he smelt of beer.</p>

<p>"Give me my photographs," I said. "I have waited a long time for you."</p>

<p>The photographer looked at me and said, "I don't know you, old man. What photographs are you talking about?"</p>

<p>"My three photographs for my identity card. I paid you two pounds for them on Tuesday. Give me my photographs immediately or my money."</p>

<p>"Your photographs? Your money? What are you talking about?" the photographer said. "Show me the paper. Show me the receipt for your money."</p>

<p>"My receipt?" I asked.</p>

<p>"Yes, where is your receipt?" the photographer asked.</p>

<p>"You didn't give me a receipt!" I shouted. "Give me my photographs or my money immediately!" And I hit the photographer hard with my stick. I am old, but I am still strong. The photographer fell on the ground. He shouted, "Help, help! This old man is killing me!" And I hit him hard again.</p>

<p>Lots of people ran out of their houses. I hit the photographer again, and two men held me. I could not get away from the two men. The photographer was very angry, and I was very angry. Lots of people were shouting.</p>

<p>Then a policeman came. The photographer shouted to the policeman, "This old man hit me three times with his stick. He's a thief and a murderer. He wants my money." The policeman held my arm and said, "Come with me to the police station." I did not say anything. We walked to the police station.</p>

<p>At the police station, the policeman asked me, "Did you hit that man three times?"</p>

<p>"Yes," I said. "He didn't give me my photographs."</p>

<p>"Show me your identity card," said the policeman.</p>

<p>"I am Adam of Minta village," I replied, "and I haven't got an identity card."</p>

<p>"Old man," said the policeman, "go back to your village. Don't come here and fight. Keep out of Darpur." And he pushed me into the street.</p>

<p>I went back to my village. I was tired and angry.</p>

<summary>Chapter Five</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Five</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Adam Changes His Money Order</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Next day, I told my story to all the villagers. The villagers were angry. Martha was very unhappy. She said, "Sol is working very hard. He is sending money and we can't have the money. What are we going to do?"</p>

<p>I did not know. Then in the evening, the schoolteacher came to my house again.</p>

<p>"Adam, perhaps I can help you," the schoolteacher said. "Here is a letter to Mr. Sheth."</p>

<p>"Mr. Sheth?" I said. "Who is he?"</p>

<p>"He is an important man in Darpur, and he is a friend of my wife's cousin," replied the schoolteacher. "This letter is to Mr. Sheth. The letter is about your money order. Perhaps he can help you."</p>

<p>I took the letter and thanked the schoolteacher.</p>

<p>So I travelled to Darpur again on Saturday for the third time. After a long time, I found Mr. Sheth's house. The door was opened by a tall man.</p>

<p>"Can I see Mr. Sheth?" I asked.</p>

<p>"And who are you?" the tall man asked.</p>

<p>"I have a letter for Mr. Sheth," I replied.</p>

<p>"I see. Can I have the letter, please?" And the tall man held out his hand.</p>

<p>"The letter is here," I said. And I took the letter out of my pocket. "But I must see Mr. Sheth."</p>

<p>"Many people want to see Mr. Sheth," the tall man told me. "He is a very busy man and a very important man. Mr. Sheth is not here at the moment, but give me your letter and Mr. Sheth will read it later."</p>

<p>I gave the tall man the letter. Then I waited. Later, a large black car came, and the man went into the house. A long time later, the tall man opened the door again.</p>

<p>"Come in now, please, and follow me," he said.</p>

<p>I followed the tall man. We went into a large room with fine carpets and big chairs. Another man was in the room. He was drinking.</p>

<p>"This is Mr. Sheth," said the tall man.</p>

<p>"I am Adam of Minta village," I replied.</p>

<p>"Yes, I know," said Mr. Sheth. "Thank you for the letter. I hope I can help you. I like to help people. Please, sit down." Mr. Sheth smiled. His clothes were new and smart.</p>

<p>"Thank you," I said.</p>

<p>"Can I see the money order, please?" Mr. Sheth asked. I took the money order out of my pocket. By now, the money order was dirty and looked very old. I gave it to Mr. Sheth.</p>

<p>"This money order is for one hundred pounds," I said. "My son sent it from a foreign country."</p>

<p>Mr. Sheth unfolded the money order and looked at it. "You can't change this money order," he said. "This money order is not worth one hundred pounds."</p>

<p>"Not worth one hundred pounds?" I asked.</p>

<p>"This money order is worth nothing. Worthless."</p>

<p>"Worthless?" I asked.</p>

<p>Then Mr. Sheth looked at the money order again. "Yes, worthless. Your son does not understand about money orders. This money order is not correct for our country," Mr. Sheth said. Then he looked at the money order again and said, "And this money order is also old. It is out of date."</p>

<p>I said nothing. Mr. Sheth gave me the money order back. Then Mr. Sheth smiled and said, "I am very sorry. You are an old man. You came a long way from your village. What can I give you to eat and drink?"</p>

<p>I was not hungry. But Mr. Sheth went out of the room. Then he brought me some coffee and some cakes. I drank my coffee.</p>

<p>"Old man," said Mr. Sheth, "I like to help people. I am a rich man. Give me your money order."</p>

<p>I gave my money order to Mr. Sheth. "Yes, this money order is worthless," he said again. "But I am going to help you. I am going to change this money order for you. I am going to give you some money." Mr. Sheth went out of the room. I felt very happy again.</p>

<p>After a few minutes, the tall man came into the room. He gave me an envelope. "This is from Mr. Sheth. You can go now," the tall man said.</p>

<p>I went out of the house. I walked along the road to the bus station. I opened the envelope and I took out my money. I counted the money. It was ten pounds.</p>

<p>I thought about my only son, Sol. My son Sol had sent me a money order for one hundred pounds. Mr. Sheth had given me ten pounds. I felt old and I felt poor again.</p>
