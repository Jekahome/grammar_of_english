  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">This story takes place in San Francisco and in Bodega Bay. San Francisco is a beautiful city on the Pacific coast in the state of California. Bodega Bay is a small town on the coast, north of San Francisco. The story talks about a big marine sanctuary called the Gulf of the Farallones National Marine Sanctuary, twenty-seven miles west of San Francisco, and the Point Reyes National Seashore, which is another protected area.</p>

<p>San Francisco is one of America's most beautiful and exciting cities. It is an international center for business, education, science, and the arts. The city is famous for its hills. It was built on forty-three hills and you can always get great views of the bay. The main tourist attractions are the Golden Gate Bridge, Alcatraz Island, Telegraph Hill, and Fisherman's Wharf. Around 800,000 people live in the city today.</p>

<p>The Farallones National Marine Sanctuary is one of the thirteen marine sanctuaries in the United States. There are seven groups of islands and it is home to whales, dolphins, great white sharks, sea lions, and seabirds. Tourists can't go onto the islands, but they can see them from boats. Only scientists can go on the islands. Point Reyes National Seashore is part of the Farallones National Marine Sanctuary. It was set up in 1962 to protect all kinds of sea life, plants, and trees. Visitors can walk along the long beach and watch whales and dolphins swim by.</p>

<p>Bodega Bay is a small town on the Pacific coast, about forty miles northwest of San Francisco. The bay was discovered in 1775 by Peruvian explorer Juan Francisco de la Bodega y Quadra. About 2,000 people live there today. The town was the setting for Alfred Hitchcock's film The Birds in 1963. It is well known for its long sandy beaches and its protected harbor.</p>

    <p class="first-para">Kate and Mike Sullivan live in San Francisco with their parents and their pet dog Lucky. Their home is on Russian Hill, and from their house Kate and Mike have a great view of San Francisco Bay and the Golden Gate Bridge. Their father, Frank Sullivan, is a professor and teaches marine biology at San Francisco University. Their mother, Liz Sullivan, works in a big bookshop in the city center.</p>

<p>Kate is fourteen and her brother Mike is fifteen. Kate goes to Lincoln Middle School and Mike goes to Galileo High School. Kate is a pretty girl with long blonde hair and blue eyes. She likes school but doesn't like math. She loves dancing and belongs to the Lincoln Dance Club. She's a very good dancer and wants to become a professional dancer one day. Mike is tall with brown hair and blue eyes. He is in his first year of high school and he always has a lot of homework. He is an excellent swimmer and is a member of the Galileo High School swimming team.</p>

<p>Our story begins on a sunny Monday morning early in June.</p>

<p>"Half past seven," said Kate, putting on her watch. "Time for breakfast."</p>

<p>"Two more weeks and school is over," said Mike happily.</p>

<p>"Summer vacation, the best time of the year," said Kate laughing. Kate was also excited because after the summer she was going to start high school.</p>

<p>"Good morning," said Mrs. Sullivan, who was preparing breakfast. "Kate, remember you have an eye doctor's appointment today after school with Dr. Lee. Please don't be late."</p>

<p>"I know, Mom," said Kate. "But I don't want to go. I don't want to wear glasses."</p>

<p>"Kate, you can't study well at school. You need a pair of glasses. If you don't get them, your eyesight will get worse," said Mr. Sullivan, drinking a cup of coffee.</p>

<p>"There's nothing wrong with glasses. I wear them. A lot of kids wear them. Susan Garcia in my class just got glasses," said Mike. "And she looks better with glasses than without them."</p>

<p>Kate slowly ate her breakfast and said, "Okay, okay. I'll wear them to see the board."</p>

<p>Suddenly there was a knock on the door. It was Julie Bennett, Kate's best friend.</p>

<p>"Hi," said Julie, a pretty fourteen-year-old girl with short black hair. "I came to pick you up so we can walk to school together."</p>

<p>"Hi, Julie," said Kate. "I'm still having breakfast. Just sit down and have some cookies."</p>

<p>"Hi, Julie," said Mrs. Sullivan as she turned on the television to watch the weather report and the morning news.</p>

<p>"Another warm sunny day in San Francisco," said the speaker. "With heavy afternoon fog along the Pacific coast, particularly in the Bodega Bay area."</p>

<p>"It's always foggy in Bodega Bay," said Mike.</p>

<p>"Do you remember our adventure up there?"</p>

<p>"When did you go to Bodega Bay?" asked Julie, eating a cookie.</p>

<p>"It was about eighteen months ago," said Kate. "And Carlos Gomez came with us. We had quite an adventure."</p>

<p>"Sounds exciting," said Julie. "Tell me about it."</p>

<p>Kate looked at Mike and her parents, and they all started laughing.</p>

<p>"Well, Mom and I have to go to work," said Mr. Sullivan. "It's getting late. See you this evening. Bye."</p>

<p>"Okay, let me tell you about it," said Kate, looking at her watch. "Mom, Dad, Mike, Carlos and I went to the Farallon Islands on Dad's new boat, the Pelican. He was studying the sea birds and the fish in the area. We were all excited about the trip, but the first night I saw the ghost of a boy near my bed. I screamed and was scared, but no one believed me. Then one night, Mom and Dad heard a ship's bell and saw a yellow light at sea. They thought someone needed help, so they took the lifeboat and left the Pelican. The next morning, we couldn't find them, and we were worried. We took the boat and went to look for them. In the fog, we saw a strange old ship, the Sonora. Later, we discovered that it was an old Spanish pirate ship that was lost at sea in 1608."</p>

<p>"What? A ship that was lost four hundred years ago?" cried Julie, who stopped eating.</p>

<p>"Yes, it was a ghost ship," said Kate. "We knew Mom and Dad were on that ship because their lifeboat was there. We were scared, but we went onto the ship and we had a terrible surprise. The ship's crew were all ghosts. Ghosts of sailors and pirates. We were terrified because we saw Mom and Dad sitting in a corner. We went to help them, but then the ghost of Captain Salamanca spoke to us. He was terrible. He killed people and they became part of his crew of ghosts. He said he needed a big crew because he was looking for some lost treasure. He wanted to kill us all. Suddenly, Lucky jumped on the table and a big candle fell and a fire started. Ghosts are afraid of fire, so they moved away and we helped Mom and Dad get off the ship. Before leaving, Mike saw Captain Salamanca's treasure map on the table and took it."</p>

<p>"What an adventure!" said Julie.</p>

<p>"Do you still have the treasure map?"</p>

<p>"Mike has it," said Kate. "But he hid it because it's a big secret."</p>

<p>That afternoon, Kate went to the eye doctor with Julie. Dr. Lee was a friendly young man. He gave her an eye test and said, "Kate, you need glasses to see things in the distance." Julie helped Kate choose her new glasses and she gave them to Dr. Lee, who said, "Good choice. They'll be ready on Thursday, Kate."</p>

<summary>Chapter Three: Sharks</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Three</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Sharks</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">A shark is a kind of fish. There are about 400 different kinds of sharks. Sharks live in different parts of the world in the warm seas of the south and in the cold waters of the north. Some live at the bottom of the sea and others swim near the coast. Sharks swam in the sea before dinosaurs walked on the earth.</p>

<p>A lot of sharks are gray and have long bodies. But some sharks have different shapes. For example, hammerhead sharks have big, flat heads. Angel sharks live on the bottom of the sea and have flat bodies. Most sharks are between sixty and ninety centimeters long. The biggest shark is the whale shark, and it is longer than a bus. The smallest is the pygmy shark, and it is about the size of a banana.</p>

<p>Most sharks have a lot of very strong teeth that look like triangles. When a shark loses a tooth, another grows in its place. Sharks usually hunt alone, but sometimes they hunt in groups. Sharks are very good hunters. They can feel the movements of other animals near to them, and they can see well. Sharks can hear sounds from far away, like the sound of a boat or a ship. Sharks also have an excellent sense of smell.</p>

<p>Most people think all sharks are dangerous, but some of the biggest sharks eat only very small plants and animals in the sea called plankton. One of the most dangerous sharks is the Great White. It usually attacks and kills seals, dolphins, other sharks, and sometimes people.</p>

<p>The Great White Shark is about six meters long and weighs about 2,000 kilograms. It can live from thirty to one hundred years. In Steven Spielberg's 1975 film Jaws, the Great White Shark is a terrible man-eater. But Great White Sharks don't usually eat people. They often think a person in the sea is a seal and attack it by mistake. Sometimes Great White Sharks attack boats. They are always very hungry, and sometimes they even eat objects like tins and bottles.</p>

<p>Today, sharks are in danger. People kill them for sport, food, and sometimes by mistake. Soup made from shark fins is a popular dish in some parts of the world. Sometimes fishermen catch sharks and cut off their fins. Then they throw the sharks back into the sea. Without their fins, sharks cannot swim, and they die. Every year, more than 100 million sharks are killed, and some kinds of sharks are in danger of extinction.</p>

    <p class="first-para">Mike's science class had a great time at Muir Woods National Park. Miss Williamson, the science teacher, showed them some of the world's oldest and tallest trees. <p>"It won't be easy," said Carlos. "Pirates were clever people, and their maps had secret messages that are difficult to understand."</p>

<p>"No, I hid it on the bookshelf in my room," said Mike. "Only Kate knows where it is."</p>

<p>"Do your parents know about it?" asked Carlos.</p>

<p>"Don't worry, Dad," said Mike. "Kate, Carlos and I can stay on the beach, play in the sea, or go for walks in the hills."</p>

<p>"Alright," said Mrs. Sullivan. "You can stay on the beach, but you mustn't swim in the sea. Remember, this is the Pacific Ocean, and there are a lot of sharks, and they swim close to the beach. They're very dangerous."</p>

<p>Mike laughed and said, "We'll be careful, Mom. And we won't go swimming."</p>

<p>"You're free to do what you want during the day," said Mrs. Sullivan. "But we'll meet for dinner on the Pelican every evening at about six. If you have any problems, just call or text Mom or me on our cell phones."</p>

<p>Kate and Mike went upstairs and phoned Carlos.</p>

<p>"Hi, Carlos," said Mike. "Can you come to my house right now? We can look at the map and start making plans."</p>

<p>"I'll be there in ten minutes," said Carlos happily.</p>

<p>Mike took the old treasure map from the bookshelf and put it on his desk. When Carlos arrived, the three of them sat around the desk and looked at it carefully.</p>

<p>"Gee, this is exciting," said Carlos, touching it. "An old pirate map. Look, that's where Bodega Bay is, but it doesn't say that. It just says Bahía. That's interesting because today in Spanish, bay is bahía. But maybe the spelling was different in the 1600s. Looking at this map, we can see a town already existed there at that time. It was probably a pirate town," said Mike.</p>

<p>"There's a big harbor, the perfect place for pirates to hide. This is also very interesting because my book on the history of California says that Bodega Bay wasn't discovered until 1775. Looking at this map, people lived in the area long before."</p>

<p>"Look on the corner of the map," said Kate. "There's a date, 1608."</p>

<p>"That's the year of the shipwreck of the Sonora," said Carlos. "Captain Salamanca probably hid his treasure that year before the shipwreck in November."</p>

<p>"Hmm. But what else does it say on the map?" asked Mike.</p>

<p>"Hmm, north of the town, there's the word Cementerio. In Spanish, that means cemetery," said Carlos. "It was probably the town cemetery."</p>

<p>"Near the cemetery, there's a place called Hacienda Estrada," said Mike. "And there's a small river near it called Río Estrada."</p>

<p>"Hacienda means a big farm or ranch. And Estrada was probably the name of the family who owned it," said Carlos.</p>

<p>"Look, there's a drawing of a lighthouse on the coast," said Mike.</p>

<p>"There's a big black X east of the town with the word Agua," said Kate.</p>

<p>"Agua in Spanish means water," said Carlos. "That was probably where the pirates got their drinking water. A water well."</p>

<p>"There are some red lines that go south from the town to what is now Doran Beach," said Mike.</p>

<p>"But where's the treasure?" asked Kate.</p>

<p>"Good question," said Mike.</p>

<p>"The places on the map are probably important clues. We have almost a week to explore them," said Carlos.</p>

<p>"Why don't we start with the water well?" said Kate excitedly. "That's a good place to hide a treasure."</p>

<p>"Okay," said Mike and Carlos. "That'll be the first place we look," said Mike.</p>

<p>Kate spoke first. "Is this the treasure? It's terrible."</p>

<p>"Maybe there's something else in the treasure chest," said Carlos. He slowly moved closer to the chest and looked inside. "There's nothing inside except the skeleton," he said sadly.</p>

<p>"Why did someone put the body of a pirate inside the treasure chest and lock it?" asked Mike.</p>

<p>"What's happening?" asked Kate nervously. "Why is water coming in here?"</p>

<p>"Oh no, the tide!" said Mike. "It's high tide and these caves fill up with seawater. We have to get out of here now."</p>

<p>"But wait!" said Carlos. "Perhaps there's a clue or another map in the skeleton's clothes, in his pockets."</p>

<p>"Are you going to touch it?" asked Kate, who was scared.</p>

<p>"We have to hurry," said Mike. "If more water comes in, we'll be in big trouble."</p>

<p>Carlos started touching the skeleton's clothes. He looked in all the pockets but found nothing. Then he took off the big black hat and looked inside it.</p>

<p>"There's something inside the hat," cried Carlos. "It's an old piece of paper that says Juan Martínez de los Robles." He took the old piece of paper and showed it to Kate and Mike.</p>

<p>"Maybe that was his name," said Kate.</p>

<p>"No, I don't think so," said Mike. "You don't write your name on a piece of paper and put it in your hat. I think it's a clue."</p>

<p>Carlos quickly put the piece of paper in his pocket. Lucky started barking loudly again because a lot of water was now coming into the cave. Mike picked him up. The three friends couldn't get out because water was coming down the stone steps. And the cave was quickly filling up with water. They were in big trouble.</p>

<p>"How can we get out of here?" asked Kate, who was now starting to get very scared. "The water is almost up to my knees."</p>

<p>"There's only one way out," said Mike, looking at the small hole high above their heads.</p>

<p>"But we can't climb up there," said Carlos. "It's impossible. We need someone on the outside to throw down a rope."</p>

<p>"I have an idea," said Mike. "Kate, if you hold Lucky, Carlos and I can lift you. Then you can push Lucky up to the top. He is small and light and can get out of the hole."</p>

<p>Mike looked at Lucky, who was in his arms and said, "Listen carefully, Lucky. Go and find help."</p>

<p>Carlos and Mike lifted Kate and Lucky. It wasn't easy, but they were able to get Kate quite high and she pushed Lucky outside.</p>

<p>There were a lot of people on the beach that day. And when Lucky got out of the hole, he started barking loudly and running around.</p>

<p>"What's that dog doing?" said a tall young man who was sitting on the beach with some friends.</p>

<p>"He's very excited," said his friend.</p>

<p>"He's trying to tell us something," said another young man. "Perhaps someone needs help."</p>

<p>"Let's follow him and see," said another young man.</p>

<p>The four young men followed Lucky to the small hole and looked inside.</p>

<p>"Does anyone need help?" shouted the tall young man.</p>

<p>"Yes, the water in the cave is up to our knees and we can't get out," shouted Mike. "Can you please help us?"</p>

<p>"It's high tide," shouted the young man. "Don't worry, we can get you out of there. I've got a rope in my car. Wait a moment and I'll be back." He ran across the beach to the parking lot, got a long rope from his car and ran back.</p>

<p>"Okay, listen," shouted the young man who was standing over the hole. "I'm going to send this rope down to you. Hold on to it. Then my friends and I will pull you up."</p>

<p>"Okay," shouted Kate, Mike, and Carlos.</p>

<p>The four young men pulled Kate up first, then Carlos, and then Mike. Lucky jumped into Kate's arms and looked at her.</p>

<p>"Thanks, Lucky," said Kate, smiling at the little black dog. "And thank you," she said, looking at the four young men.</p>

<p>"Yeah," said Mike. "Thanks a lot."</p>

<p>"You were very kind to help us," said Carlos. "We were in big trouble down there."</p>

<p>"You guys are lucky," said one of the men. "I always keep a rope in my car because my friends and I are mountain climbers."</p>

<p>"What were you doing in the cave?" asked the tall young man.</p>

<p>"Oh, we were just looking around," said Mike, and his face became red.</p>

<p>"Oh, well, it's getting late," said Kate. "We have to go back to Bodega Bay. Thanks again for your help."</p>

<p>"We were happy to help you," said the tall young man. "But next time, be careful."</p>

<p>"Okay. We will," said the three friends.</p>

<summary>Chapter Seven: Pirates</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Seven</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Pirates</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">A long time ago, ships were the only form of transport to cross the sea. The seas were full of pirates and they robbed ships. Sometimes they killed the crew too. Pirates were dangerous people. Their sailing ships usually had the Jolly Roger flag. Let's take a look at some pirates from the past.</p>

<p>The most famous pirate was probably Edward Teach (1680–1718). He was called Blackbeard because he had a long black beard. He always wore a lot of knives and guns because he wanted to scare people. He and his pirates robbed a lot of ships in the Caribbean Sea and in the Atlantic Ocean. Blackbeard had a very big treasure, but no one knows where he hid it. He was killed during a long battle with the British Navy.</p>

<p>Bartholomew Roberts (1682–1722) was another famous pirate. He was also called Black Bart and he was a very successful pirate. Between 1719 and 1722, he robbed more than 470 ships in the Caribbean Sea and in the Atlantic Ocean. He was an unusual pirate because he was always clean and well dressed. He liked reading books and listening to music. There were always musicians on his ships. He was killed during a battle at sea.</p>

<p>Anne Bonny (1700–1782) was Irish and Mary Read (1690–1721) was English. They were female pirates in the early 1700s. They dressed like men and fought like men. Anne Bonny loved another pirate, Calico Jack (1682–1720). Anne, Mary, and Calico Jack lived together as pirates in the Caribbean Sea. Together they robbed a lot of ships and had a big treasure. In 1720, after a battle with a British ship, Anne, Mary, and Calico Jack were put in prison. Calico Jack was hanged and Mary died in prison. People think that Anne left prison and went to live in the American colonies.</p>

<p>Today there are modern pirates in different parts of the world — Indonesia, the Philippines, the South China Sea, Somalia, Brazil, and the Florida coast. They are dangerous and have fast modern boats and big guns. They go on to ships and rob them. Military forces around the world and international organizations, such as the United Nations, have a difficult job trying to stop them.</p>

<summary>Chapter Eight: The Hacienda Estrada</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Eight</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">The Hacienda Estrada</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Mike, Kate, and Carlos got back to the Pelican at dinner time.</p>

<p>"Hi," said Mr. Sullivan. "We did a lot of interesting work today at Point Reyes. We saw two great white sharks swimming by. Tomorrow we're going to the Farallon Islands. How was your day?" asked Mrs. Sullivan.</p>

<p>"Ah, interesting," said Mike, looking at his sister and Carlos.</p>

<p>"We explored the beach," said Carlos.</p>

<p>"Are you guys getting bored?" asked Mr. Sullivan.</p>

<p>"No, of course not," said Kate, smiling.</p>

<p>The next morning after breakfast, Mr. and Mrs. Sullivan sailed to the Farallon Islands. It was a foggy morning and the three friends stood on the pier and talked about their scary adventure in the cave. Carlos took out the old piece of paper he found in the skeleton's hat and looked at it.</p>

<p>"Is this a clue? What does it mean?" he asked.</p>

<p>"It's the name of a man. Or perhaps it isn't," said Kate.</p>

<p>"Let's take another look at the map," said Mike.</p>

<p>"Why don't we go to the tourist office and ask about the Hacienda Estrada?" said Kate. "Perhaps we can get some useful information."</p>

<p>"Okay," said Carlos. "I'm sure the treasure is here somewhere."</p>

<p>Margaret Healy worked at the Bodega Bay tourist office and knew all about the history of the town.</p>

<p>"The Estrada family was a rich and important family in the 1600s," said Margaret. "They were the first to build a home and a big farm here, the Hacienda Estrada. The family's only daughter was called Isabella and she married a sea captain called Juan Martínez. Soon after their marriage he was killed during a terrible fight with pirates and his young wife never married again. She was always sad and dressed in black. She rarely left the hacienda."</p>

<p>"What a sad story," said Kate.</p>

<p>"Was the captain's last name Robles or de los Robles?" asked Mike.</p>

<p>"No," answered Margaret. "His last name was Martínez."</p>

<p>"Where was he buried?" asked Carlos.</p>

<p>"No one knows," said Margaret.</p>

<p>"What about the old town cemetery?" asked Kate.</p>

<p>"It's about 400 years old and no one can read the names on the tombstones anymore," she said. "If you're looking for old Spanish things, go and see the lighthouse at Twin Oaks Point. It's on the cliff."</p>

<p>The three friends thanked her and left. They decided to follow the treasure map and go to the area northwest of Bodega Bay. They climbed up the green hills and stopped near a stone wall.</p>

<p>"What's this?" asked Mike looking at the map.</p>

<p>"It looks like part of the old Hacienda Estrada," said Carlos. "Look, there's a small river nearby too. The same river that's on the map."</p>

<p>They looked around and saw another stone wall and some steps. "This looks like a wall of a house. Maybe the hacienda," said Kate.</p>

<p>"Yeah, but there's almost nothing here," said Mike. "Let's move on and see if we can find the cemetery."</p>

<p>They walked to the cliff and a strong wind was blowing. From the top of the cliff they could see the Pacific Ocean.</p>

<p>"What a beautiful place," said Kate, looking at the green-blue sea. "Let's stop here and have lunch. I'm hungry."</p>

<p>"We are too," said Mike and Carlos. They sat down and had chicken sandwiches and fruit juice.</p>

<p>"Hey, look down there," said Carlos eating his sandwich. From the top of the cliff they saw the old cemetery that was on Salamanca's map and the old Spanish lighthouse.</p>

<p>After lunch they slowly climbed down and got to the old cemetery.</p>

<p>"There isn't much here," said Mike. "Just a few very old tombstones and lots of grass."</p>

<p>"Gee, look at this one," said Kate. "I can't read the name, but the date is 1624. This place is probably full of ghosts. Let's go. I'm scared."</p>

<p>Carlos laughed and said, "We'll never find the treasure here."</p>

<p>"Perhaps there isn't any treasure," said Kate.</p>

<p>"I'm sure there's a treasure," said Mike. "Don't forget that Captain Salamanca was looking for it. And we have his map."</p>

<p>"You're right, Mike," said Carlos. "We have to study the clues carefully. The name Juan Martínez de los Robles means something. But what? We have to find out."</p>

<summary>Chapter Nine: The Old Lighthouse</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Nine</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">The Old Lighthouse</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">"Let's go and see the old lighthouse at Twin Oaks Point," said Carlos. "Lighthouses are always interesting."</p>

<p>It was a windy day and they walked along the cliff for almost an hour to get there. There was an old stone lighthouse on the cliff above the sea near two big oak trees.</p>

<p>"This was probably an important lighthouse in Captain Salamanca's days," said Mike. "Those two big oak trees look very old too."</p>

<p>"That's why people called it Twin Oaks Point," said Kate laughing.</p>

<p>Carlos suddenly turned around and looked at the two oak trees. "Wait a minute," he said. "The Spanish word for oak is roble. And the words 'de los Robles' are written next to Juan Martínez's name. That means 'of the oaks.' Perhaps Robles isn't part of his last name but a clue."</p>

<p>"Oh, you're right, Carlos," said Mike. "Robles could be a clue. Perhaps he is buried under the oak trees."</p>

<p>"There are only two oaks in this area," said Kate, looking at the two big trees.</p>

<p>The three friends ran to the oak trees and looked around. There was a lot of tall grass under them. Suddenly Mike said, "Look, under the grass there's a small tombstone. And half of it is underground. Let's try and pull it out."</p>

<p>They slowly pulled it out and looked at the name on the tombstone.</p>

<p>"Well, it's difficult to read but I think it says Juan Martínez, 1570 to 1605," said Mike.</p>

<p>"It's the name of the sea captain the pirates killed," said Carlos excitedly. "He was Isabella Estrada's husband."</p>

<p>"Could Captain Salamanca's treasure be here?"</p>

<p>"What do you mean?" asked Kate.</p>

<p>"This is where Juan Martínez is buried," cried Carlos. "It's the perfect hiding place."</p>

<p>"There's only one way to find out," said Mike. "We have to dig."</p>

<p>"Aren't you afraid?" asked Kate. "Isn't it bad luck?"</p>

<p>"I just want to dig under the tombstone," said Mike.</p>

<p>"But we don't have a spade," said Carlos. The three friends were silent for a moment.</p>

<p>"You know," said Kate. "Dad has two spades on the boat. He uses them for his work."</p>

<p>"That means we have to tell Mom and Dad," said Mike.</p>

<p>"I think so," said Kate. "I'm sure they'll help us. They always do. We can tell them at dinner."</p>

<p>Kate, Mike and Carlos told Mr. and Mrs. Sullivan the whole story. They stopped eating and were very surprised. But they were not angry. They decided to help the three friends.</p>

<p>"This is an exciting adventure," said Mrs. Sullivan. "And you guys are very good detectives. We'll help you."</p>

<p>"If you find the treasure, we'll have to call the California Archaeological Society," said Mr. Sullivan. "Because the treasure belongs to the state of California. Tomorrow morning, we'll go to the old Spanish lighthouse and start digging. Who knows what we'll find."</p>

<p>By ten o'clock the next morning, they were at the old Spanish lighthouse. Mr. Sullivan gave Mike and Carlos two spades and they started digging. They dug for a short time. And suddenly Carlos's spade hit something hard. He stopped digging and looked at Mike.</p>

<p>"I found something," cried Carlos.</p>

<p>"Let me help you," said Mr. Sullivan.</p>

<p>They pulled a small wooden treasure chest out of the ground. It was heavy.</p>

<p>"Wow," said Kate and Mrs. Sullivan looking at it.</p>

<p>"This is hard to believe," said Mr. Sullivan.</p>

<p>"Can we open it?" asked Mike.</p>

<p>"Yes, of course," said Mr. Sullivan. "Let's see what's inside."</p>

<p>The lock was broken and Carlos and Mike slowly opened the treasure chest. It was full of gold and silver coins, jewelry, pearls and a king's crown.</p>

<p>"Wow!" they all cried. They couldn't believe their eyes.</p>

<p>"Look at all of these gold and silver coins," cried Kate.</p>

<p>Everyone stared at the treasure in silence. But no one touched it.</p>

<p>"A pirate probably hid this treasure here in the 1600s," said Carlos.</p>

<p>"Another pirate knew about it and planned to come here and dig up the treasure," said Mike.</p>

<p>"Yeah," said Mike. "And someone killed him because he didn't want to tell his secret."</p>

<p>"But he wrote his secret on a piece of paper and put it in his hat," said Kate. "And we found it after 400 years."</p>

<p>"Why did Captain Salamanca have the treasure map?" asked Carlos.</p>

<p>"That's a mystery," said Mike smiling.</p>

<p>"What an exciting story," said Mrs. Sullivan.</p>

<p>"Captain Salamanca's treasure map brought you good luck," said Mrs. Sullivan.</p>

<p>"People don't find treasures every day in California," said Mr. Sullivan. "Now, I'm going to call the California Archaeological Society in San Francisco. They'll be very surprised and happy."</p>

<p>"What's going to happen to the treasure?" asked Mike.</p>

<p>"Archaeologists will study the treasure first," said Mr. Sullivan. "And then they'll probably take it to one of the Californian museums."</p>

<p>"Journalists may want to write about you in the newspaper."</p>

<p>"Our names in the newspaper?" said Kate happily. "That's exciting."</p>

<p>"Of course," said Mrs. Sullivan. "Your adventure will be in all the papers and maybe on TV."</p>

<p>"We're proud of you," said Mr. Sullivan smiling. "And of you too, Lucky."</p>

<p>Lucky barked happily and ran around the treasure chest. Everyone laughed.</p>

<p>And Mrs. Sullivan said, "Well, you didn't get bored on this vacation."</p>

<p>"Bored?" the three friends said. "Never."</p>
