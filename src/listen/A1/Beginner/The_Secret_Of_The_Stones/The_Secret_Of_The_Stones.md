<p>"What does Uncle Stephen do exactly?" Laura asks. Her mother replies, "He studies history." Max is confused. "But I study history too. At school. But it's not my job."</p>

<p>Now, Mum remembers. Yes, Mr. Carter is the caretaker. He looks after the cottage when no one is staying in it.</p>

<p>"Thank you, Mr. Carter. It's very kind of you, but we have no problems." Mr. Carter wants a problem to solve, so he is not very happy. He goes to the door. Then he stops suddenly, turns around and looks at them.</p>

<p>"Hello. My name is Mrs. Black."</p>

<p>Mrs. Black is a housekeeper. She helps with the cooking and cleaning. When a family comes for a holiday in the cottage, she helps them. She lives in a different cottage in the village with her son.</p>

<p>Mrs. Black is very nice and they start to relax. Later they eat a big dinner and then say goodbye to their new friend, the housekeeper. They are very tired and soon everyone is sleeping. Everyone but Uncle Stephen. He can't sleep because he is thinking about the book he is studying.</p>

<summary>Stonehenge Fact File</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Stonehenge</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Stonehenge Fact File</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Stonehenge is a very old monument in the south of England. We don't know exactly what it is. Is it a temple? Is it an astronomical observatory? Is it from the times of Merlin and King Arthur?</p>

<p>Where? It is near Salisbury in the south of England.</p>

<p>When? No one knows exactly, but there are three construction periods.</p>

<p>One: In 3000 BC, there is a circular ditch with a diameter of around 100 metres.</p>

<p>Two: In 2500 BC, there is the original monument. It is made of wood.</p>

<p>Three: Between 2500 and 1500 BC, people build the stone monument.</p>

<p>Who? There are lots of interesting theories. Druids. People from Atlantis. Merlin the Magician. Aliens. Probably primitive British people are the architects.</p>

<p>Why? No one knows, but here are some ideas. Perhaps people use Stonehenge to study the sky. They want to see the changes in the sun, the moon and the stars. With this information, they can understand the seasons and the weather. Perhaps Stonehenge is an enormous calendar, an observatory or a computer. Perhaps it is a magic temple to an ancient god or the tomb of an important king.</p>

<p>How? This is a mystery too. The stones are very big and they are very heavy. Some stones are bluestones from the Preseli Mountains in South Wales, 385 kilometres away. No one knows how the people moved them.</p>

<p>"Wow," says Max.</p>

<p>"Yes," they all reply.</p>

<p>"Now, let's tidy the house. Everyone must keep their eyes and ears open."</p>

<p>"Yes," they all reply.</p>

<p>"It's got a pin in the back. It's a brooch."</p>

<p>"Yes. Perhaps it belongs to the thief. Let's give it to Uncle Stephen."</p>

<p>They tell their uncle the story and he looks at the small object. He puts it in his desk and his face is serious. He doesn't say anything.</p>

<p>Max and Laura go into the living room. "There's something strange about that object, Laura," says Max. "I know that symbol."</p>

<p>"Yes," says Laura. "Me too. I recognize it, but where from?"</p>

<summary>Chapter Ten: A Doctor's Visit</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Ten</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">A Doctor's Visit</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Max and Laura play near a river for some time, but later go back to the cottage. Everything is quiet. They don't have a key, so they knock on the door. Nobody answers. They knock again. And Mrs. Black answers the door. She is wearing an apron, and she has a rolling pin in her hand. She says, "Hello, children. I'm making a delicious chocolate cake for lunch. Your uncle Steven doesn't feel very well. Your parents aren't here, and I want to help."</p>

<p>"What do you mean? Uncle Stephen's fine," says Max.</p>

<p>"He has a headache and a temperature," Mrs. Black replies. "He works too hard."</p>

<p>This is very strange. Uncle Stephen is never ill. Max and Laura don't believe the housekeeper.</p>

<p>"I want to talk to my uncle," says Laura.</p>

<p>"Me too," says Max.</p>

<p>Mrs. Black is a big woman. She stands in the door, and the twins can't pass. "It's not a good idea," she tells them. "Your uncle is upstairs. He's resting."</p>

<p>"I want to see my uncle now," repeats Laura.</p>

<p>"You can't see him. There is a doctor with him now," Mrs. Black shouts. Laura pushes the woman, and she falls to the floor. Max runs past her too. They go to the study, but their uncle is not there. They run upstairs.</p>

<p>"Uncle Stephen, are you all right?" shouts Laura, but there is no answer. Max opens the bedroom door. Uncle Stephen is lying on the bed. He cannot move his arms or legs because there is a rope around them. There is a big bump on his head, and his eyes are closed.</p>

<p>"Is he dead?" asks Laura in a frightened voice.</p>

<p>"No, he's sleeping," Max replies. "Look, he's breathing."</p>

<p>"Yes, he's sleeping," says a familiar voice. They look up and see a man in the room. "Martin Knight," they say together.</p>

<p>"Oh yes," he laughs. "Your uncle is sleeping. Now, we can take his book and discover the secret of the stones. Now, no one can stop us, especially two stupid children."</p>

<p>At that moment, Mrs. Black arrives. "I see you know my son," she says. She is holding the rolling pin. Suddenly, Max understands what she wants to do. "Quick, Laura!" He pushes his sister to the floor. The rolling pin misses Laura's head. Max jumps up. He tries to take the rolling pin from Mrs. Black. Laura gets up and starts to run away. Martin Knight tries to stop her, but she is very fast.</p>

<p>Suddenly, outside, they hear the sound of a siren. It is a police car.</p>

<p>"Quick, the police!" shouts Martin. He and Mrs. Black run. When they arrive at the door, there are two policemen waiting for them. They put handcuffs on the two criminals and push them into the police van.</p>

<p>Laura telephones for an ambulance for Uncle Stephen. Max speaks to the policeman. "Thank you," he says. "But I don't understand why..." He stops. Under a tree in the garden, he sees an old man. Mr. Carter!</p>

<p>Mr. Carter comes and talks to Max. "Remember, boy, you must tell me your problems," he says. "I know these people. I know this place. I know when there's a problem."</p>

<p>"Thank you," says Max.</p>

<p>"You're welcome," the old man smiles. "Now, how about a nice cup of tea?"</p>

<summary>Chapter Eleven: The Secret of the Stones</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Eleven</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">The Secret of the Stones</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">The next day, everyone is at the hospital. Uncle Stephen is in bed. The doctor says that there is only one problem — the big bump on Uncle Stephen's head. Uncle Stephen opens his eyes.</p>

<p>"Hello, Barbara. What day is it?"</p>

<p>"It's Thursday, darling."</p>

<p>"Oh, hello, Andrew. Hello, everyone. What are you doing here? Don't you have school today, children?"</p>

<p>"It's the summer holiday," Laura replies.</p>

<p>"We're in Stone Cross, remember?" says Max.</p>

<p>"Stone Cross? Holiday? No, I don't remember. We're in London, I think."</p>

<p>Uncle Stephen doesn't remember. He doesn't remember about the holiday. About Mrs. Black and Martin Knight. And most important of all, he doesn't remember about the secret of the stones.</p>

<p>"You remember the symbol? The brooch? The old book of mysteries?" they ask him.</p>

<p>He smiles and says, "No, sorry."</p>

<p>A policeman arrives. He tells them that the two criminals, Martin Smith and his mother Dorothy Smith, are part of a secret organization. The name of the organization is the Black Knights, he says. "They want to find the secret of Stonehenge and use the secret to become rich and powerful. Mr. Morrell is the only person who knows the secret."</p>

<p>"But I don't know the secret," says Uncle Stephen. "I'm sorry, everyone, I can't remember."</p>

<p>"Perhaps it's the best thing," says Auntie Barbara. "The stones can keep their 5,000-year-old secret. It's not right to take it from them now." Everyone agrees.</p>

<p>"Perhaps we can find another secret next holiday," say Max and Laura together.</p>

<p>"Perhaps next holiday I can stay in London," says Uncle Stephen. He rubs the bump on his head.</p>

<p>"Poor Uncle Stephen." Everybody laughs.</p>

<p>"No more adventures this summer," says Max.</p>

<p>"But perhaps at Christmas," says Laura. "Who knows?"</p>

  <details>

  </details>

  </details>
