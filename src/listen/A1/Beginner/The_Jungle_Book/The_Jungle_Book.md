<summary>Chapter One: Mowgli's Family</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter One</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Mowgli's Family</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">One evening in the hills of South India, Father Wolf wakes up. Mother Wolf is near him with four little cubs.</p>

<p>"It's time to look for food," says Father Wolf. He gets up and leaves the cave. There is a big moon in the sky.</p>

<p>"Hello," says a voice. It is the jackal, Tabaqui. He eats everything, even old clothes from the villages. The wolves do not like him and are afraid of him.</p>

<p>"What do you want, Tabaqui?" asks Father Wolf.</p>

<p>"The tiger, Shere Khan, is coming here to look for food," says Tabaqui.</p>

<p>"What?" says Father Wolf, surprised. "He can't do that. He must tell us first because that's the law of the jungle."</p>

<p>"Shere Khan has a bad leg and he can't run fast," says Mother Wolf. "He only kills bulls and the people of the village are angry with him. That's why he's coming here. He wants to start hunting in a new place."</p>

<p>"Listen," says Tabaqui. "You can hear him in the woods."</p>

<p>Father Wolf listens and says, "What a stupid animal. He's making a lot of noise and all the animals are going to run away and hide. How can I look for food now?"</p>

<p>"Shh," says Mother Wolf. "It's the noise of a man."</p>

<p>"A man?" cries Father Wolf. The law of the jungle says that animals must not eat man because then a lot of men with guns come to the jungle and all of the animals are in danger.</p>

<p>"Listen," says Mother Wolf. "Something's coming to the cave."</p>

<p>Father Wolf sees a baby standing under a tree. The baby looks at Father Wolf and laughs.</p>

<p>"Is that a man cub?" asks Mother Wolf. "Bring it here. I want to look at it."</p>

<p>The baby is small and has no clothes. Father Wolf gently carries it to Mother Wolf. It starts drinking her milk with the other cubs. Mother Wolf is happy and says, "Look. He's drinking my milk with the cubs. He's not afraid."</p>

<p>It is suddenly dark in the cave because Shere Khan puts his big head inside.</p>

<p>"We're pleased to see you, Shere Khan," says Father Wolf. But his eyes are angry.</p>

<p>"I know. There's a man cub here," says Shere Khan. "It's mine. You must give it to me."</p>

<p>"Shere Khan's very big so he can't come into the cave," thinks Father Wolf. "The man cub is ours," says Father Wolf. "The leader of the pack's going to decide what to do with the cub. We listen to him, and not to a tiger."</p>

<p>"The man cub is mine," cries Shere Khan angrily. He starts roaring.</p>

<p>"No!" cries Mother Wolf. "The man cub is mine and you can't kill him. He's going to live with us. He's my son. Now go away."</p>

<p>Shere Khan walks around the cave slowly and looks at the man cub. "No. I'm Shere Khan. And the cub is mine."</p>

<p>"You're wrong," says Mother Wolf. "The man cub is ours. He's going to live with us. He's my son. Now go away, Shere Khan."</p>

<p>Father Wolf is surprised, but he knows Mother Wolf is brave and strong. Shere Khan goes away. He knows he cannot fight Mother Wolf in the cave. "One day, I'm going to have that cub," he cries.</p>

<p>After Shere Khan leaves, Father Wolf says, "Do you really want to keep him?"</p>

<p>"Yes, I do," says Mother Wolf. "He isn't afraid of us, and he's happy here. I want to call him Mowgli the Frog."</p>

<p>"Mowgli," says Father Wolf. "Yes, I like the name Mowgli. We must show Mowgli to the pack. The law of the jungle says that the pack must see all of the wolf cubs when they can walk, and look at them carefully."</p>

<summary>Chapter Two: Council Rock</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Two</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Council Rock</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">When the four wolf cubs can walk, Father Wolf takes them and Mowgli to Council Rock. Mother Wolf goes too. Hundreds of wolves in the wolf pack meet at Council Rock once a month when the moon is full.</p>

<p>Akela is the leader of the pack and he sits on a big rock. He is a grey wolf and he is very brave. Each new wolf cub stands in front of him and he says, "Look carefully, wolves. Look carefully."</p>

<p>At last, Father Wolf pushes Mowgli in front of Akela and the other wolves. "This is Mowgli the frog," says Father Wolf. Mowgli just laughs.</p>

<p>Suddenly, the wolves hear Shere Khan's voice behind the trees. "The man cub is mine. Give him to me," he cries.</p>

<p>Akela does not move but says, "Look carefully. Who speaks for this man cub? Two voices must speak for him, but not his father and mother."</p>

<p>A big sleepy brown bear appears in front of Akela. "Baloo!" says Akela, surprised.</p>

<p>Baloo teaches the law of the jungle to the wolf cubs and he speaks at the meetings of the council. Everyone likes him because he eats only nuts and honey. He doesn't hunt.</p>

<p>"I can speak for the man cub," says Baloo with his low voice. "I can teach him to run with the pack."</p>

<p>"Very well," says Akela.</p>

<p>Another animal silently jumps down into the circle of wolves. It is Bagheera the panther. He is black, brave and strong. He is also very clever.</p>

<p>"Akela, can I speak?" asks Bagheera softly.</p>

<p>"Yes, you can," says Akela.</p>

<p>"The law of the jungle says it's possible to buy the life of a cub," says Bagheera. "It's bad to kill a man cub because he can't hurt you. Let him live and I can give you a fat bull to eat."</p>

<p>"Good, good," say the young wolves. They are always hungry and want to eat the fat bull. "Let him live."</p>

<p>"Very well," says Akela. "Look carefully, wolves. Look carefully."</p>

<p>The wolves walk near Mowgli and look at him carefully. Then they go away and only Akela, Baloo, Bagheera and Mowgli's wolf family are at the council.</p>

<p>They can hear Shere Khan roaring in the night.</p>

<p>"Men and their cubs are clever," says Akela. "One day this man cub can help us. Now take him away and teach him everything he must know."</p>

<p>Thanks to Baloo's good words and Bagheera's fat bull, Mowgli now belongs to the Seeonee wolf pack. He grows up in the wolf family and he learns the songs of the birds and the sounds of the animals in the jungle. Baloo and Bagheera are very good teachers. He learns to climb trees, swim in the river and hunt for his food. He is a happy boy.</p>

<summary>Chapter Three: Lessons with Baloo</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Three</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Lessons with Baloo</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Mowgli is now around ten years old. Baloo and Bagheera love him and they are good teachers. Baloo likes teaching Mowgli because he is a good student and learns quickly.</p>

<p>First he teaches him to hunt only when he is hungry and not for fun. Then he teaches him to speak to the different jungle people and he teaches him the important master words. Sometimes Mowgli gets tired of these lessons.</p>

<p>One day he is not listening to his lesson and Baloo gets angry. He hits Mowgli gently on the head and the little boy runs away and climbs a tree.</p>

<p>"Baloo, don't hit little Mowgli," says Bagheera. "He is young. How can his little head remember all those long words?"</p>

<p>"Those long words keep him safe from all the birds, snakes and other animals in the jungle," says Baloo. "I know he is young but the jungle is big and not always safe. There are dangers everywhere."</p>

<p>Then he turns around and calls Mowgli. "Mowgli, come and say the master words again."</p>

<p>Mowgli climbs down the tree and goes to sit with Baloo and Bagheera. "I am going to say the words to Bagheera, not to you, old Baloo," says Mowgli. "I know all the words."</p>

<p>"All right," says Baloo. "Say the words for the hunting people."</p>

<p>"We are of one blood, you and I," says Mowgli.</p>

<p>"Good. Now say the words for the birds," says Baloo. Mowgli says the same words but with the sound of a bird.</p>

<p>"And now for the snake people," says Baloo. Mowgli says the same words but with the long sound of a snake.</p>

<p>"Very good," says Baloo happily. "With these words you are safe in the jungle because no bird, snake or animal can hurt you."</p>

<p>"Now I want to go up in the trees with my people," says Mowgli happily.</p>

<p>"What?" says Baloo. "Do you know the monkey people?"</p>

<p>Suddenly Baloo and Bagheera are angry.</p>

<p>"Yes, I know the monkey people," says Mowgli. "They take me up in the trees and give me good things to eat. They're kind to me and call me brother. They play all day and don't do lessons. I like playing with them."</p>

<p>"Oh no," says Baloo angrily. "You know the law for all of the jungle people but the monkey people don't have a law. They don't have a leader. They're dirty and noisy. They talk all day and forget everything they say. The jungle people don't drink where the monkeys drink. We don't talk to them or think about them."</p>

<p>"Forget the monkey people, Mowgli," says Bagheera. "They live in the trees and they throw nuts on our heads."</p>

<p>"Oh," says Mowgli sadly. "I'm very sorry."</p>

<p>All this time the monkey people are sitting in the trees and they are listening to Baloo, Bagheera and Mowgli. Suddenly they throw a lot of nuts and sticks on their heads. Baloo and Bagheera take Mowgli away to another part of the jungle and they take a nap.</p>

<summary>Chapter Four: Kaa</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Four</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Kaa</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">When Baloo and Bagheera wake up after their nap, they can't find Mowgli.</p>

<p>"Where's Mowgli?" asks Bagheera.</p>

<p>Baloo looks up at a tree and sees Mowgli. The monkey people are holding his legs and arms. Baloo shouts angrily and Bagheera tries to climb up the tree. But he is too heavy for the small branches. The monkeys laugh loudly and carry Mowgli away.</p>

<p>Baloo and Bagheera are very angry and worried. They follow the monkeys and Mowgli but the monkeys move very quickly. It is exciting for Mowgli to fly in the sky. The monkeys jump quickly from tree to tree. But Mowgli is afraid of falling. "Where are the monkeys taking me?" he thinks. "I must tell Baloo and Bagheera. But how?"</p>

<p>Suddenly he sees Rann. He is a big bird and he flies high in the blue sky. Rann sees the monkeys with a man cub. He flies down and hears the master words of the birds. "We are of one blood, you and I. You speak our language," says Rann. "Who are you?"</p>

<p>"I'm Mowgli, the man cub. Please help me. Find Baloo and Bagheera and tell them about the birds. And tell them about the monkeys."</p>

<p>"All right," says Rann. "I can do that." And he flies away.</p>

<p>The monkeys move quickly and soon Baloo and Bagheera are far behind.</p>

<p>"We're too slow," says Baloo. "We can't follow the monkeys. Poor Mowgli. He is in danger."</p>

<p>"Poor Mowgli," says Bagheera. "Oh, this is terrible." His big green eyes are sad.</p>

<p>"I have an idea. Where's Kaa, the giant python? He climbs up trees quickly and easily. And he eats monkeys. Perhaps he can help us."</p>

<p>"A clever idea, Bagheera," says Baloo. "Let's look for him."</p>

<p>They find Kaa lying in the sun. He is a beautiful yellow and brown snake. And he is ten meters long. He is very strong and very dangerous.</p>

<p>"Hello," says Kaa when he sees them.</p>

<p>"Hello, Kaa," says Baloo. "We're looking for food."</p>

<p>"Oh, I'm very hungry," says Kaa. "Let me come with you."</p>

<p>"We're following the monkey people," says Baloo. "They have our man cub and we want to find him."</p>

<p>"Why are you following the monkey people?" asks Kaa.</p>

<p>"They took our man cub and we're angry," says Baloo. "We must find him now," says Bagheera.</p>

<p>Kaa looks interested and says, "Your man cub?"</p>

<p>"Yes," says Baloo. "Our man cub, Mowgli."</p>

<p>Bagheera is very clever and says, "The monkey people always call you bad names. They say you're an old yellow worm."</p>

<p>"What?" says Kaa angrily. "Those noisy dirty monkeys must not call me bad names. Where are they? And where is your man cub?"</p>

<p>Suddenly they hear a voice from the sky. "Look up, Baloo," shouts Rann.</p>

<p>"Hello, Rann," shouts Baloo.</p>

<p>"Mowgli's with the monkey people," cries Rann. "He's in the monkey city. The lost city. Mowgli's clever. He knows the master words. Good luck."</p>

<p>"The lost city?" says Bagheera, surprised. "It's a very old city. And no one lives there."</p>

<p>"Yes," says Baloo. "Only the monkey people go there. We must leave immediately because it's far away. You move quickly, but I'm slow. I can follow you."</p>

<p>"Yes. Let's go," says Kaa. "I'm very hungry. Those noisy monkeys can't call me an old yellow worm. I'm going to teach them something."</p>

<summary>Chapter Five: The Lost City</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Five</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">The Lost City</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Everything in the Lost City is very old. There is a big palace on a hill and a water fountain near the King's Garden. The houses don't have any roofs and trees are growing inside them.</p>

<p>The monkey people call this place their city and run around everywhere. Some monkeys play with the oranges and flowers in the King's Garden. And then they fight and shout. Others jump up and down and say silly things. The monkey people are happy because Mowgli is with them.</p>

<p>"This boy can teach us how to make things," says one monkey.</p>

<p>"Yes, men make things with their hands," says another monkey. But monkeys always forget the things they say.</p>

<p>Mowgli is tired and hungry and says, "Bring me some food."</p>

<p>About thirty noisy monkeys go to get some fruit. But they start fighting and forget to bring the fruit to Mowgli. Mowgli doesn't like the monkeys or the Lost City.</p>

<p>"Baloo's right," he thinks. "The monkey people have no law. They are different from us. I must try to run away. Baloo and Bagheera are probably angry with me."</p>

<p>Mowgli tries to run to the walls of the Lost City. But the monkeys pull him back. "You mustn't go away," they shout. "You're happy here with us. We're great. We're wonderful. We all say this and it's true."</p>

<p>Mowgli is tired and confused. "When do these monkeys sleep?" he thinks. He looks up at the sky and sees a big cloud near the moon. "Perhaps that cloud is going to hide the moon and I can run away when it's dark."</p>

<p>Bagheera and Kaa are also watching the same cloud. They are outside the walls of the Lost City.</p>

<p>"We must be careful," says Bagheera. "There are only two of us and there are hundreds of monkeys. They are near the walls and they're talking to Mowgli. When the cloud hides the moon, I'm going to attack them."</p>

<p>"Good idea!" says Kaa. "I'm going to go up the hill and then I'm going to come down fast."</p>

<p>Bagheera enters the city walls silently and attacks the monkeys. He hits them with his strong black paws. The monkeys are scared and scream. But one of them shouts, "Bagheera is alone. Kill him! Kill him!"</p>

<p>Suddenly hundreds of monkeys jump on Bagheera and hit, kick and bite him. Bagheera fights bravely but he is alone. Other monkeys take Mowgli to an old broken building. They throw him into a dark room with one small window.</p>

<p>"Stay here with the poison people until we kill your friend Bagheera," says one monkey. "Then we can play."</p>

<p>In the dark room Mowgli can hear the hissing sounds of the poison people. "This room is full of snakes," he thinks. "I must give the snakes' call."</p>

<p>"We are all one blood, you and I," he says, giving the snakes' call.</p>

<p>The snakes answer. "We're not going to bite you. But please don't move because your feet hurt us."</p>

<p>"All right," says Mowgli, listening to the noise of the fight outside. "But Bagheera's fighting for his life. Thanks to me. How can I help him?"</p>

<p>He remembers the big water fountain near the king's garden. He goes to the small window and shouts, "Bagheera, go to the water fountain and jump into the water."</p>

<p>Bagheera hears him and thinks, "Oh good, Mowgli is safe." He slowly goes to the water fountain. Finally, Baloo arrives and shouts, "Bagheera, I'm here."</p>

<p>The monkeys jump on Baloo and he hits them with his big strong arms. Mowgli hears a loud splash when Baloo jumps into the water fountain. The monkeys are afraid of water and they stand around the fountain. Baloo cannot get out to help Bagheera.</p>

<summary>Chapter Six: Kaa's Dance</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Six</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Kaa's Dance</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Suddenly, Kaa comes down the hill fast and he is ready to attack the monkeys. They all know that Kaa can kill any animal and they are afraid of him. When they see him, they shout, "It's Kaa! Run! Run!"</p>

<p>Then Kaa opens his mouth and speaks one hissing word. The monkeys are suddenly quiet and everything in the Lost City is silent.</p>

<p>Bagheera gets out of the fountain. "Let's take Mowgli and go," he says. "Thank you, Kaa."</p>

<p>"Yes. Thank you, Kaa," says Baloo. "You're great. Look at the monkeys. They're terrified."</p>

<p>"I'm happy to help," says Kaa. "Where's the man cub?"</p>

<p>"I'm here with the snakes," cries Mowgli. "I can't get out."</p>

<p>"Take him away," shout the snakes. "He jumps around and hurts us with his feet."</p>

<p>"The man cub has friends everywhere," says Kaa. He hits the wall with his heavy body three times and makes a hole in it. Mowgli jumps out and runs to Baloo and Bagheera.</p>

<p>"Thank you for helping me," says Mowgli, smiling.</p>

<p>"You must thank Kaa," say Baloo and Bagheera.</p>

<p>Mowgli looks at the great python. "He looks a bit like a monkey," says Kaa. "We are of one blood. You and I," says Mowgli. "I'm your friend and I can always help you."</p>

<p>"You're brave and you speak well," says Kaa. "Now you must go with your friends."</p>

<p>Kaa moves softly in front of the sitting monkeys and begins his dance. The hunger dance. His head moves slowly from right to left. His long body makes circles, squares and other shapes. He dances slowly and never stops.</p>

<p>The monkeys are terrified, but they cannot move. Baloo and Bagheera look at Kaa and cannot move. Mowgli cannot understand what is happening.</p>

<p>"Monkeys!" cries Kaa. "Can you move?"</p>

<p>"No, Kaa," they answer loudly. "Without your word, we cannot move."</p>

<p>"Come close to me, monkeys," says Kaa in his low voice. "Come very close."</p>

<p>The monkeys move close to Kaa and Baloo and Bagheera move close too. Mowgli is worried and he pulls Baloo and Bagheera away.</p>

<p>"Oh, thank you, Mowgli," whispers Bagheera.</p>

<p>"What's happening? Your eyes are closing. Are you feeling well?" asks Mowgli.</p>

<p>"A python's dance is magic," whispers Bagheera. "It's very dangerous to watch, even for us. Because it's easy to walk into his mouth."</p>

<p>"Oh!" says Mowgli, surprised.</p>

<p>"The monkeys are moving close to Kaa. Kaa is going to have a big dinner tonight with all of those monkeys," whispers Bagheera. "Let's go back to the jungle quickly."</p>

<p>Baloo, Bagheera and Mowgli leave the Lost City. They go back to the jungle.</p>

<p>"And now, Mowgli?" says Bagheera. "We must follow the law of the jungle. We must punish you for talking to the monkeys."</p>

<p>"You're right," says Mowgli. "I'm a bad man cub." He looks at Baloo sadly.</p>

<p>"We're sorry, but we cannot go against the law of the jungle," says Baloo.</p>

<p>"You must learn, Mowgli," says Bagheera. Bagheera hits him softly with his big paw six times. But Mowgli does not cry.</p>

<p>"You're a brave man cub," says Baloo.</p>

<p>"Now, Bagheera says, jump on my back, little brother, and always remember to follow the law of the jungle. It's a good law and it keeps you safe. Let's go home."</p>

<p>"Yes," says Mowgli. "I want to go home." He soon falls asleep on Bagheera's back.</p>

<summary>Chapter Seven: The Red Flower</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Seven</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">The Red Flower</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">All of the jungle people are Mowgli's friends, but not Shere Khan. Bagheera tells Mowgli, "Be careful of Shere Khan. He wants to kill you. Akela likes you, but he's old now. He's not strong. The young wolves don't listen to him. They listen to Shere Khan. One day, you must kill him."</p>

<p>"I'm not afraid," says Mowgli, laughing. "I have you and Baloo."</p>

<p>"Mowgli, you must listen to me," says Bagheera softly. "Remember, you're a man cub, not a wolf cub. You belong to the world of men. They're your brothers. One day, you must return to that world. You're clever, and you can do a lot of things we can't do. Because you're a man."</p>

<p>"Yes, Mowgli," says Baloo. "Shere Khan's making trouble for you. The young wolves want to kill you."</p>

<p>"But why?" asks Mowgli.</p>

<p>"Look at me," says Bagheera. Mowgli looks him in the eyes, but Bagheera turns his head away quickly. "That's why," he says. "You see, Mowgli. Even I can't look into your eyes. You're a man. You're clever. The young wolves know this, and they hate you."</p>

<p>Mowgli listens to Bagheera and Baloo quietly.</p>

<p>"You must go to the men's village and take some of their red flower," says Bagheera. "All of the animals are afraid of it. It's a strong friend, and it can help you."</p>

<p>The red flower is fire, but the animals do not call it by its name.</p>

<p>"Very well," says Mowgli. "I'm going to the men's village to get the red flower now."</p>

<p>In the village, he sees a young boy cover a fire pot with a cloth. Mowgli takes the pot from the boy and runs back to the jungle.</p>

<p>That evening, the jackal, Tabaqui, goes to see Mowgli and says, "There's a meeting at Council Rock tomorrow morning."</p>

<p>When Mowgli gets to Council Rock, Akela is not sitting on top of the big rock. He is sitting next to it. Shere Khan is there too, and all of the young wolves are sitting around him. Mowgli sits next to Bagheera with the fire pot between his legs.</p>

<p>Shere Khan starts speaking, and Mowgli jumps up. "Is Shere Khan your leader?" asks Mowgli. "Does he belong to the wolf pack?"</p>

<p>"Akela's old now. He's not important," says Shere Khan. "Listen to me. A man cub doesn't belong to a wolf pack. Give him to me."</p>

<p>"A man!" cry the young wolves. "A man doesn't belong to our wolf pack."</p>

<p>Akela is angry and says, "Mowgli is our brother. He respects the law of the jungle. Let him go away safely."</p>

<p>"But he's a man," cry Shere Khan and some of the wolves.</p>

<p>Mowgli is very angry. He stands up with the fire pot in his hands. He takes a piece of wood and puts it in the fire. It starts burning. The wolves and the tiger are afraid of the fire and move away from Mowgli.</p>

<p>"You're the leader now," says Bagheera. "Help your friend Akela."</p>

<p>"I want to go to my people," says Mowgli. But first he goes to Shere Khan and says, "You want to kill me, but now you're afraid of me." He hits Shere Khan on the head with his burning stick and burns it a bit. The tiger is terrified.</p>

<p>"Go away now," says Mowgli. "But remember, I'm going to kill you one day and you must not kill my friend Akela. He is free to live in the jungle."</p>

<p>Mowgli jumps at the young wolves with his burning stick. They are terrified and run away with Shere Khan. Only Akela, Bagheera and some old wolves stay at Council Rock.</p>

<p>Suddenly, something inside Mowgli hurts and he starts crying. "What's happening to me?" he asks Bagheera. "Am I dying?"</p>

<p>"You're a man and these are tears," says Bagheera. "They're only tears. Now you must go to your people. You can't stay in the jungle anymore, my little brother."</p>

<p>"Yes, I must go to the world of men," says Mowgli. "But first, I want to say goodbye to my mother and father."</p>

<p>He goes to the cave and cries on his mother's coat. "Please don't forget me," says Mowgli, crying.</p>

<p>"Forget you?" says Mother Wolf. "Never. We love you."</p>

<p>"Come and visit us often," says Father Wolf. "Your mother and I are getting old."</p>

<p>"When I come to visit, I'm going to bring Shere Khan's coat," says Mowgli.</p>

<p>The next morning, he goes down the hill to the world of men.</p>

<summary>Chapter Eight: Tiger, Tiger</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Eight</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Tiger, Tiger</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">"I have a lot of enemies now," Mowgli thinks. "I must go far away." He runs and runs until he comes to a village near a lot of rocks and ravines. He sees cows and buffaloes everywhere. Some boys are looking after them. When they see Mowgli, they run away.</p>

<p>"Men here are afraid of the people of the jungle," thinks Mowgli.</p>

<p>Mowgli sits near the village gate. When he sees a man, he opens his mouth to show that he wants some food. The man is surprised and runs to the village shouting. He comes back with about a hundred people. Everyone looks at Mowgli.</p>

<p>"Look. He has the bite marks of wolves on his arms and legs," says one man. "He's a wolf child. He comes from the jungle."</p>

<p>"These men don't understand," thinks Mowgli. "I don't have bite marks. I have play marks. Wolf cubs always bite lightly when they play."</p>

<p>"He's a good looking boy," says one woman. "But he's thin. He must eat, poor boy."</p>

<p>"He can come home with me," says Messua, another woman.</p>

<p>Messua takes Mowgli to her house and gives him some bread and milk. She is kind to him.</p>

<p>"This is my first time in a house and I don't like it," thinks Mowgli. "But I'm a man now and I must do what men do. I know the languages of the jungle and now I must learn the language of men."</p>

<p>That day, Mowgli learns a lot of words from Messua. But at night, he does not want to sleep inside a house. He climbs out of a small window and goes to sleep under a big tree near the village.</p>

<p>He meets Grey Brother, a wolf cub. "Hello, little brother," says Grey Brother. "I have news for you. Shere Khan's in the mountains now. But he wants to kill you when he comes back."</p>

<p>"Please come to the big tree and tell me when Shere Khan comes back," says Mowgli.</p>

<p>"Yes, little brother," says Grey Brother. "And don't forget your wolf family."</p>

<p>"I can't forget the family I love," says Mowgli, smiling.</p>

<p>For three months, Mowgli lives in the village. He learns to wear clothes, use money and work. Every day, he looks after cows and buffaloes.</p>

<p>One day, Mowgli sees Grey Brother at the big tree. "Shere Khan is back," says Grey Brother. "He wants to meet you outside the village gate tonight. But now he's hiding in the big ravine."</p>

<p>"Thank you, Grey Brother," says Mowgli. He thinks for a moment. Then he looks at Grey Brother and says, "The big ravine. I can take the buffaloes to the top and run after Shere Khan down the ravine. The cows can stay at the bottom of the ravine. In this way, he's between the buffaloes and the cows. And he can't run away."</p>

<p>"You're right," says Grey Brother excitedly.</p>

<p>"Can you help me?" asks Mowgli.</p>

<p>"Akela and I can help you," says Grey Brother. And Akela comes out from the trees.</p>

<p>"Akela!" cries Mowgli happily. "You're a true friend. Take the cows to the bottom of the ravine, Grey Brother," says Mowgli. "Akela and I are going to take the buffaloes to the top."</p>

<p>When Mowgli is ready, he shouts, "Shere Khan! It's me! Mowgli! Are you ready?"</p>

<p>Mowgli gets on Rama, one of the buffaloes. And they all start running down the ravine. Shere Khan is sleeping. And he wakes up slowly when he hears the noise. The buffaloes are running towards him. He starts running down the ravine, but he is heavy with food. At the bottom of the ravine, he meets the cows. He falls under the feet of the buffaloes, and they run over him.</p>

<p>Mowgli jumps off Rama's back, runs towards the tiger and shouts, "Shere Khan's dead! He's dead!" He takes his knife and starts cutting the coat from Shere Khan's body. It is hard work.</p>

<p>The village hunter, Buldeo, sees Shere Khan's coat and thinks, "I want that tiger coat." He goes to talk to Mowgli. "Go and look after your cows," he says. "The tiger coat is mine. I'm the village hunter. I can sell it for a lot of money."</p>

<p>"No!" cries Mowgli. "Don't touch this coat!"</p>

<p>"Listen, boy!" says Buldeo. Suddenly, Akela jumps on him, and he is on the ground with a big grey wolf on top of him.</p>

<p>"Now, go back to the village, Buldeo," says Mowgli. Buldeo is afraid and goes away.</p>

<p>That evening, Mowgli goes back to the village and sees the people at the gate. "Go away, wolf child! Jungle boy!" they shout. "We don't want you here!" They start throwing stones at him.</p>

<p>Mowgli doesn't understand. "Shere Khan kills their cows and takes their children," he thinks. "But the people of the village are angry with me. They're like the bad young wolves of the pack."</p>

<p>He looks at the stars in the night sky and says, "I don't want to sleep in houses anymore." He turns to Akela and says, "Let's take Shere Khan's coat and go away."</p>

<p>Mowgli and the two wolves go to Mother Wolf's cave. "The people of the village don't want me, Mother," says Mowgli. "This is my home. I want to stay here. And this is Shere Khan's coat."</p>

<p>Mother Wolf is very happy. Bagheera comes to the cave and says, "We're happy to see you, little brother."</p>

<p>Mowgli takes Shere Khan's coat and puts it on Council Rock. Akela sits on it and says, "Look carefully, wolves. Shere Khan is dead."</p>

<p>"Now I don't belong to the wolf pack or to the man pack," says Mowgli. "But I can hunt alone in the jungle."</p>

<p>"We can hunt with you," say Grey Brother and the other cubs.</p>

<p>Mowgli returns to the jungle and lives and hunts with his brothers.</p>
