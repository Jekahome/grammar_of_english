<summary>About the Author</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">About the Author</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Robert Louis Stevenson</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Robert Louis Stevenson was born in Edinburgh, Scotland, in November 1850. When he was a child, his father told him stories of pirates and adventures. Robert liked these stories very much. When he went to university, he first studied engineering, but he did not like it, so he decided to study law. But he never worked as a lawyer, because his dream was to become a writer. And his dream came true. He became a popular writer and wrote novels, short stories, and poems. He wrote a lot of stories. Many of them are adventures and fantasy stories. He is very popular as a writer of children's stories.</p>

<p>Because he wrote famous stories such as Treasure Island (1883) and Kidnapped (1886). Another very famous children's classic is the Black Arrow (1888). His great success was the Strange Case of Dr Jekyll and Mr Hyde (1886). It is the story of a doctor and a drug he discovers. This changes him from a good person into a monster.</p>

<p>Stevenson suffered from tuberculosis and was often ill. Scotland is a cold and wet country, and this did not help him. So he decided to leave Scotland and travel to Spain and France, because there was warm weather. His first books describe his time there. During a trip in California in 1876, he met an American woman, Fanny Osbourne. He fell in love with her, but she was already married. In 1879, Fanny Osbourne divorced her husband, and in 1880 she married Stevenson.</p>

<p>In 1888, they decided to go to the South Seas. They visited many islands in the Pacific, including Tahiti, Samoa, and Hawaii. They enjoyed this trip very much, and decided to move to the island of Samoa. Stevenson continued to write many stories. In 1891, inspired by his visit to Hawaii, he wrote The Bottle Imp. In 1893, he published this story in a collection of eight stories called Island Nights' Entertainments. All of these stories were about the islands in the Pacific.</p>

<p>Robert Louis Stevenson died in Samoa on the 3rd of December, 1894. He was 44 years old. The islanders decided to bury him on Mount Vaea.</p>

<summary>Chapter One</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter One</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">A Strange Meeting</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Keawe was a courageous but poor man from Hawaii. His real name is secret because he is still alive. He could read and write, and he was an excellent sailor. One day he decided to go and see the world. So he went on a ship to San Francisco. The city was beautiful with a lovely harbour, and there were many rich people.</p>

<p>One day Keawe went for a walk on a hill. There were some beautiful houses. He looked at them and thought: <i>These houses are nice. The people must be very happy. They are rich, so they don't worry about the future.</i> He arrived at a small but lovely house. A man looked out of a window. Keawe looked at him, and the man looked at Keawe too. The man was old. He was bald and had a long black beard. He seemed to be very unhappy. Keawe was envious of the man because he had a beautiful house. But the old man was also envious of Keawe.</p>

<p>Suddenly the old man said, "Come and see my house." He showed Keawe every room, and Keawe was very surprised. The house was perfect.</p>

<p>"You live in a beautiful house. Why are you so sad?" asked Keawe.</p>

<p>"You can have a house like this. Do you have any money?" asked the old man.</p>

<p>Keawe shook his head sadly and said, "I only have $50, so I can't buy a beautiful house like this."</p>

<p>"I am sorry you do not have much money. So take this. It is yours for $50."</p>

<p>"The house?" asked Keawe, surprised.</p>

<p>"No. The bottle. Because all I have comes from this small bottle."</p>

<summary>Chapter Two</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Two</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">The Bottle Imp</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">The old man showed Keawe a bottle with a long neck. It was white, but there were also many different colours. The colours of the rainbow. Keawe looked at it closely. He saw the colours change and he saw something inside move.</p>

<p>The old man said, "You can't break this bottle because it is magic. It comes from hell and inside there is an imp. This imp is very powerful. You can have everything you want. Love, money. Napoleon and Captain Cook bought it and they became powerful."</p>

<p>"So why do you want to sell it?" asked Keawe.</p>

<p>"The imp cannot make me live for a long time. I am old and I must sell it before I die because I don't want to burn in hell forever."</p>

<p>"Well, I don't want to burn in hell, so I won't buy it," said Keawe.</p>

<p>"You can buy the bottle and then sell it one day. You can be very happy," said the old man.</p>

<p>Keawe was confused. "There are two things that I can't understand. The bottle can make you happy, but you are very sad. And the bottle can make you powerful, but it is very cheap."</p>

<p>The old man said, "I am sad because I am old and ill and I must sell this bottle before I die. In the past, the bottle was very expensive, but every time someone sells it, the price must go down. You cannot sell the bottle for more money or for the same amount of money. It always returns to you. Also, you must pay with coins. People do not believe you because it is so cheap."</p>

<p>"Why don't you try it? Give me your $50 and make a wish. Wish for another $50."</p>

<p>So Keawe gave the old man the money and wished for $50. Immediately, there was another $50 in his pocket. Keawe was very surprised, but he was also afraid and didn't want to keep the bottle.</p>

<p>The old man said, "It is yours now. Goodbye."</p>

<summary>Chapter Three</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Three</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Keawe's Wish Comes True</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">At first, Keawe thought the bottle wasn't really magical, so he left it in the middle of the road. But when he turned the corner, the bottle was once again in his pocket. Then he bought a corkscrew and tried to open the bottle, but it was impossible. He suddenly became very afraid. He went into a shop and sold it for $60, but later he found the bottle in his chest.</p>

<p>Keawe told his good friend Lopaka the story, and asked him to keep it a secret. Lopaka told him, "I am afraid this bottle will give you problems. Decide what you want from it, and then sell it to me, because I have a project. I want a sailing ship so that I can travel and buy and sell many objects on the islands."</p>

<p>Keawe thought for a moment and then said, "My dream is to have a beautiful house in Hawaii. I want to be happy there with my family and friends."</p>

<p>So Keawe and Lopaka decided to go to Hawaii. When they arrived, an old friend met them and said to Keawe, "I am very sorry, but your uncle and cousin died while you were away." Keawe was very sad.</p>

<p>Lopaka told his friend, "Your uncle's land is yours now, and you can build the house of your dreams." But Keawe was very unhappy. Did the imp kill his relatives?</p>

<p>Keawe and Lopaka went to see a lawyer. Keawe discovered that his uncle was very rich and he now had a lot of money. The lawyer told them about a brilliant new architect, so they went to see him. When they arrived, the architect already had a drawing of a house on his table. Keawe was surprised. It was perfect. It was the house of his dreams. The architect wanted exactly the same amount of money that Keawe received from his uncle.</p>

<p>Keawe said it was his destiny to have this house and signed a contract. But he also thought about the old man's words. <i>It comes from hell.</i></p>

<p>Keawe decided not to make any more wishes, and he and Lopaka left for Australia. They wanted to return when the house was ready.</p>

<summary>Chapter Four</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Four</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Keawe Sells the Bottle</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">When they returned to Hawaii, the house was ready, and it was exactly what Keawe wanted. The rooms, the furniture, the ornaments, the garden — everything was beautiful. Lopaka said, "It is possible that the imp has nothing to do with this. I promise to buy the bottle from you, but before I do, I want to be sure that it really has power."</p>

<p>Keawe replied, "I can't ask the imp for another wish. I promised."</p>

<p>"I only want to see the imp," said Lopaka. "Then I will buy the bottle from you."</p>

<p>Keawe was afraid, but he was also curious. So he ordered the imp to come out of the bottle. "Come out now!" The imp looked out, but disappeared inside again immediately. Keawe and Lopaka were very shocked and terrified. They could not move or speak for hours. Finally Lopaka said, "I want to keep my promise. So here is the money. I will buy my ship, and then I will sell this bottle as soon as possible."</p>

<p>Keawe said, "It is night, and the roads are bad. But I can't sleep, eat or pray with this bottle in my house. So please take what you want from my house and leave now." Lopaka was afraid too, but he understood his friend. He wished Keawe good luck and left. As Keawe watched his friend leave, he prayed for him. But he was also very happy that he didn't have the bottle.</p>

<p>Keawe loved his house, and many people came to visit and admire it. One day he saw a beautiful girl swimming in the sea. He immediately fell in love with her. Her name was Kokua. He did not tell her his name, because he did not want her to know he was rich. She took him to her father's house. Kokua's father knew Keawe, and the girl understood that he was very rich and that he wanted to marry her.</p>

<summary>Chapter Five</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Five</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Keawe Must Find the Bottle Again</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Keawe was very happy and he sang when he went home. He sang when he ate his dinner on the balcony and his Chinese servant heard him sing when he prepared his bath. Suddenly, the singing stopped. When Keawe took off his clothes, he saw a mark on his body. He understood immediately. He had leprosy. In a moment, all his dreams disappeared and he was desperate because he knew it was impossible to marry Kokua.</p>

<p>Then he suddenly thought of the bottle. The bottle could cure his illness. It was his only possibility. He wanted to marry Kokua very much. He was afraid, but he decided to look for Lopaka. Somebody told him Lopaka had a big ship, but he was far away. He thought of Lopaka's friends and remembered the lawyer. He discovered that he was very rich now and had a lovely house on a beach. When he saw Keawe, the lawyer seemed worried, but he gave Keawe the name of another person. Keawe met a lot of rich people, but nobody had the bottle.</p>

<p>Finally, one evening, he found a young man. This man was rich like the others, but he looked terrible. His face was white and he had dark rings around his eyes. Keawe knew that this was the person. He had the bottle. Keawe said, "I am here to buy the bottle."</p>

<p>"The bottle? To buy the bottle?" he whispered.</p>

<p>"Yes. How much does it cost now?" asked Keawe.</p>

<p>The man was shocked and the glass in his hand fell to the floor. "The price? Don't you know the price? I paid two cents for it," he said.</p>

<p>Keawe answered, "So, you can only sell it for one cent and the person who buys it..." He could not finish the sentence. He understood. He could never sell the bottle again. The young man fell on his knees and begged him. "Please buy the bottle. I will give you everything I have."</p>

<summary>Chapter Six</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Six</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">A Great Idea</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Keawe gave the man one cent. He made his wish and then he took the bottle. He looked in a mirror. It was a miracle. The mark was not there. The problem now was that he did not care about his health or for Kokua. He thought only about the bottle imp and his destiny. He was very depressed.</p>

<p>Keawe remembered that there was a concert in town that evening. He decided to go because he did not want to be alone. Courage returned to him when they played Keawe's favourite song and he decided to return to Hawaii. He married Kokua and carried her to his beautiful house. When they were together, he was very happy and calm. But when he was alone, he was always very worried and depressed.</p>

<p>Kokua loved him very much and brought him much happiness. But Keawe saw that Kokua was unhappy and she did not understand. She thought that she was responsible and decided to ask him. She told him, "Before we married, you were always happy. But now you do not smile anymore."</p>

<p>Keawe told her his story so she could understand how much he loved her. Kokua cried and said, "I will save you."</p>

<p>"You cannot help me," he answered sadly.</p>

<p>But Kokua said, "I went to school in Honolulu. I'm not stupid. There are other countries in the world where people use different money, like in England and France. For one cent, we can have four French centimes. Let's go to the French Islands, to Tahiti. We can sell the bottle for some centimes."</p>

<summary>Chapter Seven</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Seven</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Kokua Can't Sell the Bottle</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Keawe was very happy and they prepared for the voyage. They took all their best clothes and jewels because they wanted to seem rich. This was important to sell the bottle imp. When they arrived, they rented a nice house, a carriage and some horses. Kokua was very courageous and used the bottle imp for money. They learned the local language and when they could speak it, they tried to sell the bottle. It was very difficult. People didn't think they were honest because the bottle was so cheap. People avoided them and Keawe and Kokua were very depressed. They could not sleep at night because they were afraid.</p>

<p>One night Keawe woke up but her husband was not in bed. She found him under the banana trees, crying silently. She understood that she must save him. She left the house and went for a walk. She saw a poor old man under a tree. A stranger on the island. And she told him their story. She asked him to go to Keawe and buy the bottle. Then she wanted to buy the bottle from the old man and save her husband.</p>

<p>So the old man went to Keawe, bought the bottle and came back. "Your husband cried with happiness. I am sure he will sleep tonight," he said. At first Kokua hesitated. But then courage came back to her and she bought the bottle back from him and went home. Her husband slept like a child but she couldn't sleep or be happy.</p>

<p>When Keawe woke up, he was very pleased. He did not notice that Kokua was sad and could not eat. He told her the story, thanked her, planned their return home and laughed at the stupid old man. Kokua was angry because Keawe was not happy and he left her alone. When he came back, he told her she was not a good wife because she was not happy for him.</p>

<p>Kokua left the house and went out with some friends and they drank a lot. One of the drinkers, a sailor and an ex-prisoner, was a bad and dishonest person. He wanted an expensive drink and so he said, "Hey, Keawe, you are very rich. Bring some money so we can buy some rum."</p>

<p>Keawe replied, "I'll go home because my wife keeps our money."</p>

<p>The sailor replied, "Don't leave your money with her. You can never trust women. They are all false." Keawe started to think this was true because Kokua was so unhappy.</p>

<summary>Chapter Eight</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Eight</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">A Surprising End</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">When Keawe went home and opened the door, he saw his wife with the bottle near her. He was very shocked and did not believe it. He immediately thought that the bottle was there again because there was an error when he sold it to the old man. He closed the door and waited for a moment. Then he opened it again, loudly, but this time the bottle was not there. He said to Kokua, "I only want some money because I want to drink some expensive rum with my friends." He went to his chest and pretended to look for the money, but the bottle wasn't there.</p>

<p>Finally, he understood. He stopped and looked at his wife. He knew that expression on her face. He was desperate, so he took some money and left the house. He did not want to drink now. He went to the bad sailor and said, "My wife has the bottle. We can have money for a drink, but first you must help me. I must become the owner of that bottle again. Here are two centimes. Go and buy the bottle from her. Then I will buy it from you, but don't tell her I am sending you."</p>

<p>Then Keawe said, "When you come out of the house with the bottle, you can make a wish for some money in your pocket or a very good bottle of rum. You will then understand that this bottle is magic."</p>

<p>So the man left and Keawe waited for him. His heart was bitter. He waited for a very long time and then finally he heard the man's voice in the dark. He was very drunk. He had the devil's bottle in his pocket and another bottle in his hand.</p>

<p>"Give me the bottle now," said Keawe.</p>

<p>"Hands off!" cried the sailor. "This is a good bottle. I can't understand how I got it for only two centimes. I certainly will not give it to you for one centime. But I can give you some rum."</p>

<p>"Do you mean you will not sell it? But you will go to hell," said Keawe.</p>

<p>"I am already going to hell and I like this bottle. Go and look for another one," the bad sailor answered.</p>

<p>"Please sell this bottle to me," insisted Keawe.</p>

<p>"I don't believe what you say about this bottle. You thought I was stupid, but now you can see I am not. If you don't want any of this rum, I'll drink it. Good night." The sailor left and the bottle disappeared from this story with him.</p>

<p>Keawe ran to Kokua very fast. Their joy was great that night and from that moment they lived happily and peacefully in their lovely house.</p>

* Где и при каких обстоятельствах Ки-Аве (Ki-Awe) впервые встречает старика с бутылкой?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Он встречает старика в Сан-Франциско (San Francisco), когда гуляет по холму и смотрит на красивые дома.

* Что находится внутри бутылки и какие у неё магические свойства?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Внутри бутылки находится имп (imp) — маленькое существо с магической силой. Оно может исполнять любые желания владельца.
  </details>

* Какое главное правило продажи бутылки?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Бутылку можно продать только дешевле, чем за неё заплатили (cheaper than you paid for it), и только за монеты (coins). Если владелец умрёт, не продав бутылку, он попадёт в ад (burn in hell forever).
  </details>

* Какую цену Ки-Аве заплатил за бутылку и какое первое желание он загадал?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Он заплатил 50 долларов ($50) и загадал ещё 50 долларов.
  </details>

  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Бутылка всегда возвращается к нему (the bottle was once again in his pocket / in his chest).
