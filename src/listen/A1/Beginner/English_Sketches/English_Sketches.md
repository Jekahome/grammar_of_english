<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter One</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">The Ticket Inspector</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">"Tickets?"</p>

<p>"No thanks."</p>

<p>"Pardon?"</p>

<p>"I don't want a ticket, thank you."</p>

<p>"You're not selling tickets, sir."</p>

<p>"No?"</p>

<p>"I want to see your ticket."</p>

<p>"Oh, I haven't got a ticket."</p>

<p>"You haven't got a ticket?"</p>

<p>"No, I never buy a ticket."</p>

<p>"Why not?"</p>

<p>"Well, they are very expensive, you know."</p>

<p>"Sir, you're travelling on a train. When people travel on a train, they always buy a ticket. And this is a first class compartment."</p>

<p>"Yes. It is very nice, isn't it?"</p>

<p>"No, sir. I mean, this is a first class compartment. When people travel in a first class compartment, they always buy a first class ticket."</p>

<p>"No, they don't."</p>

<p>"What?"</p>

<p>"A lot of people don't buy tickets. The Queen doesn't buy a ticket, does she, hey?"</p>

<p>"No, sir. But she's a famous person."</p>

<p>"What about you? Where's yours?"</p>

<p>"Mine?"</p>

<p>"Yes, yours. Your ticket. Have you got a ticket?"</p>

<p>"Me, sir?"</p>

<p>"Yes, you."</p>

<p>"No, I haven't got a ticket."</p>

<p>"Oh. Are you a famous person?"</p>

<p>"Famous? Well, not very."</p>

<p>"Sir, I am a ticket inspector. I inspect tickets. Are you going to show me your ticket?"</p>

<p>"No. I haven't got a ticket."</p>

<p>"I see. What are you going to do?"</p>

<p>"I'm going to write your name in my book."</p>

<p>"Oh. What is your name, sir?"</p>

<p>"Mickey Mouse."</p>

<p>"Your name, sir."</p>

<p>"Karl Marx. William Shakespeare. Charles Dickens."</p>

<p>"I see, sir. Well, if you're not going to tell me your name, please leave the train."</p>

<p>"Pardon?"</p>

<p>"Leave the train."</p>

<p>"I can't."</p>

<p>"You can't what?"</p>

<p>"I can't leave the train."</p>

<p>"Why not?"</p>

<p>"It's moving."</p>

<p>"Not now, sir. At the next station."</p>

<p>"No."</p>

<p>"It's in the book, sir. When you travel by train, you buy a ticket. And if you don't buy a ticket, you leave the train."</p>

<p>"Leave the train?"</p>

<p>"Here we are, sir. We're coming to a station. Please leave the train now."</p>

<p>"Yes, sir. I'm sorry, but..."</p>

<p>"Oh, that's okay. It's in the book."</p>

<p>"What did you say?"</p>

<p>"I said, that's okay."</p>

<p>"Okay?"</p>

<p>"Yes. This is my station. Goodbye."</p>

<p>"Have you got a reservation?"</p>

<p>"Er, no."</p>

<p>"Ah. That's a problem."</p>

<p>"Is it? Oh, yes. Er, a table for two. Yes. Here's a lovely table for two."</p>

<p>"Thank you."</p>

<p>"Is everything all right?"</p>

<p>"Yes. Thank you."</p>

<p>"Good. That's, er, £12.50, please."</p>

<p>"What?"</p>

<p>"£12.50."</p>

<p>"What for?"</p>

<p>"For the chairs."</p>

<p>"The chairs?"</p>

<p>"Yes. £6.25 each."</p>

<p>"There must be some mistake."</p>

<p>"Oh, er, sorry. £6.30. That's £12.60, altogether. And, of course, £37 for the table."</p>

<p>"£37 for the table?"</p>

<p>"That's, er, £49.60, altogether."</p>

<p>"Look here."</p>

<p>"Service not included. Would you like to pay separately or together?"</p>

<p>"Look, we don't want the table or the chairs."</p>

<p>"Oh, you want to sit on the floor?"</p>

<p>"No. We don't want to take them away."</p>

<p>"That's good. We don't have a takeaway service."</p>

<p>"Oh, we want to sit here and eat something."</p>

<p>"Eat something?"</p>

<p>"Yes."</p>

<p>"Ah. Can we see the menu, please?"</p>

<p>"Er, yes, er, there you are."</p>

<p>"It's a very small menu."</p>

<p>"It's a very small restaurant. Now, what would you like?"</p>

<p>"Let's see. Er, egg and chips."</p>

<p>"Double egg and chips. Double egg and double chips."</p>

<p>"Um, isn't this a French restaurant?"</p>

<p>"Oh, yes. Sorry. Give me the menu. Thank you. Have you got a pencil?"</p>

<p>"Here you are."</p>

<p>"Thank you. Mmm hmm, parlais yan guar. There. A French menu. Earth a pom-fort."</p>

<p>"What if you don't like eggs? Have the chips. What if you don't like chips? Have the eggs. If you don't like eggs or chips, have a sandwich."</p>

<p>"A sandwich?"</p>

<p>"Yes. I've got one here in my pocket."</p>

<p>"Thank you. What's in this sandwich?"</p>

<p>"Sand? Yes. That's why it's called a sandwich — because of the sand which is inside it."</p>

<p>"Come on, let's go."</p>

<p>"What's the matter? You're not going already, are you?"</p>

<p>"Yes."</p>

<p>"Why?"</p>

<p>"Because this must be the worst restaurant in London."</p>

<p>"No, it isn't."</p>

<p>"Isn't it?"</p>

<p>"No. I've got another one around the corner. It's much worse than this one. Anyway, people don't come here for the food."</p>

<p>"I'm not surprised."</p>

<p>"No. They come here for the music."</p>

<p>"The music?"</p>

<p>"Yes. Allow me to present Manfred Schmidt and his Spanish guitar."</p>

<p>"Hey!"</p>

<p>"Stavros?"</p>

<p>"Yes."</p>

<p>"What can Manfred play?"</p>

<p>"Anything you like."</p>

<p>"Really?"</p>

<p>"Yes. Anything at all."</p>

<p>"Good. Tell him to play football."</p>

<p>"Football?"</p>

<p>"We're leaving. Goodbye."</p>

<p>"Oh. Goodbye. Do come again. Don't forget to tell your friends."</p>

<p>"That's the trouble with English people, Manfred."</p>

<p>"What's that, Stavros?"</p>

<p>"They don't know a good restaurant when they see one."</p>
