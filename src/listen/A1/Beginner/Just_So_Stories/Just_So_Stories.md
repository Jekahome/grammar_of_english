<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter One</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">The Elephant's Child — Part One</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">The Elephant's Child is a young elephant and he lives in the forest in Africa. He's grey and he's got big ears and his nose is small. He's a curious elephant and he asks a lot of questions.</p>

<p>One hot summer day he asks his aunt Ostrich, "Why have you got a lot of feathers?" His aunt gets angry and hits him. He asks his tall uncle Giraffe, "Why have you got a spotty neck?" His uncle gets angry and hits him. He asks his big aunt Hippopotamus, "Why have you got red eyes?" His aunt gets angry and hits him. The Elephant's Child goes to his uncle Baboon. He asks him, "Why are bananas good to eat? You eat them every day." His uncle gets angry and hits him too.</p>

<p>One beautiful day the Elephant's Child goes to the other elephants and asks a new question. "What does the crocodile have for dinner?" The other elephants get angry and say, "Be quiet!" Then they hit the Elephant's Child.</p>

<p>When he sees the beautiful Kolokolo bird he asks, "Can you help me?" "What do you want?" asks the Kolokolo bird. "What does the crocodile have for dinner?" "Go to the green Limpopo River and find out," says the Kolokolo bird.</p>

<p>The next morning the Elephant's Child sees some friends and talks to them. "Good morning, my friends," says the Elephant's Child. "I'm going to the Limpopo River. I want to find out what the crocodile has for dinner." "Good luck to you," say his friends. "Thank you," he says happily.</p>

<p>He takes six bananas and some other fruit and starts walking. One day he sees a python sitting in a tall tree. "Excuse me, Python," says the Elephant's Child. "Does a crocodile live in the green Limpopo River? What does he have for dinner?" asks the Elephant's Child. The python gets angry and hits the Elephant's Child with his long tail.</p>

<p>"Oh dear," the Elephant's Child thinks. "Everyone is angry with me. I must go to the Limpopo River." He turns around and slowly goes away.</p>

<summary>Chapter Two</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Two</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">The Elephant's Child — Part Two</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">The Elephant's Child arrives at the Limpopo River. He sees a new animal in the big green river. "What a strange animal," he thinks. "What is it?"</p>

<p>"Excuse me," he asks. "Does a crocodile live here?"</p>

<p>"Yes, little one," says the animal.</p>

<p>"Please tell me what you have for dinner."</p>

<p>"Yes, of course," says the crocodile happily. "Come close to me and I can tell you."</p>

<p>The Elephant's Child goes close to the crocodile.</p>

<p>"Today I'm going to have the Elephant's Child for dinner," he says, laughing. He bites the Elephant's Child's little nose.</p>

<p>"Oh, my poor nose!" cries the Elephant's Child. "What are you doing?"</p>

<p>The python sees everything and wants to help the Elephant's Child. He quickly goes to him and says, "Pull hard! The crocodile wants to eat you and he's very strong."</p>

<p>The python helps the Elephant's Child and they pull together. The crocodile pulls and the Elephant's Child and the python pull. The Elephant's Child's nose becomes very long. At last the crocodile lets go and he is very angry. The Elephant's Child is free.</p>

<p>"Thank you, Python," says the Elephant's Child happily. "You're a good friend."</p>

<p>"Your nose is very long now," says the python, looking at it.</p>

<p>"Oh dear, you're right," says the Elephant's Child and he looks at his long nose. "What can I do with this long nose?"</p>

<p>The python thinks for a moment and says, "You can do a lot of things. Please tell me," says the Elephant's Child. "You can kill flies and you can eat green leaves from tall trees. You can have a cold shower with water from the river and you can hit other animals. Your new nose is very useful."</p>

<p>"You're right. I can do a lot of things with it," says the Elephant's Child.</p>

<p>He goes home and shows the other elephants his new nose. "Look at my new nose," he says. "It's very long and useful." He tells them about all the things he can do with it. His friends are surprised and like it a lot.</p>

<p>"We want a new nose like yours," say his friends.</p>

<p>"That's easy," says the Elephant's Child. "Go to the crocodile at the Limpopo River. He can give you a long new nose like mine."</p>

<p>The elephants go to the green river and find the crocodile. Soon they all have long new noses and they are very happy. And this is how the elephant got his trunk.</p>

<summary>Chapter Three</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Three</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Old Man Kangaroo</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">The kangaroo is a grey animal with four short legs and a long tail. He lives in Australia. He likes dancing and he's clever.</p>

<p>One day at six o'clock in the morning the kangaroo goes to the little magician. "Good morning, little magician," says Kangaroo. "Please make me different from the other animals." "What?" cries the little magician angrily. "Go away, Kangaroo."</p>

<p>The kangaroo goes away and has breakfast. At eight o'clock he goes to the middle magician. "I want to be different from the other animals," says the kangaroo. "And I want to be popular. Make me different and popular." "What?" cries the middle magician angrily. "Go away."</p>

<p>Before lunch the kangaroo goes to the big magician. "Hello, big magician," says the kangaroo. "Make me popular and different. I want the other animals to run after me."</p>

<p>The big magician is having a bath but looks at him and has an idea. "All right, Kangaroo," says the big magician. "Come here, Dingo."</p>

<p>The dingo goes to the big magician. "What must I do?" asks the dingo. "Do you see that grey animal?" asks the big magician. "Yes, I do," says the dingo. "You must run after him," says the big magician.</p>

<p>The dingo starts running after the kangaroo. The two animals run everywhere — through the green trees of the forest and through the hot desert. They run up and down the hills and the mountains. They run through the tall green grass and the short brown grass.</p>

<p>At first the kangaroo likes running fast. He thinks it's great fun. But after a few hours he gets tired. "I can't stop running now," thinks the kangaroo. "The dingo wants to catch me." The dingo likes running fast but he is tired too. "I can't stop running now," thinks the dingo. "But I'm hungry."</p>

<p>The kangaroo and the dingo run all over Australia. When they get to the Wolgong River there is no bridge. The kangaroo stops running. "How can I cross the river?" thinks the kangaroo. "There is no bridge, so I must hop across." He hops across the river and gets to the other side. "That's easy," he thinks. "It's easy to hop and I like it." He pulls up his front legs and uses his tail to help him. Then he hops again and again. "I love hopping," he thinks.</p>

<p>So he hops through the deserts of Australia and the dingo follows him. "When is he going to stop?" thinks the dingo. "I'm very hungry."</p>

<p>At five o'clock in the afternoon the kangaroo and the dingo see the big magician. He is sitting in the river having another bath. They stop and sit by the river.</p>

<p>"I'm very tired," says the kangaroo. "And I'm very hungry," says the dingo. "Why don't you thank the dingo?" the big magician asks the kangaroo. "Thank him?" says the kangaroo. "Why?"</p>

<p>"You want to be different," says the big magician. "Now you're different. Look at your legs and your long tail." "But my long legs are funny," says the kangaroo, looking at his legs.</p>

<p>"Do you want the dingo to run after you until midnight?" asks the big magician. "No, no," says the kangaroo. "My legs and long tail are fine. I like them. But now I'm very hungry."</p>

<p>"I am too," says the dingo. "What can I have for dinner?" "Ask me tomorrow," says the big magician.</p>

<p>So the kangaroo and the dingo are tired and hungry in the middle of Australia. They look at each other. "All of this is your fault," says the dingo. "No, it's not," says Kangaroo. "It's your fault." "You're wrong," says the dingo. "It's your fault." And at midnight they are still arguing.</p>

<summary>Chapter Four</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Four</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">How the Leopard Got His Spots — Part One</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">All the animals of the hot African savanna are a dark yellow or light brown colour. They are the same colour as the grass and trees around them. It's difficult to see the dark yellow giraffe and the light brown zebra. And the leopard is very difficult to see because he is exactly the same colour as the savanna.</p>

<p>The leopard often hunts giraffes and zebras with a young man. They are both very good hunters. All the giraffes and zebras are afraid of them. "I'm afraid of the leopard because I can't see him and he always surprises me," says the zebra. "You're right," says the giraffe. "I'm afraid of him too. But I'm also very afraid of the man."</p>

<p>"We're always in danger here," says the zebra. "Why don't we go somewhere else?" "Yes, let's go away," says the giraffe. "Follow me."</p>

<p>So the giraffe and the zebra go to a big forest. "This is a lovely forest with beautiful colours," says the giraffe happily. "Yes, everything is green, brown or black and it's not hot here," says the zebra. "I like it."</p>

<p>There are a lot of tall green trees in the forest. The trees and plants make strange shadows. They fall on the animals and they don't go away. "Look at me," says the giraffe. "I've got some patches on my body." "Yes, you have," says the zebra. "They are beautiful." "And you've got some wonderful stripes on your body," says the giraffe.</p>

<p>The zebra looks at his body and sees the stripes. He likes them and he's happy. "Now we can hide from the leopard," says the giraffe. "He can smell us and he can hear us, but he can't see us."</p>

<p>The zebra and the giraffe are happy and safe in the big forest. The leopard and the man go hunting but they can't find the zebra and the giraffe. "Where are the zebra and the giraffe?" asks the man. "I can smell them and I can hear them, but I can't see them," says the leopard.</p>

<p>They go and ask a clever baboon called Bavian. "Where are the zebra and the giraffe?" asks the leopard. "We can't find them and we're hungry." "You must go through the big green forest," says Bavian. "You can find them there. And you can look for spots too."</p>

<p>"Spots?" asks the leopard. "Yes, spots," says Bavian. "I don't understand," says the leopard. "When you're in the green forest, look for spots," says Bavian.</p>

<p>The leopard and the man go to the forest. But they can't find the animals. "What a strange place," says the leopard. "Yes, it's dark but it's full of spots of light," says the man. "Are these the spots Bavian talks about?" asks the leopard. "Perhaps," says the man.</p>

<p>"I can smell a giraffe and I can hear a giraffe," says the man. "But I can't see it." "And I can smell a zebra and I can hear a zebra," says the leopard. "But I can't see it." They look around the forest. "Why can't we see them?" asks the man. "That's a good question," says the leopard.</p>

<summary>Chapter Five</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Five</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">How the Leopard Got His Spots — Part Two</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">"I know the animals are here somewhere," says the leopard. "But I can't see them." "Let's wait until the night," says the man. "All right," says the leopard. They sit down and wait.</p>

<p>When it is dark they hear and smell the zebra and the giraffe. The man catches the giraffe. "Ah, now you're mine," he says happily. And the leopard catches the zebra. "And now you're mine," he shouts, looking at the zebra.</p>

<p>But there's something strange about these animals. "You're right," says the man. "Let's wait until morning and see."</p>

<p>The next morning the leopard and the man look at the two animals carefully. "What's this?" says the leopard. "A zebra with stripes!" "And what's this?" says the man. "A giraffe with patches!" The leopard and the man are very surprised. The giraffe and the zebra laugh.</p>

<p>"Why are you different?" asks the leopard, looking at the zebra and the giraffe. "Please let us go and we can show you our trick," says the giraffe. "Yes, show us," say the leopard and the man. The giraffe and the zebra go under the tall green trees. Suddenly the leopard and the man can't see them anymore. "Where are you?" asks the man.</p>

<p>The giraffe and the zebra laugh. "I can hear you laugh but I can't see you," says the leopard. "Where's your breakfast now?" laughs the giraffe.</p>

<p>The leopard and the man don't know where they are. "We're here under the tall trees," says the zebra. "But it's almost impossible to see us because we look like the shadows of the forest." "You're right," says the leopard. "That's a very good trick," says the man.</p>

<p>"I think I need spots too," says the leopard. "That's what Bavian says and he's clever. It's a good idea," says the man. "It's more difficult to see you with spots."</p>

<p>The leopard goes into the forest and its shadows fall on him. Now he has dark spots on his body and they don't go away. "You've got some lovely dark spots all over your body," says the man. "I like them," says the leopard. "Now I can hide in the forest like the giraffe and the zebra." "Yes," says the man. "Now no one can find you in the forest." "I'm very happy," says the leopard. "And I'm hungry. Let's go hunting." "Yes, good idea," says the man.</p>

<summary>Chapter Six</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Six</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">How the Whale Got His Throat</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">The whale is a big animal and he lives in the sea. He is always hungry and eats many different fish and sea animals. He doesn't have a throat and this means he can eat very big things as well as very small things.</p>

<p>A very small fish called the little fish also lives in the sea. He is clever. The whale is eating all the fish in the sea. The little fish thinks, "I'm afraid. I don't want him to eat me. I must hide behind his ear."</p>

<p>But suddenly the whale stands up on his big tail and sees the little fish. "I'm hungry," says the whale in a loud voice. "What's this? A little fish!"</p>

<p>"Hello, great whale," says the little fish. "Do you like eating man?" "I don't know," says the whale, looking at the little fish. "I prefer dolphins or sharks. What is man like?" "Oh, man is very good," says the clever little fish. "Really?" says the whale. "Yes," says the little fish. "Believe me."</p>

<p>"Can you find a man for me?" asks the whale. "Of course I can," says the little fish. "You must swim to the north and in the middle of the sea there is a young sailor on a raft. He's wearing blue trousers and braces." "Good, let's go," says the whale.</p>

<p>The whale and the little fish swim across the sea. Soon the whale sees the sailor sitting on a raft. "Good, I'm very hungry," says the whale. He opens his big mouth and eats the young sailor and the raft. "Mmm," says the whale. "Man is very good."</p>

<p>The sailor and his raft are inside the whale's stomach. "What's happening to me?" thinks the sailor. He is afraid. "Where am I? It's warm and dark in here. Oh no, I'm inside a whale! What can I do?"</p>

<p>The sailor is very clever and looks around. He starts thinking. "This whale can't eat me!" he says angrily. He starts jumping, kicking and dancing inside the whale's stomach. The whale is very unhappy and feels ill. "I've got a terrible stomach ache," says the unhappy whale.</p>

<p>"I'm sorry, whale," says the little fish. "What's the sailor doing? Do you know, little fish?" asks the whale. "I don't know," says the little fish. The sailor continues jumping, kicking and dancing inside the whale's stomach. "Oh," says the whale. "I feel sick."</p>

<p>"I'm going to ask the sailor to come out," says the little fish. "Thank you, that's a good idea," says the whale. "Please come out!" cries the little fish. "What?" answers the sailor. "The whale's got a terrible stomach ache," says the little fish. "Please come out right now."</p>

<p>"No, I'm not coming out," says the sailor angrily. "You must take me home to England first." "All right," says the poor whale. "I can take you home."</p>

<p>The whale and the little fish start swimming fast. They swim across the sea to England. While the whale and the little fish swim, the sailor breaks up the raft and ties the pieces together with his braces.</p>

<p>When they get to the beach in England the sailor has a big surprise for the whale. "Whale, open your big mouth," says the sailor. "I want to get out." The whale opens his mouth and the sailor walks out. But he leaves his raft in the whale's mouth.</p>

<p>"Now my raft is your throat and you can never eat men again," says the sailor. "With this raft you can only eat very small fish." "What? Only very small fish?" says the whale angrily. "I don't want this in my throat."</p>

<p>The whale coughs and coughs but the raft doesn't move. He is very sad and slowly swims away. The sailor is on the beach and watches the whale swim away. "Goodbye, whale," says the sailor happily. "Now you can't eat men any more."</p>

<p>"Men aren't nice," says the whale. "And they give me a stomach ache. Small fish are much better."</p>

<summary>Chapter Seven</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Seven</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">How the Camel Got His Hump</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">The horse, the dog and the ox work for the farmer. But the camel does not. He has a long neck and long legs. He lives alone in the desert and doesn't want to work. He eats and sleeps and when someone speaks to him he says, "Humph!"</p>

<p>On Monday morning the horse goes to see the camel. "Camel," says the horse, "come and work like all the other animals. I carry men on my back and work hard all day." "Humph!" says the camel and the horse goes away.</p>

<p>On Tuesday morning the dog goes to see the camel. "Camel," says the dog, "come and work like all the other animals. I guard the farmer's house. I work hard day and night." "Humph!" says the camel and the dog goes away.</p>

<p>On Wednesday the big ox goes to see the camel. "Camel," says the big ox, "come and work for the farmer. I work hard in the country all day." "Humph!" says the camel and the ox goes away.</p>

<p>The animals go and tell the farmer about the lazy camel. "The camel is very lazy," says the horse. "He doesn't want to work," says the ox. "He just eats and sleeps and says 'Humph!'" says the dog.</p>

<p>The farmer thinks for a moment and then says, "You three must work more." "What?" the animals say. "But we don't want to work more." "You must work more because the camel doesn't want to work," says the farmer.</p>

<p>The horse, the ox and the dog are very angry and they start talking. The camel walks by and listens to them. "Humph!" says the camel and goes away laughing. The animals don't know what to do. "We need help," says the horse. "Yes," says the dog sadly. "But who can help us?"</p>

<p>Suddenly the desert magician arrives on a big cloud. He is a great magician and lives in the desert. He is everyone's friend. "Hello," he says. "What are you talking about? Why are you angry?" "Oh, desert magician," says the horse. "We have a big problem." "What's your problem?" asks the desert magician. "Tell me about it."</p>

<p>"The camel in the desert doesn't want to work," says the horse. "Why doesn't he want to work?" asks the desert magician. "He is lazy," says the ox. "Yes," says the horse. "Very lazy." "And we must do more work," says the dog. "We already work hard all day long," says the ox.</p>

<p>"What does the camel say?" asks the desert magician. "He always says 'Humph!'" says the horse. "I must go and talk to the camel," says the desert magician.</p>

<p>The desert magician's magic cloud carries him to the desert and he sees the camel. He is standing near a pool of water. "Why don't you want to work, Camel?" asks the desert magician. "Humph!" says the camel, looking at the pool of water. "The other animals must work more because you don't work," says the desert magician. "Humph!" says the camel. "Don't say 'Humph!'" says the desert magician. "You must start working."</p>

<p>The camel looks at himself in the pool of water. "I'm a great magician," thinks the desert magician. "What magic can I do? The camel must work." The desert magician sits down and thinks about something magic.</p>

<p>Suddenly the camel has a big hump on his back. He looks in the pool of water and sees it. He is very surprised. "What's that on my back?" he asks the desert magician. "It's your 'Humph!'" says the desert magician. "I don't like it and I don't want it," says the camel angrily. "How can I work with this hump on my back?"</p>

<p>"Now you can work more than the other animals," says the desert magician. "With your hump you don't need to eat or drink for three days." "What?" says the camel angrily. "I don't want to work." "Now be quiet and start working," says the desert magician.</p>

<p>The camel walks away slowly and joins the other animals. When the other animals see him they laugh and laugh. On that day the camel starts working. But he still isn't friendly.</p>

<summary>Проверить понимание текста</summary>

* Какой вопрос Слонёнок (Elephant's Child) задаёт снова и снова, и куда он отправляется, чтобы найти ответ?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Он спрашивает: «Что крокодил ест на обед?» (What does the crocodile have for dinner?). Он отправляется к реке Лимпопо (Limpopo River), чтобы узнать ответ.
  </details>

* Что происходит с носом Слонёнка, когда крокодил хватает его?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Крокодил тянет его за нос, а Питон (python) помогает Слонёнку тянуть в другую сторону. Нос становится очень длинным (very long) и превращается в хобот (trunk).
  </details>

* Что Слонёнок может делать с новым носом?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Он может убивать мух (kill flies), есть листья с высоких деревьев, принимать холодный душ из реки и бить других животных.
  </details>

* Почему Кенгуру (Kangaroo) просит волшебников сделать его популярным и непохожим на других?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Он хочет, чтобы другие животные бегали за ним (the other animals to run after me).
  </details>

* Кто преследует Кенгуру и как он начинает передвигаться?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Динго (Dingo) преследует его. Кенгуру начинает прыгать (hop), потому что ему нужно перепрыгнуть реку Вулгонг (Wolgong River).
  </details>

* Почему Леопард (Leopard) и Человек (Man) не могут найти Жирафа (Giraffe) и Зебру (Zebra) в лесу?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  У жирафа появляются пятна (patches), а у зебры — полоски (stripes). Они сливаются с тенями леса (shadows of the forest), и их трудно увидеть.
  </details>

* Как Леопард получает свои пятна?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Тени леса падают на него (its shadows fall on him), и на его теле остаются тёмные пятна, которые не исчезают.
  </details>

* Кто помогает Киту (Whale) найти человека для обеда и что делает моряк (sailor), оказавшись внутри кита?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Маленькая рыбка (little fish) помогает. Моряк прыгает, танцует и бьёт ногами (jumping, kicking and dancing) внутри желудка кита, и киту становится плохо.
  </details>

* Что моряк оставляет в горле кита и почему кит больше не может есть людей?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Моряк оставляет свой плот (raft), который становится горлом кита. Теперь кит может есть только очень маленькую рыбу.
  </details>

* Почему Верблюд (Camel) отказывается работать и что у него появляется на спине?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Он ленивый (lazy). Волшебник пустыни (desert magician) даёт ему горб (hump), с которым верблюд может работать три дня без еды и воды.
  </details>
