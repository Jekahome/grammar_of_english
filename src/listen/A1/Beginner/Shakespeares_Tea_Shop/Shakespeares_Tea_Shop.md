    <p class="first-para">"Pass the sugar, dear."</p>

<p>"Yes, dear."</p>

<p>"Can I have the sugar, dear?"</p>

<p>"Yes, dear."</p>

<p>"Well?"</p>

<p>"Sorry, dear."</p>

<p>"Oh, it's all right. I can do it myself."</p>

<p>"What are you doing today, Pete?"</p>

<p>"Sorry."</p>

<p>"I said, what are you doing today?"</p>

<p>"I'm not sure at the moment, dear. But don't worry. I will."</p>

<p>"But I do worry, Pete. You have no job. We have no money. What are we going to do?"</p>

<p>"Something will turn up. You see?"</p>

<p>"Nothing will turn up, Pete. You just sit at home all day."</p>

<p>"Well, what do you suggest?"</p>

<p>"We need to think, see what ideas we can find. Maybe I can get a job."</p>

<p>"You can't do anything, Mum."</p>

<p>"I can do lots of things."</p>

<p>"Say something to our daughter, Pete."</p>

<p>"Well, let's face it, Josie does have a good point. What can you do?"</p>

<p>"Thank you for your support. It's obvious everyone thinks I'm stupid."</p>

<p>"But you don't have any qualifications or experience, my dear."</p>

<p>"That's because I married you. I wanted to be..."</p>

<p>"I told you not to leave school, Nancy. Your father said he wasn't good enough for you. We wanted you to go out with Frank from the bank."</p>

<p>"Not now, Mum. Please."</p>

<p>"Your mum is getting worse. I told you we need to look for an old people's home."</p>

<p>"No, I don't want to go into it."</p>

<p>"Don't start arguing at the breakfast table. Be quiet. This isn't helping. We're trying to find some way of bringing some money into the house."</p>

<p>"Why not send your mother out, Nancy? The local supermarket is always looking for old people to work at night, stacking shelves."</p>

<p>"Don't be so stupid, Pete."</p>

<p>"Can you feed Boris, Josie?"</p>

<p>"But, Mum, why do I always have to do it?"</p>

<p>"But, Mum — OK. I can do it."</p>

<p>"Nobody does anything in this house. Can't you say something to Josephine, Pete?"</p>

<p>"Yes, dear."</p>

<p>"Fairy cakes."</p>

<p>"Sorry?"</p>

<p>"Why are you talking about fairy cakes?"</p>

<p>"You're good at cooking. Why not make fairy cakes — sell them at the church bazaar or a car boot sale?"</p>

<p>"No, Mum. I don't think that's a very good idea."</p>

<p>"Not again. How often do I tell you, Josephine? Take that rat up to your room now."</p>

<p>"But Dad, he's hungry."</p>

<p>"Who, as I tell you? I hate you all."</p>

<p>"Wait a minute. Maybe what you say, Mum, is a good idea. Um — I know. What about a tea shop? Yes! We could open a tea shop, Mum. You and I could make the cakes."</p>

<p>"Oh, I love making cakes. My friends at the Bingo Club love my Swiss roll."</p>

<p>"That's not a bad idea, Nancy. One thing you're good at is cooking."</p>

<p>"But wait a minute. Where do we get the money? It's not cheap opening up a shop."</p>

<p>"Maybe we can get a loan from the bank."</p>

<p>"Do you think they would give us some money?"</p>

<p>"Hmm. Banks always want to loan money. But first, we need to write a business plan. We need to look professional and show the bank we are serious and know what we are doing."</p>

<p>"You're good at that sort of thing, Pete."</p>

<p>"OK. I can start on it today. We need a concept. We need a detailed list of costs and our expected income."</p>

<p>"But where should we have a tea shop?"</p>

<p>"Let's consider the competition. I think it's better if it's in a village nearby. Perhaps a traditional English tea shop in an old-fashioned village."</p>

<p>"Yes, Nancy."</p>

<p>"Oh, this is so exciting. I always wanted to have a shop. Our own tea shop."</p>

<p>"Perhaps we can call it Nancy's Nibbles."</p>

<p>"I don't think that's a very good name. What about Britain's Best?"</p>

<p>"First, let's find a shop. Switch on the computer, Pete. See if we can find an empty shop in the area. Can you go and get Josie, please, Mum? She needs to be involved as well."</p>

<p>"Of course, Nancy."</p>

<p>"We mustn't get carried away. Are you sure we're doing the right thing, Nancy? I'm a bit worried. We don't have much money. What happens if it's a disaster?"</p>

<p>"It won't be a disaster. I feel it in my bones. But you know what they say in life."</p>

<p>"What's that?"</p>

<p>"No risk, no fun."</p>

<p>"I just hope we're doing the right thing."</p>

<p>"All right."</p>

<p>"Ooh, this is so exciting. I want to put on some make-up."</p>

<p>"We're not meeting royalty, Mum. Come on, Josie. And bring Boris with you."</p>

<p>"Then why don't you?"</p>

<p>"What's he saying about me? He knows I can't hear very well."</p>

<p>"Oh, truly scrumptious."</p>

<p>"Yes. They're OK."</p>

<p>"It's OK. The car is a bit full today. Perhaps eating two pieces of toast for breakfast was a bad idea."</p>

<p>"Yes, Josie."</p>

<p>"I hope you don't mind me mentioning it, but..."</p>

<p>"Well, Why a Piddle is very near Stratford."</p>

<p>"So?"</p>

<p>"Oh, Mum. Did you ever go to school?"</p>

<p>"Yes, I did."</p>

<p>"Dad can dress up in a pair of tights in the shop to look like William Shakespeare."</p>

<p>"It's so peaceful."</p>

<p>"There's more life in a funeral procession. Look, there it is."</p>

<p>"Isn't it cute?"</p>

<p>"Is that it?"</p>

<p>"Next to the village shop."</p>

<p>"God, it's awful."</p>

<p>"Oh, it's lovely. Lots of wooden beams."</p>

<p>"It needs a lot of work doing on it."</p>

<p>"We have to lower the price. The bank won't give us enough money for this place."</p>

<p>"Don't be so negative, Pete. We can ask in the village shop next door. Perhaps they know the owner. Come on."</p>

<p>"Good morning."</p>

<p>"I wonder if you can help us."</p>

<p>"I certainly hope I can. What an enchanting blouse. It looks new. Silk, isn't it?"</p>

<p>"Yes, it is. And thank you. My name is Nancy Britton, and this is my husband, Pete."</p>

<p>"And a lovely little doggy woggy. Doggy woggy. What a doggy woggy."</p>

<p>"And this is my daughter, Josephine, and my mother."</p>

<p>"It's so enchanting to meet you all. What can I do for you?"</p>

<p>"Well, my husband and I are interested in the shop next door. We're thinking of opening a tea shop."</p>

<p>"How enchanting — new neighbours. Come on. I have a key."</p>

<p>"Oh, how terrible of me. I didn't introduce myself. I'm Simon. Call me Si. Everyone does."</p>

<p>"Julian, dear. Look after the shop. Back in two shakes of a lamb's tail. Come on. Come on. We can look together."</p>

<p>"What a wonderful shop."</p>

<p>"It's perfect."</p>

<p>"God, who lived here — Henry VIII?"</p>

<p>"Does it have electricity and running water?"</p>

<p>"Of course it does."</p>

<p>"It's too expensive."</p>

<p>"I need the loo."</p>

<p>"Oh, wait for me. I do too."</p>

<p>"There's a lot of work to do. It looks rather run down."</p>

<p>"Hmm. It's very old-fashioned. Even older than your mother, if that's possible."</p>

<p>"You're so funny."</p>

<p>"I wasn't trying to be funny."</p>

<p>"Oh, I see."</p>

<p>"I can see we're going to have such fun together."</p>

<p>"Yes."</p>

<p>"Is there a kitchen?"</p>

<p>"Of course. Julian and I always kept it spotless. We're real little housewives together."</p>

<p>"I don't understand."</p>

<p>"Oh no, of course. Julian and I own this shop. We moved our village shop next door because it's bigger. So now we want to rent this shop."</p>

<p>"The toilet's in the garden. It's an outside lav."</p>

<p>"Oh, it's like the middle ages."</p>

<p>"It's so wonderful. Reminds you of your childhood, does it?"</p>

<p>"There you go again. Your husband's so funny. We're going to get on like a house on fire. I'm going back to my shop. Bring the key when you finish looking round. Bye."</p>

<p>"Well, what do you think?"</p>

<p>"I'm very happy I'm married to you."</p>

<p>"Oh, I mean the shop. What do you think?"</p>

<p>"It's a lot of work. I'm a bit worried."</p>

<p>"It's wonderful. We must all help and that includes you, Josie."</p>

<p>"Of course, Mum."</p>

<p>"Have you told Dad about the tights?"</p>

<p>"Sorry. What did you say?"</p>

<p>"Tights."</p>

<p>"Don't worry. You don't have to."</p>

<p>"Don't have to what?"</p>

<p>"Come on then. Let's take the key back to Julian and Si. I want to have a word with them. It's much too expensive. There's so much to renovate."</p>

<p>"What's this about? Tights, Josie? Don't tell Julian and Si. They'll get excited."</p>

<p>"What are you all talking about?"</p>

<p>"Oh, hello again. Well, what do you think?"</p>

<p>"We really like it. It's just what we're looking for."</p>

<p>"Ah, I think it's a bit expensive and then there's the deposit as well. And it needs a lot of renovation. We'll have to do it ourselves."</p>

<p>"I know, I know. Why don't we all meet next week at the estate agent's office? We can then discuss the money and the contract together. I'm sure we can agree. What do you think?"</p>

<p>"Yes, that's a good idea. Here's our name and phone number. Just ring us up to tell us when the meeting is."</p>

<p>"Wonderful. Thank you so much. Come on, everyone. Let's go and look around the village and meet the locals."</p>

<p>"Not so fast, dear. One step at a time. A lot of things can go wrong, you know."</p>

<p>"Don't talk such rubbish, Pete. Come on, everyone."</p>

<p>"Women, I know what you mean."</p>

<p>"Yes, dear. You've told me the same story at least ten times since breakfast."</p>

<p>"Yeah, I mean. You can't complain. I think we've had at least twenty new customers so far."</p>

<p>"Excuse me. You over there. Come here, please."</p>

<p>"Are you referring to me?"</p>

<p>"Yes, you are."</p>

<p>"I thought slavery had been abolished."</p>

<p>"Try to be more polite to the customers, Pete."</p>

<p>"Yes, dear."</p>

<p>"Yes, dear."</p>

<p>"How can I help you, madam?"</p>

<p>"This is not a proper scone. What are these?"</p>

<p>"I believe, in the catering world, they are usually called sultanas, madam. Maybe you haven't heard of them before."</p>

<p>"Don't be so stupid. I want you to take this away and bring me another scone without sultanas."</p>

<p>"Of course, madam. Whatever you say. Would you also like the strawberries removed from the strawberry jam?"</p>

<p>"No, thank you. The jam is fine."</p>

<p>"Well, I'm pleased to hear that."</p>

<p>"Boris."</p>

<p>"The people we get in here."</p>

<p>"What's the matter now?"</p>

<p>"Queen Victoria at Table Six wants a scone without sultanas."</p>

<p>"I'm just making some more. I can make some without sultanas."</p>

<p>"Thank you, Mum. You see, Pete? You shouldn't get so stressed."</p>

<p>"I'm not stressed. It's just the people we get in here."</p>

<p>"What are you doing? Don't put a knife in the mixer, Pete."</p>

<p>"I just want to try the..."</p>

<p>"Bloody hell. No!"</p>

<p>"Frank, why did you turn the mixer on? I didn't see you."</p>

<p>"Get out of the kitchen, Pete. God, I'm going."</p>

<p>"Good morning, sir. I'm a local health inspector and I want to inspect your premises, please."</p>

<p>"Yes, and I'm..."</p>

<p>"I'm still waiting for my scone without sultanas."</p>

<p>"Just be quiet and wait."</p>

<p>"Do you always speak to your customers like that?"</p>

<p>"No. Only the ones I would like deported to the continent. Now, who did you say you were?"</p>

<p>"The local health inspector. Here to check your kitchen."</p>

<p>"Yes, wonderful. Yes, that's all we need."</p>

<p>"Are you feeling all right?"</p>

<p>"Yes, yes. Lovely day, isn't it? Pity about the cricket score."</p>

<p>"Please, just wait there. Nice to meet you. I mean..."</p>

<p>"He's here."</p>

<p>"What are you talking about?"</p>

<p>"Careful where you walk. Not there. He's here. Now look what you've done. The floor is slippery. Be careful."</p>

<p>"He's here. The man who phoned. The man who phoned."</p>

<p>"Oh, no."</p>

<p>"What the hell, Inspector?"</p>

<p>"Yes."</p>

<p>"Don't let him come in here. Look at this mess. Keep him in the shop."</p>

<p>"How?"</p>

<p>"Use your imagination. Sing songs."</p>

<p>"Sing songs?"</p>

<p>"Sing songs to him."</p>

<p>"Are you completely mad?"</p>

<p>"What do you suggest?"</p>

<p>"Handel's Messiah. — Make songs. — Requiem might be more appropriate."</p>

<p>"Just do something, but get out of here."</p>

<p>"Hello."</p>

<p>"Where's he gone?"</p>

<p>"Where's he gone?"</p>

<p>"If you are referring to that gentleman, he's gone to inspect the sanitary arrangements."</p>

<p>"Just had a look at your toilets. I'm afraid it is forbidden to have outside toilets like those in a restaurant."</p>

<p>"But... but..."</p>

<p>"I want to inspect your kitchen now, please."</p>

<p>"Maybe you would like a cup of tea first."</p>

<p>"No, thank you. Just your kitchen, please."</p>

<p>"I know we can... look at the flowers in the garden. Do you like flowers?"</p>

<p>"Just the kitchen, please. It's through this door, isn't it?"</p>

<p>"Maybe you would like to hear me sing."</p>

<p>"I beg your pardon?"</p>

<p>"Stupid idea. It wasn't my idea."</p>

<p>"Good morning, madam."</p>

<p>"Whoa! Let me help you up. Sorry about that."</p>

<p>"Oh, perhaps you would like a scone. We're all out of sultanas... I mean..."</p>

<p>"No, thank you. What a mess in here. It's not acceptable. And what's that noise?"</p>

<p>"It's coming from the cupboard over there. Let me see."</p>

<p>"There's a rat in a box in here. I'll kill her! Where is Josephine? Where is he?"</p>

<p>"You must close this shop now. It's an absolute disgrace. I'll be writing a full report and you can expect a very heavy fine."</p>

<p>"Oh, no. It's the end."</p>

<p>"What's going on? Stop it, Pete. Don't be stupid."</p>

<p>"Come here, Josephine. What's happening here? Are you all going bonkers? And who are you?"</p>

<p>"I'm the local health inspector and I'm closing this shop now."</p>

<p>"You can't be. It was a joke."</p>

<p>"It's not a joke, Josephine. He phoned a few days ago."</p>

<p>"That was me. I pretended to be the health inspector. It was a joke."</p>

<p>"What's happening here? What are you talking about?"</p>

<p>"Ask Gran. She knows."</p>

<p>"Yes. I was there when Josie phoned you. She's very clever, isn't she? She should become an actress. Maybe a famous Hollywood actress in the films."</p>

<p>"But who are you? Have you asked him to show you his credentials?"</p>

<p>"Oh, I must go now. I have another tea shop to visit. Don't forget what I said."</p>

<p>"Oh, hello, Trevor. What a surprise. Haven't seen you for a long time. How are you?"</p>

<p>"Oh, hello, Si. Yes, I'm fine. Thanks. Goodbye."</p>

<p>"Strange man."</p>

<p>"What's all the noise? Julian and I were worried. What's happening in here?"</p>

<p>"It's a terrible mess. The health inspector was here."</p>

<p>"When? Today?"</p>

<p>"When you came in, you said hello. You must know him."</p>

<p>"Oh, yes. That's Trevor McMubble. He's an old school friend. I sometimes see him in Stratford. He owns a tea shop there."</p>

<p>"But I don't understand. Why did he say he was a health inspector?"</p>

<p>"Hmm, I think I'm beginning to understand. He's worried about the competition from our tea shop. Since I did my advertising in Stratford, we've had more customers. He wanted to frighten us and get us to close down."</p>

<p>"What a shock that was. What a horrible thing to do."</p>

<p>"Thank God, that's over and done with. Come on, everybody. Let's have a nice cup of tea."</p>

<p>"Gran?"</p>

<p>"I even like your scones without sultanas."</p>

<p>"Good afternoon. I'm from the Stratford Public Health Office. I've had reports that there are rats in this tea shop."</p>

<p>"Is this another friend of yours, Si?"</p>

<p>"Sorry, love. You're too late. Oh, your friend was here one minute ago."</p>

<p>"We're not worried."</p>

<p>"But this is serious. If the reports are correct, I'll have to close this shop. I want to have a look around."</p>

<p>"Of course you do, madam. But you can't get out of our shop now. We know you're gay."</p>

<p>"Go on out. We don't believe you. You don't frighten us."</p>

<p>"It's my bet."</p>

<p>"What are you doing? Here's my identity card."</p>

<p>"Yes, of course it is. You have an identity card. What a surprise. Your partner in crime, didn't you? Let me see it."</p>

<p>"Ah, Pete."</p>

<p>"Yes?"</p>

<p>"I think you should look at this identity card."</p>

<p>"It's genuine."</p>

<p>"What?"</p>

<p>"It can't be. Let me look."</p>

<p>"Oh no. The battle's lost."</p>
