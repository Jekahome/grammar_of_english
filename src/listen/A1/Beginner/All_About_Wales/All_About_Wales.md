  <div class="chapter-title">History</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">One day I was sitting in a cafe in Amsterdam drinking coffee and checking my map. A young man was sitting at a table near me. I noticed he had an unusual and interesting tattoo on his left arm. It was a beautiful big red dragon. The man saw me looking at his arm and smiled.</p>

<p>"Sut wyt ti?" he said.</p>

<p>"I'm sorry, I don't speak Dutch," I said to him. "I'm a Canadian student and I'm only here for a month."</p>

<p>The man laughed and said, "I was speaking Welsh, not Dutch. I just asked you if you are okay. I saw you looking at my tattoo of the Welsh dragon, so I thought you were a Welsh tourist like me."</p>

<p>"You were speaking Welsh!" I exclaimed. "Do people really speak Welsh today?"</p>

<p>"Of course they do, all over Wales," he laughed. "Don't you know anything about Wales? Our rugby, our castles, Welsh music, Welsh love spoons..."</p>

<p>"What are love spoons?" I thought. Perhaps I just didn't understand his accent. "Is Wales an independent country from England?" I asked. "There's no physical border or anything between them, is there?"</p>

<p>The man laughed. "Of course not," he said. "Look, I'm Owen and I'm also a student. Let's get another coffee and if you're interested, I'll tell you a little about Wales. What's your name?"</p>

<p>"I'm Ben," I replied, feeling very ignorant, "and yes, I'd really like to know more about Wales."</p>

<p>He ordered us both two more coffees and began his explanation.</p>

<p>"Well, Ben, the history of Wales is a history of battles against all kinds of invaders. In fact, Wales has got more castles than any other country in Europe. That's because each new invader built a castle to control the local population and prevent another invasion. The first settlers came to Wales from other parts of Britain and Europe with the Celtic invasions about 700 BC. Then the Romans invaded Wales, but they mainly used it for military purposes like forts, camps and military roads.</p>

<p>When William the Conqueror invaded England in 1066, he didn't intend to conquer Wales, but by 1094 William's elder son controlled most of Wales. However, the Welsh didn't welcome the Normans and the fighting continued for many years.</p>

<p>The situation got really bad for Wales in 1272 when King Henry III of England died and his son Edward I became the new king of England. He didn't like the Welsh much, and he was a strong military leader. Although Edward I found resistance from some great Welsh leaders, he conquered Wales very easily. He led three major campaigns against the Welsh, and after each victory he built stone castles to defend his territories, like Conwy Castle and Caernarfon Castle."</p>

<p>"Do these castles still exist today?" I asked.</p>

<p>"Oh yes," said Owen. "They're very popular tourist sites. Some couples have their weddings inside Caernarfon Castle because it's a very romantic place."</p>

<p><strong>English colonisation and Welsh rebellions.</strong></p>

<p>"Anyway, from 1277 to 1283, Edward I began an English colonisation in Wales and created many new towns. Welsh peasants were forced to leave their homes and land, and English peasants moved there instead. However, the Welsh rebellions didn't stop. One of the great Welsh heroes was Owain Glyndŵr, the last native Welshman to have the title Prince of Wales. He fought fiercely and bravely for Welsh independence against English oppression, but the English forces were stronger than his. Welsh discontent continued throughout the centuries, and the last major Welsh rebellions for independence occurred from 1400 to 1415. Finally, during the reign of Henry VIII of England, the Laws in Wales Acts were passed in 1535 and 1542. These acts finally made Wales an equal part of England with the same legal rights. The idea was to create one single state and to put an end to the terrible battles and bloodshed between the English and the Welsh."</p>

<p><strong>Welsh independence.</strong></p>

<p>"So do Welsh people today see themselves as English or Welsh?" I asked.</p>

<p>"That's a good question and difficult to answer," smiled Owen. "There are no more battles between the English and the Welsh, but the desire for independence is still an important issue for many Welsh people. In the 19th and 20th centuries, the movement for home rule appeared, and in 1925, Plaid Cymru, the Welsh nationalist party, was established. Now Wales has got a national assembly which decides on issues concerning Wales, and the Welsh language is still widely spoken in most parts of the country."</p>

<p>"How do you remember all these facts and figures, Owen?" I asked. "You're like a walking encyclopedia!"</p>

<p>"My girlfriend says that too," laughed Owen. "I just remember historical facts, but I always forget birthdays and anniversaries, so don't get too impressed!"</p>

  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter 2</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Geography</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Owen finished his coffee and looked at his watch. "I must go now, Ben, but we could meet later for a drink, say around seven?"</p>

<p>"Yes, that sounds great," I said. "See you later, Owen."</p>

<p>After he left, I decided to Google Wales and see how big it was. I looked at it on the map; it seemed very small to me. I started to read about the geography of the country.</p>

<p><strong>Overview.</strong> Wales has got England on its eastern side and sea on all the other sides. It's a small country, but it's got a very diverse and interesting landscape. The Welsh coastline is very long and includes many islands. There are large populated islands like Anglesey, while others are small and uninhabited. The beaches are very beautiful, and some have got high cliffs.</p>

<p>Wales is the only country in the world with a continuous coastal path. It's about 1,400 kilometres long and has got magnificent views that attract walkers and cyclists from all over the world.</p>

<p><strong>Snowdon.</strong> Wales is a very mountainous country, especially in the north and central regions, with hundreds of natural lakes and waterfalls, as well as three large national parks. The Welsh valleys are very famous. One of the most beautiful natural sites in north Wales is Snowdon, the highest mountain in Wales, standing at 1,085 metres. It's situated in Snowdonia National Park, a very popular park especially for mountain climbers. If you want to get to the top of Snowdon, you can walk it. There are six different walking routes, and they vary in difficulty. Some on the mountain are very hard to climb, like Crib Goch, which has vertical drops on both sides. If you don't want to walk to the top, you can take a ski lift or the Snowdon Mountain Railway and enjoy the views from the train windows.</p>

<p>Snowdon in Welsh is called Yr Wyddfa, which means 'tomb' or 'monument'. According to legend, when King Arthur climbed to the top of Snowdon, he met Rhita Gawr and killed him. Everybody loves a good legend!</p>

<p><strong>Cardiff.</strong> Cardiff is the capital city of Wales and the 10th largest city in the United Kingdom, with a current inner city population of around 358,400. A very popular tourist destination, Cardiff is known as the 'City of Castles' because it has got five different castles. Cardiff was always very important strategically because of its location at the centre of three rivers: the River Taff, the River Ely and the River Rhymney. Governors could control trade and movement over a large area from Cardiff. Because of this, the Romans constructed a fort there over 2,000 years ago, and later the Normans built Cardiff Castle on this fort. This magnificent castle was renovated many times during its history. Visitors to the castle can still see the remains of the Roman fort as well as the secret tunnels that provided air raid shelters during World War II.</p>

<p>The writer Roald Dahl was born and lived in Cardiff. His home and school are popular tourist sites today. Tourists also love the port, which was renovated in the 1990s and renamed Cardiff Bay. This busy area is now full of shops and restaurants, as well as the Senedd (the National Assembly of Wales) and a spectacular entertainment centre, the Wales Millennium Centre.</p>

<p><strong>Climate.</strong> The climate in Wales, like in the rest of the United Kingdom, can change very quickly. It rains a lot, especially in the autumn and winter. In the winter season, the sea storms and strong currents cause many shipwrecks. If you go diving, you might find an underwater treasure or the remains of an old ship.</p>

  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter 3</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Attractions</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para"><strong>The Quay House.</strong> The Quay House near Conwy Castle in north Wales is the smallest house in Great Britain. The building is 1.8 metres wide, 2.5 metres deep and only 3.1 metres high. Thousands of tourists come here and try one by one to enter the two little rooms of this tiny house. Only four people can be in the house at one time. The upstairs is so small that only a bed and a bedside cupboard can fit inside. A lady dressed in traditional Welsh clothes greets visitors and tells them about the history of the house. The house was built in the 16th century and it was inhabited until 1900. The last person who lived in the house was a tall fisherman, 1.91 metres; he couldn't stand up fully in the house. In the end, he had to leave the house because it was declared unsuitable to live in.</p>

<p><strong>Llanfairpwllgwyngyllgogerychwyrndrobwllllantysiliogogogoch.</strong> If you like the sound of Welsh and its strange spelling, you should visit this village on the Isle of Anglesey. Don't worry about saying its name – it's called Llanfair PG for short. This long name gives the location of the village: it means "St Mary's church in the hollow of the white hazel near a rapid whirlpool and the church of St Tysilio of the red cave". This incredible name was chosen as the name of the village in the 19th century to attract tourists. Today, thousands of tourists take pictures by the 4.6-metre-wide sign on the railway platform. It's the longest name of a place in Europe, and of course it appears in the Guinness World Records. Can you imagine a small railway ticket with a 58-letter destination printed on it?</p>

<p><strong>Portmeirion.</strong> Portmeirion is an unusual and picturesque tourist village in north Wales. It was designed by the architect Sir Clough Williams-Ellis and built between the years 1925 and 1975. This is a very special place because it looks like an Italian village but in Wales! Every building is different in colour and shape. There are lots of beautiful gardens and a path leading down to the sea. Sir Clough Williams-Ellis was an environmentalist. His plan was to build a tourist village that was an attractive place to live. The village itself is not large, but the architectural designs, the arches and the piazza make it look bigger. Not surprisingly, Portmeirion is extremely popular with tourists.</p>

<p><strong>Animal attractions.</strong> You can see dolphins, porpoises and seals in Wales in the spring and summer months, and if you're lucky, you may even see a humpback whale. And of course, there are sheep! In fact, there are more sheep than people in Wales. They're a major part of the Welsh landscape, walking around the hills and mountains, and they're not frightened of cars, so they often appear in the middle of country roads.</p>

<p><strong>Birds.</strong> There are many interesting birds in Wales, and the most famous one is the red kite. Once this bird of prey was in danger of extinction, but because of intense conservation efforts it has survived and you can see it in many parts of Wales. In fact, there are so many red kites that it is often regarded as the unofficial national bird of Wales. The best place to see red kites is mid-Wales. It's a truly wonderful sight watching these elegant birds flying in the sky. They've got beautiful long wings and a long tail. They mainly eat small mammals like rabbits and mice, but they also enjoy feeding on a carcass. There are even special farms where tourists can watch them feed.</p>

<p><strong>The Big Pit National Coal Museum.</strong> During the 19th and 20th centuries, coal and slate mining were major industries in Wales. Cardiff was one of the largest coal exporting ports in the world. Both industries still exist today but on a much smaller scale. In the past, the coal and slate mines provided work for adults and for children. Some children started to work in the mines when they were only 5 or 6 years old. Like their parents, they worked long hours in dangerous, uncomfortable and badly ventilated conditions. There were many accidents where miners were killed or badly hurt. The Big Pit National Coal Museum in Blaenavon, south Wales, is one of the few remaining mines where visitors can tour 90 metres below the ground and learn about life in the mines. Around 140,000 tourists visit it every year.</p>

  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter 4</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Traditional Food and Drink</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Traditional Welsh food is quite simple and represents the lives of the working people. Since many Welsh people lived in remote areas, they had to cook using ingredients that were available and economical. Vegetables came from the garden or local farms, the fish from the rivers, lakes or the sea, and the meat usually came from the family pig. Although lamb is a popular meat in Wales today, in the past it was only eaten on special days or holidays. Historically, there were no written recipes; recipes were passed down orally from generation to generation.</p>

<p><strong>Cawl.</strong> Cawl is a traditional Welsh soup and is often considered the national dish of Wales. It's a winter dish that originated in the 14th century. Once it was made with bacon or beef together with vegetables like carrots or potatoes. Today, cawl is often made with lamb and leek.</p>

<p><strong>Laverbread.</strong> Laverbread is a traditional Welsh dish made from laver, an edible seaweed found on the rocks along the coastline of Wales. It's extremely nutritious because it's rich in minerals, proteins and vitamins. The laver is cooked for hours and then made into a greenish-black purée or paste. A traditional Welsh breakfast usually consists of laverbread, bacon, sausage, eggs and cockles. Some people use laver as sauce or make it into soup. During the 18th century, laver was a basic and important food for the miners. The miners' wives collected it from the rocks on the coast and either sold it in the market or prepared laverbread with it. A typical miner started his day with laverbread as part of his traditional Welsh breakfast. Although the production of laver has become industrialised, the traditional methods of preparing it still remain in some parts of Wales today.</p>

<p><strong>Glamorgan sausage.</strong> The Glamorgan sausage is different from regular sausages because it's completely vegetarian. It's traditionally made with a white Welsh cheese called Caerphilly, leek and breadcrumbs. These sausages were particularly popular during World War II, when it was hard to find meat to buy.</p>

<p><strong>Bara brith.</strong> Bara means 'bread' and brith means 'speckled'. So bara brith literally means 'speckled bread'. It is a cake with dried fruit and spices. Bara brith is served with butter at tea time. In fact, in Argentina, there's a kind of bara brith called torta negra. This comes from the Welsh settlers who brought their traditional dishes with them when they arrived in Patagonia, and it is still served today in Welsh tea houses in that area.</p>

<p><strong>Drinks.</strong> Wales produces beer, whiskey and wine. A popular new kind of gin called 'seaweed gin' is made in Wales. It's made with seaweed from the Welsh coast and is processed at a local distillery. The colour and taste of the seaweed depends on the season, and this influences the taste of the gin. The seaweed makes the gin a bit salty, but it's got a very distinct and unique taste.</p>

  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter 5</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Festivals and Traditions</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para"><strong>St David's Day.</strong> The most famous national holiday in Wales is St David's Day. On the 1st of March, there are lots of celebrations. Traditional songs are sung and traditional food is eaten. Some children dress in the Welsh national costume and hold leeks and daffodils too. St David is the patron saint of Wales. He was born about 1,500 years ago and he was educated at a monastery. Later he became a monk. According to legend, St David performed many miracles in his life. People believe that he helped a blind man to see again and brought a child back to life by wetting him with his tears. However, according to legend, his most famous miracle was when he was giving a sermon to a large number of people and many couldn't see or hear him. Then a white dove landed on David's shoulder and the ground that David was standing on rose up to a large hill. Suddenly everybody could see and hear him. A church was built on this hill with pictures and stained glass windows showing St David with the white dove on his shoulder. To this day, the white dove is the emblem of St David. There are many churches and chapels in St David's name as well as a flag. The name Dewi is the Welsh name for David and many boys in Wales are called that.</p>

<p><strong>St Dwynwen's Day.</strong> On the 25th of January, the Welsh celebrate St Dwynwen's Day, which is a romantic day for lovers. People exchange cards and there are celebrations all over Wales. Dwynwen is the Welsh patron saint of lovers. In the 5th century, there was a king called Brychan Brycheiniog who had 24 daughters. Dwynwen was the most beautiful. She was in love with a man called Maelon, but her father had a different man for her to marry. Dwynwen was sad and asked God to help her forget Maelon. That night, an angel visited Dwynwen in her sleep and gave her a sweet potion. This helped her forget her feelings for Maelon and turned him into a block of ice. God then gave Dwynwen three wishes. Her first wish was for Maelon to be thawed. Her second wish was for God to help all lovers, and her final wish was that she would never marry. God answered all three wishes. In return, Dwynwen became a nun and dedicated her life to God. She built the church of St Dwynwen and founded a convent on the island of Llanddwyn near Anglesey. The remains of the church and Dwynwen's well can be seen on the island. The well is still a favourite place for young Welsh lovers even today.</p>

<p><strong>Eisteddfod.</strong> Eisteddfod, meaning 'sitting together' or 'gathering', is a Welsh festival of music, literature and performance. The first Eisteddfod took place in the 12th century. Poets and musicians from all over Wales were invited to recite their poetry or play their music, and judges chose the best one. An elegant high-backed chair with arms was awarded to the winner. This was a prestigious prize because from the medieval period until the 1700s, ordinary people sat on simple stools. High-backed chairs were kept for royalty and people of high status only. Today, the National Eisteddfod of Wales takes place every August for eight days, and it's the largest festival of poetry and music in Europe. The entire competition is held in Welsh.</p>

  <div class="chapter-title">Language</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">I met Owen later that evening and we decided to tour together over the following days.</p>

<p>"I know all about Wales now," I told him. "I spent an hour googling all sorts of interesting things about your country. Next time I go to the UK, I'll include Wales in the trip."</p>

<p>"Good idea, Ben," said Owen. "I'll show you lots of cool places, but I'm not speaking Welsh. It's such a hard language."</p>

<p>"You said a lot of people speak Welsh today, Owen. But what do you usually speak at home?" I asked. "Welsh or English?"</p>

<p>"Welsh, most of the time," answered Owen. "I speak it at work, with my friends and with my family."</p>

<p>"Really?" I said, surprised. "Before I met you, I thought Welsh was a dead language, like Latin."</p>

<p>"Absolutely not. Most people can speak and write Welsh. It's an obligatory subject in all schools until the age of 16. Children learn it as either a first or second language. In fact, quite a lot of children are taught all their subjects in Welsh. When you come to Wales, you'll see and hear Welsh everywhere. There are radio stations and a TV channel with all their programmes in Welsh. Also, there are Welsh newspapers and magazines, and the road signs are in both languages.</p>

<p>Welsh is really Britain's oldest language. The Romans were the first people to write in Welsh, and there are elements of Latin in the language. From the 7th century, the people of Wales were called Combrogi, which means 'fellow countrymen', and Wales created its own laws in Welsh. But the Welsh language has also had its battles. In 1536, Henry VIII passed a law which prohibited using Welsh in the legal system and in administrative affairs. Then in 1549, the English Parliament passed the Act of Uniformity, which said all public prayer had to be in English. Fortunately for us, in 1563, Queen Elizabeth I allowed the Bible and the prayer book to be printed in Welsh, and this brought the language back again. Welsh people went to church regularly, so the translations helped the language survive. Welsh literature, especially poetry, has helped to preserve the language and national identity.</p>

<p>In 1870, the English Act of Education made English the official language of instruction at schools, to put an end to the Welsh language. Children who spoke Welsh were punished. If a teacher heard a child speaking Welsh, he would put a 'Welsh knot' sign around the child's neck. Even the names of towns were changed into English. For example, Caerdydd became Cardiff."</p>

<p>"So, how has the language survived until now, Owen?" I asked.</p>

<p>"Well, a sense of national identity has led to the revival of the language. Now there's Welsh language instruction in schools, education centres, and on Welsh radio stations and TV channels. Ben, have you heard of Patagonia?"</p>

<p>"That's in South America, isn't it?" I asked.</p>

<p>"Yes, in Argentina," Owen answered. "In 1865, several Welsh people arrived in Patagonia and stayed there. They wanted to keep their culture and language, so they spoke Welsh. Then more Welsh people started to visit the region in the 1960s, and this revived the language. Today, they still speak Patagonian Welsh there, a dialect of Welsh. Welsh teachers are sent to Patagonia to instruct local teachers in the language. Apparently, speaking Welsh is a sign of prestige in Patagonia."</p>

<p>"Can you understand Patagonian Welsh?" I asked.</p>

<p>"Oh yes," said Owen. "It's not so different from our Welsh."</p>

<p>Owen lifted his cup of coffee, looked at me and said, "Cymru am byth!" I tried unsuccessfully to repeat that.</p>

<p>"What does it mean?" I asked.</p>

<p>"Wales forever," smiled Owen, and drank some more coffee.</p>

  <div class="chapter-title">Sport</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para"><strong>Rugby.</strong> Wales participates in many world sporting events, like the Rugby World Cup and the FIFA World Cup. Although the Welsh like playing football, the sport that most people associate with Wales is rugby. Rugby is considered the national sport and it's played and followed throughout Wales. The Welsh Rugby Union (WRU) supervises the different rugby clubs, the national team, the referees, players and trainers. Rugby isn't just for men. There's a WRU team for women too. Both teams have got the same emblem: the Prince of Wales feathers, and they both wear the same uniform: red shirts, white shorts and red socks. At every game, the enthusiastic fans sing Welsh rugby songs. These are often folk songs or hymns. In fact, some of these religious songs were sung in the late 19th century. The oldest Welsh rugby song is 'Men of Harlech', which is about Welsh resistance during the siege of Harlech Castle between 1461 and 1468.</p>

<p><strong>The Millennium Stadium.</strong> The Millennium Stadium, recently renamed the Principality Stadium, opened in June 1999 and it's the biggest arena in Wales. It's got a retractable roof and it can seat 74,500 visitors. At international rugby games, the Welsh fans stand in the stadium, all loudly singing their national anthem, 'Land of My Fathers' ('Hen Wlad Fy Nhadau'). Besides the National Rugby Union team, the stadium also hosts the Welsh national football team and has had music concerts there, including Madonna, U2 and Red Hot Chili Peppers.</p>

<p><strong>Jousting.</strong> In medieval times, the most popular Welsh sport was jousting. Wales is a country of castles and jousting was once the favourite form of entertainment of kings, knights and noblemen. Two knights fought on horseback and the objective was to break the lance on his opponent's armour or shield and to make him fall from his horse. It was a battle of speed and courage. In medieval times, the winning jouster was given a prize like a gold ring, a sword or an elaborate helmet, but the loser often died. Jousting was a dangerous sport. Every year in June, Cardiff Castle has a medieval weekend with traditional jousting for the visitors. Even today, there are riding schools that teach jousting. The sport requires control and physical strength because the armour is made of steel, which makes it very heavy. Jousters must train daily and must also be good at horse riding and have good balance because they need to hold a long lance in one hand while riding. That's not so easy at all.</p>

  <div class="chapter-title">Famous Figures</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para"><strong>Owain Glyndŵr.</strong> One of the greatest Welsh heroes was Owain Glyndŵr. He was the last native Welshman to have the title Prince of Wales. Owain was a strong, charismatic leader who united and led the Welsh against the English in the 14th century. Welshmen from all social classes joined Owain in his revolt and supported his desire for Welsh independence from England. After years of fighting, the revolt ended, and by 1410, Owain was a wanted fugitive. No one knows exactly when he died, but his memory remains strong throughout Wales, and many parks and streets have his name.</p>

<p><strong>Dylan Thomas.</strong> Popular Welsh poet Dylan Thomas is probably one of the most important poets of the 20th century. He was born in Swansea on the southern coast of Wales in 1914. His father, an English literature professor, used to recite Shakespeare to Thomas, inspiring a love of literature from an early age. Much of his inspiration and ideas came from the Welsh countryside. He is famous for his 'Collected Poems of Dylan Thomas', radio plays like 'Under Milk Wood', and a book 'Portrait of the Artist as a Young Dog'. Surprisingly, Dylan Thomas, the most famous Welsh poet, never wrote in Welsh. Dylan Thomas died in the United States at 39 years of age, and there is a plaque to his memory in Poets' Corner in Westminster Abbey, London.</p>

<p><strong>Welsh celebrities.</strong></p>

<p><strong>Gareth Bale</strong> (1989, Cardiff) – Top footballer, plays for Real Madrid and the Welsh national team.</p>

<p><strong>Sir Anthony Hopkins</strong> (1937, Port Talbot) – Actor who works in cinema, theatre and television. One Oscar for Best Actor in 'The Silence of the Lambs' (1991) and two Emmys. Knighted by Queen Elizabeth II in 1993. Received a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame.</p>

<p><strong>Sir Tom Jones</strong> (1940, Pontypridd) – Popular singer who has sold over 100 million records, with 36 top 40 hits in the UK and 19 in the United States. Received a Grammy Award and two Brit Awards. Knighted by Queen Elizabeth II for services to music.</p>

<p><strong>Roald Dahl</strong> (1916-1990, Cardiff) – Writer, poet and screenwriter. His most famous works include 'Charlie and the Chocolate Factory', 'Matilda', 'The Witches', 'Fantastic Mr Fox' and 'James and the Giant Peach'. Sold more than 250 million books worldwide and received several awards for his contribution to literature.</p>

<p><strong>Catherine Zeta-Jones</strong> (1969, Swansea) – Actress who works in theatre, cinema and television. Has won several awards including an Oscar for 'Chicago' and a CBE for her film and humanitarian works. She appeared in 'Intolerable Cruelty', 'The Terminal' and 'The Legend of Zorro'.</p>

  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para"><strong>The Flag.</strong> The Welsh flag has got two large horizontal stripes, one green and one white, and a large red dragon in the middle. Green and white were the colours of the 15th century Tudor royal family who came from Wales. They added the red dragon to the Tudor flag to emphasise their Welsh descent. However, it was only in 1959 that the flag officially became the National Flag of Wales. The red dragon supposedly comes from the time of King Arthur. According to legend, the wizard Merlin had a vision of a red dragon (the native Britons) fighting a white dragon (the Saxon invaders), and the red dragon won the battle. Today, the red dragon represents all things that are connected to Wales.</p>

<p><strong>The Leek.</strong> It might surprise you to read that one of the national emblems of Wales is the leek. According to one legend, King Cadwaladr ordered his soldiers to wear a leek on their helmets when they were fighting the Saxons in a leek field. The leeks helped them identify themselves and gave them good camouflage from their enemy. Another legend says that St David always ate leeks even when he was fasting, so they became his personal symbol. Today, many Welsh people wear a leek on St David's Day to celebrate their victory against the Saxons.</p>

<p><strong>The Daffodil.</strong> The daffodil is another symbol of Wales, but that may be due to some language confusion. A leek in Welsh is 'cenhinen', and a daffodil in Welsh is 'cenhinen Pedr', which means 'Peter's leek'. Daffodils are spring flowers, and since St David's Day is on 1st March, many people wear a daffodil on that day too.</p>

<p><strong>Traditional Welsh costume.</strong> Welsh women had a traditional costume, but it was mainly poorer rural women who wore it at the market. There are many different variations of this costume, but it generally consists of a striped underdress with a shawl and an apron. And the most important part of the Welsh costume is the tall chimney hat. The Welsh costume became a symbol of national identity, and today women wear it on special occasions like St David's Day or at Eisteddfodau.</p>

<p><strong>Love spoons.</strong> You can't visit a Welsh gift shop without seeing a variety of love spoons. The Welsh love spoon is a very popular souvenir. Made by hand from one piece of wood, it is carved with a special message or symbol, for example, a heart for love, a horseshoe for luck, a cross for religion, and bells for marriage. The tradition of carving and giving a love spoon to a girl originated in Wales in the 17th century. The spoons were not actually used; they were displayed as ornaments on the wall. Young men carved their love and intentions on a love spoon. If a girl kept the love spoon, it was a sign that she was interested in him. If she returned it, it was clear she didn't want the man. The love spoon tradition disappeared in the 19th century, but over the last 40 years, there's been a revival, and people, especially tourists, buy hand-made love spoons for their loved ones on special occasions, like anniversaries, births or graduations. It's certainly more original than a box of chocolates or flowers.</p>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter 10</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Myths and Legends</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">It's not surprising that a country with so many castles has got lots of legends and ghost stories. There are plenty of stories of haunted castles and buildings in Wales. Strange things seem to happen in Cardiff Castle, particularly in its southwest corner. Apparently, at 3.45 every morning, the heavy doors of the dining room open and close by themselves and the lights go on and off. Another scary story is the sudden appearance of the second Marquis of Bute. He lived in Cardiff Castle in the 19th century. According to some people, he sometimes appears in the library dressed in a long red coat – that's enough to terrify the tourists!</p>

<p>Another castle with a reputation for ghosts is Castell Coch ('Red Castle'), which is nine kilometres from Cardiff. It looks beautiful and romantic, but it's got dark dungeons and corridors. In fact, on Halloween, there are special ghost tours there. There's a legend about the Castell Coch ghost who continues to search for the treasure he hid in the walls of the castle. Some people say they experience a strange feeling in the chapel, as if someone is watching them. Some hear strange sounds in the Great Hall, and others maintain that a spirit touched them. If you're a nervous person, it's probably best not to take the Castell Coch night tour!</p>

<p><strong>Devil's Bridge.</strong> Another popular tourist site is a village called Pontarfynach, which means 'the bridge on the Mynach'. This unusual bridge is actually not one bridge, but three bridges, one built on the other. The first bridge was built of stone in the 11th century, the second in 1753, and the third iron bridge in 1901. Visitors can walk down stone steps, called Jacob's Ladder, from the third bridge to the first bridge to see a beautiful waterfall. So how did this interesting feature get its name, Devil's Bridge? According to legend, the bridge was built by the devil. Apparently, in the 11th century, the devil visited Wales and met an old woman with her dog. The old woman was worried about her cow, which was trapped on the other side of the river. The devil wanted the woman's soul, so he agreed to build a bridge across the river in return for the soul of the first living thing that crossed the bridge. But the old woman was clever. As soon as the devil finished building the bridge, she threw some bread onto the bridge. Her dog ran after the bread and became the first living thing to cross the bridge. The devil was so humiliated by this that he left Wales and never returned again.</p>

<p><strong>King Arthur.</strong> There are many legends of King Arthur and Wales. Two lakes in Snowdonia, Lake Llydaw and Lake Ogwen, are both reputed to contain King Arthur's magical sword Excalibur. There are also rocks that supposedly show the hoof prints of King Arthur's horse. A stone on the banks of Lake Barfog has strange hoof prints, supposedly from the time when King Arthur and his horse pulled a monster from the lake. There are lots of fantasy tales and legends about the western coast of Wales. These remote places inspire frightening thoughts and wild imagination, which make the best ghost stories.</p>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter 11</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Arts</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para"><strong>Wales: Land of Song.</strong> Wales is known as the 'Land of Song', and it has got many Welsh male choirs. These originated in the 19th century during the Industrial Revolution, and are an important part of Welsh culture and identity. Today there are over 100 male choirs in Wales. The industrial workforce in Wales came from the working class, and most of the workers were men. They worked long hours in the ports, mines or railways, and they often organised themselves into a local choir within their workplace. The choir was a way for them to relax after a hard day's work. It gave these hard-working men a sense of identity and pride, and a unique chance to see other places in Wales and England, because choirs often competed against each other. The choirs sang mainly traditional Welsh songs and hymns, but sometimes light opera or popular modern songs. The songs were often accompanied by the triple harp, a harp with three rows of strings. This instrument was played so much in Wales that it became known as the Welsh harp. Today it's considered the natural instrument of Wales, and traditional Welsh folk music is often played on this harp.</p>

<p>"My grandfather was in a choir," Owen told Ben. "He told me that the first time he left his village was when he was 23, when his choir sang in a competition in Cardiff. He used to tell me stories of his life in the village. He said that when he was a boy, choirs were a natural part of village life. I can still hear his voice saying, 'When I was a lad, you did what your father did. You left school, got a job in the mines, and became a member of a choir. That's how it was in the village.'"</p>

<p><strong>Wales in films and TV.</strong> The Welsh countryside is a great location for films, because it has an interesting and diverse landscape, beautiful long coasts, high mountains and magnificent cliffs. In 'Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows', there is a scene when Harry and his friends hide in Dobby's Shell Cottage. This cottage was built especially for the film on the sandy beach of Pembrokeshire, west Wales. Although the cottage isn't there any more, you can still walk along the beach and imagine you are walking with Harry, Hermione and Ron. In 2010, this same location was chosen for the film 'Robin Hood'. This time, it was used to present a ferocious battle. Horsemen dressed in armour and with flags in their hands galloped along the beach. The main actor, Russell Crowe, liked the location so much that he camped on the sand for several nights instead of flying back to his luxurious hotel in London.</p>

<p>American actress Kristen Stewart was also fascinated by the beautiful Pembrokeshire sands when she played Snow White in the film 'Snow White and the Huntsman' (2012). There is an exciting scene in which Snow White rides with 80 men on horseback, and this was filmed along Marloes Sands in Pembrokeshire.</p>

<p><strong>Doctor Who.</strong> 'Doctor Who' is a British science fiction television series which began in 1963 and continued until 1989. It was later revived in 2005 due to popular demand. The series is about a doctor who travels in time in his special time travel machine, the TARDIS. The series is filmed in South Wales. For tourists who like science fiction, filming and technology, a visit to the Doctor Who Experience in Cardiff Bay is a must. There, they can learn about the filming of 'Doctor Who' and see many of the different props like the TARDIS, the monsters and the costumes that appear in the series. They can also discover how they create the amazing special effects. It's a very interesting and popular attraction.</p>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter 12</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Wales Today</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para"><strong>Tourism.</strong> In the past, coal and slate mining were the main industries in Wales. But today, the main industry is tourism, with over 8 million visitors a year. Most of the tourists come from other parts of the United Kingdom, especially England. Wales also attracts tourists from the United States and Europe, particularly France and Germany. Visiting the numerous castles and other historic sites is a popular activity for tourists, especially in the summer, when you can watch representations of battles and jousting. Wales is a wonderful country to visit if you like the outdoor life. The most popular tourist activities are climbing in the mountains and other sports, such as canoeing and cycling. But if you like more of a challenge, there's also hang-gliding or downhill mountain biking. There are over 1 million Americans of Welsh descent, so many of them come to discover their origins and learn about their ancestors' culture and traditions.</p>

<p>Wales also has got some beautiful and unusual 'glamping' sites. Glamping means 'glamorous camping'. Not everyone enjoys the normal camping experience: sleeping bags, tents, fires, or worrying about keeping their things dry when it rains. When you go glamping, you can enjoy the positive experiences of being outdoors, but with every comfort, like beds, bathrooms and heating. There are all kinds of Welsh glamping sites: tree houses, cabins and tents. Some are more luxurious than others, but they're all very comfortable.</p>

<p><strong>Fashion.</strong> Wales has got many sheep, so it's not surprising that wool has played an important part in Welsh industry throughout the years. In fact, spinning and weaving of wool already existed in prehistoric times. The production of wool in Wales was a very important industry in the country until World War I. Today, there are only a few wool mills, and these are usually operated by small families. They still produce traditional Welsh cloth using old looms, protecting the environment.</p>

<p>Like other countries, Wales is looking for ways to protect the environment and reduce carbon dioxide emissions. One solution is building a wind farm to produce electricity. Wind power doesn't consume fuel, and it doesn't generate air pollution. Gwynt y Môr is an example of an offshore wind farm. It's off the coast of north Wales, and it's the second largest offshore wind farm in the world. It has 160 wind turbines that can produce enough energy for approximately 30% of homes in Wales.</p>

<p><strong>Welsh independence.</strong> Some Welsh people prefer to maintain their national identity without leaving the United Kingdom. But there are other Welsh people who want independence, and certainly more control over decisions that affect their lives. There are political parties who want Welsh independence from the United Kingdom. One of the main parties that promotes this ideology is the Welsh nationalist party, Plaid Cymru. In the early 20th century, there was a fear among many Welsh people that their language and culture would disappear as English became the official language. As a result, Plaid Cymru was founded in 1925. One of its main objectives was to create a bilingual society by reviving the Welsh language. It also wanted to promote Wales' contribution in the global market, as well as to become a member of the United Nations.</p>

<p>The recent vote for Great Britain to exit from the European Union (Brexit) has raised the question of Welsh independence. Most Welsh people voted to leave the European Union, and Plaid Cymru's leader sees Brexit as an opportunity for Wales to become an independent country. Many Welsh people may disagree about independence, but most of them want to promote Welsh identity and language throughout Wales today. The Welsh are proud of their country, their traditions and their contribution to all areas of British life.</p>

<div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ * ~ </span></div>

<p><strong>Swansea Jack.</strong> Swansea Jack was a famous black retriever dog. He lived with his master, William Thomas, in the coastal city of Swansea. He became famous for saving people from the sea. When Swansea Jack heard a cry for help, he dived into the cold water and pulled the person to a safe place. His first rescue was in June 1931, when he was only a year old. He jumped into the sea and saved a 12-year-old boy. Nobody knew what he had done until a few weeks later when he rescued another swimmer. This time, a crowd of people saw his actions, and his photograph appeared in the local newspaper. Then, news of his first rescue became known. According to legend, he saved 27 people in his lifetime. In fact, Swansea Jack saved so many people that he won the 'Bravest Dog of the Year' award, a silver collar, a silver cup from the Lord Mayor of London, and two bronze medals. Dog lovers enjoyed meeting Swansea Jack, and he often appeared at carnivals and shows with his owner to help raise money for charity. Sadly, Swansea Jack died in 1937 after eating rat poison. The people of Swansea were so proud of their dog that they built a special monument to him, which visitors can see today near Swansea's rugby ground.</p>

<p><strong>Rollo.</strong> When people go to agricultural shows, they usually see sheep dogs herding animals. But for several years, the star of these shows in Wales and other places in the UK was a woolly white sheep called Rollo. Welsh farmer Emlyn Roberts became Rollo's owner in 2005. He rescued her on a visit to friends when she was a tiny lamb in Conwy. "Everyone thought the lamb had died," he said, "but then he saw she was still alive, so he decided to take her home." The little lamb recovered, and Emlyn called her Rollo because her favourite treat was a famous British chocolate sweet called Rolo. Rollo grew up with Emlyn's sheep dogs, and it became obvious she thought she was also a dog because she started to behave like them. She ate her meals with them and followed Emlyn's feet while he watched TV. But Emlyn was even more surprised when she started to copy the dogs and bring his ducks together and into their pen for the night. In 2006, he started to take her to agricultural shows and amazed the crowds with her talents. Besides herding ducks around a special course, she jumped over fences and did other tricks too. Sadly, Rollo died in 2013, but Emlyn is now training Rollo's nephew, Horlicks, to do tricks and dance. Emlyn hopes the people will love him the same way they loved Rollo.</p>
