<p>"All right. Let's talk on the veranda. Florence, bring drinks for my guests."</p>

<p>"You have a very beautiful home, Mr. Quatermain."</p>

<p>"Thank you, Sir Henry. Now, would you mind telling me the reason for your visit?"</p>

<p>"Before I answer that question, Mr. Quatermain, may I ask you something? About this time last year, you were in the north of Transvaal. Am I right?"</p>

<p>"Yes."</p>

<p>"Near the Lukanga River beyond Matabili?"</p>

<p>"Yes."</p>

<p>"How did you know that?"</p>

<p>"What were you doing there?"</p>

<p>"I was hunting elephants. It's what I do. I'm an ivory trader. But I'm sorry. Why all these questions?"</p>

<p>"Mr. Quatermain, I will explain. But one last question. Last year at Matabili, did you meet a man called George Neville?"</p>

<p>"George Neville? Are George Neville? Yes, I did."</p>

<p>"When was the last time you saw him?"</p>

<p>"A year ago. At the time you're referring to. I haven't seen him since then."</p>

<p>"Do you know what happened to him?"</p>

<p>"Can I once again ask the reason for these questions?"</p>

<p>"I'm here in Africa because I want to find George Neville. He is my brother."</p>

<p>"Your brother? But Neville is not his real name. His real name is George Curtis."</p>

<p>"I see."</p>

<p>"The thing is, Quatermain, George Curtis left England a year ago with a ridiculous plan to come to Africa to look for a diamond mine. He booked a passage to Cape Town and hasn't been heard of since."</p>

<p>"I see."</p>

<p>"Captain Good, what is your interest in this matter, if I may ask?"</p>

<p>"George Curtis is married to my sister. They left her and their two children in England to go off on this ridiculous adventure."</p>

<p>"So, for the second time of asking, have you any idea what his plan was?"</p>

<p>"Well, you're right. Neville — your brother — was looking for a diamond mine. He told me he intended to search for a place which is known as King Solomon's Mines."</p>

<p>"So, it's true. This is what I heard, but what are King Solomon's Mines?"</p>

<p>"King Solomon's Mines, if they exist, are said to contain more and better diamonds than any mines in Africa or indeed the world."</p>

<p>"Really? Where are they, Captain Good?"</p>

<p>"If people knew the exact location of King Solomon's Mines, they would have been discovered long before now. No one knows."</p>

<p>"They do know where they are said to be."</p>

<p>"Where?"</p>

<p>"They are said to be in the Suleiman Mountains to the north of Matabili. And that's where the name comes from. Suleiman — Solomon, you see? Nothing to do with King Solomon at all."</p>

<p>"I see. So, when you were in Matabili, you were close to these mountains?"</p>

<p>"Not at all. A few miles beyond the Lukanga River, there's nothing but desert. The Kukwana Desert, it's called. The Suleiman Mountains are beyond the desert."</p>

<p>"I see. Have you ever crossed the Kukwana Desert?"</p>

<p>"Cross the Kukwana Desert? Sir, only a madman would try to do that."</p>

<p>"Why? Is it too big?"</p>

<p>"It is indeed big, but the size of the desert is not the problem. There are other reasons too. The Kukwana Desert is named after the Kukwana tribe. The desert and the Suleiman Mountains are their homeland. They believe that the area is a spirit world inhabited by their ancestors."</p>

<p>"Mr. Quatermain, as far as you are aware, has any white man ever crossed the Kukwana Desert?"</p>

<p>"I only know of one."</p>

<p>"Who was that?"</p>

<p>"Do you know him?"</p>

<p>"I met him."</p>

<p>"Do you know where we can find him?"</p>

<p>"I'm afraid he's dead."</p>

<p>"Oh, I'm sorry. What happened to him?"</p>

<p>"Well, I will tell you what I know. About two years ago, I was at a miserable godforsaken place on the Lukanga River, called Sitanda's Kraal. While I was there, I fell ill. A fever. I could hardly move for three weeks. Sitanda's Kraal is not a place that many white people reach, so I was surprised one day when a European was shown into my tent. It turned out he was Portuguese."</p>

<p>"A map? Giving the location of King Solomon's mines?"</p>

<p>"Yes."</p>

<p>"Did you think it was genuine?"</p>

<p>"No. Of course not. Not at the time. Anyway, Silvestre could see that I was ill and that he wasn't going to persuade me to join him, so he set off with just a servant to accompany him. To be honest, I forgot all about him. But then, about two weeks later, there was suddenly a lot of shouting outside my tent. 'What is it?' I asked. 'It is the man who was here before, Master.' 'What? The Portuguese chap?' 'I do not know, Master.' 'What the man?' 'Are you there?' 'What the man?'"</p>

<p>"God, Silvestre, what the devil has happened to you?"</p>

<p>"Quatermain, I was there. I saw King Solomon's mines. But the Kukuanas came after me, and I had no water. But they are there. Quatermain, I saw them."</p>

<p>"Calm down, man. Take it easy."</p>

<p>"Poor man."</p>

<p>"Sir, did he find the mines? Do you think?"</p>

<p>"I don't know for sure. But in his bag, I found the map. And some diamonds."</p>

<p>"Do you have the map?"</p>

<p>"Yes."</p>

<p>"Can we see it?"</p>

<p>"I'll go and get it."</p>

<p>"So, this is the desert, and beyond the desert, here are the Suleiman Mountains. According to this map, there are forests and plenty of fresh water, and this mountain here is called Sheba's Breasts. I discovered that this is Kukwana for 'sacred mountain'. The mines seem to be somewhere on this mountain."</p>

<p>"I see."</p>

<p>"Did you show this map to my brother?"</p>

<p>"No, I didn't have it with me when I met him."</p>

<p>"But did you tell him about it?"</p>

<p>"Good morning, Sir Henry. How are preparations for the journey?"</p>

<p>"Not good, I'm afraid. I have the wagons that we need and the provisions, but I can't find servants who are prepared to travel with us."</p>

<p>"I have a driver. His name is Goza and he's a good man, but he's only prepared to come with us as far as Sitanda's Kraal. He won't cross the Kukwana desert."</p>

<p>"Don't worry. I have found someone. Two men, in fact."</p>

<p>"You have?"</p>

<p>"Did you tell them where we are going?"</p>

<p>"Yes, of course. I told them we were going to the Solomon Mountains to look for my brother. They seem like good, strong men. One of them is rather quiet, but the other is a very intelligent man. He speaks English well, too."</p>

<p>"Of course, you must have the final decision, Quatermain, so I have brought them here this morning."</p>

<p>"Here?"</p>

<p>"Yes. They are waiting outside. Would you be so kind as to speak to them?"</p>

<p>"Certainly."</p>

<p>"These are the men."</p>

<p>"I see. Do you speak English?"</p>

<p>"Yes, Master."</p>

<p>"And you?"</p>

<p>"Yes, Master."</p>

<p>"You know that we are planning to travel across the Kukwana desert and into the Solomon Mountains?"</p>

<p>"Yes, Master. We are Kukwana. We know those lands."</p>

<p>"Have you heard of the place we call King Solomon's Mines?"</p>

<p>"Yes, Master."</p>

<p>"Do you know where they are?"</p>

<p>"No one knows where they are, Master."</p>

<p>"I see. What are your names?"</p>

<p>"This man is Khiva."</p>

<p>"And you?"</p>

<p>"I... I am Umbopa."</p>

<p>So it was that six of us departed for the north — Sir Henry, Captain Good and myself, Goza the driver, Khiva and Umbopa. It was a journey of a thousand miles. It was the second week in March before we made camp near Sitanda's Kraal. We left Goza with one wagon there, and the five of us set off towards the Kukwana desert. Umbopa and Khiva led the oxen, and Sir Henry, Captain Good and I followed with our rifles at the ready.</p>

<p>It was then that I noticed some marks on Umbopa's back. They looked as if they had been made with a knife.</p>

<p>"Umbopa?"</p>

<p>"Yes, Master."</p>

<p>"What are those marks on your back? It looks as if you had a fight with a lion."</p>

<p>"No, Master. I don't think I would live to tell you about that."</p>

<p>Whatever the marks were, Umbopa was quite clearly not planning to tell us about them. That night, we made camp near the river for the last time. The next morning, we had to turn north towards the desert. I woke early and started preparing to leave. When I went to wake the others, there was no sign of Captain Good.</p>

<p>"Khiva!"</p>

<p>"Yes, Master."</p>

<p>"Where is Captain Good?"</p>

<p>"He went into the forest, Master. I told him there are elephants, so he went to see."</p>

<p>"Oh, my God. The man's a fool. Go and find him and tell him to come back immediately."</p>

<p>"What on earth was that?"</p>

<p>"I don't know."</p>

<p>"Umbopa?"</p>

<p>"Yes, Master."</p>

<p>"Go and find out what is going on."</p>

<p>"Yes, Master."</p>

<p>"What in God's name is happening?"</p>

<p>"I don't know, but we'd better find out. Come on."</p>

<p>Sir Henry and I began running in the direction of the noise. It was clear that Sir Henry hadn't run anywhere for a while. I was soon a long way ahead of him. Then I saw Good running towards me.</p>

<p>"Good, what the hell is going on?"</p>

<p>"I shot an elephant."</p>

<p>"You did what?"</p>

<p>"I couldn't stop myself. It was feeding. It was a sitting target. But I only wounded it."</p>

<p>"You fool!"</p>

<p>"It made a dreadful noise and ran towards me. Then Khiva arrived. He started shouting and running across the elephant's path. It turned and went for him. He tore him to pieces."</p>

<p>"I'm sorry. I think he's dead."</p>

<p>When we buried Khiva in the forest, Umbopa said a few words in Kukwana, then turned and spoke to us. "Khiva was the bravest of brave men."</p>

<p>"Hear, hear."</p>

<p>"Captain Good, Khiva saved your life. But you have done something very bad."</p>

<p>"I know that."</p>

<p>"It is worse than you think. Khiva was a Kukwana, and you caused his death. We are already on Kukwana land. You have raised the spirits of our ancestors. You must not anger the spirits again."</p>

  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Later that day, we finally reached the Kukwana desert. It stretched out in front of us. Crossing it seemed an impossible and foolish task. After three days, we could see the mountains in the distance.</p>

<p>Then, on the fourth day... "Sir Henry. Quatermain. Look."</p>

<p>"What is it, Good?"</p>

<p>"Look. Straight ahead. Trees."</p>

<p>"Thank God. Trees means water."</p>

<p>In less than an hour, we reached the trees. The good news was that there was a small lake and a forest beyond it. We all threw ourselves into the water, drinking and washing the sand off our faces. The bad news was that we were not alone. Good was the first to notice that we were being watched. On the edge of the forest stood a group of natives — about thirty of them. Most of them were carrying assegai, the spears that make them such feared warriors. One of them shouted something, and as one, they charged towards us. Good ran to the wagon and picked up a rifle.</p>

<p>"Stop or I'll shoot!"</p>

<p>The natives had clearly never seen a rifle before, so they didn't stop charging towards us. Then... One of them fell to the ground, wounded. The others stopped, then began to charge again. I thought we were all as good as dead, but then Umbopa stepped in front of the natives and began shouting at them. To my amazement, they stopped and listened to him.</p>

<p>"Quatermain, do you understand what he's saying? Not sure. I think he's telling them that he's the son of someone called Ignosi, who is the son of the great chief Imotu."</p>

<p>At this point, something extraordinary happened. Umbopa turned his back on the natives, and three of them walked towards him. Good raised his gun again.</p>

<p>"Put it down, Good."</p>

<p>Good looked astonished but did as he was told. The natives looked at the marks on Umbopa's back. To my absolute astonishment, the natives threw their assegai away and fell to the ground, as if praying at Umbopa's feet. It was some time before any of us could speak. Sir Henry was the first to try to find out what was happening.</p>

<p>"Umbopa, would you mind telling us what's going on?"</p>

<p>"These men are Kukwana warriors, but they have been banished from their tribal home, because they criticised the woman who calls herself Inkosi chief of the Kukwanas. They are outlaws. If they go back, they will have to fight for their lives. Thanks to the spirits of our ancestors, we have found each other."</p>

<p>"What do you mean, 'found each other'?"</p>

<p>"Since they were banished, they have searched these lands for their true leader. I am their true leader."</p>

<p>"You?"</p>

<p>"What?"</p>

<p>"I am the true chief of the Kukwanas. Ten years ago, my father was killed — poisoned by an evil woman, Gagool, who now calls herself Inkosi chief. She tried to kill me too, but the spirits of our ancestors saved me. And these men know this story too. They have been searching for me."</p>

<p>"Pardon me for doubting you, Umbopa, but how do these men know that you are their chief?"</p>

<p>"Because of the marks on my back. Only the son of a chief has these marks. Now these men will follow and obey me, and I must lead them."</p>

<p>"Lead them? Where?"</p>

<p>"It is time for me to take my place as Inkosi chief of my tribe. They have a leader who is not the true Inkosi. I must fight her men in battle and defeat them. Tomorrow, we will leave to do battle with Gagool and her men."</p>

<p>"Leave? But what about us? You promised to help us find George Curtis?"</p>

<p>"No, Captain Good. I promised to lead you to the Suleiman Mountains. This I have done. I have also saved all of your lives. Without me, you would all be dead now."</p>

<p>"Thomas, please, for once in your life, listen. Umbopa, I understand what you want to do, but we still need your help. We have to find King Solomon's Mines and see if my brother is there. I need to know if he came here and if anyone saw him."</p>

<p>"Wait. I will talk to my people."</p>

<p>After some minutes, he returned. "Sir Henry, I asked the men if they had seen a white man here."</p>

<p>"What did they say?"</p>

<p>"Your brother — a tall man."</p>

<p>"Yes, very tall."</p>

<p>"A tall white man was here last year. He had one finger missing from this hand. His left hand."</p>

<p>"Yes, that's right. It's George."</p>

<p>"Where did they see him? Where did he go?"</p>

<p>"They think he was captured by Gagool's men."</p>

<p>"Oh, God, no. And then what?"</p>

<p>"They took him to Kumbabwe, the tall mountain you can see over there."</p>

<p>"Kumbabwe — sacred mountain. That's where the mines are. Umbopa, we must go there."</p>

<p>"Very well. But you must know that Kumbabwe is a sacred place for us. It is the resting place of the spirits of our chiefs. It is dangerous for you to go there. When the spirits are angry, Kumbabwe also becomes angry."</p>

<p>"But we have to go there. We have to find out what happened to my brother."</p>

<p>"You will never find your way to Kumbabwe alone. You will get lost in the forest. I will tell one of the men to go with you."</p>

<p>"Infadoos!"</p>

<p>"Inkosi Umbopa."</p>

<p>"This man is my father's brother. His name is Infadoos. So he is also a descendant of the great chief Imotu. He will lead you to Kumbabwe and Kwa Suleiman."</p>

<p>"Kwa Suleiman — Suleiman's Cave — the place you call King Solomon's Mines."</p>

<p>"So you know where it is."</p>

    <p class="first-para">The next morning, we set off through the dense tropical forest towards Kumbabwe. There was no path and the walk was hard. But Infadoos knew the way. We walked for hours until we found ourselves at the foot of the mountain. After a short rest, we began to climb. Suddenly, Infadoos stopped in front of a pile of rocks.</p>

<p>"This is Kwa Suleiman. Please help me."</p>

<p>Infadoos indicated that we had to move the rocks, so we all began to pull at them. They were large rocks and it was hard work. Eventually, we could see a dark cave.</p>

<p>"It's time to light the torches."</p>

<p>We lit torches and walked into the cave. It was dark and damp and looked like any other cave. We walked further and further. Then suddenly, the light from the torches began to reflect on diamonds. Hundreds of them. Some were on the ground. Others were still in the rock on the walls of the cave.</p>

<p>"My God! Look at all these diamonds!"</p>

<p>"We have to take some of them with us, Quatermain."</p>

<p>"Quatermain, where are you?"</p>

<p>I had walked on ahead of the others and I saw something. "I'm over here."</p>

<p>"Sir Henry, I think you should come here."</p>

<p>"I'm coming. What is this?"</p>

<p>"Look."</p>

<p>"We have to find another way out."</p>

And I was beginning to panic. But I could breathe. Air was coming from somewhere. The space began to get wider. And soon, I was able to stand up. In front of me, there was another entrance to the cave. Soon, Sir Henry emerged from the tunnel behind me. We were safe.</p>

<p>"God be praised."</p>

<p>"Well done, Quatermain. I thought we were going to die in there, Sir Henry."</p>

<p>"I think we are still in trouble. Look."</p>

<p>"White men, you have entered Kwa Suleiman. And you have woken the anger of our ancestors. You will die for this. Kill them!"</p>

<p>There seemed no way to escape now. I was convinced that these were the last moments of my life. But then, suddenly, Umbopa appeared behind Gagool, alone.</p>

<p>"Gagool!"</p>

<p>"Who are you?"</p>

<p>"I am Umbopa, Inkosi chief of the Kukwanas."</p>

<p>"Umbopa? Impossible. Umbopa is dead."</p>

<p>"No, Gagool. You killed my father. But you could not kill me. The spirits saved me and brought me back here to defeat you."</p>

<p>"You will never defeat me. No one will defeat me. Kill him. Now!"</p>

<p>Before Gagool's men could kill Umbopa, Infadoos and the other outlaws appeared behind them. Gagool's men were taken by surprise and were killed or taken prisoner almost immediately. Two of Umbopa's warriors held Gagool, and another was about to drive an assegai through her heart.</p>

<p>"Stop! Don't kill her!"</p>

<p>"Gagool, you deserve to die because you have murdered the Inkosi chief of our people."</p>

<p>"Then kill me. I am not afraid of death."</p>

<p>"No. A murder is not resolved by another murder. We will go back to our homeland. And we will both face the justice of the Kukwana people. They will decide who they want to be Inkosi chief. Me or you? Take her away. I will join you later."</p>

<p>"Yes, yes."</p>

<p>"Sir Henry, you have been into Kwa Suleiman. What did you find there?"</p>

<p>"We found the body of my brother."</p>

<p>"I am sorry. And where is Captain Good?"</p>

<p>"He was killed by a rock fall."</p>

<p>"You are a noble and a brave man, Sir Henry. You did what you had to do."</p>

<p>"Did I? I don't know. I want to leave this place immediately. We must return to Durban."</p>

<p>"And how do you propose to do that, Sir Henry? You forget that we have no provisions and no one to guide us."</p>

<p>"I told you that only a fool would try to find King Solomon's Mines. But there must be some way we can get back. If you can think of a way we can do that, then please tell me. Because I have no idea how we will do it."</p>

<p>"We will help you to return to Durban. I will send five men with you. They will make sure you have food to eat. And they will protect you until you reach Sitanda's Kraal."</p>

<p>We made our way slowly and wearily back to Durban. And we were almost dead with exhaustion when we finally reached my house. Sir Henry rested for a few days and returned home to England. When he left, I decided to write this account of what happened. I have seen the legendary King Solomon's Mines. My son's future as a doctor is secure. But my health is not good, and I don't expect to live much longer. Let this serve as a warning to anyone else who tries to find King Solomon's Mines. You may not live to tell the tale.</p>

  <div class="chapter-title">The Cave</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">"Hello, Jata."</p>

<p>"Runo, hello. Come in."</p>

<p>"Is Wanjiro here?"</p>

<p>"Yes. Follow me."</p>

<p>"Wanjiro."</p>

<p>"Runo, this is an unexpected pleasure. Come in and sit down. I will bring some water."</p>

<p>"Thank you."</p>

<p>"This must be very difficult for you, Chief. To live here. Like this."</p>

<p>"Well, all right. Tell me your news."</p>

<p>"Murithi has been elected, Chief."</p>

<p>"Murithi? Hmm. Interesting. But Jumbo is the one with the power, no doubt."</p>

<p>"Runo. I have to ask you something. Did you believe Murithi's story that I secretly agreed to sell land to the white men? Do you believe that I would do that? After all the years you've known me. Do you believe me or do you believe Murithi?"</p>

<p>"No, Chief."</p>

<p>"But why would he..."</p>

<p>"You can't see why he would lie?"</p>

<p>"No."</p>

<p>"Neither can I."</p>

<p>"Goodness. So many visitors today. Oh. Hi."</p>

<p>"We're here to see the chief."</p>

<p>"Oh, I'll take them. Follow me, please."</p>

<p>"Of course."</p>

<p>"Thank you."</p>

<p>"Wanjiro, the men from the Aid Development are here."</p>

<p>"Mike, hello. So good to see you."</p>

<p>"Hello, Wanjiro. This is Runo, one of my oldest friends."</p>

<p>"Pleased to meet you, Runo."</p>

<p>"This is my colleague, Jim Ray."</p>

<p>"Hi."</p>

<p>"Wanjiro, I have something important to tell you. Can we speak privately for a moment?"</p>

<p>"Mike, anything you want to say, you can say in front of Runo."</p>

<p>"Okay. I have some news for you. I saw Jumbo this morning — here in Malindi."</p>

<p>"Really? What was he doing?"</p>

<p>"He was talking to a couple of South Africans. They're criminals, lawbreakers — a couple of poachers who are well known to the police. They make their money selling elephant tusks on the black market."</p>

<p>"This is unbelievable."</p>

<p>"Of course. Now it all makes sense. This is why Jumbo was against the nature reserve. If we have a nature reserve, the land will be patrolled by wardens, and poachers will have a difficult time."</p>

<p>"We have no proof of this, of course."</p>

<p>"No, that's right."</p>

<p>"But this is going to affect us too. So, we're planning to keep an eye on Jumbo for the next few days."</p>

<p>"Yes, please do that."</p>

<p>"I will help you."</p>

<p>"Thank you, Runo."</p>

<p>"It is the least I can do for an old friend."</p>

<p>"It's good of you to help us, Runo. We've invested a lot of money in this project. If the people in the village tell us to leave, it will all be wasted."</p>

<p>"Most people are thankful for the work you have done."</p>

<p>"I'm glad to hear it."</p>

<p>"Okay. You want us to drop you away from the village, right?"</p>

<p>"Yes. I prefer to walk the last few miles by myself."</p>

<p>"Okay."</p>

<p>"Where are you going, Mike? You took a wrong turning there."</p>

<p>"Did I? Is this the wrong way, Runo?"</p>

<p>"Well, it is also possible to get to the village down this road."</p>

<p>"So, what's down here?"</p>

<p>"It leads to the Nakuro cave — a very sacred place. No one except the chief and the medicine man are allowed to visit it."</p>

<p>"I see."</p>

<p>"Boy, this is a rough road. What's that light up ahead?"</p>

<p>"But that is where the cave is. But there should be no light there."</p>

<p>"Maybe we should take a look."</p>

<p>"Let's go and see what's going on."</p>

<p>"We are here. The Nakuro cave."</p>

<p>"Well, there's no one here now. But there was someone here a while ago. There's a fire burning. Let's take a look around."</p>

<p>"Look at this."</p>

<p>"What is it?"</p>

<p>"It's a lion's head mask. And this thing next to it looks like an old rug. Phew. What a smell. This is the coat of a lion."</p>

<p>"Are you sure? Lion coats aren't this colour. This is creamy white."</p>

<p>"Wow. Of course. The white lion."</p>

<p>"And look at this. A huge tape recorder."</p>

<p>"Jesus. Did you hear that?"</p>

<p>"A white lion's coat. And a tape recording of a roaring lion. I think we should take these things and show them to the people in the village. Come on."</p>

  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">"People, welcome to my first meeting as your new chief. I want to tell you my plans for the future of the village."</p>

<p>"Hey, sir. What is that noise?"</p>

<p>"Who is there?"</p>

<p>"The white men are here in the Land Rover."</p>

<p>"The white men? How dare they? And Runo is with them."</p>

<p>"Oh, where are those?"</p>

<p>"They're coming here to the meeting."</p>

<p>"Don't let them in."</p>

<p>"Jumbo. Please don't say that. We must let them in."</p>

<p>"Should I open the door and let them in?"</p>

<p>"Yes."</p>

<p>"Yes."</p>

<p>"Let them in."</p>

<p>"Greetings, Chief Murithi and medicine man Jumbo."</p>

<p>"What do you want?"</p>

<p>"We have come to pay our respects to the new chief."</p>

<p>"Well, you can do this and then you can leave."</p>

<p>"Okay. We'll do that. But first, we want to show you some things that we found at the Nakuro cave."</p>

<p>"Mike!"</p>

<p>"No."</p>

<p>"You have been to the Nakuro cave?"</p>

<p>"Yes. We have."</p>

<p>"Runo, you had no right to go there. The Nakuro cave is a sacred place for our people. If you took them there, you will be punished."</p>

<p>"Well, before you decide on Runo's punishment, take a look at this lion's head mask, Mike."</p>

<p>"And this lion's coat, which has been painted white."</p>

<p>"And we also found this tape recorder. Let's listen to the tape, shall we?"</p>

<p>"What? What are you doing? What are you doing?"</p>

<p>"Sorry."</p>

<p>"You see, the white men were responsible for the white lion. They tried to trick us."</p>

<p>"No, Jumbo. You and Murithi are the guilty ones. You and your South African friends, who have been killing elephants and stealing their tusks for ivory. You are the ones who killed Chege and sent Wanjiro into exile for crimes he did not commit."</p>

<p>"It was Jumbo. He killed Chege."</p>

<p>"What are you doing? Fire! Fire! Runo is trying to escape."</p>

<p>"Stop here."</p>

<p>"Don't worry. He won't get far. The police are here. Stop! You are under arrest! Stop! Ahh! Oh, my God! Oh, my God!"</p>

<p>"Wanjiro is back."</p>

<p>"Welcome back, Wanjiro."</p>

<p>"Welcome, Jata."</p>

<p>"Thank you, Runo. It is good to be back."</p>

<p>"Yes. Home again. It is a good feeling."</p>

<p>"And Jumbo and Murithi — what happened to them?"</p>

<p>"Murithi was arrested. You will face trial for murder."</p>

<p>"And Jumbo?"</p>

<p>"He tried to escape from the police, and the police officer fired at him and shot him in the shoulder. And then he ran into the forest."</p>

<p>"I see. Did the police find him?"</p>

<p>"No. Mumbi found him the next morning. Near the well. He was dead."</p>

<p>"Dead?"</p>

<p>"Yes. Mumbi said it looked as if he had been attacked by a lion. A real lion, this time."</p>

## Пролог (Свержение Умбупы)

* Кто такой Умбупа (Umbopa) и кто захватил власть над племенем кукуана (Kukwana)?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Умбупа — сын законного вождя Имоту (Imotu). Власть захватила злая колдунья Гагул (Gagul) вместе с дядей Умбупы Квази (Quasi).
  </details>

* Куда отвели Умбупу, чтобы казнить?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  На священную гору Кумбабве (Kumbabwe).
  </details>

## Глава 1 (Встреча с Квотермейном)

* Кто такие сэр Генри Куртис (Sir Henry Curtis) и капитан Томас Гудвин (Captain Thomas Good)?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Сэр Генри — брат Джорджа Невилла (Куртиса), пропавшего в Африке. Капитан Гудвин — его друг, муж сестры Джорджа.
  </details>

* Куда отправился Джордж Невилл и что он искал?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Он отправился искать Копи царя Соломона (King Solomon's Mines).
  </details>

* Кто такой Жозе Сильвестр (Jose Silvestre) и что он нашёл?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Португальский искатель приключений, который нашёл карту к копям и несколько алмазов, но умер от жажды и ран.
  </details>

## Глава 2 (Путь в пустыню)

* Какое условие поставил Алан Квотермейн (Allan Quatermain) перед тем, как согласиться сопровождать сэра Генри?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Чтобы сэр Генри обеспечил обучение его сына-медика в Лондоне в случае его гибели.
  </details>

* Как звали слуг, нанятых сэром Генри, и что заметил Квотермейн на спине одного из них?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Их звали Кива (Kiva) и Умбупа (Umbopa). На спине Умбупы Квотермейн заметил странные шрамы (marks).
  </details>

* Как погиб Кива?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Капитан Гудвин ранил слона, и разъярённый слон растоптал Киву.
  </details>

## Глава 3 (Копи Соломона)

* Кого изгнанные воины кукуана признали своим истинным вождём?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Они признали Умбупу по шрамам на спине и назвали его «Инкоси» (Inkosi).
  </details>

* Что случилось с капитаном Гудвином в пещере?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  На него упала скала, и он погиб.
  </details>

* Кого Квотермейн и сэр Генри нашли в пещере?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Скелет брата сэра Генри, Джорджа Куртиса.
  </details>

## Глава 4 (Битва и возвращение)

* Кто помог Квотермейну и сэру Генри выбраться из пещеры?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Умбупа и его воины, которые победили людей Гагул.
  </details>

* Как Умбупа поступил с Гагул?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Он не убил её, а велел отдать под суд племени.
  </details>

## Часть 2 (Белый лев)

* Кто такой Ванджиро (Wanjiro) и какая у него была идея?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Он вождь племени. Он хотел создать природный заповедник (nature reserve) с помощью организации помощи развитию.
  </details>

* Кто такой Чумбо (Jumbo) и почему он был против заповедника?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Он колдун (medicine man). Он был против, потому что хотел, чтобы браконьеры (poachers) продолжали убивать слонов и продавать слоновую кость.
  </details>

* Как Чумбо и его сообщник Мурити (Murithi) создали миф о «белом льве»?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Они использовали шкуру льва, покрашенную в белый цвет, и магнитофон с записью рыка, чтобы пугать людей и свалить вину на вождя.
  </details>

* Кто разоблачил обман Чумбо?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Руно (Runo) и сотрудники организации помощи развитию (Mike и Jim).
  </details>

* Как погиб Чумбо в конце истории?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Его убил настоящий лев (a real lion).
  </details>
