  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter One</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Arrival</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">1989. Smallville, Kansas, USA.</p>

<p>Suddenly the sky was dark. There were hundreds of meteors in the sky. They went faster and faster. There was a very small spaceship in the centre of the meteors. There was one person in this spaceship. A child.</p>

<p>Nothing happened in Smallville. It was a small town in Kansas. And it was a very boring place. Then one day everything changed.</p>

<p>Lionel Luthor arrived from Metropolis in his helicopter. He was a rich man. But he was not a nice man. He loved money, not people. His young son Lex sat next to him. Lex was nine years old. He had red hair and blue eyes. His face was white. And his eyes were closed. He was very frightened.</p>

<p>"Lex!" shouted his father. "Open your eyes now!"</p>

<p>"I can't," Lex said. "I hate this helicopter."</p>

<p>"Luthors aren't frightened of anything," Lionel said. "Look out of the window now!"</p>

<p>Lex slowly opened his eyes and looked down. "I'm going to die," he thought.</p>

<p>Two men waited for the helicopter in the car park of an old factory. The men were brothers. Dale and Bill Ross.</p>

<p>"Why does Lionel Luthor want to buy our factory?" asked Dale.</p>

<p>"I don't know," said Bill. "But we need his money. He isn't going to change anything. He told us that. And I believe him."</p>

<p>Jonathan Kent and his wife Martha went into a shop on Main Street. Their faces were brown from the sun. And their jeans were old. They lived on a farm.</p>

<p>"Afternoon, Nell," Jonathan said.</p>

<p>"Hello, Jonathan," Nell said. She smiled at him. She liked Jonathan. "Hello, Martha," she said. She didn't smile at Martha. She didn't like her.</p>

<p>"I'd like some tulips," said Martha. "Red tulips, please."</p>

<p>There was a little girl in the shop. She was very pretty with dark hair. She was three years old.</p>

<p>"Hello, Lana," said Martha. "Where are your mum and dad today?"</p>

<p>"They're at the homecoming game," Nell said. "Here are your tulips." She gave them to Jonathan.</p>

<p>In the street, Martha said sadly, "Oh, Jonathan. I looked at Lana's little face. And I..." She stopped.</p>

<p>"I know, Martha," said Jonathan. "We both want a child."</p>

<p>The meteors came closer and closer.</p>

<p>At the factory, Lex was bored. He walked away into a cornfield next to the factory. Lionel Luthor read the papers. "Where do I put my name?" he asked the Ross brothers. He smiled. The factory was his now.</p>

<p>Alone in the field, Lex suddenly heard something. "Help me!" What was it? Then he heard it again. "Help me!" He walked through the corn. And suddenly — there was a scarecrow. Lex was frightened. "That scarecrow can talk!" he thought. But then it wasn't a scarecrow. It was a teenage boy. The boy was on a big wooden pole. He had no shirt. There was a big red S on the front of his body. "Help me! Please!" he said one more time.</p>

<p>Boom! A big meteor crashed into the cornfield. Lex ran.</p>

<p>A car stopped opposite Nell's shop on Main Street. Nell took little Lana in her arms and walked out of the shop. Lewis and Laura Lang were in the car. Lana saw them. "Mummy! Daddy!" she shouted. Nell looked up — and saw a big meteor in the sky.</p>

<p>Doom! The meteor crashed into the Langs' car. Lana's parents were dead.</p>

<p>Lionel Luthor ran across the cornfield. "Lex, where are you?" he shouted. He saw some red hair on the ground. Then he found his son. Lex was on the ground. He was very frightened. But where was his beautiful red hair? He was nine years old. And he now had no hair at all.</p>

<p>Jonathan and Martha Kent were on their way home. Suddenly, a meteor hit the road behind them. "What was that, Jonathan?" asked Martha. "What's happening?"</p>

<p>Boom! A spaceship crashed into the road in front of them. Jonathan tried to stop the car. But it crashed into a cornfield. When they opened their eyes, they saw a boy. He was about three years old. And he had no clothes. The boy pulled Jonathan's car door. It opened. Then he pulled Martha's car door. It opened too. Martha and Jonathan were very surprised. They were soon out of the car. Martha took the boy in her arms.</p>

<p>"Are you okay?" asked Jonathan.</p>

<p>"Yes, I'm fine. You?" said Martha.</p>

<p>"But — yes — children don't fall from the sky!" Jonathan said. "Then, where did he come from?" Martha asked. "I don't think his mum and dad are from Kansas."</p>

<p>"We can't keep him, Martha," said Jonathan. "What can we say to people? We found him in a cornfield?"</p>

<p>"We didn't find him, Jonathan," said Martha. "He found us."</p>

  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Two</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Special and Different</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Jonathan and Martha took the boy home to their farm. They named him Clark. They didn't tell him about his arrival. They didn't tell other people either. If someone asked, they said, "He's Martha's sister's son. She died."</p>

<p>They didn't want Clark to be different from other children. But he was different. He was special. He was very strong. He was very fast. Nothing hurt him. When he was eight, a big milk can hit his head. It didn't hurt him.</p>

<p>2001. Smallville, Kansas, USA.</p>

<p>Clark sat at his computer. Every morning he used the internet. He wanted to find stories about special people. Very strong people. Very fast people.</p>

<p>"Clark Kent!" Martha called. "You're going to be late for the bus!"</p>

<p>"Okay, I'm coming!" shouted Clark. He put on his school clothes and shoes. Cleaned his room and put his school books in his bag. All in one minute.</p>

<p>Martha came into his room with some clothes. "You can wear these to the homecoming dance this weekend," she said.</p>

<p>"I'm not going, Mum," said Clark. "I haven't got a girl to go with."</p>

<p>"Did you ask anyone?" Martha asked.</p>

<p>"No," he said. He ran down to the kitchen.</p>

<p>"What's that letter, son?" asked Jonathan.</p>

<p>"It's for the football team," said Clark. "They want some new players."</p>

<p>"You can't do it," said Jonathan.</p>

<p>"Oh, please, Dad," said Clark. "I can run slowly. I can be careful."</p>

<p>"I'm sorry, Clark," said Jonathan. "But the answer is no."</p>

<p>"I don't want to be different!" shouted Clark. "I can never do normal things. People at school think I'm a loser!" He ran out of the door.</p>

<p>"We must tell him about the spaceship," Martha said to Jonathan.</p>

<p>Clark walked to the bus stop. He was late again. The school bus drove off without him. "Oh no," he said. "Everything's going wrong today." Suddenly, he smiled a big smile. He turned and ran through the field. At a hundred kilometres an hour.</p>

<p>The bus arrived at school. Clark's friends Pete Ross and Chloe Sullivan were on it. Chloe was new in Smallville. Her dad worked at LuthorCorp. She wrote for the school newspaper. And took photos for it. She was always ready with her camera.</p>

<p>"Are you going to the dance with anyone?" Pete asked Chloe.</p>

<p>"No, not yet," said Chloe.</p>

<p>"Well, if Clark doesn't ask you..."</p>

<p>"I'm not interested in Clark, Pete," she said.</p>

<p>"Right," said Pete. He knew that wasn't true. "Well, we can go to the dance together if you want," he said. "Not boy and girl. Friend and friend."</p>

<p>Suddenly Clark was there. "Hi, you two," he said. Pete and Chloe were very surprised to see their friend.</p>

<p>"We saw you at your farm," Chloe said. "You weren't on the bus."</p>

<p>"I came a different way," said Clark.</p>

<p>"Are you ready for the football team, Clark?" asked Pete.</p>

<p>"I'm not going to do it," said Clark.</p>

<p>"But, Clark!" said Pete. "Do you want to be the scarecrow?"</p>

<p>"The scarecrow?" Chloe said loudly. "What's the scarecrow?"</p>

<p>"Shh," said Pete. "This happens here every year, Chloe. Before the homecoming game, the football players choose someone — a loser. They take him to the cornfield and take off all his clothes. Then they write a big red S on the front of his body. And they put him on a big wooden pole," said Clark.</p>

<p>"So he's a scarecrow! How terrible!" said Chloe.</p>

<p>Then Clark saw a beautiful girl with long brown hair and a lovely smile. "See you later," he said to his friends. Clark walked across to Lana Lang. He was a metre from her. Suddenly he crashed to the ground. His books went everywhere. "Why does this always happen near Lana?" he thought. "Is it because I like her?"</p>

<p>"Hi, Clark. Are you okay?" she asked.</p>

<p>"Hi, Lana," he said. "Yeah, I'm fine." She gave his books to him. "You're reading Nietzsche, huh? Are you man or Superman?"</p>

<p>"I don't know," Clark said. They smiled.</p>

<p>"Lana!" shouted Whitney Fordman. A tall, good-looking student. He was Lana's boyfriend. Whitney was fantastic at sports. He was in all the school teams. He ran up and kissed Lana. They walked away together.</p>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Three</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">The Green Necklace</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">A student looked at the photos of the school sports teams. He looked at the 1989 photo.</p>

<p>"You're late for your class, young man!" said a teacher behind him. The student turned.</p>

<p>"What's your name?" asked the teacher. "Are you new?"</p>

<p>Suddenly the student hit the teacher in the face. A big blue electric shock went from the student's hand into the teacher's body. The teacher crashed into the wall. The student took the photo of the three happy football players and left the school. "I'm gonna get you," he thought.</p>

<p>Lex Luthor drove his Porsche through the country to the LuthorCorp factory in Smallville. "The Smallville factory doesn't make any money," Lionel Luthor said to his son. "Go to Smallville. Make changes." So Lex left Metropolis. And here he was. In the centre of nowhere. Great.</p>

<p>Lex arrived at LuthorCorp. He was 21 years old. And he still had no hair. He walked into the factory. A man sat at a desk with his lunch. "Mr Luthor!" the man said, surprised. "My name's Gabe Sullivan."</p>

<p>"Call me Lex."</p>

<p>"The workers aren't happy, Lex," said Gabe. "They're frightened about their jobs."</p>

<p>"It's okay," said Lex. "I want everyone to stay."</p>

<p>"But your father said..."</p>

<p>"I'm not my father," said Lex Luthor.</p>

<p>Later, Lex drove down a small country road at a hundred kilometres an hour. His car radio played loud music.</p>

<p>Not far away, Clark was on a bridge. He looked into the water. He thought about his life. And he wasn't happy. He didn't hear Lex Luthor's car. Lex answered his mobile phone. He didn't look at the road for a minute. He drove onto the bridge. Then he saw Clark. He tried to stop the car. But Clark was right in front of him. "I'm going to hit him," Lex thought. Their eyes met. Lex's car crashed into Clark.</p>

<p>Boom! The car and Clark went over the bridge and into the water. Clark's eyes closed. And everything went black.</p>

<p>Under the water, Clark opened his eyes. He was okay. He wasn't hurt. Then he remembered the car. The car! He found the car with Lex in it. Lex's eyes were closed. Clark pulled open the door. And pulled Lex out. He took him in his arms and started to swim.</p>

<p>When Clark put Lex on the ground, he thought he was dead. Clark put his left hand on Lex's body. Then he put his right hand over his left. "One, two, one, two. One, two. Come on, don't die," he said. Some water came out of Lex's mouth. He opened his eyes and saw Clark. He was very surprised. "But I hit you!" he said.</p>

<p>"Well, I'm not dead," said Clark. Clark looked at the bridge. "A car hit me at a hundred kilometres an hour. I'm not hurt. I am not normal," he thought.</p>

<p>The police and the doctor arrived. Then Jonathan Kent arrived too. Jonathan put his arms around Clark. "Are you okay, son?" he asked.</p>

<p>"I'm fine, Dad," said Clark.</p>

<p>"I'm Jonathan Kent. This is my son," Jonathan told a policeman. "Who was the stupid driver of the car?"</p>

<p>"I was," said Lex Luthor. "You have a very special son, Mr Kent," he said. "You saved my life, Clark. How can I thank you?"</p>

<p>"Drive more slowly," said Jonathan.</p>

<p>After dinner that night, Clark went up to his special room in the barn. He looked at the night sky. Then he looked over at the farmhouse next to the Kents' farm. The front door opened. And Lana Lang came out. Clark smiled. But then Whitney came out. Whitney put his arms around Lana. "Are you alone tonight?" he asked.</p>

<p>"Yes, I am now," said Lana.</p>

<p>"Did you hear about Clark?" she said. "He saved Lex's life today."</p>

<p>"Clark?" Whitney said. "Clark Kent?"</p>

<p>"Yes," said Lana. "Sometimes people can surprise you."</p>

<p>Whitney didn't want to talk about Clark. He wanted to talk about Whitney. "I've got a game on Saturday," he said. "Some important people are going to be there."</p>

<p>"That's great," Lana said. Whitney wanted to be a top football player. It was his dream. She gave her green necklace to him. The necklace was part of a meteor. A special meteor. This meteor hit Lana's parents' car.</p>

<p>"I can't take this, Lana," Whitney said. "It means everything to you."</p>

<p>"Keep it just for the game," she said. He put it in his jacket. Then he kissed her.</p>

  <div class="chapter-title">A Quick Kiss</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Tony Carrozza had a job at a car place. It was evening. It was almost time to go home. A boy came into the shop.</p>

<p>"I'm sorry," said Tony. "We're closed."</p>

<p>The boy said nothing. Tony looked at him carefully. "I know you," he said. "You were that scarecrow years ago. What happened to you? Why aren't you older?" The boy still said nothing.</p>

<p>"Answer me, stupid! I'm talking to you!" said Tony.</p>

<p>The boy suddenly hit Tony in the face. A big blue electric shock went from his hand into Tony Carrozza's body. Tony crashed onto the floor. He didn't move. On the wall there was a photo of three happy football players. The boy smiled. And left the shop.</p>

<p>It was the day after the car crash. When Clark walked into school, every student said, "Nice work, Clark."</p>

<p>"Am I at the right school?" Clark asked Pete and Chloe.</p>

<p>"You saved Lex Luthor's life," said Chloe. "This is going in the school newspaper."</p>

<p>"My dad hates the Luthors," said Pete.</p>

<p>"Yes, but Lionel Luthor bought your dad's factory. Not Lex," said Clark.</p>

<p>When Clark arrived home on the farm that afternoon, he found a new red car in front of the house. There was a letter on the car. Clark opened it. "Thank you, Clark," he read. "I hope you like red." Clark looked at the car. He was very excited.</p>

<p>"You can't keep it, Clark," Jonathan said.</p>

<p>"Why not?"</p>

<p>"Because the Luthors aren't good people. They're rich thanks to Smallville. They bought our friends' houses and their farms."</p>

<p>"Lex's father did all that," said Clark. "Not Lex."</p>

<p>"I'm sorry, Clark," Jonathan said.</p>

<p>"I know you're angry," said Clark.</p>

<p>"Angry!" shouted Clark. "You have no idea!" He ran up to his room in the barn.</p>

<p>"You must tell him now," Martha said.</p>

<p>Jonathan went to Clark's room. He put his arms around his son. "I'm going to tell you the story of the day of the meteors," he said. He gave Clark a picture. There were lots of letters and numbers on it. Was it a language?</p>

<p>"I think this is from your mum and dad," he said.</p>

<p>"What does it say?" Clark asked.</p>

<p>"I have no idea," said Jonathan. "They came from a different world."</p>

<p>"A different world!" laughed Clark. "So I arrived in a spaceship? And that's stupid!"</p>

<p>"Do you want to see that spaceship?" asked Jonathan. "It's under the barn."</p>

<p>When he saw the little spaceship, Clark was very angry. "Why didn't you tell me this before?" he shouted. He ran out of the barn.</p>

<p>Lana Lang looked at the words.</p>

<p>Lewis Lang, 1957-1989</p>
<p>Laura Lang, 1959-1989</p>

<p>She heard something behind her. "Who's there?" she called.</p>

<p>"It's me. It's Clark," he said.</p>

<p>"What are you doing here?" Lana looked at Clark. He was nice.</p>

<p>"Please don't tell anyone," she said. "I come here to talk to my mum and dad. Is that stupid?"</p>

<p>"No, it isn't stupid," Clark said. "Do you remember them?"</p>

<p>"Not very well," said Lana. "They died when I was three."</p>

<p>"Do you want to meet them?" She sat on the ground. "Mum, Dad, this is Clark Kent," she said.</p>

<p>"Hi, Mr and Mrs Lang," said Clark. "I'm sorry."</p>

<p>"What's that?" He sat down and listened. "Your mum says you're never alone, Lana," he said. "And your dad says you're beautiful."</p>

<p>Lana smiled. "Thank you, Clark," she said. "Can I walk home with you?"</p>

<p>"Yes, thanks," said Lana.</p>

<p>When they arrived at Lana's house, she said, "We talked for a long time tonight, Clark. It was nice." She kissed him quickly. "Goodnight, Clark." She ran into the house. Clark was very happy. But there was someone at Lana's house. He saw the kiss. And he was very angry. It was Whitney Fordman.</p>

<p>The next day before school, Clark drove the red car back to Lex Luthor's house. The house was big and old. The front door was open. Clark walked in. "Hello!" he shouted. "Is anyone at home?"</p>

<p>"Clark!" said Lex. "What are you doing here? How's your new car?"</p>

<p>"I love it," said Clark. "But I can't keep it."</p>

<p>"Why not? You saved my life, Clark. I want to thank you," Lex said. Then he said, "Oh, I get it. Your dad doesn't like me."</p>

<p>Clark didn't say anything.</p>

<p>"It's okay," said Lex. "I understand."</p>

<p>"It's not you," said Clark. "He doesn't like your father. Thank you for the car, Lex. I'm sorry I can't keep it."</p>

<p>"I changed after that crash," said Lex. "When I was under the water, I saw my life in Smallville in a new way. I can see now. This isn't the end of my old life in Metropolis. It's the start of my new life here. So thank you, Clark. I hope we can be friends for a long time."</p>

  <div class="chapter-title">This Year's Scarecrow</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">When school finished that afternoon, Chloe and Pete went into town. There were people and police cars in front of one of the shops. And a man was on the ground.</p>

<p>"What happened here?" asked Chloe.</p>

<p>"It's Freddy Post. He had a big electric shock," said a man.</p>

<p>Chloe turned to Pete. "He's the third one this week," she said. "And here's something interesting. They were all in the school football team in 1989."</p>

<p>Pete looked at the people by the body. He saw a teenage boy. "Who's that boy?" he asked.</p>

<p>"I don't know," said Chloe. She quickly took a photo of him.</p>

<p>The two friends walked back to the school and went to the newspaper room. "I'm going to put this photo into the computer," she said. "Can you find the 1989 year book, Pete?"</p>

<p>A few minutes later, Pete sat next to Chloe with the book. "Chloe, look," he said. "This boy, twelve years ago. His name's Jeremy Creek."</p>

<p>Chloe looked at the book and then at her photo. "It's the same person," she said. "And he's the same age. That's interesting."</p>

<p>Chloe and Pete used the internet and wrote emails. They found old newspapers.</p>

<p>"I'm going to find Clark," said Pete. "We must tell him about this."</p>

<p>Pete came back with Clark. Clark looked at the two photos of Jeremy Creek.</p>

<p>"Jeremy had a big electric shock twelve years ago," said Chloe. "He was in hospital for twelve years. A few days ago, he woke up and ran away."</p>

<p>"So, why is he back in Smallville?" asked Clark. "Why is he giving electric shocks to people?"</p>

<p>"Because twelve years ago," Pete said, "they put him on a pole in a field. He was that year's scarecrow."</p>

<p>"The meteor crashed into Smallville that day," said Chloe.</p>

<p>"Oh no," thought Clark. "All this happened because of me. That was the day of my arrival."</p>

<p>He looked at a photo in one of the old newspapers. It was a little girl. She had a terrible look on her face. It said under the photo: "Meteor hits Mum and Dad."</p>

<p>"So, what do you think, Clark?" asked Chloe.</p>

<p>Clark knew that little face in the photo. "Oh no, Lana!"</p>

<p>"Why didn't you tell me about this before?" he shouted. "I did this!" He ran from the room.</p>

<p>In front of the school, he met Whitney. "Hi, Clark," said Whitney. "I'm looking for you. You're this year's scarecrow."</p>

<p>"Leave me alone, Whitney," said Clark angrily. "Or I'm going to..."</p>

<p>"You're going to what?" said Whitney. "Come on, Clark, hit me." He took off his jacket. Clark moved nearer to Whitney. But he suddenly started to fall. He saw Lana's green necklace under Whitney's open T-shirt.</p>

<p>"What's between you and Lana?" shouted Whitney. "You can have her necklace." He took off the necklace and put it on Clark. "But you can't have Lana. I'm taking her to the dance tonight."</p>

<p>Clark didn't feel well. He tried to move. And then everything went black. Whitney and two of his friends put him in a car. Jeremy Creek watched everything. And he remembered this day twelve years ago.</p>

  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Clark woke up. He was in a cornfield on a pole. He was very cold. It was night and it was October. He looked down. There was a big red S on the front of his body. He tried to move his arms. "I can't move. What's wrong with me?" he thought. "It's the necklace."</p>

<p>Suddenly there was a boy in front of him.</p>

<p>"It hurts," said the boy. "I remember that."</p>

<p>"You're Jeremy Creek," said Clark.</p>

<p>"No, Jeremy Creek died twelve years ago," said the boy. He started to walk away.</p>

<p>"Where are you going?" asked Clark.</p>

<p>"To the school dance," said Jeremy. "I didn't go in 1989."</p>

<p>"Please help me," said Clark.</p>

<p>"No," Jeremy said. "You stay here. I don't want to hurt you." Jeremy walked down the road.</p>

<p>Lex Luthor drove by and saw him. "I know that boy," he thought. "But how? From where?" He stopped the car. Then he remembered the field twelve years ago. The boy on the pole. The big red S. He got out of the car and went into the field.</p>

<p>"Help me! Help me, please!" he heard.</p>

<p>"It's not possible," Lex thought. Then he saw him. "Clark!" he shouted.</p>

<p>Lex helped Clark. Clark pulled off the necklace.</p>

<p>"You need a doctor, Clark," Lex said. Without the necklace, Clark was suddenly Clark again. He didn't need a doctor. "I'm okay. Thanks, Lex," he said. He found his clothes and ran into the night.</p>

<p>Lex saw the necklace on the ground. He put it in his jacket and walked back to his car.</p>

<p>At the school dance, the band played. Everyone danced. Jeremy Creek didn't dance. He was behind the school building. He opened the door of a large box. "Water," it said. "They're all going to die now," he thought.</p>

<p>Suddenly Clark was behind him. "Stop this now, Jeremy," he said.</p>

<p>"You said Jeremy. What are you doing here?"</p>

<p>"You're not going to hurt my friends," said Clark.</p>

<p>"You don't understand," said Jeremy. "I'm doing this for you and the others. No more scarecrows."</p>

<p>"No, Jeremy. Stop it!" said Clark. Jeremy hit Clark in the face. But he had a surprise. Clark wasn't hurt. There was a car next to Jeremy. He opened the door and started it. He drove at Clark. Clark put his arms in front of him. But Jeremy didn't stop. Clark was on the front of the car when it hit the water box. There was water everywhere. It gave Jeremy a big electric shock. His body was blue. Then everything was quiet.</p>

<p>Clark pulled the car door. It opened. "Are you okay?" he asked.</p>

<p>"Who are you?" asked Jeremy. "I want to go home."</p>

<p>Clark watched the end of the homecoming dance. Lana and Whitney danced in the centre of the room. Then they kissed. A long kiss. Clark was sad. He turned and left.</p>

<p>There were three cars in a line in the car park. "That's Whitney's car and his friends' cars," Clark thought. He had an idea. He took one car and put it on top of another one. Then he took the third car and put it on top of the other two. He waited. When students came out and saw the cars, they laughed. Then Whitney came out with his arm around Lana. He saw his car. "Who did this to my car?" he shouted angrily. Everyone laughed. And Lana smiled.</p>

<p>"I want to go home. I want to go home. I want to go home. I want to go home. I want to go home."</p>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Epilogue</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Thanks for the Dance</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
