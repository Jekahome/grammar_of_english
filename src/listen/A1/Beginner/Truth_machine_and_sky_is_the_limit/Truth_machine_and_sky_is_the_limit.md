<summary>Chapter One: Success for Professor Verity</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter One</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Success for Professor Verity</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">"Success!" cried Professor Verity. "My new invention is ready. It is now complete. I shall call it the Truth Machine."</p>

<p>The professor ran to the telephone and phoned the newspaper office. He told them about the Truth Machine. "Come to my laboratory," he said. "You will see my new invention. I call it the Truth Machine."</p>

<p>"Please give us your name and address," said the girl at the newspaper office. "We'll send a reporter immediately." The professor gave the girl his name and address.</p>

<p>"Will the reporter be here soon?" he asked.</p>

<p>"Yes, of course," replied the girl.</p>

<p>"Good," said Professor Verity. He smiled. He waited.</p>

<p>The reporter soon arrived at the laboratory. "Who can use the Truth Machine, Professor?" he asked.</p>

<p>"Anybody can use it," replied Professor Verity. "But how does the machine work?" asked the reporter. The professor laughed. "That's the secret," he said. "But I can tell you this. The questioner sits in the little chair, I sit at the controls, and the truth speaker sits in the big chair. The truth speaker always speaks the truth. He cannot tell lies." The professor smiled.</p>

<p>"Why did you make the Truth Machine?" asked the reporter.</p>

<p>"I love truth," said Professor Verity, "and I hate lies. I love good people, and I hate bad people. I love peace, and I hate war. The Truth Machine loves truth, good people, and peace. It hates lies, bad people, and war." The reporter wrote all this in his notebook.</p>

<p>"Soon everybody will know about the Truth Machine," said the professor. "It will be a success."</p>

<p>The reporter thanked the professor, and hurried back to his newspaper office.</p>

<p>Professor Verity was right. Soon millions of people knew about the Truth Machine. The professor was on the radio. He was on the television, too. All over the world, newspapers and magazines wrote about Professor Verity and the Truth Machine.</p>

<p>Many people wrote to Professor Verity. Rich people, poor people, young people, old people, everybody sent letters. The Truth Machine was a success. "Thank you," wrote one woman. "Wonderful," wrote another. "Good luck," wrote one man. "I am sending you some money. It will help your work." Many people sent money. Some sent coins, others sent banknotes, and others sent cheques and money orders.</p>

<p>Then the professor chose his first clients. He read his letters many times. First, he chose a hundred letters. Then he chose fifty of them. Then he chose ten names. Finally, he chose three people. "This is Mrs Seeker from Australia, Inspector Sack from South America, and Dr Simple from the United States." Professor Verity wrote to them, "Please come and see me," he wrote. "You will be one of my first clients."</p>

<summary>Chapter Two: Three Visitors for Professor Verity</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Two</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Three Visitors for Professor Verity</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Two weeks later, Professor Verity had a visitor. It was Mrs Seeker. She was from Australia. She was a housewife. She was afraid.</p>

<p>"Can I help you, Mrs Seeker?" asked the professor.</p>

<p>"Yes," Mrs Seeker replied. "I wrote to you about the Truth Machine."</p>

<p>"I know," said the professor. "You will be my first client. Don't be afraid."</p>

<p>"Oh," said Mrs Seeker. "I will not be your first client, Professor. Your first client will be my husband."</p>

<p>"Your husband?" said Professor Verity. The professor was surprised. "But your husband didn't write to me."</p>

<p>"No, he didn't," said Mrs Seeker. "I wrote to you. But what do you want? Why are you here?" asked the professor.</p>

<p>"Well," said Mrs Seeker. "My husband —" Mrs Seeker stopped. She was crying. She was unhappy. "My husband —" Mrs Seeker stopped again. She was crying again.</p>

<p>"Oh," said the professor quietly. "Your husband has another woman."</p>

<p>"Yes, yes," cried Mrs Seeker. "But I don't know. Oh, I hate lies. I want the truth, Professor. I want the truth from my husband."</p>

<p>"But will he come here?" asked the professor.</p>

<p>"Oh, yes, he'll come," said Mrs Seeker.</p>

<p>"Well, bring him to my laboratory," said the professor. "I'll help you, Mrs Seeker. Of course, there will be a small charge, a fee."</p>

<p>"I'll pay anything," said Mrs Seeker. "I hate lies. I want the truth from my husband."</p>

<p>"Come here next Monday morning," said the professor. "Everything will be ready."</p>

<p>The next visitor was Inspector Sack. He was from South America. He was a policeman, and he had a gun. "Hello, Professor!" said the inspector. The inspector had a loud voice. "Where is it, Professor?" he shouted. "Where is the Truth Machine?"</p>

<p>"It's here, Inspector," said Professor Verity quietly. "You will be one of my first clients."</p>

<p>"All good," said the inspector. Inspector Sack walked up and down. Then he stopped. He looked at the professor. "Crime!" shouted Inspector Sack. "Crime, crime, crime."</p>

<p>"Crime?" asked the professor in a soft voice.</p>

<p>"Yes, crime!" shouted Inspector Sack. "I hate criminals. Murderers are criminals. They kill people. I hate murderers. Thieves are criminals. They take things from people. I hate thieves. And liars are criminals. They tell lies. I hate lies, Professor. I hate all criminals."</p>

<p>"But the Truth Machine will fight criminals," said Professor Verity quietly. "There will be no more murderers and thieves. There will be no more lies and no more crime, Inspector."</p>

<p>"No more criminals and no more crime!" shouted Inspector Sack. "Excellent!"</p>

<p>"Bring your criminals to me, Inspector," said the professor. "Of course, there will be a small charge, a fee."</p>

<p>"Money?" shouted Inspector Sack. "You shall have money, Professor. We are fighting crime, my friend. Of course, you shall have money."</p>

<p>"Thank you, Inspector," said Professor Verity. "Thank you. Come here next Monday afternoon. Bring your criminals to me. Everything will be ready."</p>

<p>"Good morning, Professor Verity. My name is Dr Simple." Dr Simple was the third visitor. He was from the United States. He was a government official. He worked for the American government.</p>

<p>"Hello, Dr Simple," said the professor. "You will be one of my first clients."</p>

<p>"I know," said Dr Simple. "I will be your third client. The other two are Mrs Seeker and Inspector Sack."</p>

<p>"Hello," said the professor. "How do you know their names?" Dr Simple looked round. "Spies, Professor Verity," said Dr Simple quietly. "Spies."</p>

<p>"Spies?" asked Professor Verity. "But Mrs Seeker and Inspector Sack aren't spies."</p>

<p>"Of course, they are not spies," said Dr Simple. He looked round. "But I am."</p>

<p>"Oh, you are a spy," the professor asked.</p>

<p>"Yes," said Dr Simple. "I'm a spy." He looked round. He was looking for enemy spies. "I fight for peace," said Dr Simple.</p>

<p>Professor Verity smiled. "Oh, I want peace too," the professor said.</p>

<p>"I know," said Dr Simple. He smiled. "You will help me, Professor. We have caught an enemy spy. He will not tell the truth. I will bring him here. I will be the questioner. He will be the truth speaker."</p>

<p>"Yes," said the professor. "Please bring him here. Of course, Dr Simple, there will be a small charge, a fee." Dr Simple smiled. He looked round again. "Of course, Professor, here is some money." Dr Simple gave the professor a lot of money.</p>

<p>"Thank you," said the professor quietly. He looked round too. "Come here with your spy next Monday evening. Everything will be ready."</p>

  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">It was Monday morning. Mrs Seeker came to the laboratory. Mr Seeker came too.</p>

<p>"How are you, Professor?" said Mr Seeker. "Where is your Truth Machine?" Mr Seeker laughed.</p>

<p>"Don't laugh," said his wife. She was afraid. "Listen to the professor."</p>

<p>Professor Verity spoke. "Mrs Seeker, you are the questioner. Sit in the small chair. Mr Seeker, you are the truth speaker. Sit in the big chair." Mr and Mrs Seeker sat in their chairs. Mrs Seeker looked at the professor. "What shall I say?" she asked.</p>

<p>"Ask your husband some easy questions," said the professor. She looked at her husband. "What is your name?" she asked.</p>

<p>"My name is Henry," said her husband. That was an easy question. He laughed.</p>

<p>"How old are you, Henry?" asked Mrs Seeker.</p>

<p>"I'm forty-seven," said Mr Seeker. Mrs Seeker looked at Professor Verity. She smiled. "That's the truth," she said. "He usually says forty-three. My husband is telling the truth."</p>

<p>"Good," said Professor Verity. "Now ask your husband some difficult questions."</p>

<p>Mrs Seeker was afraid, but she spoke. "Are you married?" she asked.</p>

<p>"Of course I'm married," replied Mr Seeker. "You're my wife." He was angry.</p>

<p>"But do you love me?" asked Mrs Seeker. Mr Seeker looked at his wife. She waited. "No," he said slowly. "No, I don't love you. I love another woman."</p>

<p>"Oh," said Mrs Seeker. "Who do you love? Tell me the truth. I hate lies. Who do you love?" Mrs Seeker was crying.</p>

<p>Mr Seeker looked at his wife. "I love Anne Elliot," he said. "I love Anne Elliot."</p>

<p>"Oh, Anne Elliot. But she's my best friend."</p>

<p>"Now you know the truth."</p>

<p>"Yes, that's the truth. What shall I do now?"</p>

<p>Inspector Sack came to the laboratory on Monday afternoon. His boss, the chief inspector, came too. They brought some criminals with them.</p>

<p>"Good afternoon, professor!" shouted Inspector Sack. "This is my boss, the chief inspector. And these are the criminals. They are murderers. They are thieves. And they are liars."</p>

<p>"But today," said Professor Verity, "they will tell the truth."</p>

<p>"Shall we begin?" said the chief inspector quietly.</p>

<p>"Yes, let's begin. Come on!" shouted Inspector Sack.</p>

<p>Inspector Sack sat in the questioner's chair. The first criminal sat in the truth speaker's chair. Inspector Sack spoke. "Did you take money from the car of Señor Cavallo?" he asked.</p>

<p>"Yes, I took the money," said the criminal.</p>

<p>"Guilty!" shouted Inspector Sack. "Five years in prison. Next criminal."</p>

<p>The next criminal sat in the truth speaker's chair. "Did you take diamonds from the house of Señorita Ruggia?" asked Inspector Sack.</p>

<p>"Yes, I did," said the criminal.</p>

<p>"Guilty!" shouted the inspector. "Ten years in prison. Next criminal."</p>

<p>The next criminal came in. He sat in the truth speaker's chair. "Did you kill Señor Ruggia?" asked Inspector Sack. The criminal was silent. Then he spoke. "Yes, I did," he said. That was the truth.</p>

<p>"Guilty!" shouted Inspector Sack. "Twenty years. Next."</p>

<p>All the criminals sat in the truth speaker's chair. They all told the truth. One of them was innocent. "You are not guilty," said Inspector Sack. "You can go home." Inspector Sack was pleased. Professor Verity was pleased. The chief inspector was pleased.</p>

<p>"Good work, Sack," said the chief inspector. "Very good work."</p>

<p>"Thank you, sir," said Inspector Sack. "We are fighting crime, sir. The Truth Machine is helping us. There are no more criminals for us, sir." Inspector Sack smiled. He was happy.</p>

<p>"Yes, Inspector Sack. No more criminals. No more work."</p>

<p>"No more work for me, sir."</p>

<p>"Yes. We don't need you now, Inspector Sack. I haven't got a job. What shall I do now?"</p>

<p>It was Monday evening. Doctor Simple came to the laboratory. Another man came with him. The other man was a spy.</p>

<p>"Good evening, Professor," said Doctor Simple.</p>

<p>"Good evening, Doctor Simple," said the Professor. "Everything is ready."</p>

<p>"Good," said Doctor Simple. "This spy is dangerous. He is an enemy. I will be his questioner. He will be the truth speaker. He will tell the truth." The Professor went to the controls. Doctor Simple sat in the questioner's chair. The spy sat in the truth speaker's chair.</p>

<p>"What is your name?" asked Doctor Simple.</p>

<p>"My name is Harvey Lime," said the spy.</p>

<p>"Where are you from?" asked Doctor Simple.</p>

<p>"I am from Russia," said the spy. The spy smiled. Doctor Simple looked at the Professor. Doctor Simple was pleased. "Excellent, Professor Verity," said Doctor Simple. "Lime is telling the truth. He will tell me everything." Professor Verity was happy.</p>

<p>Doctor Simple looked at Lime. "Are you a spy?" he asked.</p>

<p>"Yes, I am a spy. I am an important spy," said Lime.</p>

<p>"Do you know the secret of the new military aircraft?" he asked. Harvey Lime smiled. "Yes," he said. "I know the secret of the new military aircraft. And my government knows too."</p>

<p>"Ah!" cried Doctor Simple. "The Truth Machine is wonderful. The spy is telling me everything."</p>

<p>"But I have another secret," said Lime. He looked at Doctor Simple. He looked at Professor Verity.</p>

<p>"Another secret!" cried Doctor Simple. "Tell me, tell me, tell me the truth."</p>

<p>"My secret is this," said Harvey Lime. "I never tell the truth. I always tell lies." He smiled.</p>

<p>"What do you mean?"</p>

<p>"That's the truth. I always tell lies."</p>

<p>Is Lime telling the truth? Or is he lying?</p>

<summary>Chapter One: A New Start for Max</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter One</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">A New Start for Max</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Max Price was a happy man. He loved his wife Angela and his two children, Stephen and Juliet. Max and his family lived in a small, pleasant flat. They had many good friends and kind neighbours. Yes, the Price family was a happy family.</p>

<p>But one day Max lost his job. The bad news came in a letter. The letter was short and clear. Max became very worried. "What shall I do now?" he said to his wife. "I don't know, Max. I don't know," replied Angela. She was very worried too.</p>

<p>Then Max became angry. "Look at this letter," he said. "They send me best wishes. I don't want best wishes. I want a job. I've got a wife and family."</p>

<p>"Don't worry, Max," said Angela. "Everything will be all right."</p>

<p>"But who will give me a job now, Angela? I'm thirty-seven. And today, thirty-seven is old. What are we going to do?"</p>

<p>"Let's ask our friends and neighbours," said Angela. But they got no help from their friends and neighbours. Many people were looking for jobs. Life was difficult for everybody.</p>

<p>Every morning, Max looked for a new job. Every afternoon, he came home without one. And every evening, he waited for the newspaper. "Will there be a job tonight?"</p>

<p>"Jobs, situations vacant, 12 to 14. Nothing here again. Angela, look at this. The sky's the limit. Join our new company and go to the top. Friendly people, good salaries, jobs for men and women of all ages. The Star Building, city centre. Interviews every morning at nine. Mr Lele, Personnel Manager, Friendship Services International, Star Building, city centre."</p>

<p>"Friendship Services International. It's a new company," said Max. "What do you think, Angela?"</p>

<p>"Go and see them. They need new people, men and women, and all ages, too."</p>

<p>"Yes," said Max. "The Star Building. That's the new skyscraper in the city centre. It's a big company, Angela. Very big."</p>

<p>"You can go in the morning," said Angela.</p>

<p>"I will," said Max. He was excited. "I'll be there early. I'll be there at seven o'clock. I'll get a job, Angela. Don't worry."</p>

<p>Early next morning, Max was at the Star Building. At nine o'clock, he had an interview with three men from Friendship Services International.</p>

<p>"What was your last job?" asked Mr Lele, the personnel manager.</p>

<p>"I sold furniture."</p>

<p>"We don't need furniture salesmen here," said the second man, Mr Creep.</p>

<p>"But I'll learn a new job," said Max quickly. "I'll work hard."</p>

<p>"Good," said the third man, Mr Hightower. "We need new men, and we need hard workers."</p>

<p>"Mr Price," said Max quickly.</p>

<p>"Very good, Price," said Mr Hightower. "Be here tomorrow at eight-thirty. You're now working for Friendship Services International."</p>

<p>Now Max had a job. He ran home and told Angela the good news. This was a new start for Max.</p>

<p>"I like it here, Max," said Frank one day. "We've got good jobs at Friendship Services International."</p>

<p>"Yes," said Max. "I like it here too." So the two men were very happy.</p>

<p>Every lunchtime they had drinks and sandwiches with their new friends. In the evenings they went to parties with them. Max and Frank often took their wives and children to the parties. Max was pleased. And Angela was pleased too.</p>

<p>Max now had a good salary. So he and Angela bought lots of new furniture for their flat. They bought a colour television, a hi-fi, and many other things. Angela bought some new clothes, and the children had lots of new toys. The Price family was happy again.</p>

<p>"You're rich," said Angela one day to Max.</p>

<p>Max laughed. "No, not yet, Angela," he said. "But perhaps I'll be rich one day."</p>

<p>"Let's drink to your new job," said Angela.</p>

<p>"Yes," agreed Max. "Let's drink to my new employer. Let's drink to Friendship Services International. Cheers, Angela."</p>

<p>"Cheers, Max," said Angela.</p>

They only had numbers and letters. X-3Ys, L-7Ks, A-5Qs. What did these strange numbers and letters mean? Max and Frank did not know. Each day they opened hundreds of these orders. They put the orders into groups. One group for X products, one group for L products, and one group for A products. The orders came from all over the world. Some of the orders were very big.</p>

<p>"We're working very hard," said Max to Frank one day.</p>

<p>"Yes," replied Frank. "Letters, letters, letters, hundreds of them. What do they mean, Max? What do they want? What are they asking for?"</p>

<p>"I don't know," said Max. "Don't ask so many questions, Frank."</p>

<p>"Okay," said Frank. "Okay."</p>

<p>Later that day, a memo came from their boss, Mr B. Creep.</p>

<p>"Confidential. Date July 1st. From B. Creep. To M. Price and F. Stedman. Please see me now in my office, 5th floor. Copy to M. Hightower."</p>

<p>"Please sit down, gentlemen. I have some news for you. We like your work. You'll be coming up to the 5th floor. Well done, Frank. Well done, Max."</p>

<summary>Chapter Three: The Fifth Floor</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Three</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">The Fifth Floor</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Max and Frank now worked on the fifth floor. There were two other men and one secretary in their new office. The office was quiet. Its furniture was modern and expensive. The secretary had a dictaphone and an electric typewriter. The four men had large desks. One day, Max and Frank looked down on the street below. There was a lot of noise and traffic in the street. Max and Frank remembered their friends on the ground floor.</p>

<p>"They were good friends," said Frank.</p>

<p>"Yes," said Max, "but now we've got new friends on the fifth floor."</p>

<p>"But remember the parties and the friendship on the ground floor," said Frank.</p>

<p>"Yes," said Max, "but Frank, the salaries on the fifth floor are better."</p>

<p>Max now had more money. "Let's buy a new house," he said to Angela.</p>

<p>"But I like it here," said Angela. "And the children like it here too."</p>

<p>"But Angela," said Max, "I now work on the fifth floor at Friendship Services International. My salary is bigger. Fifth floor people don't live in flats. They live in houses."</p>

<p>"But Max," said Angela. "All our friends live here. And the children go to school here. And our flat is lovely now."</p>

<p>Max became angry. "We are going to move, Angela. We are going to move next week."</p>

<p>"All right," said Angela. She smiled, but she was unhappy.</p>

<p>The next week, Max and Angela moved to a new house. But Frank Stedman and his wife did not move. "We like our flat," said Frank. "All our friends are near us. And the children like their school. No, I shall not move, Max." Max said nothing.</p>

<p>Max and Frank worked hard in their fifth floor office. They received orders to Friendship Services International and priced them. For example, there was an order for 5,000 M-5Bs. The prices were in a book. One M-5B cost $10. So 5,000 M-5Bs cost 5,000 times 10 equals $50,000. But Max and Frank still did not understand M-5B.</p>

<p>"What's an M-5B?" asked Frank.</p>

<p>"I don't know," said Max. Now their boss was Mr M. Hightower. His office was on the tenth floor of the Star Building. He came down every day at five o'clock and spoke to Max and Frank.</p>

<p>"Your work is excellent, gentlemen," said Mr Hightower one day. "Excellent."</p>

<p>"Thank you, Mr Hightower," said Max.</p>

<p>"Call me Mort, please," said Mr Hightower.</p>

<p>"Thank you, Mort. I will," said Max. But Frank said nothing.</p>

<p>One day, Mr Hightower came down early. Max and Frank were alone in their office.</p>

<p>"Anything wrong, Mort?" asked Max.</p>

<p>"Thanks, Mort. That's great news."</p>

<p>One morning, Max and Frank looked down on the city below. It was a long way. The people and the cars were very small.</p>

<p>"It's very high, Frank," said Max. "I like it up here."</p>

<p>"It's too high, Max," said Frank. "I liked it on the ground floor. There's too much work up here."</p>

<p>"But we must work," said Max. "This is Friendship Services International. It's a good company. We must work."</p>

<p>Max worked very hard. He often worked late in the office. One evening, Max came home early. Angela was pleased. "Hello, Max. You're early tonight. I'm cooking your favourite meal."</p>

<p>"I'm sorry, Angela," said Max. "I must go out again in a few minutes. I've got an important business dinner."</p>

<p>"Oh, please stay," said Angela. "I don't see you a lot these days. And the children don't see you at all."</p>

<p>"I'm sorry, Angela," said Max, "but it's an important dinner. I must go. Goodbye."</p>

<p>"Goodbye, Max. Good luck." "Oh, Max," thought Angela to herself. "You are becoming a different man. And the children don't know you now. You are a stranger in our home."</p>

<p>It was a very important business dinner. Max and Frank were there. They met many foreign customers of Friendship Services International. But Mort Hightower wasn't at the dinner.</p>

<p>"Where's Hightower tonight?" asked Frank.</p>

<p>"Mort? I don't know," said Max. "He wasn't well this morning. He left the office early."</p>

<p>Max and Frank talked to the dinner guests. They came from all over the world. They were rich. They ate and drank a lot.</p>

<p>"We like Friendship Services International," said an African guest. "Oh, yes. Your products are very good," said an Asian guest. Then Max asked a question. He spoke to an American guest.</p>

<p>"Excuse me," he said.</p>

<p>"Yes," said the American.</p>

<p>"I've read your letters to FSI," said Max. "I've seen your orders. For example, you ordered 300 S-6Ts."</p>

<p>"That's right," said the American. "300 S-6Ts. They were excellent."</p>

<p>"But what is S-6T?" asked Max. "What your orders mean? I don't understand them."</p>

<p>The American laughed. "Oh, our orders. Don't worry," he said. "You'll learn one day. But not now, Max." The other guests laughed too. Max and Frank were going to ask another question. But at that moment, there was an urgent message for Max. From FSI headquarters.</p>

<p>"Hightower dead. Move to his job tomorrow. Fifteenth floor."</p>

<p>"I've got Mort's job, Frank. I'm on the fifteenth floor."</p>

<p>"Oh, Max. Be careful."</p>

I'm rising higher in FSI. I'm rising higher in the Star Building. And I've got a beautiful secretary." It was true. Max's secretary was called Donna Lot. She was beautiful. She liked Max, and Max liked her. They did a lot of work together. Max and Donna often worked late. Sometimes they went to restaurants after work.</p>

<p>"Do you understand these letters?" Max asked Donna.</p>

<p>"Do you understand these orders?" said Max. "What is a B-5R? What is a G-9Q? Do you know?" Donna spoke slowly. "They're secret things," she said.</p>

<p>"I know," said Max. "But why are they secret?"</p>

<p>"But will he help me, Donna?"</p>

They never talked about their names. Max still did not know the names of the products. He still did not understand the strange orders. But he was afraid to ask. The papers were top secret. Max did not understand them. He was not a scientist. He showed the papers to customers in many foreign countries. The customers read the documents. Then they made decisions. For example, one African scientist said, "Thank you, Mr Price. Thank you for the papers. They are very interesting. I shall order fifteen S-3Bs and four hundred M-2Gs." So Max was very popular with the other directors.</p>

<p>"Yes, excellent," said the company's vice-chairman. "We have a surprise for you, Max. You are now the new chairman of FSI."</p>
