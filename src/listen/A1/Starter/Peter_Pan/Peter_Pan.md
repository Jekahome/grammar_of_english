<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter 1</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">The Darling Family</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Wendy, John, and Michael Darling live in a big house in London. It is a beautiful house with a lot of rooms and a garden outside. They live with their parents, Mr. and Mrs. Darling. The Darlings are a happy family. Mr. and Mrs. Darling love their children very much.</p>

<p>Wendy is the first child, and she is pretty. She has got long, blonde hair and blue eyes. John is the second child, and he is tall and thin. He has got brown hair and brown eyes, and he wears glasses. Michael is the third child, and he has got brown hair and blue eyes.</p>

<p>The three children are very friendly. Wendy, John, and Michael like playing in the nursery. It is a big, sunny nursery, and there are pictures on the walls. There are a lot of toys in the nursery.</p>

<p>The children have a nanny called Nana. She is a big Newfoundland dog. She is a wonderful nanny, and looks after the children. She loves them, and they love her. Her kennel is in the nursery.</p>

<p>One evening, Mr. and Mrs. Darling put on some very nice clothes. They are going to a dinner party with their friends. Mrs. Darling goes to the nursery. The children are playing happily.</p>

<p>Wendy sees her mother and says, "What a lovely dress."</p>

<p>"Yes," says John. "You are wearing a beautiful dress."</p>

<p>"Thank you," says Mrs. Darling. "Your father and I are going to a dinner party this evening." Then she looks at Nana and says, "Nana, it is almost half past eight. It is time to put the children to bed."</p>

<p>Nana looks at Mrs. Darling with her big brown eyes and barks. She goes to the bathroom and turns on the hot water for Michael's bath.</p>

<p>But Michael does not like having baths. "No," says little Michael. "I don't want to have a bath, and I don't want to go to bed."</p>

<p>Nana is a good nanny, and the children must listen to her. Michael has his bath. Then she gives the children their pyjamas, and they are ready for bed now.</p>

<p>Mrs. Darling comes to the nursery again and smiles. "Good work, Nana. The children are ready for bed." Nana looks at Mrs. Darling and barks. Then she goes and sits near her kennel.</p>

<p>"I can hear a noise outside the window," says Wendy.</p>

<p>"Yes," says John. "I can hear it too."</p>

<p>Mrs. Darling goes to the nursery window and is very surprised. She sees a young boy outside the nursery window. Nana barks loudly and shuts the window quickly. The boy's shadow falls on the floor, and the young boy flies away.</p>

<p>"Who's there?" asks Mrs. Darling. She opens the window and looks outside, but she can't see anything. "How strange. There's no one here."</p>

<p>She turns around and sees the boy's shadow on the floor. Wendy, John and Michael see it too.</p>

<p>"What's that?" they ask, looking at the shadow on the floor.</p>

<p>"It's the poor boy's shadow," says Mrs. Darling.</p>

<p>"What can we do with it?" asks Wendy.</p>

<p>"Let's put it in the drawer," says Mrs. Darling.</p>

<p>The children get into bed. Mr. Darling takes Nana to the garden. Then he goes to the sitting room and waits for Mrs. Darling. Mrs. Darling sings to Wendy, John and Michael and kisses them. She is a wonderful mother. Soon the three children are sleeping.</p>

<p>Mr. and Mrs. Darling go to their bedroom. They put on their coats and go to the dinner party.</p>

<div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ * ~ </span></div>

<p>London. The story you are reading starts in London in the early 1900s. London is a wonderful city and it is the capital of the United Kingdom. Today almost eight million people live there.</p>

<p>London is a very green city. There are more than three hundred parks. Hyde Park is a very big park with more than four thousand trees. You can go biking in the park or you can ride a boat on the Serpentine lake. In the summer there are music concerts in the park.</p>

<p>In Kensington Gardens there is a statue of Peter Pan and a lot of visitors go and see it.</p>

<p>The River Thames runs through the city and a lot of bridges cross it. You can take a lovely boat ride on the River Thames and ride under Tower Bridge.</p>

<p>Big Ben is the name of an enormous clock on top of a tall tower. You can see the tower and the clock next to the Houses of Parliament on the River Thames.</p>

<p>The Tower of London is a very old tower. It was built between 1070 and 1100. You can visit the tower and see the Queen's crown jewels.</p>

<p>There are always a lot of interesting things to do and see in London. There are more than two hundred and forty important museums and many of them are free. There are also a lot of important theatres.</p>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter 2</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Peter's Shadow</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Everything is dark and silent in the nursery. Wendy, John and Michael are sleeping and dreaming. Outside the nursery window there is a small ball of light. The nursery window is open.</p>

<p>Suddenly the small ball of light comes into the nursery through the window and flies around. It is a fairy called Tinkerbell. She is very small and lovely. She is looking for something inside the nursery.</p>

<p>After a moment a young boy comes into the nursery through the open window and looks around.</p>

<p>"Tink," he says, "where are you? Where is my shadow? Please find it."</p>

<p>Tinkerbell flies around the nursery and stops near a drawer. She opens it carefully and finds his shadow. Then she gives it to him.</p>

<p>"Thank you, Tink," says the boy happily.</p>

<p>He goes to the bathroom and gets some soap. "Now I can put the shadow on my feet with some soap."</p>

<p>He tries to stick it on his feet, but he can't. "Why can't I put my shadow on my feet?" he thinks. "Now I haven't got a shadow."</p>

<p>He is very sad and starts to cry.</p>

<p>Wendy hears the boy crying and she wakes up. She sees a young boy, but she is not afraid. He has got red hair and a friendly face. His clothes are made of green leaves, she thinks.</p>

<p>"What's this boy doing in the nursery? Little boy, why are you crying?" Wendy asks.</p>

<p>The boy smiles and asks, "What's your name?"</p>

<p>"My name's Wendy Moira Angela Darling. And what's yours?"</p>

<p>"Peter Pan."</p>

<p>"Is that all?" asks Wendy, looking at him.</p>

<p>"Yes, that's all," says Peter. Then he thinks, "My name is very short."</p>

<p>Wendy looks at his shadow on the floor and says, "Is this your shadow?"</p>

<p>"Yes," says Peter. "But I can't put it on."</p>

<p>Wendy smiles and says, "I can sew it on."</p>

<p>"Can you? Oh, please do it," says Peter happily.</p>

<p>Wendy gets her big sewing basket and sews on Peter's shadow carefully. Peter looks at her and is very glad. After a few minutes Wendy says, "I've finished. Now you've got your shadow again."</p>

<p>Peter looks at the floor and he can see his shadow. He is happy and he dances around the nursery.</p>

<p>"Thank you, Wendy," he says. "You're a wonderful girl."</p>

<p>"Do you really think so?" asks Wendy.</p>

<p>"Yes, I do," says Peter, looking at Wendy.</p>

<p>Wendy smiles and gives Peter a kiss on the cheek.</p>

<p>"Oh," says Peter. "That's nice."</p>

<p>"How old are you, Peter?" asks Wendy.</p>

<p>Peter thinks for a moment and says, "I don't know. But I'm young and I don't want to grow up. I don't want to become an adult. I want to be a boy forever and have lots of fun."</p>

<p>Wendy listens to Peter and is surprised. Then Peter looks for his fairy. Suddenly he hears a noise and looks in a drawer. Little Tinkerbell flies out. Wendy is happy to see a fairy. But Tinkerbell is afraid. She hides behind the big clock.</p>

<p>"Where do you live, Peter?" asks Wendy.</p>

<p>"I sometimes live in Kensington Gardens," says Peter. "But most of the time I live in Neverland with the Lost Boys."</p>

<p>Wendy does not understand. "Neverland? The Lost Boys? Who are they?"</p>

<p>"The Lost Boys haven't got a mother or father," says Peter. "They're alone in the world and they live in Neverland. I'm their captain."</p>

<p>"Oh. Neverland sounds like a wonderful place," says Wendy.</p>

<p>"It is," says Peter. "And we have lots of fun there. In Neverland, we fight the pirates. We swim in the lagoon with beautiful mermaids. A lot of lovely fairies live in the trees in the forest. They're my friends."</p>

<p>"What fun!" says Wendy.</p>

<p>"Well, I must go back to Neverland now," says Peter. "I must tell the Lost Boys a story. You know, they love stories."</p>

<p>"I know a lot of stories," says Wendy.</p>

<p>"Then come with me to Neverland," says Peter. "You can tell us lots of stories. And you can see the beautiful mermaids in the lagoon. Come with me. We all want a mother."</p>

<p>"Oh. I can't come with you," says Wendy. "I can't fly."</p>

<p>"I can teach you to fly," says Peter. "It's very easy."</p>

<p>"Can you teach John and Michael to fly too?" asks Wendy.</p>

<p>"Of course," says Peter.</p>

<p>Wendy goes to John and Michael and says, "Wake up. Quickly. Peter Pan is here. He's from Neverland. It's a fun place."</p>

<p>John and Michael wake up. And they are very surprised.</p>

<p>"We can go to Neverland with him," says Wendy. "But we must learn to fly."</p>

<p>"That's difficult," says John.</p>

<p>"No, it's not," says Peter. "Look at me."</p>

<p>Peter flies around the nursery. And the children look at him. They are excited and try to fly. But they fall on the beds and on the floor.</p>

<p>"It's impossible," they say.</p>

<p>"You need some fairy dust," says Peter, laughing.</p>

<p>He puts some fairy dust on their heads. "Now try again."</p>

<p>Wendy, John and Michael can fly now. And they are very happy.</p>

<p>"Look, I can fly," says Wendy.</p>

<p>"We can too," say John and Michael.</p>

<p>"Now we can go to Neverland with Peter," says Wendy happily.</p>

<p>"Tink," says Peter. "Show us the way to Neverland."</p>

<p>They follow Tinkerbell and fly out of the nursery window. There are a lot of stars in the night sky.</p>

<p>In the garden, Nana looks at the sky and barks loudly.</p>

<p>"Woof!"</p>

<p>Mr. and Mrs. Darling return from the dinner party at 11 o'clock. They go to the nursery. But it is empty.</p>

<p>"Where are the children?"</p>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter 3</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Neverland</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Wendy, John and Michael fly over London. They see the River Thames and Tower Bridge. There are hundreds of lights in the city. Then they fly over big cities, small towns, tall mountains, green forests, and blue seas.</p>

<p>At last, they see an island in the sea below them. It is a beautiful island.</p>

<p>"That's Neverland," says Peter, looking down at the island. It is a sunny day now.</p>

<p>"Neverland!" say the children happily.</p>

<p>"Look, John," says Wendy. "There's the lagoon."</p>

<p>"And there's the big forest," says Michael.</p>

<p>The three children are excited.</p>

<p>The Lost Boys live in the green forest in a secret underground home. There are six Lost Boys. And their names are Curly, Nibs, Tootles, Slightly, Soiled and the Twins. They are waiting for Peter in the underground home.</p>

<p>Suddenly they hear the voices of the pirates.</p>

<p>"I thought I'm certain there were five of them."</p>

<p>"You want your eyes examined, Nib boy? Pass me that rope."</p>

<p>"I can hear the pirates," says Tootles. "They're in the forest. I'm afraid of them."</p>

<p>"What can we do?" asks Curly.</p>

<p>"I'm not afraid of the pirates," says Nibs. He goes out of the underground home. He hides behind a big tree and looks around. He sees the terrible pirates. They are walking in the forest and they are big and strong.</p>

<p>They live on a pirate ship called the Jolly Roger. The pirates' captain is James Hook. He is a very bad pirate. He has got black eyes, black hair and a big black beard. He hates Peter Pan. He has got only one hand. The other hand is a hook. In the past Peter cut off Captain Hook's right arm during a fight. A crocodile ate the arm because crocodiles are always hungry.</p>

<p>Now the crocodile follows Captain Hook everywhere because he wants to eat him. The crocodile has an alarm clock in its stomach. Captain Hook can always hear it.</p>

<p>"We must find Peter Pan and the Lost Boys," says Captain Hook angrily. "I know they live in this forest, but where?"</p>

<p>The pirates look everywhere, but they cannot find the secret underground home. Suddenly Captain Hook hears the alarm clock: tick tock, tick tock.</p>

<p>"The crocodile is near!" cries Captain Hook. "He wants to eat me. We must leave the forest quickly." He runs away and the pirates follow him.</p>

<p>Soon some American Indians come to the forest. They walk silently and carry bows and arrows. They are looking for the pirates. The pirates are their enemies. Their leader is Tiger Lily. She is the lovely daughter of the American Indian chief. She likes Peter Pan a lot.</p>

<p>The Indians cannot find the pirates and they soon go away.</p>

<p>Now the Lost Boys can leave the underground home and play in the forest. Nibs looks at the sky and says, "Look, there's a white bird in the sky."</p>

<p>The Lost Boys look at the sky and Curly asks, "Is it really a bird?"</p>

<p>"Of course it's a bird," says Tinkerbell. "It's a Wendy bird. You must shoot it."</p>

<p>Sometimes Tinkerbell is a bad fairy. She knows that it is Wendy, but she doesn't like her. Nibs takes his bow and arrow and looks at the sky. He shoots Wendy and she falls to the ground.</p>

<p>Then the Lost Boys go and see Wendy. "She's not a bird," they say. "She's a lovely girl."</p>

<p>Peter flies down with John and Michael and asks, "Where's Wendy?"</p>

<p>"Here she is," says Tootles.</p>

<p>Peter goes to her and asks, "Wendy, are you all right?"</p>

<p>Wendy slowly opens her eyes and smiles at Peter. "Yes, I'm all right," she says. "I'm lucky because the arrow hit the button of my night dress."</p>

<p>The Lost Boys are very sorry. "Poor Wendy," says Curly. "What can we do?"</p>

<p>"I've got a good idea," says Slightly. "We can build her a little house."</p>

<p>When the little house is ready, the Lost Boys go and tell Wendy. "Come and see the little house, Wendy," says Tootles.</p>

<p>"Do you like it?" ask the Lost Boys.</p>

<p>"Yes, I do," says Wendy happily. "It's a nice little house. Thank you."</p>

<p>"Wendy," says Nibs, "can you be our mother now? Can you tell us bedtime stories before we go to bed?"</p>

<p>"Yes, please," says Tootles. "Come and be our mother."</p>

<p>"Of course," says Wendy. "Come in and I can tell you the story of Cinderella."</p>

<p>They listen to Wendy's story and like it a lot.</p>

<div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ * ~ </span></div>

<p>Fairies and their magic. People all over the world have stories about fairies and their magic. Some of these stories are very old and some of them are called fairy tales. Fairies are part of the British, Irish, Northern European and old cultures.</p>

<p>In Peter Pan, Tinkerbell is Peter's fairy. Peter says that the fairies in Neverland are his good friends.</p>

<p>In Pinocchio, there is a kind fairy called the Blue Fairy. She always helps Pinocchio.</p>

<p>In Cinderella, a fairy godmother is Cinderella's good friend.</p>

<p>There are fairies in one of William Shakespeare's plays too. In A Midsummer Night's Dream, Oberon and Titania are the king and queen of the fairies. They live in the forest and are important characters in the play.</p>

<p>What some people say about fairies. They are small and have wings. They live in the forest, in trees, in flowers and in gardens. They are sometimes good and sometimes bad. They are magic. They can do good things and bad things.</p>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter 4</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Danger in the Lagoon</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Peter, Wendy, John, Michael and the Lost Boys often go to the Mermaids' Lagoon. The water is blue and beautiful. They like swimming and playing in the blue lagoon.</p>

<p>Beautiful mermaids live here and they are Peter's friends. They have got long hair and are very pretty. They play mermaid games and then sit on Marooners' Rock and comb their long hair. They sit in the sun and talk and laugh. The children like the mermaids and John says, "I want to catch a mermaid."</p>

<p>He tries, but the mermaid jumps into the water and swims away. Everyone laughs. "It's very difficult to catch a mermaid," says Peter.</p>

<p>Suddenly Peter hears a noise and says, "The pirates are coming. I can hear them."</p>

<p>John, Michael and the Lost Boys jump into the water and swim away. They are afraid of the pirates. But Wendy stays with Peter. They hide behind Marooners' Rock.</p>

<p>A small boat with two pirates is coming to the lagoon. One pirate is Smee. He is old and wears glasses. The other pirate is Starkey. He is young and has got a long nose. Tiger Lily is on the boat and she is a prisoner of the pirates.</p>

<p>"Let's leave her on this rock," says Smee. "When the sea rises, she's going to die."</p>

<p>The two pirates laugh loudly. It is almost night and it is dark.</p>

<p>"Poor Tiger Lily," thinks Peter. "She is our good friend. I must save her. But what can I do?"</p>

<p>He thinks for a minute and then imitates Captain Hook's voice. "Cut the ropes and let Tiger Lily go. Do as I say, Smee. Let her go."</p>

<p>The two pirates hear Captain Hook's voice and are very surprised. They look at each other and then they look around.</p>

<p>"Starkey," says Smee, "can you hear Hook's voice?"</p>

<p>"Yes," says Starkey, looking around. "But I don't see Hook."</p>

<p>"We must listen to Hook and cut the ropes. Oops," says Smee. "Are you sure?"</p>

<p>"Of course I'm sure," says Smee.</p>

<p>Starkey gets a knife and cuts the rope. Tiger Lily is free. She jumps into the water and swims away.</p>

<p>Captain Hook is on his ship, the Jolly Roger, and he sees everything. He is very angry because Tiger Lily is free.</p>

<p>"Peter Pan is very clever," thinks Captain Hook. "I must fight him."</p>

<p>Captain Hook jumps into the water and swims to the rock. He fights with Peter. It is a long fight. Captain Hook hurts Peter with his big hook. But Peter does not stop fighting. He is brave. At last Peter wins the fight. Captain Hook is angry and swims back to the Jolly Roger.</p>

<p>Peter and Wendy are now alone on the rock.</p>

<p>"What a terrible fight, Peter," says Wendy, looking at Peter's hand. His hand is badly hurt after the fight with Captain Hook.</p>

<p>"Yes," says Peter. "But I'm not afraid of Hook or the pirates."</p>

<p>"You're a very brave boy," says Wendy, smiling.</p>

<p>"Look," says Peter. "The sea is rising and we are in great danger here."</p>

<p>"What can we do?" asks Wendy.</p>

<p>"We must leave this rock immediately," says Peter, looking at the sea.</p>

<p>"Oh, Peter," says Wendy. "I can't swim or fly because I'm very tired."</p>

<p>Peter thinks for a moment. He sees a big kite with a long tail. It is flying slowly over the lagoon. He quickly takes the tail of the kite and says, "Wendy, hold on to this tail and fly away with the kite."</p>

<p>Wendy flies away with the kite. The sea is rising quickly. Peter thinks, "I must fly away."</p>

<p>When Peter gets home, everyone is happy to see him.</p>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter 5</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">A Secret Place</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">The underground home is a secret place in the big forest. It is a safe place because no one knows where it is. There is only one big room with a fireplace. It is a happy, warm home. Wendy, John and Michael are very happy there.</p>

<p>Wendy is a wonderful mother. She cooks and sews for everyone. Every evening, after dinner, everyone sits in front of the fireplace. Wendy tells great bedtime stories. The Lost Boys love these stories and are happy because they finally have a mother.</p>

<p>John and Michael are happy because there is an exciting adventure every day. They are never bored. Peter Pan is the perfect father. He brings home food and protects his family. He and Wendy play games with the children and laugh with them. Everyone is having a wonderful time.</p>

<p>But in London, Mr. and Mrs. Darling are not happy. They are very sad because they can't find their three children. And Nana is sad too. "Where are our three children?" they think.</p>

<p>Wendy sometimes thinks about her parents. One evening, she says, "This evening, I'm going to tell you a new bedtime story. It takes place in England."</p>

<p>Everyone sits down in front of the fireplace and listens to her.</p>

<p>"In the city of London, there are two parents. They're very sad because they can't find their three children. Every night, they leave the nursery window open. They want their children to return. They wait and wait, but their three children don't return. These poor parents are terribly sad."</p>

<p>"But, Wendy," say John and Michael. "That's the story of our parents."</p>

<p>Peter listens to the story and says, "Sometimes parents forget their children and other children take their place."</p>

<p>Wendy, John and Michael are surprised. They look at Peter.</p>

<p>"Are you sure, Peter?" asks Wendy.</p>

<p>"Yes, I'm sure," says Peter.</p>

<p>"Perhaps there are other children in our nursery and in our beds," says Wendy. "John, Michael, we must go home immediately."</p>

<p>John and Michael look at Wendy and say, "Do we really have to go?"</p>

<p>"Yes, we must go home," says Wendy. "Quickly, we haven't got much time."</p>

<p>"But we're happy here in Neverland with Peter and the Lost Boys," says John.</p>

<p>"There's a new adventure every day," says Michael.</p>

<p>"And we're never bored," says John. "Neverland is a wonderful place."</p>

<p>"Oh, Wendy," say the Lost Boys. "Please don't go away. Don't leave us."</p>

<p>Wendy looks at the Lost Boys and says, "Please don't be sad. You can come to London and live with us. You can be part of the Darling family."</p>

<p>"Oh, Wendy," say the Lost Boys. "Thank you. That's wonderful. We can have a real family in London."</p>

<p>Nibs and Tootles jump up and down and laugh. The other Lost Boys dance around the room. They are very happy.</p>

<p>Peter is not happy. "I'm not coming with you to London."</p>

<p>Everyone is surprised and looks at him.</p>

<p>"Why?" asks Wendy.</p>

<p>"I don't want to grow up," says Peter. "I don't want to become an adult. I want to be a boy forever."</p>

<p>"But, Peter," says Wendy. "All boys and girls grow up. They become adults."</p>

<p>"No," says Peter angrily. "I don't want to grow up. You can go, but I'm not coming."</p>

<p>He turns around and looks at the wall. Wendy, John, Michael and the Lost Boys say goodbye to Peter and leave the underground home. They start to walk in the forest. But they don't know that the pirates are hiding behind the trees. They are waiting for them.</p>

<p>Suddenly the pirates jump out of the forest. The children are very surprised and try to run away. But there are a lot of pirates. And they take the children to the Jolly Roger.</p>

<p>Peter is inside the underground home and he does not hear anything. He sits in front of the fireplace and thinks about Wendy, John and Michael and the Lost Boys. He is sad without them.</p>

<p>Tap. Tap. Tap. There is someone at the door. Peter goes to the door and asks, "Who's there?" He hears the sound of little bells and opens the door. Tinkerbell flies in and says, "The children are with the pirates."</p>

<p>"What do you mean, Tink?" asks Peter.</p>

<p>"Wendy, John, Michael and the Lost Boys are on the Jolly Roger with Captain Hook," says Tinkerbell. "They're in danger."</p>

<p>"Oh no," says Peter. "We must save them. Let's go to the Jolly Roger quickly. I want to fight Hook."</p>

<p>Peter and Tinkerbell leave the underground home and fly to the Jolly Roger.</p>

<div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ * ~ </span></div>

<p>The Great Ormond Street Hospital for Children. In London, there is a hospital for sick children called the Great Ormond Street Hospital for Children. J.M. Barrie likes children very much. He wants children to be well and happy. He often visits this hospital because he wants to help the children. Barrie is a kind man.</p>

<p>In 1929, he decides that all the money from book sales, films and theatre plays of Peter Pan goes to the hospital. And this continues today. This money helps the hospital, the children, the doctors and the nurses. The children at the hospital can watch the play Peter Pan once a year. This is a very happy moment for them.</p>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter 6</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">The Pirate Ship</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">The Jolly Roger is a big pirate ship in the bay near Kid's Creek. On the ship, there is a black flag with a white skull on it. It is the pirates' flag. It is night time. And there is a yellow moon in the dark sky.</p>

<p>Peter sees the children on the pirate ship. They are prisoners of Captain Hook. And they are afraid of him.</p>

<p>Captain Hook looks at the children and says, "Peter Pan can't save you now. You're my prisoners." He starts to laugh. And the other pirates laugh too. "Ha ha!"</p>

<p>"Smee, come here," says Captain Hook.</p>

<p>"Yes, sir," says Smee.</p>

<p>"Get the plank ready for these children," says Captain Hook.</p>

<p>"You're right, sir," says Smee. He goes to get the long plank.</p>

<p>"What's the plank?" asks John, looking at Wendy.</p>

<p>"Ha ha!" laughs Captain Hook. "You don't know what the plank is. You must all walk the plank tonight."</p>

<p>"I don't understand," says John.</p>

<p>"First you walk the plank," says Captain Hook. "And then you fall into the sea. And in the sea there's a hungry crocodile. He wants to eat you too. Ha ha!"</p>

<p>"Oh no!" shout the children. They are afraid.</p>

<p>"But perhaps I can save two of you," says Captain Hook, looking at the children. "I want two young pirates. Who wants to be a pirate?"</p>

<p>"What do you mean, John?" asks Michael.</p>

<p>"Well," says John. "The life of a pirate is exciting. There are a lot of adventures. Let's be pirates, Michael."</p>

<p>Michael does not know what to say. He and John look at Wendy.</p>

<p>"That's a terrible idea, John," says Wendy. "I'm surprised at you."</p>

<p>Captain Hook moves his hook in front of their faces and laughs loudly. "Ha ha! I want an answer from you," says Captain Hook. "Do you want to be pirates? Yes or no?"</p>

<p>John and Michael look at each other and then say, "Never!"</p>

<p>"What?" says Captain Hook angrily. "Then you must walk the plank and die."</p>

<p>Wendy is afraid. She loves her brothers and the Lost Boys. She has tears in her eyes.</p>

<p>A pirate shouts, "Who's the first to walk the plank?"</p>

<p>At that moment, everyone hears a loud noise. Tick tock, tick tock.</p>

<p>"Oh no. It's the crocodile," says Captain Hook. "He wants to eat me." His face is white and he runs to his cabin and hides there.</p>

<p>"The crocodile is hungry tonight," says a pirate.</p>

<p>"Someone must walk the plank. Who's the first to walk the plank?" asks Smee, looking at John and Michael. "Let's go!"</p>

<p>Suddenly, Peter Pan arrives and Tinkerbell follows him. Wendy, John, Michael and the Lost Boys cheer happily. They are very happy to see him.</p>

<p>"Peter's here!" they say. "He's going to save us!"</p>

<p>Captain Hook comes out of his cabin and sees Peter Pan. He and his pirates are very angry. Hook takes his long sword and says, "I'm going to fight you, Peter Pan. This is your last fight because tonight you're going to die! Die!"</p>

<p>Hook fights with his long sword and with his big hook. Peter is very brave and fights with his small sword. He pushes Hook to the back of the ship. It is a terrible fight.</p>

<p>John, Michael and the Lost Boys fight the pirates. After a long fight, they throw the pirates into the sea.</p>

<p>Peter and Captain Hook fight all over the ship. Their swords make a lot of noise. Suddenly, Peter pushes Captain Hook into the sea.</p>

<p>Hook shouts, "Oh no!" He falls into the sea and into the mouth of the hungry crocodile.</p>

<p>Everyone cheers!</p>

<p>"Oh, Peter!" says Wendy happily. "You're a very brave boy." She smiles and kisses him on the cheek.</p>

<p>Peter is happy and says, "Now the Jolly Roger is ours! We can go home!"</p>

<p>"Home!" everyone shouts. "Let's go!"</p>
