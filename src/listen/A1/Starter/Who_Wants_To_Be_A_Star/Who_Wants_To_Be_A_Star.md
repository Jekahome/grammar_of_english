  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Tina Daniels is 13 years old. She's a star. She can sing and dance. Everyone knows her. Everyone loves her. They call her Curly because she has beautiful curly hair. They all want her photos. She can have anything she wants. But is she happy? Does she want to be a star?</p>

<p>"Tina, why are you eating chocolates?" her mother asks.</p>

<p>"I like them. They're nice," Tina says.</p>

<p>"You're getting fat. And a fat girl doesn't get lots of money in Hollywood," Mrs. Daniels says.</p>

<p>"You want the money. I don't," Tina says.</p>

<p>Tina's angry with her mother. And Mrs. Daniels is angry with Tina. Tina leaves the room and cries. Tina is very sad. She doesn't want to be a star. She wants a quiet life.</p>

<p>One day she has an idea. Her school friend, Mary, has a grandmother called Mrs. White. Old Mrs. White lives in the country, in Stonechurch. Tina has a photo of Mary in front of Mrs. White's house. It looks very pretty, not like the city. Tina decides to go there.</p>

<p>Tina's at the city station. She's wearing jeans and an old jumper. People don't know who she is.</p>

<p>"Where do you want to go, young girl?" the man at the ticket office asks.</p>

<p>"Stonechurch, please. When's the next train?" Tina asks.</p>

<p>"The train leaves at two o'clock from platform three," the man says.</p>

<p>Tina buys her ticket.</p>

  <div class="chapter-title"></div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Tina arrives at Stonechurch. She sees an old house near the river. It's the house in the photo. There are flowers and trees in the garden. Mrs. White must live here, Tina thinks. She goes to the front door and rings the bell. An old lady with white hair and a kind face opens the door. A black cat comes out too.</p>

<p>"Good afternoon. Who are you?" the old lady asks.</p>

<p>"Oh, hello, Mrs. White. My name's Tina. I'm a school friend of your granddaughter, Mary."</p>

<p>"Mary's school friend. How nice to meet you. What are you doing in Stonechurch, Tina?" Mrs. White asks.</p>

<p>Tina says she hates the city life.</p>

<p>"Well," says Mrs. White. "Come in and have a cup of tea, my dear."</p>

<p>Tina and Mrs. White drink tea and talk. Mrs. White doesn't know Tina's a star. She doesn't have a television or a radio. Tina's very happy.</p>

<p>Mrs. White says, "Tina, you can stay with me for a week, but first you must telephone your mother."</p>

<p>Tina's angry with her mother and doesn't telephone her. "Mother says yes," she says to Mrs. White later. Her face is a little red.</p>

<p>Two days later, Mrs. White is taking Tina for a walk.</p>

<p>"Look at the lambs," Mrs. White says.</p>

<p>"Yes, I love them. I want to be a lamb. They can jump and run and be happy."</p>

<p>"Well, you can run and jump and be happy too, Tina."</p>

<p>"No, I can't. I..."</p>

<p>"Tina, you're young. Run!"</p>

<div class="book-page">

  <div class="chapter-title"></div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Tina's living a very different life in the country. She sees new things all the time. Now it's night and Tina and Mrs. White are waiting. It's very quiet. Every night, some hedgehogs come to the garden. Mrs. White puts out food for them.</p>

<p>"I love their long brown noses," Mrs. White says.</p>

<p>"So do I," says Tina.</p>

<p>"Oh, look, Tina. A big brown fox!"</p>

<p>A big brown fox is running across the garden. At first, Tina's very happy to see the fox. There aren't any foxes in gardens in the city. Then she sees the fox has something in its mouth. The something is Mrs. White's chicken. Mrs. White is very angry and wants to kill the fox.</p>

<p>"Look at the fox's work," says Mrs. White. "The chickens are making a terrible noise. We can't have eggs for breakfast in the morning, Tina."</p>

<p>"Why not?" asks Tina.</p>

<p>"Well, the chickens aren't very happy. When they aren't happy, they can't give us eggs," Mrs. White explains.</p>

<p>For breakfast, Tina has toast and fresh orange juice.</p>

<div class="book-page">

  <div class="chapter-title"></div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Three days later, Tina is with Bella, a horse. She loves Bella and gives her some sugar.</p>

<p>Today, the postman has a letter for Mrs. White. When he sees Tina, he says, "I don't believe it. It's Curly." Then he says to her, "Everyone's looking for you. Everyone's looking for you. You're in all the newspapers." He runs to his red van to find his newspaper.</p>

<p>Mrs. White reads the postman's newspaper.</p>

<p>"Tina," says Mrs. White. "You're the TV star Curly, aren't you?"</p>

<p>"Yes, I'm Curly, but please don't send me home. I'm happy here with you."</p>

<p>"I'm coming with you to see your mother. I've got a good idea," Mrs. White says.</p>

<p>Tina's mother is very happy to see Tina again. Mrs. White explains her idea. "Tina can go to her friend's school on the island of Guernsey, between England and France. Tina can have friends there who don't know she's Curly."</p>

<p>"What! I want Tina to be a star," Mrs. Daniels says.</p>

<p>"She wants to be happy," Mrs. White says quietly.</p>

<p>Tina's at school in Guernsey. She has new friends and new interests. One day, Tina's friend is reading a magazine. It's about film and television stars. There are lots of beautiful photos of beautiful people. Everyone looks very happy.</p>

<p>"What a wonderful life," the friend says.</p>

<p>Tina smiles to herself. "Who wants to be a star?" she says. "I don't."</p>

<summary>Проверить понимание текста</summary>

* Сколько лет Тине Дэниелс и почему её называют «Кудряшки» (Curls)?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Ей тринадцать лет. Её называют «Кудряшки» из-за её красивых вьющихся волос (beautiful curly hair).
  </details>

* Почему мама Тины злится на неё за шоколадки?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Она говорит, что Тина толстеет (getting fat), а толстая девочка не заработает много денег в Голливуде.
  </details>

* Куда и от кого Тина решает сбежать?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Она решает сбежать к миссис Уайт (Mrs White), бабушке своей школьной подруги Мэри, в деревню Стоунчерч (Stonechurch).
  </details>

* Что Тина надевает, чтобы её не узнали на вокзале?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Она надевает джинсы (jeans) и старый свитер (an old jumper).
  </details>

* Почему миссис Уайт не узнаёт Тину как звезду?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  У неё нет ни телевизора, ни радио (She does not have a television or a radio).
  </details>

* Что Тина говорит миссис Уайт о звонке маме?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Она врёт, что мама сказала «да». На самом деле она не звонит маме, потому что злится на неё.
  </details>

* Какое животное крадёт курицу миссис Уайт?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Большая коричневая лиса (a big brown fox).
  </summary>
  </details>

* Кто узнаёт Тину в деревне и приносит газету?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Почтальон (postman). Он узнаёт в Тине «Кудряшки» и говорит, что она во всех газетах.
  </details>

* Какую идею предлагает миссис Уайт маме Тины?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Она предлагает отправить Тину в школу на остров Гернси (island of Guernsey), где никто не знает, что она звезда.
  </details>

* Что Тина говорит подруге, которая читает журнал о звёздах?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Она говорит: «Кто хочет быть звездой? Я — нет» (Who wants to be a star? I don't).
  </details>
