<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter 1</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Wow!</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">My name is Mark Sellers. I'm 22 years old and I work in security in Mason's store. You can get everything here: books, TVs, hats, flowers, sandwiches, beds, bicycles. It's interesting work and I like it. Sometimes I walk around in the store and sometimes I work in the office.</p>

<p>Leon and Xiaomi work in security too. I like working with them.</p>

<p>"Look at this woman," Leon says. "Which hat is best for her? Blue or black?"</p>

<p>I look at the woman on the screen. "Oh, the black hat," I say.</p>

<p>"No," says Xiaomi. "The blue hat is nicer."</p>

<p>We watch and wait. In the end, the woman takes the blue hat.</p>

<p>"Hooray!" says Xiaomi. "You know nothing about hats."</p>

<p>Yes, it's interesting work.</p>

<p>Today I'm watching the screens. I'm looking at a man with a big bag. He's got a clock in his hand. He looks around slowly. Now he's got a clock in his bag. The man walks to the door. I talk on my radio. Xiaomi walks quietly behind the man. When he gets to the door, she puts her hand on his arm.</p>

<p>"Please come with me," she says.</p>

<p>I go back to the screens. And then I see her.</p>

<p>"Wow!"</p>

<p>"Who's that girl?"</p>

<p>"What girl? I can see lots of girls," Leon says.</p>

<p>Now the picture on the screen is bigger. "That girl there with red hair."</p>

<p>Leon looks at the screen. "Hmm, yes, she's okay. But who is she?"</p>

<p>"I don't know."</p>

<p>"Why don't you go and ask her, Mark?" He laughs and walks away.</p>

<p>But I can't stop looking at the screen. Who is that beautiful girl? What is her name? I want to meet her.</p>

<p>Every day I look for the girl with red hair. But I do not see her. Where is she?</p>

<p>"Every day 'Where's the girl with red hair, Mark?' 'What's her name?'" Leon says. "Oh, stop it," Leon says.</p>

<p>"Mark's in a dream," Xiaomi says.</p>

<p>I laugh at first. But after three days I want to hit Leon.</p>

<p>Then suddenly I see her again. I am watching a woman with a big bag. And then there she is. The girl with red hair. She's got green eyes too. But there is a baby with her. A little boy. They are looking at a book about trains.</p>

<p>"Look, Greg," she says. "Look at the big train."</p>

<p>The little boy smiles. He's got red hair and green eyes too. Her baby? So has she got a husband? A boyfriend?</p>

<p>I look at her beautiful green eyes again. And then I go back to work. But there are lots of questions in my head.</p>

<p>She comes to the store on Wednesday mornings. She usually arrives at about 11 o'clock.</p>

<p>"How's your girl?" asks Leon.</p>

<p>"She's not my girl," I say. "I don't know her name. Her little boy's name is Greg. But she's the girl with red hair."</p>

<p>"Well, let's give her a name," says Leon. "What do you think, Xiaomi?"</p>

<p>"Hmm. Scarlett. Her name is Scarlett," says Xiaomi.</p>

<p>"Apples are red," says Leon. "Apple is a nice name." Then he looks at my face and runs out of the door.</p>

<summary>Chapter 2</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter 2</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Boo-hoo</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Next Wednesday I am in the store when the girl with red hair comes in with Greg. She stops and looks at a picture. Greg takes his hat off and drops it. I pick up the hat and go over to her.</p>

<p>"Excuse me," I say. "Is this your little boy's hat?"</p>

<p>She smiles at me. "Oh, thank you," she says. "Greg, look, it's your hat." Greg laughs. She puts out her hand and takes the hat from me. Is she wearing a ring? No, there is no ring on her hand.</p>

<p>Wednesday is the best day of the week, I think.</p>

<p>Ten minutes later I am near the flowers when Leon speaks to me on my radio.</p>

<p>"Mark, go to the door," he says. "You must stop her. The girl with red hair."</p>

<p>"What? What's happening?" I say.</p>

<p>"Go quickly, now. It's the little boy. He's got one of the red planes. Those red planes. Children take them all the time because they like the colour. Then they get to the door and the alarm rings. Children cry and their mothers get angry. I hate those planes."</p>

<p>When I get to the door the alarm is ringing. The girl with red hair is standing there and her face is red.</p>

<p>"What's happening?" she says.</p>

<p>"Please come back into the store for a minute," I say. We go back into the store and the alarm stops.</p>

<p>"This young man has got the answer, I think. Where's the plane?" I say to Greg.</p>

<p>"Plane!" says Greg, and he waves a little red plane at us.</p>

<p>"Oh, I am sorry," she says.</p>

<p>"It's all right," I say. "It happens all the time. Can I have the plane? Good boy."</p>

<p>"Thank you very much," says the girl with red hair. But the alarm is ringing again. "I must go."</p>

<p>I walk into the office upstairs. Xiaomi is watching the screens. She stops and looks at my face. She says nothing, but she smiles. Then she goes back to work. I look at the screens too, but I am dreaming. I'm happy. I'm really happy.</p>

<p>But how can I meet her again? I think about this for the next six days. But I can't find any answers.</p>

<p>Then it's Wednesday again. I'm sitting in the office watching the screens. 10 o'clock comes and goes. Then 11 o'clock. I can't see her. I look on every screen in the store.</p>

<p>In the end I see her near the door. She's looking at her watch and talking to Greg. Is she waiting for somebody? Perhaps she's meeting somebody. Perhaps she's late. Perhaps her mother is coming. Perhaps...</p>

<p>But now she's waving at somebody. A good-looking man in a black jacket walks up to the girl with red hair and kisses her. Greg laughs and the man picks him up and kisses him too. Is he the baby's father? Of course he is. Anybody can see that.</p>

<p>I don't want to look. But I can't take my eyes away from the screen. Then they all go down the road away from the store.</p>

<p>I look around the room. Leon and Xiaomi are in the store and the room is very quiet. It feels cold. Then Leon and Xiaomi come in.</p>

<p>"Now that's not a happy face," Xiaomi says. "What's the matter?"</p>

<p>I tell them. I tell them about the girl with red hair and Greg and the good-looking man and the kiss.</p>

<p>Leon looks at my face and thinks for a minute. "Look, Mark," he says. "It's a dream, that's all. You see a nice girl. You think about her. You talk to her. And one day her boyfriend arrives. Boo-hoo. You stop dreaming. But there are always more girls."</p>

<p>"Yes, but..." I say, and then I stop.</p>

<p>"But they're not the girl with red hair."</p>

<p>"Right," says Leon. "But she's got a boyfriend. You need a different girl, Mark. A girl without a boyfriend. Why don't you come out with us tonight? We're going to Ocean Blue. It's a wonderful club and there are lots of nice girls there."</p>

<p>I don't really want to go, but I don't want to stay at home and do nothing.</p>

<p>"Okay then," I say.</p>

<p>"Good man," says Leon. "See you there at nine."</p>

<summary>Chapter 3</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter 3</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Yay!</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">The club is dark. There's a lot of noise and a lot of people. But the music is good. Leon is there with his girlfriend, Ellie. I talk to them and their friends. I'm feeling good. Perhaps tonight I can forget about everything. Leon and Ellie are dancing, so I go to get a drink.</p>

<p>Leon is right about one thing. There are a lot of nice girls at Ocean Blue. Tall girls, interesting girls. Girls with black hair, blue hair and red hair. Red hair.</p>

<p>I'm waiting for my drink and I'm standing next to the girl with red hair. No, two girls with red hair. Is this really happening?</p>

<p>The girl with red hair looks at me and smiles. "Just a minute," she says. "Don't I know you?"</p>

<p>She's talking to me. Why can't I say anything?</p>

<p>"In the store," I say at last. "In Mason's store. With your little boy and the red plane."</p>

<p>The girl with red hair laughs. "Oh, with Greg," she says. "I remember. But he isn't my baby. He's Claire's. This is my sister Claire. She's his mother."</p>

<p>The second girl with red hair waves at me.</p>

<p>"But I always see you with him," I say.</p>

<p>"I look after Greg on Wednesday mornings," she says. "And we always go to Mason's. Then I meet Claire. Well, usually, that is. But not this week."</p>

<p>Just then a good-looking man in a black jacket arrives. "Sorry I'm late," he says and kisses Claire.</p>

<p>"Hi, Kate." Claire smiles at him and the girl with red hair says, "Hello."</p>

<p>I'm starting to understand. This is Claire's husband and Greg's father. And the girl with red hair is called Kate.</p>

<p>My face is red. "Hey, I'm sorry," I say to Kate. "It's well, Greg's got your hair and your eyes. But I can see now he's got Claire's hair and eyes."</p>

<p>"Of course it's okay," Kate says. "It happens all the time. People say, 'Isn't your little boy happy?' and sometimes I just say, 'Yes, he is.'"</p>

<p>Claire and her husband go off to dance and I get Kate a drink.</p>

<p>"Here you are, Kate. My name's Mark."</p>

<p>"Mark. Thanks, Mark," she says. "So, you work in Mason's?"</p>

<p>"Yes," I say. "I work in security. I look for little boys with red planes and girls with red hair."</p>

<p>"And do you find them?"</p>

<p>"I find lots of little boys with red planes. And the girls? Just one."</p>

<p>Now I have the answers to my questions. The girl with red hair is Kate. She hasn't got a husband and she hasn't got a baby. And those green eyes are smiling at me.</p>

<p>Yes, Wednesday really is the best day of the week.</p>
