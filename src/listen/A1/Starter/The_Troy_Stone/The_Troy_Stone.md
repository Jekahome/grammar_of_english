  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">The Jackson family are on holiday in Turkey. They're staying at a big hotel in Istanbul. Mark, 13, his sister Fay, 12, and their parents like Turkey. It's beautiful, the weather's hot, and the food's good. There's a lot to see, too. Today, they're visiting the city of Troy.</p>

<p>It's 9 o'clock in the morning. They're getting on a bus in front of their hotel. There are 40 people on the bus. They come from Britain, America, France, Italy, and Holland.</p>

<p>At Troy, they all have lunch. Then they start to look at the old city. It's a very hot day. After an hour, Mark sits under a tree. "I'm going to stop here for a moment," he tells Fay.</p>

<p>"Okay," she answers.</p>

<p>Then Mark sees something on the ground. "What's that?" he thinks. It's a flat, yellow stone. Mark picks it up. Then he starts to clean it with his hands.</p>

<p>"But I know this stone," he thinks. "It comes from... from..."</p>

<p>Suddenly, there's a white light in his eyes. Mark closes them. The light's strong. Now there's a loud wind in his ears. He can't hear or see anything.</p>

<p>"What's happening?" he thinks.</p>

<p>Ten seconds later, the light and the wind stop. Mark opens his eyes. He's standing on a beach. He can see hundreds of ships and men. Behind him, there's a beautiful city with high walls.</p>

<p>"Where am I?" he thinks. "Where's my family?"</p>

<p>He puts the yellow stone in his pocket.</p>

<p>Then one of the men says to him, "Boy, come here."</p>

<p>"Me?"</p>

<p>Mark walks across the beach to a long black ship.</p>

<p>"Carry this," the man says. He gives Mark a lot of wood. Then he gets off the ship. "Follow me," he says.</p>

<div class="book-page">

  <div class="chapter-title"></div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Mark follows him. The man's very tall and has a short brown beard.</p>

<p>"I don't know your face," he says. "Are you new here?"</p>

<p>"Yes," Mark answers. "That's right, I'm new here."</p>

<p>"My name's Andros," says the man.</p>

<p>He and Mark walk for another ten minutes. Then they climb a small hill. At the top, Mark stops. There, in front of them, is a big wooden horse. Andros sees Mark's face and smiles.</p>

<p>"Well, what do you think of it?" he asks.</p>

<p>Mark doesn't answer. And Andros says, "This horse is going to win the war for us Greeks."</p>

<p>They walk down the hill. There are a lot of men working under the horse. One of them talks to Andros.</p>

<p>"There's a problem," he says. "We can't find a stone for the second eye. We must find one before tomorrow."</p>

<p>Mark looks at the horse's one bright eye. Then he remembers the stone in his pocket.</p>

<p>"Wait," he says. "I think I can help you."</p>

<p>He takes the stone out of his pocket.</p>

<p>"But how?" says Andros.</p>

<p>The man smiles. "That's it. That's it." He looks at Mark. "What's your name, boy?"</p>

<p>Mark tells him.</p>

<p>"He's new here," says Andros.</p>

<p>"Ah!" The man's very happy. "Well, we must thank him. I know. You can come with us in the horse tomorrow night."</p>

<p>Mark's mouth opens. "Me?"</p>

<summary>Chapter 3</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter 3</div>
  <div class="chapter-title"></div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">That evening, Mark sits near a big fire on the beach. There's an old woman beside him. Her name is Anna, and she's a cook. She tells Mark lots of stories. Stories about Greece, the war, and the beautiful princess, Helen. In the end, she goes to bed.</p>

<p>"Goodbye, boy," she says. "Come and see me again one day. Come and see me again."</p>

<p>Next morning, the horse is ready. Greek soldiers pull it to the gates of Troy. Then they leave it there. Inside the horse, there are 40 Greeks. They wait all afternoon under the hot yellow sun. Mark's sitting in the horse's head.</p>

<p>"What happens now?" he thinks.</p>

<p>Suddenly, the gates open. Trojan soldiers walk out. They pull the horse inside and shut the gates again. Mark can hear the Trojans.</p>

<p>"Why are the Greeks giving us this horse?" asks one.</p>

<p>"Do you think they want to end the war?"</p>

<p>"Yes. Look," says a soldier on top of the city walls. "Their ships are leaving."</p>

<p>After that, the Trojans eat, sing, and dance all evening. Then they go to bed very late, and Troy is quiet.</p>

<p>At that moment, the Greeks climb out of the horse. Four men go to the gates and open them. A minute later, there are thousands of Greek soldiers in the city. They and their ships are back. They run from one building to another. In five minutes, Troy is on fire.</p>

<p>Mark watches all this. Suddenly, he sees a beautiful woman. She's running along the city walls.</p>

<p>"It's Helen," he thinks. "Helen of Troy."</p>

<p>At that moment, Andros says, "Follow me, boy." He runs down a small street very fast. Mark follows, but the street's full of smoke. After a minute, he can't see anything.</p>

<p>"Andros," he says, "Where are you?"</p>

<p>Then suddenly, two Trojans with swords come out of the smoke. Mark looks at them.</p>

<p>"Please don't kill me," he says. "Don't kill me. Please."</p>

  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">"Mark, Mark, it's four o'clock. The bus is leaving in a minute."</p>

<p>Fay is looking down at Mark. After a moment, he suddenly wakes up.</p>

<p>"What?" he asks.</p>

<p>"The bus is leaving," Fay says again. Then she asks, "Are you okay? Your face is white. And what's that dirty stone?"</p>

<p>Mark looks at the yellow stone in his hand. "This? Oh, it's nothing," he says.</p>

<p>Mark stands up. He sees an old woman. She has lots of small wooden horses in a basket.</p>

<p>"Do you want one, boy?" she asks.</p>

<p>"Yes, why not?"</p>

<p>Mark buys a horse and looks at the woman. "No, it can't be," he thinks.</p>

<p>He walks to the bus. Behind him, the old woman smiles.</p>

<p>"Goodbye, boy," she says. "Come and see me again one day. Come and see me again."</p>

<summary>Проверить понимание текста</summary>

* Где семья Джексонов проводит отпуск и что они посещают в начале истории?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Они в отпуске в Турции (Turkey), в Стамбуле. Они посещают город Троя (the city of Troy).
  </details>

* Как зовут детей в семье и сколько им лет?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Марк (Mark) — 13 лет, его сестра Фэй (Fay) — 12 лет.
  </details>

* Что Марк находит на земле под деревом в Трое?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Он находит плоский жёлтый камень (a flat, yellow stone).
  </details>

* Что происходит с Марком, когда он поднимает камень?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Он видит яркий белый свет (white light), слышит сильный ветер, а затем оказывается на пляже среди сотен кораблей и воинов.
  </details>

* Кто такой Андрос (Andros) и что греки строят?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Андрос — высокий мужчина с короткой коричневой бородой. Греки строят большого деревянного коня (big wooden horse).
  </details>

* Как Марк помогает грекам?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Он отдаёт свой жёлтый камень для второго глаза коня (for the second eye of the horse).
  </details>

* Куда Марка приглашают в награду?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Его приглашают спрятаться внутри коня вместе с греческими воинами.
  </details>

* Кто такая Ана (Ana) и что она делает?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Ана — пожилая женщина, повариха (cook). Она рассказывает Марку истории о Греции, войне и прекрасной принцессе Елене (Helen).
  </details>

* Что происходит, когда троянцы втаскивают коня в город?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Троянцы празднуют, думают, что греки уплыли, а затем ложатся спать. Ночью греки вылезают из коня, открывают ворота, и город загорается (Troy is on fire).
  </details>

* Кого Марк видит бегущей по стенам Трои?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Он видит прекрасную женщину — Елену Троянскую (Helen of Troy).
  </details>

* Как Марк возвращается в своё время?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Его будит сестра Фэй: уже 4 часа дня, автобус уезжает через минуту.
  </details>

* Кого Марк видит у автобуса и что она говорит?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Он видит пожилую женщину, которая продаёт маленьких деревянных коней. Она говорит ему: «До свидания, мальчик. Приходи ко мне снова однажды. Приходи ко мне снова», — те же слова, что говорила Ана.
  </details>
