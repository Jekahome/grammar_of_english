<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapters 1-2</div>
  <div class="chapter-title"></div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Marcel is a French mouse. He lives on a beautiful old boat in Paris. His home is under the kitchen floor. He likes books, restaurants and old films. He likes the opera too.</p>

<p>One evening in June, Marcel finishes a detective story. Then he goes to have dinner with some friends. They live in the metro station at the Louvre. After dinner, Marcel waits at the station. He sees two men standing next to him. The tall one is reading a magazine.</p>

<p>"Look," he says. "Here she is. Opera star Miss Zazar. She's wearing her beautiful one million pound diamond ring, the White Star."</p>

<p>The short man looks at the photo and laughs. "Not after tonight," he says.</p>

<p>Marcel's mouth opens. "What?" he thinks. Are the men going to steal Zazar's diamond? He remembers an evening at the opera two weeks before. Zazar's green dress, the music, the beautiful White Star on her finger.</p>

<p>"No, they can't steal it."</p>

<p>The train comes and the men get on. Marcel pulls down his hat and follows them. Half an hour later, he gets off again at Lamouette Station. But there are hundreds of people and Marcel loses the two men.</p>

<p>Then he sees an old mouse. "Excuse me," he says. "Do you know where Zazar lives?"</p>

<p>But when Marcel finds Zazar's house, it's too late.</p>

<p>"Yes, two men. I'm telling the police," she's talking on the telephone. "And they've got the White Star."</p>

<p>At that moment, Marcel hears a motor start. "Where's that coming from?" he thinks. Then he sees something at the end of the road.</p>

<p>"It's them. It's the thieves. And they're stealing a car."</p>

<p>He runs across Zazar's garden and down the street. He can see the car's number plate. It's near. Very near.</p>

<p>"Can I?" he thinks. And he jumps.</p>

<p>Yes. Marcel sits on the number plate. "Good," he thinks. Then, after a second or two, "What happens now?"</p>

<summary>Chapters 3-4</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapters 3-4</div>
  <div class="chapter-title"></div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">The thieves drive across Paris very fast. Marcel can hear them in the car. They're laughing and talking. But Marcel is not laughing. He is very, very angry.</p>

<p>After half an hour, the car stops next to a cafe. It's late and the cafe is quiet. In one corner is a woman. The thieves sit at her table. Marcel sits under the table and listens.</p>

<p>"Have you got it?" the woman asks.</p>

<p>The tall man takes a box from his jacket. Then he opens it. "Look," he says.</p>

<p>The woman puts her hand to her mouth. "What a beautiful diamond."</p>

<p>"This is it," thinks Marcel. "This is the moment."</p>

<p>He bites the tall man's leg very hard. The tall man throws up his arms. The box and the White Star fly across the room.</p>

<p>"What's that on the floor?" one waiter asks.</p>

<p>"I don't know," says another. "Is it? No. It can't be a diamond."</p>

<p>But before he can finish, Marcel runs across the room. He puts the White Star around his neck. Then he runs to the door. The thieves jump up and follow him. Suddenly there are tables and chairs everywhere.</p>

<p>"What's happening?" an old man asks.</p>

<p>"I don't know," his wife answers. "Who can understand young people these days?"</p>

<p>"Stop that mouse!" says the tall man.</p>

<p>"Shut the door!" says the short one.</p>

<p>But they're too late. Marcel runs out of the cafe and doesn't look back. After ten minutes, he stops. There's nobody following him.</p>

<p>But where is he? Marcel looks right and left. Then he sees a big white church. "Ah, Sacré Coeur," he thinks. "Now I know where I am."</p>

<p>It's late and there are no metro trains. But Marcel isn't tired. He walks back to Zazar's house. When he gets there, she's asleep in bed.</p>

<p>There's a table next to the window. On it, Marcel can see lots of photographs, boxes and perfume bottles. He runs across the floor and up one leg of the table.</p>

<p>"Now," he thinks, "it's time to take off the White Star. In the morning, Zazar is going to be very..." then Marcel stops. "Oh, no."</p>

<p>He can't take the ring off. He pulls and pulls. But nothing happens.</p>

<p>"Now what?" he thinks. "Then, of course. Some soap."</p>

<p>He runs down the table leg and across the floor. Zazar's bathroom door is open. Marcel goes in and looks up. A soap dish is above his head.</p>

<p>Ten seconds later, he's sitting in it. He puts some soap on his neck. Then he closes his eyes and pulls again.</p>

<p>"Yes!" This time, the diamond ring comes off.</p>

<summary>Chapters 5-6</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapters 5-6</div>
  <div class="chapter-title"></div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">In the morning, Zazar finds the White Star. It's on her bedroom table between two perfume bottles.</p>

<p>"But I don't understand," she says. "Fairy tales don't bring things back. How?"</p>

<p>She looks at the ring. Then she looks at her face in the mirror. Then she looks at the ring again. "And why is there soap on it?"</p>

<p>She telephones the police. Next day, Zazar's story is in all the newspapers.</p>

<p>"Opera star's one million pound ring is safe," says one.</p>

<p>"Thieves bring back stolen diamond," says another. "Now police ask why?"</p>

<p>And there are lots of photos of Zazar with the White Star. At home, at the Opera House, standing in front of the Eiffel Tower. She looks very happy in all of them.</p>

<p>Marcel's happy too. Back on his boat, he reads the newspaper stories. Then he looks in the mirror. There's a thin red line on his neck.</p>

<p>"Oh, what a night," he says.</p>

<p>After that, he washes, has breakfast, and opens the window. It's a beautiful, warm morning. Marcel looks up at the blue sky and smiles.</p>

<p>"Now," he thinks, "what's going to happen today?"</p>

* Где живёт Марсель в этой книге?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Он живёт на красивой старой лодке в Париже (on a beautiful old boat in Paris). Его дом находится под кухонным полом.
  </details>

* Что Марсель узнаёт из журнала, который читает высокий мужчина на станции метро?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Он узнаёт об оперной звезде Заза Дюпон (Zaza Dupont) и её бриллиантовом кольце за миллион фунтов под названием «Белая звезда» (White Star).
  </details>

* Что говорит короткий мужчина о кольце?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Он говорит: «Не после сегодняшней ночи» (Not after tonight). Это намёк, что они собираются украсть кольцо.
  </details>

* Куда Марсель прыгает, чтобы не потерять воров из виду?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Он прыгает на номерной знак машины (numberplate) и едет на нём через весь Париж.
  </details>

* Кто ждёт воров в кафе и что Марсель делает с высоким вором?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  В кафе их ждёт женщина. Марсель очень сильно кусает высокого вора за ногу (bites the tall man's leg very hard).
  </details>

* Что происходит с коробкой и кольцом после того, как Марсель кусает вора?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Вор вскидывает руки, коробка и кольцо летят через всю комнату (fly across the room).
  </details>

* Как Марсель надевает кольцо на себя?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Он бежит через комнату, надевает кольцо на шею (puts the White Star around his neck) и выбегает в дверь.
  </details>

* Где Марсель проводит ночь и какая у него возникает проблема с кольцом?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Он приходит в дом Заза Дюпон. Проблема: он не может снять кольцо с шеи (cannot take the ring off).
  </details>

* Что помогает Марселю снять кольцо?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Мыло (soap). Он намыливает шею и снимает кольцо.
  </details>

* Где Заза находит кольцо утром и что странного она замечает?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Она находит кольцо на столике в спальне между двумя флаконами духов. На кольце есть мыло (soap on it).
  </details>

* Что газеты пишут об этой истории?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Пишут: «Кольцо оперной звезды за миллион фунтов в безопасности» и «Воры вернули бриллиант Дюпон». Полиция спрашивает: «Почему?»
  </details>

* Что остаётся на шее у Марселя после этой ночи?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Тонкая красная полоска (a thin, red line).
  </details>
