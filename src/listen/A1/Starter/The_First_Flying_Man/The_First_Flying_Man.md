  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">My story begins in the 1630s. It is the time of the Ottoman Empire. Hezarfen Ahmed Çelebi is a clever young man of those days. He lives in the district of Üsküdar in Istanbul.</p>

<p>Every morning Hezarfen sits near the Bosphorus Strait. He looks at the tall tower in the district of Galata across the water. The Galata Tower is taller than all the buildings near it.</p>

<p>"Simit! Would you like a simit?" A man near Hezarfen calls. He has some tasty Turkish bread rings on his head. But Hezarfen doesn't answer.</p>

<p>Every morning the man cries, "Would you like a simit?" But Hezarfen never answers. He doesn't hear or see the man. He is dreaming. In his dream, he is flying over the Bosphorus with the seabirds.</p>

<p>"One day I want to look down from the sky on Istanbul," he thinks. "I want to fly across the strait with you, birds. But how?"</p>

<summary>Chapter 2</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter 2</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Drawings from Italy</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Weeks later, Hezarfen is near the Bosphorus again. Again he is looking carefully up at the seabirds in the sky. These days he is making some big wings in his workshop. He needs some help with the design.</p>

<p>"Hezarfen!" Someone calls. Hezarfen sees his friend, Mehmed, with a second man. He goes over and says hello to the two of them.</p>

<p>"Hezarfen, this is my brother Ali," Mehmed says. "He's got some drawings for you from Italy."</p>

<p>"They are old drawings of birds' wings by Leonardo da Vinci," Ali says.</p>

<p>"Ah yes, I know Leonardo's work," Hezarfen says. "Let's see them then."</p>

<p>Ali gives the drawings to Hezarfen. He looks at them for a minute. "Interesting," Hezarfen says. Then he gives them back. "Thank you, Ali."</p>

<p>Days later, in Hezarfen's workshop, "My wings are ready," Hezarfen tells his friends.</p>

<p>"Let's see you fly then," Mehmed cries.</p>

<p>"Your brother, Lagari, wants to fly too. I..."</p>

<summary>Chapter 3</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter 3</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">To Galata</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Hezarfen doesn't fly. He falls. Mehmed hears a big crash and a cry from the foot of the hill. He goes quickly down the hill to his friend. Hezarfen is lying under his wings. Ali is next to him.</p>

<p>"His leg's broken," Ali says.</p>

<p>"No, it isn't," Hezarfen answers. "My wings are broken, but I'm okay. This time."</p>

<p>Mehmed cries, "But, what about next time? Be careful, my friend. You don't want to die."</p>

<p>They take the broken wings back to Hezarfen's workshop.</p>

<p>"Now what?" Ali asks.</p>

<p>"I understand more. The design of my wings needs to be different. Bigger and better. But that can wait," Hezarfen says. "Right now, I must go and see the district chief of Galata."</p>

<p>"Galata? Why?" Mehmed cries.</p>

<p>"I want to fly from the Galata Tower, so I need his permission for that."</p>

<p>"Forget it. The district chief never lets people into his office," Ali says.</p>

<p>"Let's see," Hezarfen cries. "Come on!"</p>

<p>And he and his friends take a boat across the Bosphorus.</p>

<p>The three friends walk from the Bosphorus up to the Galata Tower. Two tall guards stop them when they arrive.</p>

<p>"What do you want?" one of them asks.</p>

<p>"We want to see the district chief of Galata," Hezarfen answers. Ali and Mehmed are afraid.</p>

<p>"What's your name?" the guard asks.</p>

<p>"Hezarfen Ahmed Çelebi."</p>

<p>"Ah, the brother of the rocket maker, Lagari Hassan Çelebi. All Istanbul is talking of him," the guard says.</p>

<p>"Yes," Hezarfen answers tiredly. "So, can I see the district chief?" he asks the man.</p>

<p>"Wait!" the guard says. And he goes into the district chief's office.</p>

<summary>Chapter 4</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter 4</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">A Brother's Help</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Sometime later, the guard takes Hezarfen to the district chief's office. Mehmed and Ali wait at the door. The district chief listens to Hezarfen's story.</p>

<p>"Very well," he smiles. "You can fly from the tower. But be careful. Don't fall and die."</p>

<p>"No, district chief," Hezarfen answers.</p>

<p>"And I'd like to see your brother. Tell him."</p>

<p>"Yes, district chief," Hezarfen says.</p>

<p>"I got permission!" Hezarfen laughs when he comes out into the street.</p>

<p>"Good!" Ali cries happily.</p>

<p>"What now?" Mehmed asks.</p>

<p>"I must visit Lagari," Hezarfen says.</p>

<p>So, Hezarfen visits his older brother. Lagari is working on his rocket when Hezarfen arrives.</p>

<p>"Nice design," Hezarfen says.</p>

<p>"Yes," Lagari answers. "Perhaps early next year, I can fly up into the sky in it. What about you?"</p>

<p>"Me, brother. I want to fly across the sky with my wings later this year."</p>

<p>"So, it's a race," Lagari laughs.</p>

<p>"Yes," Hezarfen smiles. "But listen. The district chief of Galata wants to see you."</p>

<p>"Right. Well, thank you for your visit. Good luck with your wings, Hezarfen."</p>

<p>"Good luck with your rocket, Lagari."</p>

<p>And Hezarfen leaves.</p>

<p>Two weeks later, Hezarfen's new wings are ready. "Now I can fly from Galata to Üsküdar!" he cries.</p>

<p>"Wait! You must ask the Sultan's permission for that, I think," Mehmed says. "And the Sultan says no to most people when they ask for things."</p>

<p>"So, what can I do?" Hezarfen cries.</p>

<p>"Listen. These days, the district chief of Galata talks about your brother a lot. Now the Sultan's interested in him too. Perhaps Lagari can ask the Sultan for a meeting," Ali says.</p>

<p>So, Hezarfen goes and speaks to his brother again.</p>

<p>"Yes, I can ask the Sultan," Lagari smiles. "But, he can always say no to a meeting."</p>

<summary>Chapter 5</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter 5</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">What Does the Sultan Say?</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">So, Lagari speaks to the Sultan. And the Sultan says yes to a meeting.</p>

<p>Seven days later, Hezarfen goes to the palace. He walks past the Hagia Sophia. He is nervous. "Please give me permission," he thinks.</p>

<p>Near the Blue Mosque, he is more nervous. "Please say yes," he thinks.</p>

<p>Hezarfen stops near the palace. "The Sultan can, of course, say no. Or worse, he can say, kill that man," he thinks.</p>

<p>"I'm Hezarfen Ahmed Çelebi. The Sultan wants to see me," he tells the palace guard. The man goes in. Hezarfen waits. He is very nervous now.</p>

<p>After an hour, the guard comes back. "Come with me," he says to Hezarfen.</p>

<p>Hezarfen bows very nervously when he meets the Sultan. "Your Highness. I want to fly across the Bosphorus," he says.</p>

<p>The Sultan smiles. "Ah, yes. You're Lagari Hassan's brother," he says.</p>

<p>"Yes, your Highness," Hezarfen answers. Then he asks for the Sultan's permission.</p>

<p>"You have it," the Sultan says. "You must fly across the Bosphorus tomorrow."</p>

<p>"With a good wind," Hezarfen says. "But without it, what then, your Highness?"</p>

<p>"You must ask God for a wind," the Sultan smiles.</p>

<p>"Your Highness," a vizier says quietly. "People can walk with their legs. But we don't have wings. Is a flying man good in God's eyes?"</p>

<p>"Listen," the Sultan answers. "We can think of new things with our brains. And our brains come from God. The first flying man must be an Ottoman, I say."</p>

<p>"Yes, your Highness," the vizier says. But he looks angrily at Hezarfen.</p>

<p>That evening, Hezarfen is not happy. "There's no wind," he tells Mehmed and Ali.</p>

<summary>Chapter 6</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter 6</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Hezarfen's Reward</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">The next morning, there is lots of wind. Hezarfen stands happily with his wings at the top of the Galata Tower. Suddenly, he jumps.</p>

<p>"He's falling!" people cry. But he is not falling. He is flying with his big white wings over the houses of Galata.</p>

<p>Hezarfen's friends Mehmed and Ali run with the people down the streets to the Bosphorus. Hezarfen flies out over the water.</p>

<p>"I'm flying!" he cries. "People can fly!"</p>

<p>He lands across the strait in Üsküdar, a happy man.</p>

<p>The next day, the Sultan has a meeting with his viziers at the palace. "People can fly!" he cries excitedly. "And the first flying man is an Ottoman. That's good for our empire. Hezarfen is a good man. Hezarfen needs a reward."</p>

<p>"But your highness," the vizier nearest to him says. "Flying men can land in our palaces and kill us. They're dangerous."</p>

<p>The Sultan thinks about this. "Yes, I see. Perhaps you're right. So, what must we do with Hezarfen then?" he asks.</p>

<p>"Kill him," the vizier answers.</p>

<p>"I can't do that!" the Sultan cries.</p>

<p>"Then, exile him," the vizier says.</p>

<p>So, the Sultan calls Hezarfen Ahmed Çelebi to the palace. He gives a reward of a thousand gold coins to him. Then, he exiles him to Algeria.</p>

<p>Hezarfen says goodbye to his brother and his friends. He leaves Istanbul that evening. After that, he can fly over the Bosphorus with the birds in his dreams. But he can never go back to Istanbul again.</p>

<p>"And what about Lagari and his rocket?" you ask. Well, perhaps one day I can tell you that story too.</p>

* В каком веке происходит действие и где живёт Хезарфен Ахмет Челеби?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Действие происходит в 1630-х годах (1630s), во времена Османской империи (Ottoman Empire). Он живёт в Стамбуле, в районе Ускюдар (Üsküdar).
  </details>

* О чём Хезарфен мечтает каждое утро у пролива Босфор?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Он мечтает летать над Босфором с морскими птицами и смотреть на Стамбул с неба.
  </details>

* Что за рисунки привозит Али из Италии?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Это старые рисунки птичьих крыльев (old drawings of birds' wings), сделанные Леонардо да Винчи (Leonardo da Vinci).
  </details>

* Кто такой Лагари и что он хочет сделать?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Лагари — старший брат Хезарфена. Он хочет сделать ракету (rocket).
  </details>

* Что происходит во время первой попытки Хезарфена полететь с холма?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Он не летит, а падает (He falls). Крылья ломаются, но сам он не серьёзно травмирован (только нога болит, но не сломана).
  </details>

* С кого хочет лететь Хезарфен и чьё разрешение ему нужно?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Он хочет лететь с Галатской башни (Galata Tower). Ему нужно разрешение районного начальника Галаты (District Chief of Galata).
  </details>

* Кто должен дать окончательное разрешение на полёт через Босфор?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Султан (the Sultan).
  </details>

* Как визирь (vizier) пытается отговорить султана от разрешения?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Он говорит: «Люди ходят на ногах. У нас нет крыльев. Хорошо ли летающий человек в глазах Бога?»
  </details>

* Что отвечает султан визирю?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Он говорит: «Мы можем придумывать новое нашим мозгом. А наш мозг от Бога. Первый летающий человек должен быть османом».
  </details>

* Что происходит на следующее утро после разговора с султаном?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Поднимается сильный ветер (lots of wind). Хезарфен прыгает с Галатской башни и летит через Босфор. Он приземляется в Ускюдаре.
  </details>

* Какую награду и какое наказание получает Хезарфен?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Он получает 1000 золотых монет (a thousand gold coins), но султан отправляет его в ссылку в Алжир (exiles him to Algeria).
  </details>

* Почему султан отправляет Хезарфена в ссылку?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Визирь говорит, что летающие люди могут приземляться во дворцах и убивать их. Они опасны (They're dangerous).
  </details>
