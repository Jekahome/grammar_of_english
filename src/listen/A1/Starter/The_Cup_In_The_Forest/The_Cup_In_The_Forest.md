  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Per and his girlfriend Nina live in a big town in Norway. They are on holiday in the country. They are driving on a road near a big forest.</p>

<p>"It's beautiful here," Per says. "Let's stop the car and walk in the forest."</p>

<p>But the forest is very dark and quiet.</p>

<p>"I don't like this place," Nina says. "I'm cold. Let's go back to the car."</p>

<p>Suddenly, Per falls over a tree.</p>

<p>"Are you okay?" Nina asks.</p>

<p>"Yes," Per answers. Then he sees some old stones. "These are very strange stones," he says. He moves them with his hands. They are very heavy.</p>

<p>"There's a hole under the stones," he says. He puts his hand into the hole.</p>

<p>"Be careful," Nina says. "Perhaps an animal lives in there."</p>

<p>Per takes his hand from the hole. He has an old cup in his hand.</p>

<p>"Look at this old cup, Nina," he says. "It's very interesting. But why is it here? And look, there are some strange words on it."</p>

<p>Nina looks at the cup too. "It's beautiful," she says. She looks again. "And very expensive," she thinks.</p>

<p>The forest is very dark and cold. "Let's go now," Nina says. "I don't like this forest."</p>

<p>They walk back to their car. Suddenly, Per stops.</p>

<p>"What is it?" Nina asks.</p>

<p>Per looks into the trees. "There's a man there," he says.</p>

<p>Nina looks too. "I can't see a man," she says.</p>

<p>Per looks again. "You're right," he says. "It's only a tree."</p>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter 2</div>
  <div class="chapter-title"></div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Per and Nina drive to a small town. "It's getting dark," Per says. "Let's stay in this town."</p>

<p>They find a small hotel. The people are very friendly. "I like this hotel," Nina says. "Let's stay here."</p>

<p>Near the hotel is a museum. "We can take the cup to the museum in the morning," Per says. "We can ask the curator about it."</p>

<p>Later, Per can't sleep. He goes to the window of his room and looks into the hotel garden. A strange man is standing there. He doesn't move.</p>

<p>"Who are you?" Per calls. "What do you want?"</p>

<p>Then Nina comes into the room.</p>

<p>"There's a strange man in the garden," Per says.</p>

<p>"What man?" Nina asks. "I can't see a man."</p>

<p>In the morning, Per and Nina take the cup to the museum. The museum curator is an old man.</p>

<p>"Do you know about this cup?" Per asks.</p>

<p>The curator looks at the cup for a long time. "Where is it from?" he asks.</p>

<p>"From a hole under some old stones in the forest," Per says.</p>

<p>"Take the cup back to the forest," the curator says.</p>

<p>"But why?" Per asks.</p>

<p>"Do you see the old words on the cup?" the curator asks. "They say, 'This cup belongs to Harkon. Be careful.'"</p>

<p>"Who is Harkon?" Per asks.</p>

<p>"Harkon is an old Viking name," the curator says. "There are some Viking graves in the forest. This cup is from Harkon's grave. Take it back there. Now."</p>

<summary>Chapter 3</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter 3</div>
  <div class="chapter-title"></div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Per and Nina are drinking coffee in the hotel.</p>

<p>"Let's take the cup back to the forest," Per says.</p>

<p>"Are you crazy?" Nina says. "Why do you listen to the old man's stories? Harkon is only a dead Viking."</p>

<p>"Yes," Per says. "But I'm afraid. The cup belongs to him."</p>

<p>Suddenly, Per sees the strange man across the room. "Look," he says. "Harkon is here. He's in this cafe."</p>

<p>Per runs across the room, but Harkon isn't there. Then Per goes back to the table. But Nina isn't there. And the bag with the cup isn't there.</p>

<p>He runs to the door of the hotel. He sees Nina in the car. She is driving away from the hotel very fast.</p>

<p>"Stop, Nina!" Per calls. "Where are you going?"</p>

<p>But Nina doesn't stop. She is driving away with the cup.</p>

<p>Nina drives away from the town. She is driving fast, very fast. But she isn't afraid. She is thinking about money. Money is important to Nina. The cup is in a bag near her. Nina looks at it and smiles.</p>

<p>"Per is crazy," she thinks. "He's afraid of old stories about dead Vikings. But the cup belongs to me now. I'm taking it to Oslo. I can sell it there."</p>

<p>Per runs to the museum and finds the old curator.</p>

<p>"Where's the cup?" the curator asks.</p>

<p>"My girlfriend has it now," Per answers.</p>

<p>"That's bad," the curator says. "The cup belongs to Harkon."</p>

<p>"But Harkon is dead," Per says.</p>

<p>"Yes," the curator says. "But there are strange stories about Viking ghosts. Take my car and go after your girlfriend."</p>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter 4</div>
  <div class="chapter-title"></div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Nina drives for a long time. It is getting dark. There are no cars on the road. She can't see any towns or houses or people. Only forests. Dark, black forests.</p>

<p>But Nina thinks about money again and she is happy. Now it is raining.</p>

<p>Suddenly Nina sees a strange man. He is standing on the road. He is looking at Nina. His face is dark and angry.</p>

<p>Nina screams and screams. She is very afraid. She can't stop the car and she can't see the road. She only sees the man's angry face. His eyes are cold and strange. They are the eyes of a dead man.</p>

<p>The car leaves the road and hits a tree. Then it falls into a river. The water closes over the car. It is very quiet. The car, Nina and the cup are in the river.</p>

<p>Later the police come and take the car from the river. A policeman looks into the car.</p>

<p>"There's a girl in here," he says. "She's dead."</p>

<p>Per is there too. He is very unhappy about Nina. Suddenly he sees a man. The man is walking into the forest.</p>

<p>"The ghost of Harkon," Per thinks. He is very afraid.</p>

<p>But Harkon is walking away now. He has his cup.</p>

<summary>Проверить понимание текста</summary>

* Кто такие Пер и Нина и откуда они?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Пер и его девушка Нина живут в большом городе в Норвегии (a big town in Norway).
  </details>

* Что Пер находит в лесу под камнями?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Он находит старую чашу (an old cup) со странными словами на ней.
  </details>

* Кто такой Хакон (Hakon)?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Хакон — это древнее имя викинга (old Viking name). В лесу находятся могилы викингов, и чаша принадлежит Хакону.
  </details>

* Что говорит смотритель музея (curator), когда видит чашу?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Он говорит: «Верни чашу обратно в лес... сейчас же» (Take the cup back to the forest... now).
  </details>

* Что написано на чаше?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  «Эта чаша принадлежит Хакону. Будь осторожен!» (This cup belongs to Hakon. Be careful!)
  </details>

* Что делает Нина, когда Пер видит странного человека в кафе?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Она уезжает на машине с чашей (driving away with the cup). Деньги для неё важнее (Money is important to Nina).
  </details>

* Куда Нина хочет продать чашу?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  В Осло (Oslo).
  </details>

* Кого Нина видит на дороге во время грозы?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Она видит странного мужчину (a strange man) с тёмным и злым лицом — призрака Хакона.
  </details>

* Что происходит с машиной Нины?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Машина съезжает с дороги, врезается в дерево и падает в реку (hits a tree and falls into a river). Нина погибает.
  </details>

* Что видит Пер после того, как полиция достаёт машину из реки?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Он видит человека, который идёт в лес. Это призрак Хакона (the ghost of Hakon), и он забирает свою чашу.
  </details>
