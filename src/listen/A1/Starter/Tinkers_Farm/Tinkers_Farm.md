  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">New York, June, 1800. It's seven o'clock on a warm summer morning. A ship is arriving in New York. Its name is the Red Rose. Two of the people on it are Sam Tinker and his daughter, Jenny. Sam and Jenny are English, but now they're starting a new life in America.</p>

<p>"Look, Dad!" says Jenny. "We're here! We're really here!"</p>

<p>The Tinkers say goodbye to all their friends. Then they leave the Red Rose. There are lots of people, horses and carts beside the ship. Sam looks at them.</p>

<p>"Yes, we're here," he thinks. "But now what? We haven't got any money."</p>

<p>He stops a young man with a green coat. "Excuse me?" he says. "I'm looking for a job." The man turns to his right. "Talk to Jack Crane," he answers.</p>

<p>Jack Crane is a farmer. He has a long, thin face and only three fingers on his right hand. He listens to Sam's story. Then he takes off his hat.</p>

<p>"Okay," he says. "You can both have jobs. I need a new man to work in the fields. And we want someone to help in the kitchen too. Isn't that right, Blue Sky?"</p>

<p>There's an Indian girl on top of the cart. "Yes," she says, and smiles at Jenny.</p>

<p>Two hours later, the Tinkers leave New York with Jack Crane and Blue Sky. They are both very happy.</p>

<p>"How far is it to your farm, Mr Crane?" asks Sam.</p>

<p>"80 kilometres," Jack Crane answers.</p>

<p>They drive all afternoon. Jenny's sitting next to Sam. She's tired, but she doesn't want to sleep. "This is America," she thinks. "And now, I'm American."</p>

<p>After six hours, Jack Crane stops the cart. "Well, there it is," he says. Jenny stands up. It's evening now, and the sun's going down. In front of them, there are lots of beautiful yellow fields and a big farmhouse.</p>

<p>Jenny looks to her left. "Where's that smoke coming from?" she asks.</p>

<p>"That's the Indian village," answers Blue Sky. "My family lives there."</p>

<div class="book-page">

  <div class="chapter-title"></div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">The next day, the Tinkers start work. Jenny helps Blue Sky in the farmhouse. First, the two girls cook everyone's breakfast. Then they make all the beds. After that, they clean the kitchen floor.</p>

<p>At the same time, Sam's working in the fields. After four or five hours, he stops for a minute.</p>

<p>"What are you stopping for?" asks Jack Crane. "Come on, get back to work."</p>

<p>Sam and Jenny have only one free day every week. Sunday. It's Blue Sky's free day, too. That's when she visits her family. One Sunday, Jenny goes with her. She makes a lot of new friends. She meets the village chief, too, Silver Cloud. He has a beautiful wife and a young son.</p>

<p>After that first visit, Jenny often goes to the village. She starts to learn some Indian words, too. Jenny's always happy with her new Indian friends. But she's not happy on the farm. Sam's unhappy, too.</p>

<p>"I don't want to work here on Jack Crane's farm any more," he says one evening. "I want to work on my own farm. Tinker's farm."</p>

<p>"I know, Dad," Jenny answers. Then she thinks. "But how? We can't buy any land. Jack Crane pays us only two dollars a week."</p>

<p>Four days later, Jenny and Blue Sky are making the beds. Suddenly, they hear something. Two men are shouting. Jenny looks out of the window. She can see Jack Crane and her father in front of the farmhouse. Jack Crane's face is very red.</p>

<p>"Do it!" he shouts.</p>

<p>"No! I'm not going to!" Sam shouts back. "It's Sunday, and I don't work on Sundays. You do it!"</p>

<p>Jenny runs downstairs and out of the house. There's a strong wind and it's raining.</p>

<p>"What's happening?" she asks her father.</p>

<p>"We're leaving in the morning," Sam answers. He's walking very fast. Jenny looks at him.</p>

<p>"But how are we going to eat? We haven't got any money."</p>

<p>Sam doesn't answer. His eyes are cold and hard.</p>

<summary>Chapter 3</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter 3</div>
  <div class="chapter-title"></div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">That evening, Jenny and Blue Sky go to the Indian village. "This is going to be my last visit," Jenny thinks. Blue Sky's sad too. She doesn't want to lose her friend.</p>

<p>The two girls say nothing for 20 minutes. Then Blue Sky stops her horse. She can see something in the river to their right. It's a small boy.</p>

<p>"Help!" he's shouting. "I can't swim! Help me! Please!"</p>

<p>"It's the chief's son," says Blue Sky. "What are we going to do?"</p>

<p>But before she can say another word, Jenny gets off her horse.</p>

<p>"I'm coming!" she shouts. Then she jumps into the cold river.</p>

<p>"Help!" shouts the boy.</p>

<p>Jenny swims to him. His head is going under the water. She puts one arm around him.</p>

<p>"It's okay," she says. "You're safe now."</p>

<p>Half an hour later, Jenny and Blue Sky arrive at the Indian village. The chief comes to meet them.</p>

<p>"But I don't understand," he says to Blue Sky. "Why is my son with you? And why are he and the English girl wet?"</p>

<p>Blue Sky tells him the story. After that, the chief's very happy. He smiles at Jenny.</p>

<p>"How can I thank you?" he says. "Please, let me give you something. Anything."</p>

<p>At nine o'clock, Sam's putting some boots into a bag. "What's going to happen tomorrow?" he thinks. Suddenly, the door opens. It's Jenny.</p>

<p>"Come with me, Dad," she says. "I want to show you something."</p>

<p>Sam looks at his daughter. Her eyes are bright. And she's smiling.</p>

<p>"What are you talking about?" he asks.</p>

<p>"There isn't time for any questions," says Jenny. "Come on!"</p>

<p>She takes her father to a hill. At the top, she says, "Look! Can you see that land over there?"</p>

<p>"Yes," Sam answers.</p>

<p>Then Jenny tells him about Silver Cloud's son. After she finishes, Sam says, "You mean...?"</p>

<p>"Yes!" Jenny smiles at her father. "The Indians are giving us our own farm. Tinker's farm. Oh, Dad. Isn't it wonderful? Now we can really start a new life."</p>

* Как называется корабль, на котором Сэм и Дженни Тинкер прибывают в Нью-Йорк?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Корабль называется «Красная Роза» (Red Rose).
  </details>

* Кто такой Джек Крейн и сколько пальцев у него на правой руке?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Джек Крейн — фермер (farmer). У него только три пальца на правой руке.
  </details>

* Как зовут индейскую девушку, которая работает в доме Джека Крейна?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Её зовут Голубое Небо (Blue Sky).
  </details>

* Сколько километров ехать от Нью-Йорка до фермы Джека Крейна?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Восемьдесят километров (eighty kilometres).
  </details>

* Сколько свободных дней в неделю у Сэма и Дженни?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Только один день — воскресенье (Sunday).
  </details>

* Как зовут вождя индейского племени?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Серебряное Облако (Silver Cloud).
  </details>

* Почему Сэм ссорится с Джеком Крейном?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Джек Крейн приказывает Сэму работать в воскресенье, но Сэм отказывается.
  </details>

* Кого Дженни спасает из реки?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Она спасает маленького мальчика — сына вождя (the Chief's son).
  </details>

* Как вождь благодарит Дженни за спасение сына?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Индейцы дарят Сэму и Дженни собственную ферму (their own farm) под названием «Ферма Тинкеров» (Tinkers Farm).
  </details>

* О чём Дженни говорит отцу в конце истории?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Она говорит: «Теперь мы действительно можем начать новую жизнь» (Now we can really start a new life).
  </details>
