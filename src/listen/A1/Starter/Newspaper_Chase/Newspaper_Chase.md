    <p class="first-para">The time is one o'clock in the morning. The place is the Rixville Town Art Gallery. A window opens and a man comes in. His name is Harry Black, and he is a thief.</p>

<p>It is dark in the art gallery, but Harry has a light. He looks across the room at a painting.</p>

<p>"There it is," he says.</p>

<p>Harry moves quickly across the room. He stands and looks at the painting.</p>

<p>"A million dollars for this," he thinks. "I don't understand it."</p>

<p>But he takes a knife from his coat. Then he takes the painting very, very slowly from its frame.</p>

<p>Harry goes back across the room to the window, but he walks into a table. There is a beautiful blue glass vase on the table. It falls on the floor and breaks into a hundred pieces.</p>

<p>Harry smiles. "Is that a million dollar vase?" he thinks. "It isn't now."</p>

<p>He runs across the pieces of glass to the window.</p>

  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Harry has a room in Mrs. Allen's house. He runs quietly up to his room and closes the door. Mrs. Allen and her daughter, Janie, are sleeping. They don't hear him.</p>

<p>In his room, Harry takes the painting from his bag. He puts it in a newspaper. Then he puts the newspaper under his bed.</p>

<p>In the morning, Janie Allen is in the kitchen. She's putting old bottles into a box.</p>

<p>"Recycling is important," Janie thinks.</p>

<p>On the TV, a reporter is at the Rixville Art Gallery. He is talking about the painting.</p>

<p>"It's a million dollar painting," he is saying. "Here's a photo of it."</p>

<p>Now the reporter is talking about the blue glass vase. "It's in a hundred pieces now," he says.</p>

<p>Janie looks at the photo of the vase. Then she asks her mother, "Do you have any old bottles?"</p>

<p>"No," Mrs. Allen says. "That's all, Janie. But the newspaper recycling truck is coming today."</p>

<p>"Of course," Janie says. "It's Friday."</p>

  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Harry isn't in his room. He is talking on his telephone to a man in Seattle. The man wants the painting, but Harry isn't happy.</p>

<p>"$5,000?" Harry says. "No, it's a million dollar painting. What? No, I want 50,000, not five. What?"</p>

<p>"The painting. Yes, I have it. And it's okay."</p>

<p>Janie is looking for old newspapers. Early on Friday mornings, she takes them from every room in the house. Then, later, the newspaper recycling truck arrives.</p>

<p>Janie opens Harry's door and looks into his room. She always takes his old newspapers or bottles for recycling.</p>

<p>"Ah," she thinks. "There's a newspaper under Harry's bed."</p>

<p>Janie puts the old newspapers into a black recycling box. She runs from the house and sees the truck.</p>

<p>"Wait," she says, and she quickly gives the box to one of the men.</p>

<p>Harry is coming back to the house. He sees the recycling truck, and he sees Janie.</p>

<p>"It's Friday," he says. "The newspaper. Oh, no!"</p>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter 4</div>
  <div class="chapter-title"></div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Harry chases after the truck.</p>

<p>"Wait, wait!" he says.</p>

<p>Janie watches him. "What is he doing?" she thinks.</p>

<p>Harry jumps into the back of the truck. "I want my newspaper," he says. "Where's my newspaper?"</p>

<p>But there are thousands of newspapers in the truck. Janie walks quickly from the house to the truck.</p>

<p>"Why is your newspaper important, Harry?" she asks. "It's two days old."</p>

<p>But Harry doesn't hear her. He is thinking, "My million dollar painting. Where is it?"</p>

<p>The men from the recycling truck are watching Harry too. But now Janie is looking at Harry's shoe. There's some blue glass on Harry's shoe, Janie thinks.</p>

<p>"Where?"</p>

<p>Suddenly, she remembers the photo of the blue glass vase on the TV. She looks again at the piece of glass on Harry's shoe.</p>

<p>"Is it from the vase in the art gallery?" she thinks.</p>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter 5</div>
  <div class="chapter-title"></div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">The men from the recycling truck are angry. "We're going now," they are saying. "We're late."</p>

<p>"But I want my newspaper!" Harry says.</p>

<p>In the house, Janie is talking to the police on the telephone.</p>

<p>"Maybe I'm wrong," she is saying. "But there's blue glass in his shoe."</p>

<p>"What?"</p>

<p>"Yes, he's looking for the newspaper now."</p>

<p>Two policemen arrive quickly. "Let's look at your shoe," they say to Harry.</p>

<p>Harry doesn't understand. "What's wrong?" he asks.</p>

<p>One of the policemen takes the glass from Harry's shoe. "This is a very expensive piece of glass," he says, "from a very expensive vase. Remember?"</p>

<p>Suddenly, Harry understands. "Oh, no," he says.</p>

<p>Police cars and policemen arrive. The men look in the truck for the million dollar painting. Later, they find the right newspaper and they find the painting.</p>

<p>"Good work, Janie," one of the policemen says. "And there's a reward."</p>

<p>"Maybe I can buy a painting with the reward," Janie says. "I like pictures."</p>

<summary>Проверить понимание текста</summary>

* Кто такой Гарри Блэк и что он делает в художественной галерее в час ночи?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Гарри Блэк — вор (thief). Он залезает в галерею, чтобы украсть картину за миллион долларов.
  </details>

* Что происходит, когда Гарри идёт обратно к окну?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Он натыкается на стол (walks into a table). На столе стоит красивая синяя стеклянная ваза. Она падает на пол и разбивается на сотню кусочков.
  </details>

* Где живёт Гарри и кто его хозяйка?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Он живёт в комнате в пансионе миссис Аллен (Mrs. Allen's rooming house). У неё есть дочь Джейни (Janey).
  </details>

* Куда Гарри прячет украденную картину?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Он заворачивает её в газету и кладёт под кровать (puts it in a newspaper, then puts the newspaper under his bed).
  </details>

* Что Джейни делает по утрам в пятницу?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Она собирает старые газеты и бутылки из каждой комнаты для переработки (recycling).
  </details>

* Какую газету Джейни забирает из комнаты Гарри?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Она забирает газету, которая лежит под кроватью Гарри — ту самую, в которую завернута картина.
  </details>

* Почему Гарри бежит за мусоровозом?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Он хочет вернуть свою газету, потому что в ней находится украденная картина за миллион долларов.
  </details>

* Что Джейни замечает на ботинке Гарри?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Она замечает кусочек синего стекла (blue glass) и вспоминает фото разбитой вазы из галереи по телевизору.
  </details>

* Кого Джейни вызывает и что полицейские находят в ботинке Гарри?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Она вызывает полицию. Полицейские достают из ботинка Гарри очень дорогой кусочек стекла (a very expensive piece of glass) из той самой вазы.
  </details>

* Чем заканчивается история?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Полиция находит картину в мусоровозе. Джейни получает награду (reward) и говорит, что купит на неё картину.
  </details>
</details>
