<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter 1</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Holmes is Ill</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">It is 1890 in London. At 221B Baker Street, Mrs Hudson, Sherlock Holmes's servant, is sad. "Things are very bad. Holmes is dying. Three days now. And he is worse," she thinks.</p>

<p>Mrs Hudson likes Holmes very much. But she is also a little afraid of him. He is different. He has strange visitors. And they come at all hours of the day and night. His rooms have many strange things in them. But he is very nice to Mrs Hudson. She respects him. And she does not want to lose him.</p>

<p>"I must visit Dr Watson," she thinks. "He can help."</p>

<p>At this time Dr Watson is living in a different street in London. Mrs Hudson goes quickly to his rooms.</p>

<p>"Dr Watson!" she cries when the doctor opens the door to her. "Please come with me. It is Mr Holmes. He is dying."</p>

<p>"What?" Watson cries. "I know nothing about this."</p>

<p>"He doesn't want a doctor. But you must come, sir. After three days he is very, very ill. He is saying strange things. I am afraid for him. You must be quick."</p>

<p>Watson gets his coat and bag, and they leave the house. In the cab he asks Mrs Hudson some more questions.</p>

<p>"I can't tell you much, sir. Lately Mr Holmes is working in Rotherhithe near the river. He comes home and he brings this illness with him. He is much worse now. Can he live through this?"</p>

<summary>Chapter 2</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter 2</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Watson Cannot Help</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">When they arrive at Baker Street on that dark November day, Holmes is alive. But Watson is afraid for his friend. The detective's face is yellow and his eyes are red with fever. His hands move without stopping. When he speaks, his voice is weak.</p>

<p>"Well, Watson, bad days are here, I think." He smiles weakly.</p>

<p>"My poor man!" cries Watson, and he moves towards the bed.</p>

<p>"Don't come near me."</p>

<p>"But why? I only want to help."</p>

<p>"I know, but you can't help me. My illness is from the Far East and it's very contagious."</p>

<p>"I'm a doctor," says Watson quietly. "These things don't matter to me."</p>

<p>"Don't move and I can talk to you. If not, you must leave the room now."</p>

<p>Holmes looks at Watson with fever in his eyes. "I can't trust you with this, Watson. You know nothing of these strange illnesses. Do you know about Tapuli fever, for example? Or the black Formosa fever? No, you don't. You aren't a specialist. I'm sorry to say this, but I must see a specialist."</p>

<p>Watson is not happy at these words, but he also respects the detective.</p>

<p>"Very well, Holmes. Dr Ainstree, the best specialist in these illnesses, is now in London. I'm going to him now."</p>

<p>Watson walks towards the door. Suddenly Holmes is out of bed. He goes quickly to the door and locks it. Then he goes very slowly back to bed.</p>

<p>Watson looks at his friend in shock. What is the matter with Holmes?</p>

<summary>Chapter 3</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter 3</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Holmes is Worse</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Holmes cries excitedly, "Stay here, Watson. I've got the key. What time is it?"</p>

<p>"Four o'clock."</p>

<p>"At six o'clock you can go, but not before."</p>

<p>Watson looks at his friend sadly. "Holmes. This illness is doing strange things to your head. But I can wait until six."</p>

<p>"Good. One more thing, Watson. Don't bring that man Ainstree. Now perhaps I can sleep a little."</p>

<p>Watson waits, looking at the pictures and books in the room. On a table he sees a little black and white box. "Hmm, that's interesting," he thinks. He moves his hand towards it.</p>

<p>Suddenly a terrible cry comes from the bed. "Stop, Watson! Don't move my things! I don't like it! Sit down, man! I need to sleep."</p>

<p>At six o'clock Holmes says, "Let's have some light, Watson. But only a little. Put the box near me. And not with your hands. Use your handkerchief. That's right. Now you can go to Mr Culverton Smith of 13 Lower Burke Street."</p>

<p>"But who is this man, Holmes? I don't know the name."</p>

<p>"He isn't a doctor. He has a plantation in Sumatra, but he's visiting London. He knows this illness well because his workers often have it. Things are not good between us. Smith's nephew, Victor Savage, is dead. There is something wrong about his terrible death. I don't trust Smith and he knows it. But only he can help me. There is one more thing. Don't come back with him. You must be here before him. This is very important, Watson."</p>

<p>Watson takes the key and he goes into the street. He sees Inspector Morton of Scotland Yard.</p>

<p>"How is Mr Holmes, sir?" Morton asks.</p>

<p>"He's very ill," Watson answers.</p>

<p>Morton smiles strangely. "I know something about it," he says.</p>

<p>Watson doesn't understand. But he cannot wait. He gets into a cab and leaves.</p>

<summary>Chapter 4</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter 4</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">The Specialist</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">At 13 Lower Burke Street, a servant answers the door. Watson goes into the house. He can hear Culverton Smith's voice.</p>

<p>"Oh, it's Watson. What does he want? I need to work. I cannot see him."</p>

<p>Watson thinks of Holmes. Quickly, he goes into the room. The man in the room stands up from his chair. Watson sees his dark, angry eyes.</p>

<p>"What's this?" the man cries.</p>

<p>"I'm sorry," says Watson. "But I can't wait. It's about Mr Sherlock Holmes."</p>

<p>Culverton Smith's face changes. He watches Watson carefully. "What about Holmes? How is he?"</p>

<p>"He's very ill." Watson sits down. Is that a smile on Smith's face?</p>

<p>"I'm sorry to hear this," Smith says. "I don't know Holmes well, but I respect him. We're not very different. He studies criminals, and I study microbes." He looks at the bottles on the table next to him and laughs.</p>

<p>"Holmes needs you," Watson says.</p>

<p>"Why? Is his illness from the Far East? Perhaps his work lately is with people from there."</p>

<p>"Oh, I see." Smith smiles. "How many days is it now? About three? Is he thinking well?"</p>

<p>"Not always."</p>

<p>"That is bad. I must go and help him."</p>

<p>Watson remembers Holmes's words. He says, "I can't go with you. I need to make a visit."</p>

<p>"Very well," Smith says. "I can be there in half an hour."</p>

<summary>Chapter 5</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter 5</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">A Killer at Baker Street</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Watson runs into Holmes's room.</p>

<p>"Is he coming?" Holmes asks.</p>

<p>"Yes, he is."</p>

<p>"Very good, Watson. Hmm. Now you must hide behind the bed. Don't speak or move. Just listen very carefully."</p>

<p>Watson hides behind the bed and waits. Soon he hears Culverton Smith's voice.</p>

<p>"Can you hear me, Holmes?"</p>

<p>"Is that you, Mr Smith?" Holmes says weakly. "Thank you for coming."</p>

<p>Smith laughs. "What's the matter with you, Holmes?"</p>

<p>"It's Victor's illness, I think."</p>

<p>"Yes. My nephew. Four days ill and he's dead." Smith laughs again. "And you are the criminal, Smith. But you can't prove it. And now you need me."</p>

<p>"Give me water," Holmes says weakly.</p>

<p>"All right. But only because I want to say something to you."</p>

<p>Smith moves towards the bed. "Your illness is from men working near the river, your friend says. But that's wrong. You aren't very quick, are you, Holmes?"</p>

<p>"I can't think. You must help me."</p>

<p>"Think carefully. Think about a box arriving here last Wednesday."</p>

<p>"A box. Yes. I remember now. The spring in the box... on my hand. The blood."</p>

<p>"That's right. And there are microbes of your illness on that spring. This box is the only proof. And I'm taking it with me. Now you know the truth. Now you can die."</p>

<p>"More light," says Holmes weakly.</p>

<p>Smith moves. And suddenly there is more light in the room.</p>

<p>"What more can I do for you?" Smith asks.</p>

<p>"And more water, please," Holmes says.</p>

<p>But what is happening? Holmes is speaking normally. His voice is not weak. Watson hears this, and he nearly cries out. He can feel Culverton Smith's shock.</p>

<summary>Chapter 6</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter 6</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">The Truth</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">"What is this? Aren't you ill?" Culverton Smith asks angrily.</p>

<p>"After three days without food or drink, I'm not very well. But I'm much better than you think," Holmes says in his normal voice. "Ah. A friend is coming, I think."</p>

<p>The door opens and Inspector Morton comes in.</p>

<p>"This is your man, Inspector," Holmes says.</p>

<p>"I arrest you for the murder of Victor Savage," Morton says to Smith.</p>

<p>"He wants my murder too," Holmes laughs. "The proof is in the box in his coat. But don't put your hand on it, Inspector."</p>

<p>Smith cries, "You can't prove anything, Holmes!"</p>

<p>"Don't forget my friend, Dr Watson," Holmes says. "He can tell an interesting story. Come out, Watson."</p>

<p>Morton leaves with Culverton Smith. Holmes dresses, and then he talks to Watson.</p>

<p>"I'm a good actor, Watson. And no food or drink for three days is easy for me. This yellow face is only make-up. You can't come near me and not see the truth. So my illness is contagious. Bad light in the room helps with my act. Victor Savage's murder is all about money. Money is more important than family to Culverton Smith. I get some strange things at this house. Happily, I always open them very carefully. Now we must go to Scotland Yard. After that, I want to sit down with you and eat until midnight."</p>

* Кто такая миссис Хадсон и где происходит действие?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Миссис Хадсон — служанка (servant) Шерлока Холмса. Действие происходит в Лондоне, на Бейкер-стрит, 221b.
  </details>

* К кому миссис Хадсон идёт за помощью и почему?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Она идёт к доктору Ватсону (Dr Watson). Холмс очень болен, отказывается от врача и говорит странные вещи.
  </details>

* Как выглядит Холмс, когда Ватсон приходит на Бейкер-стрит?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Его лицо жёлтое (yellow), глаза красные от лихорадки (red with fever), руки постоянно двигаются, голос слабый.
  </details>

* Почему Холмс не подпускает Ватсона к себе?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Он говорит, что его болезнь пришла с Дальнего Востока (Far East) и очень заразна (contagious).
  </details>

* Кого Холмс называет специалистом по таким болезням?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Доктора Эйнстри (Dr Ainstree).
  </details>

* Что Холмс делает, когда Ватсон хочет выйти из комнаты?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Он выпрыгивает из кровати, быстро идёт к двери и запирает её.
  </details>

* Кого Холмс просит привести вместо доктора Эйнстри?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Мистера Калвертона Смита (Mr Culverton Smith) с Лоуэр-Берк-стрит, 13.
  </details>

* Кем работает Калвертон Смит и что его связывает с болезнью?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  У него есть плантация на Суматре (plantation in Sumatra). Его рабочие часто болеют этой болезнью.
  </details>

* Кто такой Виктор Сэвидж и что с ним случилось?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Виктор Сэвидж — племянник (nephew) Калвертона Смита. Он умер при странных обстоятельствах, и Холмс подозревает убийство.
  </details>

* Что говорит Смит, когда Ватсон сообщает ему, что Холмс болен?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  На лице Смита появляется улыбка. Он говорит, что они с Холмсом похожи — Холмс изучает преступников, а Смит изучает микробов.
  </details>

* Где прячется Ватсон, когда Смит приходит к Холмсу?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Он прячется за кроватью (behind the bed).
  </details>

* В чём признаётся Смит Холмсу?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Он признаётся, что убил своего племянника и что Холмс заразился от пружины в коробке (spring in the box), которую Смит прислал.
  </details>

* Почему Холмс не ел и не пил три дня?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Это часть его актёрской игры (he's a good actor). Он притворялся очень больным, чтобы Смит признался в убийстве.
  </details>

* Что было на самом деле с лицом и голосом Холмса?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Жёлтое лицо — это грим (make-up). Слабый голос — притворство.
  </details>

* Кто арестовывает Калвертона Смита?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Инспектор Мортон из Скотланд-Ярда (Inspector Morton of Scotland Yard).
  </details>
