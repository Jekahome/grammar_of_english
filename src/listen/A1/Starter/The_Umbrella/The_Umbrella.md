    <p class="first-para">It is raining. The sky is grey. Carla goes to the market. She looks at the umbrellas. She likes a red umbrella. It's very smart. It costs ten dollars.</p>

<p>Carla is a student. She doesn't have much money.</p>

<p>"I can give you seven dollars for that umbrella."</p>

<p>"OK, young lady. This is your lucky day. Give me seven dollars. The umbrella is yours."</p>

<p>"This is my lucky day," says Carla.</p>

<p>She holds the red umbrella above her head. The rain falls on the umbrella. Carla goes to a cake shop. She wants to buy some cakes. Her little brother loves cakes. She goes into the shop. She leaves her red umbrella near the door.</p>

<p>It is quiet inside the shop. Carla chooses three small cakes. She talks to the shop assistant. A customer is leaving the shop. She is a young woman. She is holding a cake box.</p>

<p>Carla is going to leave the shop. But where is her umbrella? There is one umbrella near the door. It is not a red umbrella. It is not Carla's umbrella. This umbrella is old and black. It has a pattern of yellow ducks. It is not smart. Carla takes the umbrella.</p>

<p>"This is not my lucky day."</p>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter 2</div>
  <div class="chapter-title"></div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Carla walks to the town square. A young man speaks to her.</p>

<p>"Hello?" He smiles.</p>

<p>Carla does not know this young man. She walks away quickly. The young man is following Carla. She walks into a crowd of people. The young man follows the black and yellow umbrella.</p>

<p>"Hello? Wait!"</p>

<p>Carla turns. She looks at the young man. She is angry.</p>

<p>"Go away," she says.</p>

<p>The young man is sad. "Marisa, I'm sorry," he says. "I'm very late."</p>

<p>"I'm not Marisa!" Carla shouts.</p>

<p>"You are not Marisa," says the young man. He points at the black umbrella with yellow ducks. "That's her umbrella."</p>

<p>"Oh," says Carla. "Who is Marisa?"</p>

<p>"I don't know Marisa," says the young man. "I'm going to meet her. We are going to have coffee. It is my cousin's idea. Marisa works with my cousin."</p>

<p>"I am not Marisa," says Carla again.</p>

<p>"My cousin has a photo of Marisa," says the young man. "She's tall. You're tall. Her hair is short and dark. Your hair is short and dark. In the photo, she has an umbrella. It's a black umbrella with yellow ducks. You have a black umbrella with yellow ducks."</p>

<p>Carla looks up at the old umbrella. "Marisa is a thief," she thinks. "She has my new red umbrella." Carla is angry again.</p>

<p>"Please don't be angry," says the young man. He looks at his watch. "It's 3 o'clock. Marisa goes to work at 2.30. I can't meet her now. It's too late." He smiles at Carla. "Let's have coffee together," he says.</p>

<p>Carla thinks for a moment. "OK," she says. "Let's go to my aunt's cafe."</p>

<p>The young man smiles again. "That will be great," he says. "My name is Paul. I'm a law student, third year."</p>

<p>"I'm Carla. I'm a student, too. I'm studying science."</p>

  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Carla sits in the cafe with Paul. They drink coffee. They talk. They laugh. They laugh and talk.</p>

<p>Suddenly, Carla jumps up. "Oh, no!" she says. "It's late. I must go home. I must study. I'm going to have an exam tomorrow."</p>

<p>"Good luck."</p>

<p>"Will you meet me again?"</p>

<p>"Yes. That will be nice."</p>

<p>It is almost dark. The town square is quiet. Carla sees a tall young woman. The young woman has short, dark hair. She has a smart umbrella. It is a red umbrella. The young woman is Marisa.</p>

<p>Marisa sees the old black umbrella with yellow ducks. She is worried. Suddenly, her face is red.</p>

<p>"Don't worry," says Carla. "Keep my red umbrella. I like this umbrella. It's a lucky umbrella. This is my lucky day."</p>

<p>The rain falls on the umbrellas. Carla smiles. Then, she runs home.</p>

* Какого цвета зонтик нравится Карле и сколько он стоит?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Ей нравится красный зонтик (red umbrella). Он стоит десять долларов.
  </details>

* Сколько Карла в итоге платит за зонтик?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Она платит семь долларов (seven dollars).
  </details>

* Куда Карла идёт после рынка и где оставляет свой зонтик?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Она идёт в магазин тортов (cake shop) и оставляет красный зонтик у двери (near the door).
  </details>

* Какой зонтик Карла забирает вместо своего?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Она забирает старый чёрный зонтик с жёлтыми утками (old black umbrella with yellow ducks).
  </details>

* Почему молодой человек (Пол) думает, что Карла — это Мариса?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  У Марисы короткие тёмные волосы, она высокая, и у неё чёрный зонтик с жёлтыми утками. Карла тоже высокая, с короткими тёмными волосами и с таким же зонтиком.
  </details>

* Кем работает Мариса?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Мариса работает с двоюродным братом Пола (works with my cousin). Подробности не уточняются.
  </details>

* Что Карла и Пол делают вместе?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Они идут в кафе тёти Карлы, пьют кофе, разговаривают и смеются.
  </details>

* Почему Карла спешит домой?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  У неё завтра экзамен (exam tomorrow), и ей нужно учиться.
  </details>

* Кого Карла видит на городской площади, когда уже темнеет?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Она видит высокую молодую женщину с короткими тёмными волосами и красным зонтиком. Это Мариса (Marisa).
  </details>

* Что Карла говорит Марисе, когда та волнуется из-за перепутанных зонтиков?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Она говорит: «Не волнуйся. Оставь мой красный зонтик себе. Мне нравится этот чёрный. Он счастливый. Сегодня мой счастливый день».
  </details>
