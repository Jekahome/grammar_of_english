    <p class="first-para">"Please don't go to Glossia," says Sara. "You're only 19."</p>

<p>Alex is saying goodbye to Sara, his girlfriend. "I must," answers Alex. "I'm a soldier. It's my job. I must go where the army says."</p>

<p>"But why are you going there?" asks Sara.</p>

<p>"We're going to stop the fighting," says Alex. "Lots of people are dying."</p>

<p>"But why are they fighting?" asks Sara.</p>

<p>"I don't know," answers Alex.</p>

<p>"Why don't you ask?" says Sara. "You never ask questions."</p>

<p>"I know," answers Alex. "I'm sorry. I hate asking questions."</p>

<p>"Please change, Alex," says Sara. "For me."</p>

<p>"I'm going to think about you every day," says Sara.</p>

<p>"Me too," Alex answers. "I can send you a text from my phone tomorrow. And I think there's email."</p>

<p>"That's good," Sara says with a little smile. "I love you, Alex."</p>

<p>"And I love you too," answers Alex. "Now, don't be sad."</p>

<summary>Chapter 2</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter 2</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">We're in Glossia Now</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Alex is on the plane to Glossia. He's sitting next to Sergeant Ken. It's Ken's second time in Glossia.</p>

<p>"Excuse me, Sergeant," Alex starts.</p>

<p>"You can call me Ken," the sergeant says.</p>

<p>"Why are they fighting in Glossia?" Alex asks.</p>

<p>"That's not an easy question," Ken answers. "I think it's like this. Some people have guns. They use the guns because they want something. Then other people get guns to fight back with. Now everyone has guns. And everyone forgets what they are fighting about."</p>

<p>"But where do the guns come from?" is Alex's next question.</p>

<p>"People from other countries sell them to the Glossians," Ken answers.</p>

<p>"Why?" asks Alex.</p>

<p>Ken smiles. "Money. That's why."</p>

<p>"Ken, can we stop people buying and selling guns?" Alex asks.</p>

<p>"No, we can't. The people who sell the guns, they're thousands of kilometres away. All we can do is help the people here in this country," answers Ken. "We're going to a famous old town called Tonic. In Tonic, lots of people are fighting each other. And some of them are also fighting us."</p>

<p>There's a very loud noise, and the plane goes over to the right.</p>

<p>"What's that?" shouts Alex.</p>

<p>"What do you think?" answers Ken. "We're in Glossia now."</p>

<p>"Is it always like this?" Alex asks.</p>

<p>"Yes," Ken answers.</p>

<p>"But the papers say the Glossian people want us here," Alex says.</p>

<p>The sergeant smiles. "Some Glossians do, and some don't," answers Ken. "But we're here to help all the people."</p>

<p>"And is it always hot?" asks Alex.</p>

<p>"Yes," laughs Ken.</p>

<p>"Alex, this is where you're living for the next six months," says Ken. "Your bed's over there, and this is Hawk."</p>

<p>A tall man with very short hair stands up and puts out his hand. "Hi," he says.</p>

<p>"Ah, that's Stefan on the bed," Ken says. Stefan smiles at Alex. "Hello," he says. "Put your things down. Do you want something to eat or drink?"</p>

<p>"I'm going now," Ken says. "You must be tired, Alex. Try and get some sleep tonight. We start work in the morning."</p>

<p>Hi, Sara. It's very hot in Glossia. Sergeant Ken says we're here to help. But can we? I don't know. All my love, Alex.</p>

<summary>Chapter 4</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter 4</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">First Patrol</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">The next day, it's time for Alex's first patrol.</p>

<p>"Just do what I do," Ken tells Alex. "Stefan and Hawk are going to walk behind us. Use your eyes and ears and look everywhere. And don't forget, we never know who is a friend and who isn't."</p>

<p>"What are we looking for?" asks Alex.</p>

<p>"Guns," answers Ken with a quick laugh.</p>

<p>"And gunmen," Hawk says. He isn't laughing.</p>

<p>"But what about the people who sell the guns?" Alex asks.</p>

<p>"They're not here," Hawk answers. "They're far away with their money."</p>

<p>Some people are happy to see the soldiers. They stop and say hello. But some boys aren't happy that the soldiers are there. They're throwing things at the patrol and shouting. The soldiers don't understand what the boys are shouting.</p>

<p>Something hits Alex in the mouth. He turns with his gun. Alex isn't slow, but the boys are very fast. They run away. Alex feels his mouth.</p>

<p>"Are you okay?" Stefan shouts.</p>

<p>"Yes!" answers Alex.</p>

<p>Then they hear a gun. Someone's shooting at them. Alex looks across the dirty street at a house. He sees someone at a bedroom window.</p>

<p>"Look! Up there!" Alex shouts. Hawk turns, looks up, and shoots at the window. Ken and Alex run across the street to the house. They open the front door and run up to the bedroom. Hawk and Stefan wait in the street.</p>

<p>Hi, Sara. What a bad day. My first patrol. There's shooting, and I see someone at a bedroom window. Hawk shoots at the window. Then Ken and I run into the house and up to the bedroom. But there isn't a gunman there. It's a little boy. The boy's mother comes and shouts at us. I don't understand what she's saying, but I can see she's very angry. I want to say sorry, but I don't speak Glossian.</p>

<p>When we leave, the woman is shouting and the small boy is crying. I don't feel well. It's all wrong. What am I doing here? All my love, Alex.</p>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter 5</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">We Have No Names</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">The next day, Sergeant Ken, Alex, Hawk, and Stefan are on patrol again. It's 12 o'clock.</p>

<p>A kilometre away, a family is finishing lunch. There's a 12-year-old boy called Happy and an 8-year-old girl called Marina. Their mother's name is Ada, and she's very beautiful. There's no father. He's dead.</p>

<p>In the afternoon, Ada puts away the lunch things and makes coffee. Happy is sitting on the floor in the living room with Marina. The brother and sister are laughing and playing with a doll. Ada doesn't hear the back door.</p>

<p>Two men come in. One is tall and one is short. The short man has a bag. They're wearing white t-shirts and black trousers, and they have black masks.</p>

<p>Ada looks at the two men. Then she shouts to her children, "Come here now!" Happy and Marina run to their mother and stand by her. They're afraid of the two men in masks.</p>

<p>"Who are you?" Ada asks the men. They don't answer. The short man puts the bag down, and the tall man looks at Ada.</p>

<p>"What are your names?" asks Ada.</p>

<p>"We have no names, and we have no faces," answers the tall man.</p>

<p>"We are not rich," says Ada. "What do you want?"</p>

<p>"Nothing," answers the short man. "All we want is your house for ten minutes."</p>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter 6</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Don't Ask Stupid Questions</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">A young woman with a baby comes in the front door.</p>

<p>"Who's she?" asks Ada.</p>

<p>"Shh," says the tall man. "The baby."</p>

<p>The young woman picks up the baby. It's sleeping. Under the baby is a long gun. The tall man takes the gun, and the woman puts the baby down again.</p>

<p>"What are you going to do?" asks Happy.</p>

<p>"A soldier's going to die," answers the short man.</p>

<p>"Why?" asks Happy.</p>

<p>"Don't ask stupid questions," says the tall man. "This is our country. We don't want soldiers from other countries here."</p>

<p>"You," the short man shouts to Marina. "Give me your doll. Do it!"</p>

<p>Ada tells her daughter, "Do it." Marina gives the man her doll. He takes something out of his bag and puts it in the doll. Then he takes a phone and puts it in the doll too.</p>

<p>"Listen, you two," the short man tells the children. "You mustn't play with the doll now. Do you understand?"</p>

<p>"Yes!" Happy answers.</p>

<p>"Here," the short man says to the young woman with the baby. "Take the doll and leave it in the street in front of the house."</p>

<p>The short man speaks to the children. "Go and play in front of the house now. But when you see the soldiers, you must run in again." He looks at Ada. "Then you take your children out of the house by the back door. Do you understand?"</p>

<p>"Yes!" says Ada. "Happy and Marina. Go out and play in the street now. But don't go near the doll."</p>

<p>The short man gives a phone to the tall man. "This phone's for you, and there's a phone in the doll," he says.</p>

<p>The tall man takes the gun and the phone up to the bedroom. The young woman goes with him. Out of the window, they can see the children in the street. And the doll. The tall man has the phone in his hand.</p>

<p>"All we can do now is wait," he tells the woman.</p>

<p>"Listen!" says the woman. "What's that? Can you hear something?"</p>

<p>"Yes," the tall man answers. "It's the soldiers. They're coming."</p>

<p>"Come on! The soldiers are coming!" shouts the short man. "You must see a doctor!"</p>
