# Listen: Alice's Adventures in Wonderland
  
<h3>Step 1: Read</h3>

<summary>Chapter One</summary>
 
<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter One</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">The White Rabbit</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>

  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">It is a sunny afternoon.</p>
    <p>Alice and her sister are sitting by the river. Alice's sister is reading a book with no pictures.</p>
    <p><em>I don't like books without any pictures,</em> Alice thinks. She is sleepy.</p>
    <p>Just then she sees a white rabbit. He looks at his watch and says, <em>I'm late!</em></p>
    <p><em>How strange!</em> Alice thinks. <em>A rabbit with a watch!</em></p>
    <p>She follows the rabbit across the grass and into a big hole. She falls slowly down the hole. Then she stops falling and stands up. She looks around.</p>
    <p>Alice sees the rabbit again and follows him. He is running around a hall with a lot of doors. Then she sees a glass table with a small key on it.</p>
    <p>She takes the key and tries to open the doors, but she can't. Then she sees a very small door and opens it. There is a beautiful garden.</p>
    <p><em>I want to go into that garden,</em> thinks Alice, <em>but I'm too big.</em></p>
    <p>She puts the key on the glass table and sees a bottle on it. It says <em>DRINK ME</em> on the bottle. She takes the bottle and drinks it all.</p>
    <p><em>Oh, how strange!</em> she says. <em>Now I'm very small and I can go into the garden.</em></p>
    <p>But the door is closed and the key is on the table. She is too small now and she can't get the key. She is very sad and starts to cry.</p>
    <p>Alice sees a small glass box under the table. Inside the box there is a cake with the words <em>EAT ME</em> on it.</p>
    <p><em>I'm going to eat it,</em> says Alice. <em>Perhaps I can grow and take the key from the table.</em></p>
    <p>She eats the cake but nothing happens. Then suddenly she becomes big. <em>Now I can get the key,</em> she thinks.</p>
    <p>She takes the key and goes to the door to the garden. But she is too big and can't go in. She sits down and starts to cry again.</p>
    <p>Her tears make a big pool. Suddenly she sees the White Rabbit again. He is wearing a lovely jacket, and he has white gloves in one hand and flowers in the other.</p>
    <p><em>Oh, the Duchess is going to be angry because I'm late,</em> says the White Rabbit.</p>
    <p><em>Excuse me, sir...</em> says Alice. The White Rabbit is afraid and runs away. His white gloves fall to the floor. Alice looks at her hand and she is suddenly wearing one of the White Rabbit's gloves.</p>
    <p><em>Oh, no,</em> she says, <em>I'm becoming small again! What's happening to me?</em></p>
    <p>Alice is small again and she suddenly falls into a pool of water. <em>I'm in the sea,</em> she thinks. But it is not the sea, it is the pool of Alice's tears.</p>
    <p>She sees a mouse swimming near her.</p>
    <p class="dialogue-line"><em>Hello, Mouse!</em> says Alice.<br><em>I'm tired. I want to get out of this pool.</em></p>
    <p>The Mouse does not answer.</p>
    <p><em>Perhaps it doesn't understand English,</em> Alice thinks. <em>Maybe it understands French.</em></p>
    <p><em>Où est ma chatte? (Where is my cat?)</em> asks Alice. This is the first sentence in her French book.</p>
    <p>The Mouse is angry and says in English, <em>I don't like cats! My family doesn't like cats! I'm a mouse!</em></p>
    <p>He swims away, and Alice follows him.</p>
  </div>

<summary>Chapter Two</summary>
 
<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Two</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">The Blue Caterpillar</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>

  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Soon there are other strange animals in the pool: a duck, a dodo, a parrot and a baby eagle.</p>
    <p>Alice gets out of the pool and the animals follow her.</p>
    <p><em>We're all wet,</em> says the Dodo. <em>Let's run a race and get dry!</em></p>
    <p><em>That's a good idea,</em> says the Parrot.</p>
    <p>Alice and the animals run a race and they get dry.</p>
    <p>After the race the Duck asks, <em>Who wins this race?</em></p>
    <p><em>Everyone wins and everyone gets a prize,</em> says the Dodo. <em>And Alice is giving the prizes.</em></p>
    <p><em>But I haven't got any prizes,</em> Alice thinks.</p>
    <p>Alice does not know what to do. She puts her hand in her pocket and finds a box of sweets.</p>
    <p><em>Here are some sweets for everyone,</em> she says.</p>
    <p>She gives one sweet to every animal. <em>Alice must have a prize too,</em> says the Mouse.</p>
    <p><em>Of course,</em> the Dodo says. <em>What have you got for a prize, Alice?</em></p>
    <p><em>I've only got this box,</em> says Alice and she gives him the empty sweet box.</p>
    <p><em>Good!</em> says the Dodo. <em>Here's your prize, Alice — a beautiful box.</em></p>
    <p><em>How strange!</em> she thinks. She looks at the Dodo and says <em>Thank you.</em></p>
    <p>The animals go away and Alice is alone. She hears a noise and sees the White Rabbit.</p>
    <p><em>The Duchess!</em> says the White Rabbit. <em>She's going to be angry! Where are my gloves?</em></p>
    <p>Alice looks around. Suddenly everything is different and she is in the countryside.</p>
    <p>The White Rabbit sees Alice and says, <em>What are you doing here, Mary Ann? Run home and bring me my white gloves.</em></p>
    <p><em>He thinks I'm his servant,</em> thinks Alice. She runs to the White Rabbit's house and gets his gloves. Then she sees a bottle on a table and thinks, <em>Every time I eat or drink something here, interesting things happen.</em></p>
    <p>She drinks it and starts to grow. She is now very big and puts one hand out of the window of the house.</p>
    <p><em>Mary Ann! Where are you?</em> asks the White Rabbit. <em>Where are my gloves?</em></p>
    <p>He tries to open the door of his house. But he can't because Alice's arm is against it.</p>
    <p>The White Rabbit calls his gardener. <em>Pat! Pat! Where are you?</em></p>
    <p><em>I'm here, sir,</em> says Pat. There are other animals near the house and they want to help the White Rabbit.</p>
    <p><em>Come and help me,</em> says the White Rabbit angrily. <em>What's that in the window, Pat?</em></p>
    <p><em>It's a hand,</em> says Pat.</p>
    <p><em>A hand!</em> says the White Rabbit. <em>What are you saying? Listen to me. We must burn the house!</em></p>
    <p><em>What!</em> shouts Alice.</p>
    <p>The animals are silent for a moment. Then they throw stones through the windows.</p>
    <p>The stones become little cakes. Alice eats some of them and becomes small.</p>
    <p>She is happy and runs out of the house. The animals try to catch her but she runs away into the wood.</p>
    <p>Alice sees a big mushroom in the wood. On top of the mushroom there is a sleepy caterpillar. He is smoking a long pipe.</p>
    <p><em>Who are you?</em> he asks quietly.</p>
    <p><em>I... I don't know,</em> says Alice. <em>My size changes all the time. Now I'm very small.</em></p>
    <p><em>I don't understand,</em> says the Blue Caterpillar.</p>
    <p><em>I can't explain,</em> says Alice. <em>Let me give you an example; one day you're going to become a butterfly. That's strange, isn't it?</em></p>
    <p><em>No, that's not strange at all,</em> says the Caterpillar. <em>But who are you?</em></p>
    <p><em>I want to know who you are first,</em> says Alice.</p>
    <p><em>Why?</em> asks the Caterpillar.</p>
    <p>Alice can't answer the question. She is angry and walks away.</p>
    <p><em>Come back!</em> says the Caterpillar. <em>I want to tell you something.</em></p>
    <p>Alice goes back to the Caterpillar and looks at him.</p>
    <p><em>You must never be angry,</em> he says.</p>
    <p><em>Is that all?</em> asks Alice angrily.</p>
    <p><em>No,</em> says the Caterpillar. He smokes his pipe and then gets off the mushroom.</p>
    <p><em>One side of this mushroom makes you big and the other side makes you small.</em></p>
    <p>Alice looks at the mushroom and thinks, <em>What side of the mushroom have I got to eat?</em></p>
    <p>She takes a piece from each side and then eats the first piece. Suddenly she grows very small. She eats the other piece and her neck grows long.</p>
    <p>Then she eats another piece and she becomes the right size. Now she is happy.</p>

<summary>Chapter Three</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Three</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">The Cheshire Cat</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>

  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Alice walks in the woods. She sees a nice garden and a small house.</p>
    <p><em>I'm too big,</em> she thinks. <em>I can't get into that house. I must eat a piece of mushroom and become small again.</em></p>
    <p>Soon she is nine inches (about 23 cm) tall.</p>
    <p>Suddenly a servant comes out of the wood and goes to the small house. His face is like a fish. Another servant opens the door and his face is like a frog.</p>
    <p>The fish-servant has got a big letter in his hand and says, <em>For the Duchess. An invitation from the Queen to play croquet.</em></p>
    <p>The frog-servant says, <em>From the Queen! An invitation for the Duchess to play croquet.</em></p>
    <p><em>What strange servants!</em> says Alice, laughing.</p>
    <p>Alice goes to the house and says, <em>Can I come in?</em></p>
    <p><em>Just open the door and go in,</em> says the servant.</p>
    <p>Then she sees the Duchess. She is sitting on a small chair with a baby in her arms. There is a cook in the kitchen. She is making some soup.</p>
    <p><em>There's too much pepper in the soup,</em> Alice thinks and she sneezes.</p>
    <p>The cat sits near the cook and smiles. Alice asks the Duchess, <em>Why does your cat smile?</em></p>
    <p><em>Because it's a Cheshire Cat,</em> says the Duchess. <em>All Cheshire Cats smile, don't you know?</em></p>
    <p><em>No, I don't!</em> says Alice.</p>
    <p><em>You don't know much,</em> says the Duchess.</p>
    <p>Suddenly the cook starts throwing plates, cups and pots at the Duchess and the baby. There is a terrible noise and Alice is afraid.</p>
    <p><em>Oh, please be careful!</em> says Alice. <em>The poor baby...!</em></p>
    <p><em>Don't think about the baby,</em> says the Duchess. <em>It's mine!</em></p>
    <p>She starts singing to the baby, but suddenly she throws it to Alice.</p>
    <p><em>Here, take the baby!</em> she says. <em>I must go and play croquet with the Queen.</em></p>
    <p>She runs out of the house. The cook throws a plate at her but it does not hit her.</p>
    <p>Alice goes outside with the baby. It makes strange noises. Alice looks at it carefully... it is a baby pig!</p>
    <p><em>A pig!</em> says Alice, surprised. She puts it down immediately and it runs into the wood.</p>
    <p>Just then Alice sees the Cheshire Cat in a tree. It looks at Alice and smiles.</p>
    <p><em>Hello, Cheshire Cat,</em> says Alice. <em>Where can I go now?</em></p>
    <p><em>Well, where do you want to go?</em> asks the Cheshire Cat.</p>
    <p><em>I... I don't know,</em> says Alice.</p>
    <p><em>You can go that way on the right and you can visit the Hatter,</em> says the Cheshire Cat. <em>Or you can go that way on the left and you can visit the March Hare. It doesn't matter — they're both mad.</em></p>
    <p><em>Oh dear,</em> says Alice, <em>I don't want to visit mad people.</em></p>
    <p><em>Then you're in the wrong place,</em> says the Cheshire Cat. <em>We're all mad here. You're mad too.</em></p>
    <p><em>How do you know I'm mad?</em> asks Alice.</p>
    <p><em>You're here,</em> says the Cheshire Cat, <em>so of course you're mad.</em></p>
    <p><em>Are you going to play croquet with the Queen today?</em> asks the Cheshire Cat.</p>
    <p><em>No, I haven't got an invitation,</em> says Alice.</p>
    <p><em>I'm going to be there,</em> says the Cheshire Cat and he suddenly disappears.</p>
    <p>Then he comes back and asks, <em>Where's the baby?</em></p>
    <p><em>It's not a baby,</em> says Alice, <em>it's a pig!</em></p>
    <p><em>Oh!</em> he says and goes away again.</p>
    <p>Alice goes to the March Hare's house. <em>What a big house!</em> she thinks. <em>But I'm very small.</em> She eats another piece of mushroom and she becomes big.</p>
  </div>

## Глава 1 (Белый Кролик)

* Что Алиса увидела, сидя на берегу реки со своей сестрой?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Белого Кролика (White Rabbit) с часами, который говорил: «Я опаздываю» (I'm late).
  </details>

* Куда упала Алиса, преследуя Кролика?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  В большую нору (a big hole).
  </details>

* Что случилось с Алисой после того, как она выпила из бутылочки с надписью «Выпей меня» (Drink me)?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Она стала очень маленькой (very small).
  </details>

* Что произошло, когда Алиса съела пирожок с надписью «Съешь меня» (Eat me)?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Она стала очень большой (very big).
  </details>

## Глава 2 (Синяя Гусеница)

* Какое животное плавало в пруду из слёз Алисы?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Мышь (mouse).
  </details>

* Какую гонку предложил Додо (Dodo), чтобы все обсохли?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Гонку по кругу (run a race), где все выиграли и получили призы.
  </details>

* Что случилось с Алисой, когда она выпила из бутылочки в доме Белого Кролика?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Она снова стала расти (start to grow) и её рука высунулась из окна.
  </details>

* Что сказала Синяя Гусеница (Blue Caterpillar) о грибе?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Одна сторона гриба делает тебя большой, а другая — маленькой (One side makes you big, the other side makes you small).
  </details>

## Глава 3 (Чеширский Кот)

* Кем оказался ребёнок Герцогини (Duchess), которого Алиса вынесла из дома?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Поросёнком (a pig).
  </details>

* Почему Чеширский Кот (Cheshire Cat) улыбается?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Потому что он чеширский кот, а все чеширские коты улыбаются (All Cheshire cats smile).
  </details>

* Куда Чеширский Кот посоветовал Алисе пойти?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Направо к Шляпнику (Hatter) или налево к Мартовскому Зайцу (March Hare), добавив, что они оба сумасшедшие (mad).
  </details>

## Глава 4 (Чайная вечеринка)

* Что показывали часы Шляпника?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  День месяца (the day of the month), но не время.
  </details>

* Почему Мартовский Заяц опустил часы в чай?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Это был странный поступок, который удивил Алису; он просто был сумасшедшим.
  </details>

* Какое странное действие совершил Шляпник с Соней (Dormouse)?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Он попытался засунуть Соню в чайник (put the dormouse into the teapot).
  </details>

## Глава 5 (Крокет)

* Что делали садовники (двое, пятеро и семерка) у розового дерева?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Они перекрашивали белые розы в красные, потому что Королева ненавидит белые розы (The Queen hates white roses).
  </details>

* Что Королева кричала, когда злилась?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  «Отрубить ему голову!» (Cut off his head!).
  </details>

* Что служило в этой стране клюшками (mallets) и мячами (balls) для игры в крокет?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Клюшками были фламинго (flamingos), а мячами — ежи (hedgehogs).
  </details>

## Глава 6 (Суд)

* В чём обвиняли Червонного Валета (Knave of Hearts)?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  В том, что он украл пирожные Королевы (he takes the tarts away).
  </details>

* Каким был первый свидетель (Шляпник)?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Он нервничал, откусил кусочек от чашки вместо хлеба с маслом и убежал, когда Королева приказала отрубить ему голову.
  </details>

* Какое правило (Правило 42) зачитал Король в конце суда?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Все люди ростом больше мили должны покинуть зал суда (All people more than a mile tall must leave the courtroom).
  </details>

## Глава 7 (Домой к чаю)

* Что случилось с картами в конце суда?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Они взлетели в воздух и посыпались на Алису.
  </details>

* Чем на самом деле оказались карты, когда Алиса проснулась?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Листьями (leaves).
  </details>

* Что сказала Алиса, когда проснулась?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  «Какой странный сон» (What a strange dream).
  </details>
