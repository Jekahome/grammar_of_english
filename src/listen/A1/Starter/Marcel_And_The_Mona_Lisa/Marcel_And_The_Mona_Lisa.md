  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Marcel is a French mouse and a detective. He has lots of friends in Paris. One of them is Céline. She paints pictures and is very beautiful. Céline's home is at the Louvre. Marcel often goes there for dinner.</p>

<p>One evening in May he arrives with some pink flowers. There is a guard at the door. "I don't know him," Marcel thinks. "He must be new." Then he walks inside.</p>

<p>The two friends eat, drink and talk all evening. Céline shows Marcel her new paintings. They talk about their summer holidays in Los Angeles. They laugh, play jazz records and tell lots of stories. Then at eleven o'clock Marcel puts on his coat.</p>

<p>"It's late," he says. "I must go home."</p>

<p>Two minutes later he leaves. "Well, good night, Céline," then she closes her front door.</p>

<p>Marcel walks across the floor. He's very happy. Then he stops. The room is dark but he can see something. What is it? A man? A man with a long knife?</p>

<p>Yes! Suddenly Marcel's mouth is very dry. He runs to the wall. Then after five seconds he looks again. This time he can see the man's face.</p>

<p>"It's that new guard," he thinks. "And he's stealing the Mona Lisa."</p>

<p>Next to the thief there is a black bag. Two minutes later the Mona Lisa is inside it. The thief smiles and picks up the bag. But a second later he puts it down again.</p>

<p>"Car keys!" he says and begins to look in all his pockets.</p>

<p>"All right. This is it," Marcel thinks. "It is now or never."</p>

<p>He runs along the wall very fast, climbs up the tall black bag and jumps inside it. At the bottom of the bag, Marcel can see a face. The Mona Lisa's face. She's smiling at him.</p>

<p>"Now what?" he asks her.</p>

<p>There is no answer, but at that moment the bag starts to move. Marcel can hear lots of noises. A motor starts. Traffic goes by. A radio plays. Then the bag suddenly stops.</p>

<p>Marcel climbs the painting and looks out. "Gare de l'Est."</p>

<p>Five minutes later the Louvre guard gets on the train. He sits next to a thin man in sunglasses and a white jacket.</p>

<p>"Have you got it, Antoine?" the thin man asks.</p>

<p>The guard answers, "Yes."</p>

<p>After that the train starts and there is a lot of noise.</p>

<p>"Oh no. Now I can't hear them," Marcel thinks. But he can hear one or two words. "Italy," for example, "and all those cats."</p>

<p>"Cats," Marcel looks at the Mona Lisa. His eyes are two big saucers. "But cats can kill mice," he thinks. "They eat them. And where are we going in Italy? Rome? Milano? Napoli?"</p>

<p>But at that moment, Antoine puts the bag under the seat. "Now I really can't hear," Marcel thinks. Then he goes to sleep and has a very bad dream.</p>

  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Early next morning the sun is shining. Marcel opens his eyes and sees the Mona Lisa. Then he remembers where he is. He runs up the painting and looks at Antoine and Auri.</p>

<p>"Good," he thinks. "They are asleep."</p>

<p>Ten seconds later, Marcel is standing at the window. He can see a small village and some mountains. Then a sign goes by: "180 kilometres to Venice."</p>

<p>Two hours later, Antoine and Auri are on a gondola.</p>

<p>"Look," says Antoine, and laughs. He shows Auri a newspaper story. It says: "Thieves take Da Vinci painting."</p>

<p>Auri says, "Be quiet." He turns to the boatman. "Do you see that big palace on the left? Signor Spandini's house. Yes, stop there."</p>

<p>Inside the bag, Marcel hears every word.</p>

<p>An old woman answers the front door. "Come in," she says to the two thieves. "Signor Spandini is waiting for you."</p>

<p>She takes them to a big dark room. A fat man is sitting behind a desk.</p>

<p>"However, you got it," he asks.</p>

<p>"Yes, boss," Auri answers. The bag is beside him.</p>

<p>"I cannot stay here," Marcel thinks. He jumps out of the bag and hides behind a chair. "Good," he thinks. "Now I can..." but then he goes cold.</p>

<p>"Oh, cats." There are seven, eight. No, nine of them in the room. Suddenly, Marcel remembers Auri's words. "Oh, those cats." Then he remembers his dream on the train. What can he do? Where can he go? But it's too late. One of the cats sees him.</p>

<p>"Help!" Marcel thinks and climbs up the red curtain. A moment later, the cats start climbing too. Marcel can hear them below him. He has to do something and fast. But what?</p>

<p>Then he sees two candles above his head. "That's the answer," he thinks. He jumps onto the bookcase and starts to push the candles over. They're heavy, but in the end, he does it.</p>

<p>Below him, he hears, "Yow, yow, yow!"</p>

<p>"What is all that noise?" asks Spandini.</p>

<p>"Look, the carpet's on fire," says Auri.</p>

<p>Spandini stands up. "Angelina, quick, bring some water."</p>

<p>Marcel looks over the bookcase. He can see the Mona Lisa on Spandini's desk. "OK," he thinks. "This is it."</p>

<p>After that, he runs down the curtain, across Spandini's desk, picks up the Mona Lisa and runs out of the room.</p>

<p>Marcel runs for a long time. He thinks, "I want to leave the Mona Lisa somewhere safe, but where?" Then, after 20 minutes, he stops in a quiet street. In front of him, there is a police station. The front door has a letterbox.</p>

<p>"Of course," Marcel thinks. "That's it." He stands up tall, then he pushes the Mona Lisa through the letterbox.</p>

<p>Two days later, Marcel is in Paris again. At the station, he sees a newspaper. It says: "Italian police find Mona Lisa."</p>

<p>Then, he goes to the Louvre and tells Céline everything.</p>

<p>"Oh, nine cats," she says. "Oh, Marcel, are you all right?"</p>

<p>"Yes, I am fine," Marcel answers. He goes to Céline's window. "And the Mona Lisa is fine too. Look, Céline, she's smiling."</p>

* Кто такой Марсель и где происходит действие?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Марсель — это французский мышонок (French mouse) и детектив. Действие происходит в Париже, в Лувре (Louvre).
  </details>

* Как зовут подругу Марселя и чем она занимается?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Её зовут Селин (Celine). Она рисует картины (paints pictures) и очень красивая.
  </details>

* Что Марсель видит в тёмной комнате после ужина?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Он видит мужчину с длинным ножом (a man with a long knife). Это новый охранник, который крадёт Мону Лизу (Mona Lisa).
  </details>

* Куда Марсель прыгает, чтобы не попасться?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Он залезает в чёрную сумку (black bag), куда вор положил картину.
  </details>

* Как зовут двух воров и куда они едут на поезде?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Их зовут Антуан (Antoine) и Анри (Henri). Они едут в Италию (Italy), в Венецию (Venice).
  </details>

* Кто такой Синьор Спандини и сколько кошек в его комнате?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Синьор Спандини (Signor Spandini) — главарь (boss). В комнате девять кошек (nine cats).
  </details>

* Как Марсель спасается от кошки?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Он залезает на красную штору (red curtain), затем прыгает на книжный шкаф и сталкивает две свечи (candles). Кошка получает ожог.
  </details>

* Что Марсель делает с картиной после того, как выбегает из комнаты?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Он приносит Мону Лизу к полицейскому участку (police station) и проталкивает её через почтовый ящик (letterbox).
  </details>

* Что написано в газете, которую Марсель видит на вокзале в Париже?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Там написано: «ИТАЛЬЯНСКАЯ ПОЛИЦИЯ НАШЛА МОНУ ЛИЗУ» (ITALIAN POLICE FIND THE MONA LISA).
  </details>

* Что делает Мона Лиза в конце истории?
  <details> 
  <summary>ответ:</summary>
  Она улыбается (She's smiling).
  </details>
