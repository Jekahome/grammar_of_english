<p>Judy goes to the window and looks out. There's a police car in the street. Two big policemen are walking towards her house.</p>

<p>Judy checks in and goes through passport control to the departure gate. On the plane she finds a seat by the window.</p>

<p>A young man comes and sits down by her. Judy looks at him. He is tall and dark, about 25 and very well dressed.</p>

<p>Judy is not interested in him. He has dark brown eyes, a straight nose, a wide humorous mouth and strong brown hands with long sensitive fingers. He is incredibly handsome.</p>

<p>"Excuse me. Would you like a drink?"</p>

<p>"Oh, yes. Thank you very much."</p>

<p>"Are we still pleased?"</p>

<p>The young man gave Judy her drink and smiled at her. He had an incredibly attractive smile. He really looked very nice, calm, friendly and kind.</p>

<p>Perhaps he's a doctor, she thought. A surgeon with those strong, sensitive hands. Or perhaps an artist. Or a musician. Yes. He looked like an artist.</p>

<p>She looked at him again and smiled.</p>

<p>"What time is it, please?"</p>

<p>"2:25. Thank you."</p>

<p>He smiled at her. She smiled back at him. He smiled again. He took a gun out of his pocket, stood up and walked to the front of the plane.</p>

<p>Judy's head was going round and round. First Sam, then the police, and now the hijacker. Where would it all end? Life was really much too complicated.</p>

<p>She drank her whiskey. It didn't make any difference. She looked out of the window. The sky was full of big, dark clouds. So was her head.</p>

<p>Sometime later, the plane started going down. The pilot's door opened and the hijacker came out, still holding his gun. He walked up to her and smiled.</p>

<p>"You know, you're in here extremely beautiful. Come on, put on your parachute."</p>

<p>Judy fainted.</p>

<p>Judy opened her eyes. The sun was shining and the cool wind was blowing on her face. She felt very light and happy.</p>

<p>"Hold on, love. A hundred feet above Loch Ness. Can you swim?"</p>

<p>Judy fainted again.</p>

<p>When she opened her eyes, she was lying on the bank of the loch with her head on her parachute.</p>

<p>"I ought to introduce myself. My name is Jasper McDonald."</p>

<p>"Why did you hijack the plane?"</p>

<p>"It's my birthday. Now let's go to my castle and find some dry clothes."</p>

<p>It didn't take long to get to Jasper's castle. It was an enormous building about half a mile from Loch Ness, with tall towers, battlements and a moat, and at least 200 rooms.</p>

<p>"What a place!"</p>

<p>"Well, it's not much, but it's home. Let me show you to your room, and I'll see if I can find you some of my sister's clothes."</p>

<p>Judy's room was about ten minutes' walk from the main entrance, up a lot of stairs, and along a lot of corridors. It was beautiful. It was decorated in light blue and white, with some wonderful pieces of antique furniture. There was a splendid view of the loch and the mountains.</p>

<p>"This is lovely. How many of you live here?"</p>

<p>"Just my sister and I, and the ghost, of course. See you later."</p>

<p>Judy had a bath, and then put on some of Jasper's sister's clothes. They fitted her perfectly. She looked at herself in the mirror, smiled, frowned, and went downstairs.</p>

<p>"Hello. Did I tell you how beautiful you are?"</p>

<p>"Yes, you did."</p>

<p>"Fine. Let me show you around the castle before tea."</p>

<p>"I want to go to Rio."</p>

<p>"This part of the castle is built in 1580."</p>

<p>"I want to go to Rio."</p>

<p>"This is a portrait of my ancestor, Donald McDonald. He was a friend of King James VI."</p>

<p>"I want to go to Rio."</p>

<p>"James VI? Maybe you've changed your mind."</p>

<p>"Oh, Sam Watson. I love him."</p>

<p>"You don't want to see him."</p>

<p>"Yes, I do."</p>

<p>"No, you don't."</p>

<p>Out of a door came an old man with white hair and very strange clothes. He was carrying his head under his arm. He looked very like the portrait.</p>

<p>"The ghost?"</p>

<p>"You don't want to go and see Sam. You want to stay here with Jasper."</p>

  <div class="chapter-title">Сэм в Рио и второй угон</div>

  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Sam Watson was standing at the arrivals gate at Rio Airport holding a bunch of flowers. He was worried.</p>

<p>Judy's plane was three hours late and nobody knew why. Sam walked over to the bar and had a drink. He walked back to the arrivals gate. No news. He walked back to the bar and had another drink. Still no news. Back to the bar. Two hours and eight drinks later, Judy's plane landed.</p>

<p>And after another half hour, the passengers started coming out. Sam smiled and looked for Judy. After a time he stopped smiling. Finally the last passenger came through. It wasn't Judy.</p>

<p>Sam said a big bad word. What had happened? He went over to the information desk.</p>

<p>"My name is Sam Watson. Have you got any messages for me?"</p>

<p>"No. A terrible message from Scotland. 'Mr. Sam Watson, Rio Airport. Haven't you got a holiday? Don't come back. Love, Jasper McDonald.'"</p>

<p>Sam said another big bad word. Tore up the paper and gave the flowers to the stewardess. "What time is the next plane to London?"</p>

<p>Behind Sam a tall beautiful girl was listening to his conversation. When she heard the word London, she smiled.</p>

<p>As the night plane took off, Sam closed his eyes. He loved travelling but he was always a little afraid of flying. He couldn't really understand how the plane stayed up in the air. Also he was worried about what could happen to him.</p>

<p>Would there be detectives waiting for him at London airport? It was crazy to leave Brazil. In Brazil there was sun, freedom and beautiful women. He could have been happy for years with his £50,000. In Britain there was rain, trouble, policemen and a strong chance of prison.</p>

<p>But he had to see Judy. Judy was different. Judy was special. Sam smiled and opened his eyes.</p>

<p>Next to him was sitting a tall, incredibly beautiful girl.</p>

<p>"Hello. My name's Sam."</p>

<p>"I know. My name's Detective Sergeant Honeybone from London."</p>

<p>Sam closed his eyes again.</p>

<p>When Sam woke up, he felt terrible. He had a headache and there was a horrible taste in his mouth. He looked out of the window. The sun was shining and through a gap in the clouds he could see the sea. It was a long way down.</p>

<p>Sam shivered and turned to look at Detective Sergeant Honeybone. She looked fresh and lovely, even more beautiful than before.</p>

<p>"Good morning. Did you sleep well?"</p>

<p>"No. Excuse me."</p>

<p>He got up and walked forward to the toilets. After washing and shaving, Sam felt a little better. He brushed his hair, put his jacket back on, and looked at his tongue in the mirror. Not a pretty sight.</p>

<p>Sam put his tongue back in, took out his gun, and looked at his watch. Time to move.</p>

<p>He came out of the toilet, glanced round quickly, and then walked to the front of the plane. Opening the door of the cockpit, he stepped inside.</p>

<p>"This is a hijack. Take me to Loch Ness."</p>

<p>"Oh, God. Not again. What's so special about Loch Ness?"</p>

<p>"Jasper McDonald."</p>

<p>"Good morning. I've cooked breakfast for you."</p>

<p>"Would you like some breakfast?"</p>

<p>"Where's Jasper?"</p>

<p>"He's gone out."</p>

<p>"Oh dear."</p>

<p>Quickly, she wrote a note to Jasper.</p>

<p>"Jasper, it was wonderful, but I have to go. I'm sorry. I wanted to say goodbye to you, but perhaps it's better like this. Thank you for a beautiful memory. Judy."</p>

<p>She said goodbye to the ghost, who looked sad, and walked out of the castle. Not far along the road, there was a bus stop. If she could get to Inverness before lunch, she could catch the afternoon plane to London, and buy some new clothes before catching the night flight to Rio. Tomorrow morning, she could be in Sam's arms. How wonderful.</p>

<p>Judy started crying.</p>

<p>At the bus stop, Judy read the timetable. Buses to Inverness came every three hours, but she was lucky. There was one in 20 minutes.</p>

<p>As she stood waiting, she looked out over the lake. A few hundred yards away, there was a man fishing in a boat. She could hear him singing in the clear still air. He had a wonderful voice. The voice sounded familiar, and he was singing an old Scottish love song.</p>

<p>It was Jasper.</p>

<p>Tears came into Judy's eyes, and she looked away from the boat, up into the peaceful sky. High above Loch Ness, a golden eagle was flying in circles. There were pretty little clouds looking like splashes of white paint against the deep blue, and two parachutes.</p>

<p>Sam and Detective Sergeant Honeybone hit the water together. Sam went under, and came up. He shouted, going under again.</p>

<p>"I can't swim!"</p>

<p>He shouted as he came up again and went under for the third time.</p>

<p>Sam's life passed in front of his eyes as he went down, down, down into the green water. His childhood in London, visiting his mother and father in prison, his first girlfriend, his first bank robbery, Judy. Judy. He had never seen her again.</p>

<p>"Don't worry, you lovely man! I'm an Olympic swimmer. I've got two gold medals!"</p>

<p>Detective Sergeant Honeybone swam over to Sam with beautiful, strong strokes. She put her arms under his arms as he came up again, and started pulling him towards the boat.</p>

<p>"How's the patient?"</p>

<p>"What are you doing here, and why have you got a gun with you?"</p>

<p>"It's a long story. I'll tell you later. Wait, why don't you give Sam the kiss of life?"</p>

<p>"I don't need the kiss of life. Are you out of your mind?"</p>

<p>"All right, all right. We'd better go back to the castle and find you some dry clothes."</p>

<p>"Pity. I was hoping for a quiet morning's fishing."</p>

<p>Jasper rowed the boat over to the bank of the loch, and they got out. Detective Sergeant Honeybone picked up Sam in her beautiful, strong arms, and they started walking down the road towards the castle.</p>

<p>As they passed the bus stop, Jasper walked over to Judy, who was staring up at the sky.</p>

<p>"Good morning, you beautiful creature. Going shopping? Don't forget lunch is at 12:30."</p>

<p>Judy turned her back, tears streaming down her face. "Don't talk to me about lunch. I'm going to Rio to see Sam."</p>

<p>"But Sam's here."</p>

<p>"Don't try to talk round me. I've made up my mind."</p>

<p>She turned around and looked across the road.</p>

<p>Standing with a soft smile on her lips, looking down at Sam, with him lying in her arms with his eyes closed.</p>

<p>Judy's mouth fell open.</p>

<p>"Who is that woman?"</p>

<p>"Let me introduce my sister Isabelle. Isabelle, this is Judy."</p>

<p>"If you're Jasper's sister, why is your name Honeybone?"</p>

<p>"It's a long story."</p>

<p>"Don't kiss Sam. Put that man down at once."</p>

<p>"Who is that woman? The girl's very wet."</p>

<p>"I'm a doctor. I'm looking after him."</p>

<p>"Oh no. You're not playing doctors with my Sam. He's not your Sam. He's my Sam."</p>

<p>"Come on, ladies, please. Stop!"</p>

<p>"Sam, this woman is mad."</p>

<p>"It's all right, Sam. Don't pay any attention to her. I'll look after you."</p>

<p>"Sam, where are you going? Sam, come back!"</p>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapters 21-25</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Полиция и Лох-несское чудовище</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">"North East Highlands Police Control, calling all cars. Calling all cars. The hijackers of the Boeing 707 from Rio are believed to be in the Loch Ness area after leaving the plane by parachute about 20 minutes ago. Proceed at once to the vicinity of Castle Clan Donald and begin searching. Description as follows. Man, British, medium height, dark hair, small moustache, small brown eyes, wearing a blue suit and black shoes. He is believed to be Sam Watson, who is wanted in connection with a series of bank robberies. He is carrying a gun and may be dangerous. Woman, nationality unknown, tall, blonde, blue eyes. Attractive, athletic build, wearing dark clothes and shoes. Nothing is known about her identity. Approach these people with caution. Repeat. Approach with caution."</p>

<p>When Jock McVitie was a little boy, he always wanted to be a bus driver. His father hoped he would go into the family business and didn't think a bus driver was the right thing to be. But Jock just wasn't interested. He loved buses. All kinds of buses. He loved the way they looked, the smell of diesel fuel, and most of all the wonderful noise they made.</p>

<p>When he was 14, he went on his first real holiday, a bus trip to the south of England and back. When he left school two years later, he went straight into the Highland Bus Company.</p>

<p>Now, Jock was one of the company's most experienced drivers, working on the Fort William to Inverness route. This morning was fairly typical. He had eight passengers on board. He would probably pick up one or two more on the way. He was about 20 minutes late. Jock had stopped to pick up a parcel and had chatted at the Strathness Post Office, but it didn't matter. Time was not a word of any great importance in the Scottish Highlands.</p>

<p>Jock leaned forward and looked at his watch and smiled. It was a lovely day. The sun was shining on the loch, and the bus was running beautifully. Jock changed gear as they started up a long hill towards Clan Donald Castle.</p>

<p>Loch Ness is a very deep lake in the north-eastern Highlands of Scotland. It is overlooked by wooded hills and wild moorland, the perfect setting for strange and unexplained events.</p>

<p>In 1933, a motorist on the new lakeside road saw a tremendous upheaval in the loch. The waters churned and boiled, and a huge monster, its body the size of a whale, broke the surface. The incident was reported in the local paper, and soon the national press was buzzing with news of what came to be called the Loch Ness Monster.</p>

<p>But legends of large water creatures in Loch Ness go back much further than 1933. In the 6th century A.D., the Irish missionary St. Columba was said to have banished a monster which attacked a swimmer. And local folk tales, going back centuries, speak of water horses and water bulls inhabiting Loch Ness.</p>

<p>Scientists have seriously suggested that large creatures may have been stranded in the loch when, 60 million years ago, it was cut off from the sea. Perhaps their descendants live there still. But despite hazy photographs, mostly highly magnified, of strange humps in the water, there is very little evidence as yet to go on.</p>

<p>In her fast Porsche, Dr. Wagner was getting a little impatient. She was in a hurry to get to her hotel in Inverness and have a bath and a rest, after driving all night from London. But for the last 15 miles she had been stuck behind a bus which was driving very slowly in the middle of the road, and it seemed impossible to get past.</p>

<p>"Calm down, Mary," she said to herself. "You've got plenty of time."</p>

<p>She started thinking about the holiday that was just starting. Every year she drove up to the Scottish Highlands and spent two weeks looking for the Loch Ness Monster. Dr. Wagner was a member of the West London Society for the Investigation of Strange and Unexplained Phenomena. And she was very interested in monsters, ghosts, flying saucers and things of that kind.</p>

<p>She had never yet seen anything in Loch Ness, but she always had a wonderful holiday and went back home feeling happy and relaxed. She had a feeling about this year though. This year was going to be special. Somehow she just knew.</p>

<p>A sudden noise brought her out of her dreams. She looked in the mirror. Behind her the road was full of police cars, with lights flashing and sirens howling. Dr. Wagner frowned. She didn't like police cars.</p>

<p>With all that noise, she'd never get past the bus.</p>

<p>Down at the bottom of Loch Ness, things were very calm. The monster scratched her ear with the third leg on the left and decided that it was time to do something. She didn't usually go up to the surface during the day because the light hurt her eyes. But she was getting bored out of her mind sitting down at the bottom of the loch, with nothing to do except talk to the fish.</p>

<p>She scratched her ear again, yawned, stretched and started swimming slowly up towards the light.</p>

<p>A little later, the monster reached the surface, stuck her head and 15 metres of neck out of the water, and looked around. She closed her eyes and opened them again. It was a little difficult to understand what was happening.</p>

<p>Scotland was generally a fairly quiet place, but today a lot of things seemed to be going on. On the bank of the loch, two women were fighting. A man was trying to stop them. Another man was trying to learn to swim. The road was full of cars with pretty blue lights on top coming from all directions. A helicopter landed on the bank of the loch and some soldiers got out and lay down.</p>

<p>There was a bus driving along very slowly with the driver looking out of the window. The bus driver caught sight of the monster and drove into the loch. The two women stopped fighting and stood with their mouths open. All the police cars crashed into each other. The sports car stopped and the woman got out and started taking photographs.</p>

<p>It was all too much. The monster closed her eyes and went back down to the bottom of the loch.</p>
