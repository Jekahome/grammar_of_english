<summary>Chapter One</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter One</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">The Flying Doctor</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Joanna Jimbuku is a nurse. She lives and works in North Australia. Her boss is Bob Mills, a flying doctor. He and Joanna go everywhere in the Bluebird, Bob's plane.</p>

<p>One morning in August, Joanna answers the phone.</p>

<p>"Hello?" she says. "Dr. Mills' office."</p>

<p>Then, a baby.</p>

<p>"Yes, I see. And you're in Womara? Okay. We can be there in half an hour."</p>

<p>Five minutes later, Joanna and Bob are in the Bluebird.</p>

<p>"How old is this baby?" asks Bob.</p>

<p>"Eighteen months," says Joanna.</p>

<p>After a moment, Bob looks at her. "Womara's near your hometown, isn't it?"</p>

<p>"Yes. That's right." Joanna smiles. "It's a very small place."</p>

<p>She looks out of the window. "There it is," she says. "Can you see between those trees and the river?"</p>

<p>Down on the ground, eight or nine people are waiting for the flying doctor. They take him and Joanna to a small house. The baby's mother, Jane, is inside it.</p>

<p>Bob looks at the little girl for 20 minutes. Then, he says, "I'm sorry, but Mary is very, very ill. It's her heart. She must go to a hospital in Sydney."</p>

<p>"Sydney?" says Jane.</p>

<p>"Yes." Bob puts his hand on the woman's arm. "But it's okay. You can go with her."</p>

<p>Jane looks sad. "How? I've got six other children. I can't just leave them."</p>

<p>Suddenly, Joanna has an idea. "Wait a minute," she says. "Why don't I go to Sydney with Mary? After all, I'm on holiday for two weeks after today."</p>

<p>"Good idea," says the flying doctor.</p>

<p>They look at Jane.</p>

<p>"Okay," she says.</p>

<p>24 hours later, Joanna arrives in Sydney for the first time.</p>

<p>Two nurses are waiting at the airport. They drive her and Mary very fast to a big hospital. Then they take Mary into room 308. A doctor's waiting there.</p>

<p>"Hello," he says. "My name's Paul Griffin."</p>

<p>Joanna smiles at him. "Hello."</p>

<p>Then she asks the nurses, "Can I help?"</p>

<p>Joanna spends all day at the hospital. But after eight hours, she's very tired. Paul Griffin sees this.</p>

<p>"Why don't you stay with me and my wife tonight?" he asks.</p>

<p>"Thanks," says Joanna. "That's really nice of you."</p>

<p>At six o'clock, she and Paul drive to his house.</p>

<p>There, Joanna meets Paul's wife, Fran. She meets their daughter too. Her name is Polly.</p>

<p>After dinner, Paul shows Joanna to her room. Joanna sits on the bed.</p>

<p>"Is Mary going to be okay?" she asks.</p>

<p>"I don't know," Paul replies. Then he says, "But we're going to do everything we can."</p>

<summary>Chapter Two</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Two</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Mary's Heart</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">After that, Joanna goes to the hospital every day. She helps the nurses and sits beside Mary's bed.</p>

<p>For three days, nothing happens. Then one afternoon, Joanna's reading a book in room 308. Suddenly, she hears a noise.</p>

<p>"What's that?" she thinks. Then she hears it again.</p>

<p>She stands up and walks to Mary's bed.</p>

<p>"Mary, it's you. You're sitting up. You're talking."</p>

<p>She runs out of the room and finds Paul.</p>

<p>"Quick, Dr. Griffin," she says. "It's Mary. Come and see."</p>

<p>Later that day, Joanna telephones Bob Mills.</p>

<p>"Yes, that's right. Mary's going to be okay," she says with a big smile. "I can bring her home next week."</p>

<p>That evening, the Griffins take Joanna to an expensive restaurant. Joanna sits next to the window. Outside, she can see the lights of Sydney.</p>

<p>"This is beautiful," she says.</p>

<p>Next morning at breakfast, Paul says, "Joanna, you can stay here at home today. After all, Mary's okay now."</p>

<p>"No thanks," says Joanna. "I want to be with her."</p>

<p>At the hospital, Paul sees Sister Clark.</p>

<p>"One of my girls is leaving next week and we need someone new," she says. "What about Joanna Jimbuku? She's a very good nurse. Do you think she wants a job here?"</p>

<p>"Me?" says Joanna that evening. She looks at Paul.</p>

<p>"Why not?" he says. "After all, Sister Clark's right. You're a very good nurse."</p>

<p>Joanna is quiet.</p>

<p>"Don't say anything now," Paul tells her. "Just think about it. That's all I'm asking."</p>

<p>He walks away, then turns. "But not for too long, okay?"</p>

<p>The next day is Saturday. Joanna goes to the hospital and sees Mary.</p>

<p>"Hello, little one," she says. "You're going home on Monday." Then she thinks, "Or are we going home?"</p>

<p>Two hours later, Fran and Polly meet Joanna for lunch.</p>

<p>"Oh, Joanna. You are going to stay in Sydney, aren't you?" says Polly.</p>

<p>"I don't know," says Joanna.</p>

<p>Later in the afternoon, Joanna goes for a long walk. The weather is hot. She watches the people in the streets. She looks at all the shops, cinemas, museums and theaters too.</p>

<p>"Sydney's great," she thinks. "It's very beautiful and there's a lot to see and do. But do I really want to live here? That's the question."</p>

<p>Across the street, there's a shop window. Joanna turns and looks in it. She can see a big painting of some red hills.</p>

<p>"Wait a minute," she thinks. "I know that place. It's near Womara."</p>

<p>Ten minutes later, a man comes out of the shop.</p>

<p>"Are you okay?" he asks.</p>

<p>Joanna smiles. Her eyes are wet.</p>

<p>"Yes," she says. "I'm fine. Really. I'm fine."</p>

<p>Two days later, Bob gets into the Bluebird. Joanna is with him. She waves goodbye to Jane and Mary.</p>

<p>Bob starts the plane and pushes his hat back.</p>

<p>"Listen, thanks for everything, Joanna. This is in Sydney, I mean. It's a beautiful city, isn't it?"</p>

<p>Joanna looks at the red hills of Womara. Then she smiles too.</p>

<p>"Yes," she says. "But it's good to be home."</p>
