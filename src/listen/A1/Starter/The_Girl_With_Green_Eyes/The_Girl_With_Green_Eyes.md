  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Greg is a porter at the Shepton Hotel in New York. After work, he always goes for a coffee across the street. A girl is sitting in the coffee shop near the window.</p>

<p>"It's her again," thinks Greg. "She's here every afternoon. I'm going to say hello."</p>

<p>Greg goes into the coffee shop and gets a coffee. Then he goes across to the girl's table.</p>

<p>"Hi," he says. "I'm Greg. Can I sit with you?"</p>

<p>"Okay," she says. "I'm Cassie."</p>

<p>"I come here for a coffee every day after work," says Greg. "I'm a porter at the Shepton Hotel." He smiles. "You have beautiful green eyes."</p>

<p>"Have I?" she says. She doesn't smile.</p>

<p>"Is something wrong, Cassie?" he asks. "You don't look very happy."</p>

<p>"It's nothing," she says.</p>

<p>"Tell me," says Greg. "Maybe I can help."</p>

<p>"Well," she begins.</p>

<p>"Go on," says Greg.</p>

<p>"My stepfather is staying at the Shepton Hotel," she says. "He has my sketchbooks. I'm an artist. When I ask him to give them to me, he says, no. I want you to come back home to Boston. But I don't want to go back to Boston. He's not nice."</p>

<p>"How long is he going to be at the hotel?" asks Greg.</p>

<p>"Two more days," says Cassie. "He's in room 724."</p>

<p>"Maybe I can get the sketchbooks for you," says Greg. "Maybe I can get into his room."</p>

<p>"Can you?" she says.</p>

<p>"Meet me here tomorrow afternoon at 4.30," says Greg.</p>

<p>"Okay. Thank you," says Cassie. "Thank you very much." She looks at her watch. "I've got to go now. See you tomorrow."</p>

<p>When Cassie gets back to her hotel room, she makes a telephone call.</p>

<p>"Hello?" she says. "It's me. It's okay. The boy's going to do it. Yes. I'm going to get them from him tomorrow afternoon. Okay. I can meet you there the morning after at 10 a.m. Don't forget to bring the money. $2,000."</p>

<div class="book-page">

  <div class="chapter-title"></div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Next morning, Greg arrives at work early. He goes to find room 724.</p>

<p>"I have to wait for Cassie's stepfather to go out," he thinks. "I don't know his name, but it doesn't matter."</p>

<p>Greg watches the door of room 724. He sees the girl come to clean the rooms. Some minutes later, a man comes out of room 724.</p>

<p>"That must be Cassie's stepfather," thinks Greg.</p>

<p>He waits for the man to leave, and for the cleaning girl to go into room 724. "I need to look in the room," he thinks.</p>

<p>Greg waits for the girl to leave the bedroom. Then, he goes into the room and begins to look for the sketchbooks.</p>

<p>"I must be quick," he thinks. "Where are they?"</p>

<p>Suddenly, Greg sees the sketchbooks by the bed. "Got them," he thinks. He begins to look at the pictures. "Wow, Cassie is a great artist. These are good."</p>

<p>Greg is leaving the room when he hears the cleaning girl call out to him.</p>

<p>"Hey," she says. "What are you doing? Come back."</p>

<p>Greg does not stop.</p>

<summary>Chapter 3</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter 3</div>
  <div class="chapter-title"></div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Greg finishes work that afternoon and leaves the hotel through a door in the next street. When he gets near the front of the hotel, he sees the man from room 724.</p>

<p>"That's him again," thinks Greg. "That's Cassie's stepfather."</p>

<p>Greg waits for the man to go into the hotel. Then, he goes to the coffee shop. Cassie is at the same table in the coffee shop. Greg gets a coffee and goes to sit with her.</p>

<p>"I have them," he says, and he puts the sketchbooks on the table.</p>

<p>"That's great," says Cassie. "How can I thank you, Greg?"</p>

<p>"You can meet me later," he says. "After I go home and change out of my porter's uniform."</p>

<p>"I'm sorry, Greg," says Cassie. "I can't tonight. Maybe tomorrow night."</p>

<p>"Okay," says Greg. "Tomorrow night. We can get something to eat first."</p>

<p>"Yes, okay," says Cassie. "I have to go now, but meet me here tomorrow evening at six o'clock."</p>

<p>Cassie gets up, ready to leave.</p>

<p>"Don't forget your key," says Greg.</p>

<p>"Oh, thanks," says Cassie. She takes it from him quickly. "See you tomorrow."</p>

<p>Greg is finishing his coffee when he sees his friend, Mike.</p>

<p>"You're in trouble, Greg," says Mike. "Jake Russo's sketchbooks are not in his room. Room 724. And there are CCTV pictures of you coming from that room."</p>

<p>"Who is Jake Russo?" asks Greg. Suddenly, he's not feeling very well.</p>

<p>"An artist," says Mike. "His pictures sell for thousands of dollars. And his sketchbooks sell for thousands too. Do you have them?"</p>

<p>"No, a girl has them," says Greg.</p>

<p>"What girl?" asks Mike.</p>

<p>"A girl with green eyes," says Greg. And he tells Mike about Cassie and her stepfather.</p>

<p>"But it's not true," says Mike, when Greg finishes speaking. "Jake Russo doesn't have a wife or a stepdaughter. You have to find that girl and get the sketchbooks, Greg. And you have to do it before the police find you."</p>

<p>Greg walks home to his one-room apartment. "How am I going to find her?" he thinks. "She has Jake Russo's sketchbooks now, so she's not going to meet me again. Is she going to sell them for a lot of money?"</p>

<p>There is a police car in the street, next to Greg's apartment. "Oh no," he thinks. "They're waiting for me. I can't get into my apartment now."</p>

<summary>Chapter 4</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter 4</div>
  <div class="chapter-title"></div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">That night, Greg sleeps on a train in the subway. But early the next morning, "Hey you," says a man. He's the subway cleaner. "Get up. You can't sleep here all day."</p>

<p>"Sorry," says Greg. People are arriving to get their trains now. One of them stops and looks at a photograph in his newspaper, and then at Greg.</p>

<p>"It's him," thinks the man. "It's the young man in the newspaper photograph. The police are looking for him."</p>

<p>Suddenly, Greg sees the man looking at him, and he sees his picture on the front of the man's newspaper. "I have to get out of here quickly," he thinks.</p>

<p>The man with the newspaper finds a policeman. "It's him," he tells the policeman. "The hotel porter."</p>

<p>"Hey, you!" The policeman calls to Greg. Greg runs.</p>

<p>"Stop!" calls the policeman. Greg doesn't stop.</p>

<p>"I have to get out of this uniform," thinks Greg. "People know I'm the porter. Maybe Mike can give me something different to wear."</p>

<p>When he gets to Mike's apartment, Mike opens the door.</p>

<p>"I need something to wear," Greg tells him. "I can't go back to my apartment. I have pictures in the newspaper, and yes, I know," says Mike. "It's okay. Come in."</p>

<p>"Where is the girl?" asks Mike. "Do you know?"</p>

<p>"No," says Greg.</p>

<p>"What can you remember about her?" says Mike. "There must be something to help you find her."</p>

<p>"Yes," Greg says suddenly. "Her key. A key to a room in the Dolphin Hotel. It's a small, cheap hotel near 42nd Street. She must be staying there."</p>

<p>"What's the time?"</p>

<p>"Nearly nine o'clock," says Mike.</p>

<p>"Maybe she's there now," says Greg. "I have to go. Thanks, Mike."</p>

<p>Twenty minutes later, Greg is near the Dolphin Hotel. "Am I too late?" he thinks. Suddenly, Greg sees Cassie come out of the hotel.</p>

<p>"Cassie!" he calls. "Wait! I have to talk to you."</p>

<p>Cassie sees him, but she doesn't stop. There is a car waiting for her. She says something to the driver and gets into it quickly.</p>

<p>"Oh, no," says Greg.</p>

<p>"Pier 83," says an old man in the street. Greg looks at him. "What?"</p>

<p>"The girl is going to Pier 83, West 42nd Street, for the Circle Line boat," says the old man.</p>

<p>Greg smiles. "Thanks," he says. And he begins to run.</p>

<summary>Chapter 5</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter 5</div>
  <div class="chapter-title"></div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Greg runs all the way to Pier 83. People are getting on to the boat, and he gets a ticket.</p>

<p>On the boat, Greg looks for Cassie. But a woman with a newspaper is looking at him.</p>

<p>"It's the young man in the picture," she tells the man with her. "Call the police, Eddie."</p>

<p>The man gets his phone and calls the police. Some time later, Greg sees Cassie. She is talking to a man. But Greg does not see two policemen get on the boat. They quickly find Greg.</p>

<p>"Where are Jake Russo's sketchbooks?" the first policeman asks him.</p>

<p>"I don't have them," says Greg. "You don't want me. You want her. She has the sketchbooks."</p>

<p>"Hey, look. He's right," says the second policeman.</p>

<p>The two policemen move quickly to Cassie and the man.</p>

<p>"I don't understand," says Cassie. "How...?"</p>

<p>"The two of you are coming with us," says one of the policemen. "You're in trouble."</p>

<p>Later, Greg and a policeman take the sketchbooks back to the hotel. Jake Russo is very happy to see them again.</p>

<p>"I'm sorry, Mr Russo," says Greg, and tells him everything.</p>

<p>"It's okay," says the artist, smiling. "But stay away from girls with beautiful green eyes."</p>
