<p>"Yes," says the bus driver. "There's a nice motel across the river. It's not expensive."</p>

<p>"Is there a bus to San Francisco tomorrow?" asks Kenny.</p>

<p>"Yes," says the bus driver. "There's a bus every day."</p>

  <div class="chapter-title">Give me the money!</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">In a street in Los Angeles, somebody is robbing a security guard outside a supermarket. The robber is sitting on a motorcycle and has a gun.</p>

<p>"Give me the money!" the robber tells the security guard.</p>

<p>"OK, OK!" says the security guard, and gives the robber the money.</p>

<p>Then the robber rides away.</p>

<p>But a camera is taking pictures. After the robbery, the supermarket manager phones the police.</p>

<p>"Yes," he says. "A robbery. Yes, long blond hair, and with a motorcycle. A man or woman? We don't know. Yes, we have some pictures. OK! Quickly."</p>

<summary>Chapter Three</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Three</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Room 23</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">In the little town, 50 kilometers away from Los Angeles, Kenny goes into the motel and asks for a room.</p>

<p>"Room 23," says the man at the desk. He gives Kenny a key.</p>

<p>"Thank you," says Kenny. And he goes up to his room.</p>

<p>An hour later, Kenny comes down to eat. "I'm hungry," he thinks.</p>

<p>He sees a girl arrive on a motorcycle. She walks to the front desk, and the woman gives her a key.</p>

<p>"Room 17," says the woman at the desk.</p>

<p>"I think I know her face," thinks Kenny. "Is she somebody famous?"</p>

  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>

    <p class="first-para">Early the next morning, Kenny puts on the TV in his room. A newsreader is talking about the supermarket robbery. There are some pictures from the supermarket camera. Kenny watches the news.</p>

<p>"The robber is somebody with long blond hair and with a motorcycle," says the newsreader.</p>

<p>Suddenly, Kenny remembers the girl at the motel desk.</p>

<p>"Is it her?" he thinks. "Is she the robber?"</p>

<summary>Chapter Five</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Five</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Can I sit here?</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">He goes for breakfast. The girl is eating her breakfast at a table near the window. Kenny looks at her.</p>

<p>"Is it her?" he thinks. "I think I know her," but he walks across to her table.</p>

<p>"Can I sit here?" he asks.</p>

<p>The girl looks up. "Well... OK," she says.</p>

<p>Kenny sits down.</p>

<p>"My name's Kenny," he says.</p>

<p>"Pleased to meet you," she says. "I'm Mel."</p>

<p>"Where are you from?" asks Kenny.</p>

<p>"San Francisco," says Mel. "I'm going there later this morning."</p>

<p>"Where are you from?"</p>

<p>"Boston," says Kenny. "I'm going across America by bus. And I'm going to San Francisco later this morning, too."</p>

<p>"Are you?" the girl says. "I don't like buses. I like motorcycles."</p>

<p>"Look, excuse me. I need to make a phone call." She gets up and leaves the room.</p>

<summary>Chapter Six</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Six</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Not the police</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">A young man near the door is watching Mel. "Why is he watching her?" thinks Kenny. "Does he know her face, too? Is he thinking she's the girl from the robbery, too?"</p>

<p>Kenny goes to sit next to the young man.</p>

<p>"Hi," he says. "My name's Kenny Muir."</p>

<p>"Hi," says the young man. "I'm Ned."</p>

<p>They begin talking. Kenny tells Ned about the news of the robbery on TV. He looks across at Mel.</p>

<p>"Are you thinking that's the same girl?" he asks Ned.</p>

<p>Ned looks across at her. "I don't know," he says. "Perhaps it is."</p>

<p>"What can we do?" says Kenny. "She's leaving soon. Let's phone the police. I think it's her."</p>

<p>"No!" Ned says quickly. "Not the police. Wait."</p>

<p>Kenny looks carefully at him. "What's the matter? Are you afraid of something?" he asks.</p>

<p>"No, but perhaps she's got a gun!" says Ned. "I'm sorry."</p>

<summary>Chapter Seven</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Seven</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Perhaps I'm wrong</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">"Is it her?" thinks Kenny. "I want to know. Perhaps the money from the robbery is in her room."</p>

<p>Kenny goes upstairs. Suddenly, he sees the girl again. She is coming out of room 17. Kenny watches her. She locks her door and goes downstairs.</p>

<p>"How can I get into her room?" he thinks.</p>

<p>Then Kenny sees a woman working in the motel.</p>

<p>"Excuse me," says Kenny. "Can you open the door of my room? My key's in the car. It's room 17."</p>

<p>"Yes, of course," she says, and opens the door of room 17.</p>

<p>"Thank you," says Kenny, and he goes into the room.</p>

<p>Kenny searches the room, but he cannot find the money from the robbery.</p>

<p>"Perhaps I'm wrong about her," he thinks. Then he hears somebody opening the door with a key.</p>

<p>It's Mel!</p>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Eight</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">I must get away</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Kenny runs into the bathroom. A minute later, he hears Mel talking into the phone.</p>

<p>"Hi, Fran," says Mel. "No, I'm at the motel. No, I'm leaving in an hour. Yes, OK."</p>

<p>Kenny looks out carefully from the bathroom. He cannot get to the door. But he can get to the window. He moves quickly.</p>

<p>Mel puts down the phone. Kenny watches her through the window.</p>

<p>"I must get away from here!" he thinks.</p>

<p>Kenny cannot go back into room 17, but he can open the window to room 18.</p>

<p>"I must get away from here."</p>

<summary>Chapter Nine</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Nine</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">You're the robber</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Kenny climbs into room 18. There is nobody there. He goes across to the door, but then he sees something.</p>

<p>"What's that?" he thinks. "Is it a wig?"</p>

<p>It is. It's a blond wig!</p>

<p>Kenny searches the room. "Here's a black motorcycle coat," he says to himself. "And what's this in this bag? The money from the robbery!"</p>

<p>Kenny hears somebody coming. He wants to get out of the room, but he cannot.</p>

<p>Ned comes into the room. He sees Kenny.</p>

<p>"What are you doing in my room?" says Ned. Then he sees the blond wig and the money. He looks at Kenny again.</p>

<p>"You know!" says Ned.</p>

<p>"Yes, I know!" says Kenny. "You're the Los Angeles supermarket robber!"</p>

<p>Suddenly, Ned takes a gun from his coat.</p>

<p>"Don't move!" he says, and he takes the bag from Kenny.</p>

<p>"You can't get away," says Kenny.</p>

<p>"Yes, I can," says Ned. He hits Kenny on the head. Then he locks him in room 18.</p>

<summary>Chapter Ten</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Ten</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">I can't swim</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">A minute later, Mel is looking across at the river from her room. Suddenly, she sees a man running.</p>

<p>"What's the matter with him?" she thinks. "Why is he running?"</p>

<p>Then she sees Kenny come out of the next room.</p>

<p>"That man!" says Kenny. "He's the Los Angeles supermarket robber! Stop him, somebody!"</p>

<p>Ned hears him, and looks up. Then he gets on his motorcycle and drives off quickly.</p>

<p>"I can stop him," says Mel.</p>

<p>She climbs down from her room. She runs around the motel, gets on her motorcycle, and goes after Ned.</p>

<p>Mel rides her motorcycle quickly towards the river. When she gets to the river, she jumps across the river on the motorcycle and stops in front of Ned.</p>

<p>He cannot stop, and his motorcycle goes into the river.</p>

<p>"Help! Help!" he cries. "I can't swim!"</p>

<summary>Chapter Eleven</summary>

<div class="book-page">
  <div class="chapter-label">Chapter Eleven</div>
  <div class="chapter-title">Can we go slowly, please?</div>
  <div class="chapter-divider"><span> ~ ~ ~ </span></div>
  <div class="book-body">
    <p class="first-para">Some people help Ned out of the water. More people are coming from the motel.</p>

<p>Kenny runs across to Mel. "Are you OK?" he asks her.</p>

<p>"Yes, I'm OK," she says. "Are you all right?"</p>

<p>"Yes," says Kenny. "I'm all right."</p>

<p>Soon after, the police arrive and take Ned away. Kenny tells a policeman his story. Then, Mel tells the policewoman her story.</p>

<p>"Do I know your face?" the policewoman asks Mel. "Are you famous?"</p>

<p>Mel laughs. "Perhaps you do," she says. "I'm on TV sometimes."</p>

<p>"Now I remember you," Kenny says to Mel. "You're Mel Palmer, the famous stunt girl! I see you on 'Stunt Girls, USA' on TV sometimes."</p>

<p>Mel smiles. "Yes, that's me," she says. "Now, do you want to come to San Francisco on my motorcycle, or do you want to go on the bus?"</p>

<p>"I'm coming with you!" says Kenny, laughing. "But can we go slowly, please?"</p>
